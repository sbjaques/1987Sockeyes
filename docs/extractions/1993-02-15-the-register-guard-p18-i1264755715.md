The toughest part for beginners is mastering skating and then tackling the moves and stick-and-puck work of the game. Without knowing how to skate well, trying to play hockey is like trying to play tennis without knowing how to walk. For players that progress beyond the novice level, there is the Lane Amateur Hockey Association, a six-team league that plays on Wednesday nights from 7 to 11:30. There are also a pair of 40-years and older teams that play on Sunday nights. Players from the latter league frequently play in the LAHA as well. "We're now generating hockey people from the community and not relying on people who move here," Smith said. "That's great." To help cultivate the future hockey lovers, Lane County Ice runs youth hockey sessions on Wednesdays and Saturdays (for more information call Lane County Ice at 687-3615).

Before the opening of Lane County Ice three years ago, the last time Smith played an organized hockey game was for the Canadian Navy in 1945. "There's hockey all around us here, it's just that it only found its expression when this rink opened," Smith said. "There's a surprisingly large number of people in this town from Minnesota, Illinois, Wisconsin and especially Canada who love the game and were just waiting for a place to play." Smith and Bahls both believe there's an undiscovered appeal to hockey for newcomers. The sport combines the aerobics of nearly constant movement with the fun and skill of a game - in a way like basketball. "I've played both," Smith said, "and basketball doesn't hold a candle to the kick you get out of hockey."
