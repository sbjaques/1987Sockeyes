Sockeyes deflate Millionaires 4-3 RICHMOND, B.C. (CP) - The Richmond Sockeyes overcame the effects of a 10-day layoff to register a 4-3 win over the Quesnel Millionaires in the opening game of the B.C. Tier-Two junior hockey championship Friday night. The B.C. Junior Hockey League champions held period leads of 3-1 and 3-2 over the Peace-Cariboo champions in the quest for the Mowatt Cup. Dave Tomlinson and Jim Gunn each had one goal and one assist for Richmond while Mike Claringbull and Nanaimo product Bill added singles. Three Sockeyes goals were disallowed - one was kicked in, another was scored while a Richmond player was in the crease and the other came while a penalty was being called. Quesnel's marksmen were Marty Peters, Clint Schwartz and Greg Schulte. Sockeyes goaltender Frank Romeo stopped 25 shots while Damian Holland and Scooter Hanson combined for 37 saves for the Millionaires. The second game of the best -three series goes tonight +. In Kamloops, the Blazers blanked Victoria Cougars 7-0 in Western Hockey League action Friday night to register a 5-0 sweep of the teams' best-of-nine West Division semifinals. Kamloops plays host to the Portland Winter Hawks Thursday for the first game of best-of-nine Division final. Two shorthanded goals early in the second period broke open what had been a close 1-0 game. Steve Nemeth and Darcy Norton both scored while teammate Kelly Para sat out a five-minute major. Other Blazers' goals came from Mike Needham, Robin Bawa, Warren Babe, Kelly Para and Steve Nemeth with his second of the night. Kamloops led 1-0 after one period and 5-0 through two periods, outshooting Victoria 51-24 with Stacey Nickel picking up the shutout. "When we lost to Victoria early in the season it was cause we lost our composure," said Kamloops assistant coach Donnie Moores. "We were very patient in winning this series."
