"They're the biggest BU fans you can find." No more so than last weekend, when Sullivan limped onto the ice at Michigan State a bit sooner than expected. Sullivan had badly sprained an ankle in the season finale against (who else?) BC and was expected to sit out the best-of-three series against Michigan State. "It was really hard sitting up in the stands, just watching," he says, "but there was little I could do about it." Except teammate Dave Tomlinson was banished from the second Michigan State game because of a spearing incident, "and it all came down to me, coach Parker and the trainer," says Sullivan. "We decided I'd play." We? "Well. ... me." Parker said he expected Sullivan "would play only a few shifts ... it was a case where we really needed a body. So he goes out and scores a big [game-winning] goal in that game and comes back with a big assist in Sunday's game."
