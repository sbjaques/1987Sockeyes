## Paragraphs

New Westminster Bruins have completed a Western Hockey League trade to divest themselves of Erin Ginnell, son of Pat Ginnell, the coach the Bruins dismissed Nov. 24. Ginnell, who had 17 goals, 13 assists and 14 penalty minutes in 22 games with the Bruins, said earlier this week that he wanted to play in the WHL, but not in New Westminster. Ernie McLean, who took over as coach Nov. 28, announced Friday that Ginnell has been traded to Calgary Wranglers for Darin Kimble, a 17- year-old all-purpose forward. Kimble, 6'3" and 215 pounds, compiled 28 goals and 32 assists with 264 penalty minutes last season in the Saskatchewan Junior. Hockey League. The Bruins also received the rights to Jason Talo, a bantam-age centre playing midget in Richmond. Also acquired by New Westminster was Alan May, 20, a 6-1, 200-pound right winger who had 51 goals, 47 points and 409 penalty minutes last season with Estevan Bruins of the SJHL.
