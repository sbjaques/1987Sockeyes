Cory Watson, who is 6-foot-4 and weighs in the vicinity of 200 pounds, has stepped up from the New Westminster intermediate club. "We think he can step up and do quite well in junior," said Fulton. Another promising newcomer is Craig Smith, a Junior B player from Ottawa who "did quite well" in the Ontario league. Richmond Sockeyes: New coach Pat Ryan, who called the shots for the Richmond intermediate club last season, said only a handful of players returned from last season. "It's not looking too bad," he said, "but most of the guys haven't played together before." The Sockeyes, who finished fifth last year, are in a recovery mode. •We're still in a rebuilding process," said Ryan. "We're the underdogs for sure." Richmond will be anchored by veterans Gary Frederickson, Dean Allan, Dave Haller and goaltender Robert Cook. Three intermediate grads - Darren Goundrey, Greg Reid and Brad Dickson - - have played prominent roles in pre-season games and workouts. Dickson was the intermediate league scoring champion last season. Surrey Rebels: "We're a somewhat improved team," said coach Bob McClelland. The Rebels, who finished "second-to-last" in the 1987 season, defeated New Westminster in the recent tournament and was only narrowly beaten by both Coquitlam and Burnaby. Edmonton, which played half of the league schedule and placed last in 1987, won't be competing this year. McClelland said his squad has seven holdovers from last season and the newcomers are looking good. "They're taking up the slack," he added. Veterans Justin Bosher and Todd Evans, who combined for 30 points in the pre-season tournament, anchor the team along with other holdovers Ken Hinds, Neil Gailey and Dennis Bull. Rookie Dan Rodrigues (from the Killarney Junior B organization) and Pat Hagel, who sat out last season with a shoulder injury, have also been impressive. McClelland has particularly high hopes for the 6-foot-3, 245-pound Hagel. "We're expecting him to be our policeman," he explained.

Esquimalt also dropped a 14-6 decision to revitalized Victoria Pay Less of the Western Lacrosse Association in the annual John Crowther Memorial game Wednesday at Esquimalt Sports Centre. Legion coach Nirmal Dillon said the club is playing better with each game while adapting to his new fast-break system. Dillon said the club's running and checking improved tremendously in the second game against the Timbermen. And the tactics even gave Pay Less a little trouble at times. Several players are still missing from the lineup. Tory Thompson is nursing a broken finger suffered in the first game against Nanaimo, and three of Esquimalt's main guns, twins Gary and Paul Gait as well as Ravi Dhillon, are expected to rejoin the team about June 1 after completing studies at United States universities. In addition, Dillon expects former Victoria Cougar hockey player Greg Batters to turn out again this season. Esquimalt, which won the league playoff title and then lost in the national Minto Cup championship last year, opens its season May 7, battling the Coquitlam Adanacs at 8 p.m. at Esquimalt Sports Centre. Elsewhere in the league: Coquitlam Adanacs: Coach Les Wingrove said the team, which finished in a tie for top spot with Esquimalt and was awarded first place on the basis of games between the teams, is looking g good this year. "We've got some talent equal to last year," said Wingrove. The Adanacs only lost half a dozen players through graduation. "We're a little slow getting going, but we're getting there,' added Wingrove. Evidence of Coquitlam's strength lies with a victory in a pre-season tournament involving all the teams in the league except Esquimalt. The Adanacs beat the Richmond Sockeyes 11-9 in the final of the single-knockout tourney. Outstanding returnees include 1987 rookie-of-the-year Lorne Hotseth, Scott Parson, Ken Thomas, goaltender Jeff Dobish, Wade Dean and Wingrove's son, Trevor. Tops among the newcomers is Troy Cordingley of Brampton, who scored four goals in his first preseason game with Coquitlam. Burnaby Cablevision: "We've only lost two or three people we're looking pretty good," said coach Randy Delmonico. The Cablevision crew finished third last year behind Coquitlam and Esquimalt. "I think we're going to have a really balanced team this year," added Delmonico. One of the leading returnees in the pre-season has been Ron Anhofer, who scored four goals in a recent game against the Surrey Rebels. Tod Cullum and Steve Pongratz have also proved impressive among the veterans. Top newcomers have been Curtis Smith, who sat out last season following knee surgery after playing on the Canada Games team two years ago, and Trevor Andres, who was with the Langley Junior B club last year.
