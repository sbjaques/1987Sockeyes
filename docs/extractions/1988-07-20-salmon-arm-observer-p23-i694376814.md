Salmon Arm Observer, Wed., July 20, 1988 - 23 Sockeyes finish third in meet

The Salmon Arm Sockeyes were in Kamloops last weekend, competing in the fortieth annual Kamloops invitational swim meet. The Sockeyes were just one of 25 teams at the meet, together with all of the Okanagan clubs and several strong teams from the coast, Coquitlam, Mission, Boundary, Burnaby, and including, Langley. Arm ended up third out of the 25 teams, with 208 team aggregate points. Host team Kamloops ran away with the meet, coming up with 563 points; Coquitlam was second with 361, Boundary Bay fourth with 207, and Burnaby rounded out the top five with 141 team points. This meet was an excellent one for the top St Sockeye swimmers; Sockeye head coach Hamish Tucker estimated that one-third of B.C.'s swimming talent was at Kamloops which bodes well for those swimmers who placed well in the meet. Tucker hopes to see the team's best showing ever at the provincials, which are five weeks away. Next weekend the Sockeyes will travel to Golden, followed by the Sockeyes invitational meet in Salmon Arm and the regional championships in Revelstoke. Those who placed in the top three in an event at the regional championships are eligible to attend the provincials, held this year in Victoria.

Salmon Arm Sockeyes finished third in the Kamloops swim meet on the weekend. Seen here are medal winners (from back left) Jennifer Brown, Barry Say, David Say, and Lori Jenks. In the front row (from left) are Carmen Lazzarotto, Melissa Sawyer, Ricky Say and David Mah.

Rob Flockhart is assistant coach for Tigers

Rob Flockhart brings a wealth of hockey experience to his position as an assistant coach of the Salmon Arm Tigers junior A hockey team. Coach Tom Marsh announced the hiring of Flockhart last Wednesday. "His experience and knowledge of the game will be a big help," Marsh said enthusiastically. Ken Davies of Enderby is Marsh's other assistant coach, and will "definitely be a big help this season." Flockhart has played professional hockey for the past 12 years, right up to six months ago.
