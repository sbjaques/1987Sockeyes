By MARTY KNACK Journal Staff Writer When the Canadian women's field hockey team returned Monday from Australia, the players signed contracts promising to practise their stick work for three hours a day. The team which includes Shona Schleppe, Liz Czenczek and Deb Covey of Edmonton, plus Michelle Conn of Calgary will reassemble next month for another tour, as it prepares for the Summer Olympics in Seoul, Korea. Schleppe, Czenczek, Covey and Conn joined the team in 1986 after playing on the national junior team. They helped C Canada finish third at the World Cup two years ago and believe "team unity" is one of their strengths. The women went through a series of physiological tests Thursday and Friday, under the supervision of University of Alberta coach Dru Marshall. The testing was to determine their fitness, following the tour which began Feb. 17 and included nine test matches against Korea, the Netherlands, Great Britain, Australia and New Zealand. "It should show we're pretty fit," Czenczek said after her 12 minutes on the treadmill, at a speed of 7½ miles per hour going uphill. The battery of tests also included situps, pushups, "fat testing" and a 5,000-metre run on the Universiade Pavilion track. "It was mainly as a learning phase," Schleppe said of the Australian visit. "It was basically a feeler sort of tournament." The Canadians won only one and tied one in four test matches against the Koreans, two against the Dutch - ranked No. 1 in the world - and one each against the British, Australians and New Zealanders. But there's no cause for alarm, because the matches are used only to determine where the teams are at this stage of the season and what they have to do to be ready in September. "It's good we didn't finish first," said Czenczek. "Now we know we have to work harder." Canada is ranked sixth and is in a pool with Australia, West Germany (seventh) and Great Britain (eighth) in Seoul. The Aussies are challenging the Dutch to be the best in the world and the Canadians hope to advance into the medal round with them. "We haven't played West Germany in three years," said 1 Schleppe, discussing their hopes against a "patient" opponent. "However, we do have a tour

of West Germany, at the end of May. "We just have to go for it in every game. We have to capitalize on what opportunities we get." "Their coach predicted they'd tie all their games and make it through," Czenczek said of the strategy the West Germans are expected to employ in Korea. The Canadians rely on a defensive style and she emphasized "our aim will be to win every game 1-0" under coach Marina van der Merwe, who asked them to sign the contracts concerning their training routine during their brief stay at home. They will prepare further in their May 25-July 5 tour which includes two test matches in West Berlin, a tournament in Essen and trip to England for games and training.
