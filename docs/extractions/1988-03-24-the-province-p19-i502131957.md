By FRANK LUBA Sports Reporter It wasn't exactly a defensive gem but the Richmond Sockeyes aren't complaining. The Sockeyes' wild 10-9 win against the Vernon Lakers was their first victory against the Interior Division champions this season. More importantly, though, the comeback victory was the Coastal Division winners' first triumph in the best-of-seven B.C. Junior Hockey League championship. Lakers 9 Sockeyes 10 The Sockeyes, the defending league and Canadian junior A champions, still face an upstream battle. They trail the Lakers 3-1 going into Saturday's game in Vernon. But at least they've finally solved the puzzle of how to beat the Lakers. "Somewhere along the line we had to prove that those guys (Lakers) were mere mortals," said Richmond coach Orland Kurtenbach, who lifted starting goaltender Mike Doyle in favor of backup Dave Charron to shake up his squad in the first period and then went with just four defencemen and two lines for the final portion of the game.
