by Stephen Gold It's official! The Richmond Sockeyes B.C. Junior Hockey League team will be moving , to Chilliwack starting in the 1990-91 season. The franchise, under a new name, will be playing in Chilliwack next season, officials of the club and the District of Chilliwack announced at a press conference yesterday (Tuesday) morning. (The Progress reported on Jan. 17 that the Sockeyes would be the club transferring to this community after it was announced the week before that an unnamed BCJHL team would be moving). The transfer, however, still has to be approved by the BCJHL's board of governors. Team owner Allan Brew does not expect a problem obtaining that approval either before or at the annual general meeting in June. "Definitely it's going to be a positive move for the league." The league has nine teams, six in the Coastal Division and three in the Interior. The BCJHL is a league for junior players up to the age of 20 years and differs from Junior A (for example, the Western Hockey League) in that most its successful graduates move onto U.S. college hockey before turning professional. The Sockeyes play in the Coastal division of the league but the Chilliwack-based club may be moved to the Interior grouping, current coach and general manager Orland Kurtenbach said.
