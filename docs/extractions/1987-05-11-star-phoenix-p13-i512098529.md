Sockeyes win Centennial al Cup

HUMBOLDT - Frank Romeo looked like a walking trophy shop as he ambled into the champagne war going on in the Richmond Sockeyes' dressing g room following their 5-2 Centennial Cup-clinching win over Humboldt Broncos at the Uniplex on Saturday. With a plaque for MVP of the final game under one arm, a trophy as tournament MVP under the other, and the Centennial Cup firmly in his grasp, Romeo surveyed the scene with wide eyes, saying with a grin: "This is unbelievable. I'm the happiest guy in the world. Winning the Cup, being MVP, it's like Christmas all over again." If this was the holiday season, the Humboldt shooters would be calling the Sockeye Richmond 46-23, but stingy Romeo was netminder Scrooge. The, Broncos outshot giving away nothing. It was the third time Romeo was named player of the game during the tournament, winning the award in each of his starts. "I said to him when we were shaking hands, 'Frank, you know you really played over your head this tournament and that's what you've got to do to be great,' " said a disappointed Bernie Lynch, coach of the Broncos. "I told him he came up really big and played great in the tournament and that's the sign of a great hockey player. That's what you've got to do to be a champion."
