Last season the Tri-City Americans made their fans sweat it out, skating through 71 games and the next-to-last day of the Western Hockey League season before reserving a spot in the playoffs. Friday night, the playoff clinching met with far less excitement. There were no streamers. People weren't dancing in the aisles or hugging each other. And if it weren't for "Noisemaker Night" in the sold out Tri-Cities Coliseum, there wouldn't have been much fanfare as the Americans dueled to a 1-1 tie with the Spokane Chiefs. The point the Americans earned with the tie gave the third TriCities a 30-24-5 record and 65 points in the six-team Western Division. It also presented the Americans with at least a tie for fourth place. Last place Victoria was eliminated long ago. Fifth-place Portland, idle Friday, has 41 points and just 12 games to play. Even if the Winter Hawks won all 12 games for 24 points, that would give them 65 points. Portland plays in Spokane (25-32- 5) tonight. A loss by Portland puts the Americans in the first-round of the playoffs for sure. But using the 1- 1 outcome as evidence, Tri-City Coach Rick Kozuback said his team's offensive punch needs to find some heavy hitters. Especially if they meet the high-flying Seattle Thunderbirds in the first round. "(The Chiefs) played great. It was the most solid defensive effort I've seen out of Spokane," said Kozuback. "'The right guys had a chance to score for us. (Kyle) Reeves. (Brian) Sakic. (Stu) Barnes. They had their chances and couldn't do anything with it." For Spokane goaltender Jeff Ferguson, the low-scoring tie meant the Chiefs are close to breaking the spell the coliseum seems to have over them. "We've had a lot of trouble in this building," said Ferguson, who stopped 45 of 46 shots. "Last year I think we managed a 44 tie.
