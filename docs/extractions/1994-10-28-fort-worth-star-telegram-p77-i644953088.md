BY BRIAN FISHMAN Special to the Star-Telegram FORT WORTH - The Fort Worth Fire made off-season roster changes, coach Steve Harrison said, to add experienced professional players to a young and talented foundation. This weekend, Harrison will get to see if the mix helps as the Fire opens its third Central Hockey League season with home games tonight against the Dallas Freeze and tomorrow night against the Memphis RiverKings at the Fort Worth-Tarrant County Convention Center. Last year, the Fire finished last in the CHL, missing the playoffs by 18 points. Harrison, in his second year as coach, said the team's lack of experience was apparent in pressure situations. The Fire won just six of 32 road games and finished last in powerplay opportunities (16.5 percent) and penalty killing (75.8 pecent). The Fire was outscored, 123-72, in the third period, losing five games and tying two after taking a lead into the final period. "I figured if we won five or six of those games we probably would've made the playoffs," Harrison said. To improve its chances in close games, particularly on the road, the Fire signed 29-year-old defenseman Bruce Bell, who played 209 National Hockey League games for four teams and was a former NHL all-rookie team member, and 28- year-old Scott Allen, an eight-year minor-league center. Bell will carry extra responsibility as an assistant coach. Harrison said Bell, who played for Quebec, St. Louis, the New York Rangers and Edmonton during his six-year NHL career, should improve the power play and can stabilize a defensive core that returns just two players. "He's going to have a big influence on the team," Harrison said. "He's a mainstay back there, and he brings a little bit of everything to the lineup as far as skill and physical toughness." The Fire will carry five defensemen, led by Bell and returning players Eric Ricard and Darren Srochenski. Srochenski had 195 penalty minutes last year with the Fire and the Wichita Thunder.
