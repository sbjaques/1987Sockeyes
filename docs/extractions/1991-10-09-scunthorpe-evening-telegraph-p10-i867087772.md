## Paragraphs

A MIDDLE Saxon settlement which was uncovered at Flixborough looks set to reveal fascinating insights on the life and times of the period. Scunthorpe Evening Telegraph reporter PHIL GOULD went to see some of the finds and talk about their significance with dig supervisor Dave Tomlinson.

The discovery has already been hailed as a Saxon Pompeii as many of the finds have been preserved in sand which over the years had been windswept across the site. Humberside archaeological unit dig supervisor Dave Tomlinson believed the uncovering of the site was of great significance for a number of reasons. "The Flixborough site was occupied during the Middle Saxon time between 670 and 870 AD and we don't know great deal about that period of history. "It will also give good information about the time, as it had not been occupied prior to the settlement and after the inhabitants left it remained unoccupied."
