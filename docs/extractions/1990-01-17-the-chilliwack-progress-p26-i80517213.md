by Stephen Gold The Richmond Sockeyes are coming to Chilliwack. The B.C. Junior Hockey League team will transfer to this community next season (this fall), The Chilliwack Progress has learned. A reliable source close to the Sockeyes organization told The Progress it is the Richmond franchise that will be based out of Chilliwack and playing its home games at the Chilliwack Coliseum come the 1990-91 hockey season. The owner of the Sockeyes would neither confirm nor deny that his team was on the move. "I guess I'm in a position right now where I have to say 'no comment'," Allan Brew said in a Jan. 11 telephone interview. "We're in an awkward position right now," he said. "So I don't want to confirm or deny anything. "We'll probably make an announcement in the next month or two." Official word that an existing franchise was moving to Chilliwack came on Jan. 8 from Jack Andrews, district recreation manager. At that time, however, the district would not state which team was on the move as the transfer still had to be officially approved by the BCJHL's board of governors.
