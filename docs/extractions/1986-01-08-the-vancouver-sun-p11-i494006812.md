## Paragraphs

By The Canadian Press Penticton Knights got four goals from Lee Davidson 11 in a 9-4 victory Tuesday night over Merritt Warriors to give the Knights a two-point edge over Kelowna Packers at the top of the B.C. Junior Hockey League's Interior Division. In the evening's only other game, Coast-Division-leading Richmond Sockeyes lost 6-3 at home to second-place Delta Flyers. In addition to Davidson's four goals, Penticton got singles from Cal Brown, Keven Melrose, David Shields, Chris Anderson and Scott Rawson. The Knights, playing under assistant coach Grant Konopaki while Rick Kozuback sits out an eight-game suspension for a brawl and for using an ineligible player, ground out the first two periods with leads of 2-1 and 4-2, then opened up with five goals in the third. Replying for the fifthplace Warriors, who were outshot 64-23, were Kevin Thorlakson, Brad Smiley, Rob Revell and Mike Catchpole. Gord Macinness scored twice as the Flyers outhustled Richmond, which Delta now trails 10 points. It was the Sockeyes' first loss to Division team.
