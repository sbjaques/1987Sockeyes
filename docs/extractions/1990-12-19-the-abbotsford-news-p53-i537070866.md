by Alan Watson Sam Styles and Brian Silverson are a couple of young guys coaching a hockey team - but that team seems intent on adding a few premature greys to their hairlines. For the second game in a row, the Abbotsford Pilots bleve a third period lead then came back to win in overtime, the latest one a 7-6 win over the first-place Richmond Sockeyes, Friday night at MSA Arena. The win puts the Pilots within two points of the league leaders in the West Coast Junior Hockey League, and with one game in hand. The two teams met again last night in Richmond (no score available before deadline). "It's hard on the coaching staff but it's great for the fans," Styles said Monday of Friday night's win, which followed on the heels of a 6-5 overtime win over the cellar-dwelling Grandview Steelers five nights earlier. "It seems to be a never saga." And, true enough to recent form, the Pilots started quickly, looked like a dominant team and then presto - things were close again. An outstanding opening period, in which they outshot Richmond 14-8 and scored three times, set the stage. After giving up one early in the second, a couple of well-earned markers put Abbotsford up 5-1 and seemingly in command. Uh-uh. Richmond struck at 15:09, 16:57 and 18:56 of the second period, the latter on the powerplay, and suddenly the game was back on. Hard-working Trevor Harms, whom Styles commended for his consistent play of late, finished off a nice three-way passing play with linemates Tyler Boucher and Kirk Olischefski to put the Pilots up by two at 3:42 of the third. But back came Richmond, fueled by the efforts of centre Travis Evans, who had a five-point night. They got one back before the period was half over and notched the equalizer with less than four minutes to play, again, on the powerplay. Then it was up to Pilots goaltender Jason Holt to bar. the door, which he did. His biggest came with less than five seconds on the clock as he first stopped a partial breakaway then blocked the rebound while lying on his back in the crease. Then, at 2:24 into overtime, centre Jay Nagamatsu, the first player from the Abbotsford-Matsqui Minor Hockey Association to actually graduate to the team since it shifted operations here from Mission three seasons ago, fed a beautiful strike of a pass to a streaking Rod Spitzig. He went in alone on Richmond goaltender Jason Scott, made a couple of moves and deposited it behind him to the delight of close to 400 fans " in attendance. Styles of course has no concrete answers to his team's propensity for blowing leads, but offered a couple of theories. "It almost seems the players can't play without pressure.
