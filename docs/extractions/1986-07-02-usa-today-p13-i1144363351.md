COLLEGES: The Loyola College (Md.) men's basket ball program has been placed on probation for one year for violations that occurred from 1982-1984, the NCAA said. The penalty does not affect the college's future eligibility for postseason competition or television appearances. Female athletes at the University of Georgia outperformed male athletes in the classroom last year with 35 percent of the 63 non-freshman women athletes receiving AllSoutheastern Conference academic honors, compared to 14 percent of the 128 men eligible. OLYMPICS: Organizers of the 1988 Seoul Summer Olympics said Monday they are waiting for the verdict on a "final" compromise offer to shift some events to neighboring North Korea, which has threatened to lead a Communist boycott of the event. International Olympic Committee president Juan Antonio Samaranch had set Monday as a deadline for a decision. The arrangement calls for North Korea to co-host four events - all of archery and table tennis, and part of road cycling and soccer. It also would stage cultural events related to the games.
