(18-33-1, sixth) Ex-Richmond Sockeye owner Bruce Taylor has brought his never -empty bank account to Queen's Park Arena. Taylor dropped his interest in the Sockeyes to purchase the Abbotsford franchise and move them to New Westminster, where he should continue his winning ways. Royals' coach Eddie Johnstone has an impressive array of talent to deal with, including high-scoring centre Frankie Biller, who notched 57 goals and 106 points in Merritt last season. A key newcomer of sorts is Jeff McLean, who scored 81 points as a 16-year-old two years ago in Richmond, and Langley. He played last season at University of North Dakota as the youngest player in U.S. college hockey. He decided to take a year off school and play with the Royals. Other recruits include WHL veterans Tye Cameron, Kurt Lehner and Alex Bell and a talented trio of youngsters from Alaska, defencemen Joe Kramer and Jason White and leftwinger Corky Brown.
