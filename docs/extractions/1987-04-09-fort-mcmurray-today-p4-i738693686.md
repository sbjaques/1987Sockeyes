RICHMOND, B.C. (CP) - The Red Deer Rustlers paid a heavy price for penalties Wednesday night as the Richmond Sockeyes fired four powerplay goals in the third period for a 6-4 Centennial Cup playoff victory. However, the comeback victory that gave Richmond a 2-0 lead in the best-of-seven Western semifinal was spoiled by a post -game incident involving a thrown stick and a Red Deer player who stormed into the stands. The third and fourth games in the rough Tier II junior hockey series will be played Saturday and Sunday in Red Deer, Alta. An elderly woman was struck by a Red Deer stick thrown into the crowd. She received a cut and medical aid while the Rustlers' Brad Wilson took a match penalty. Teammate Rick Anderson, who climbed over the glass behind the Red Deer bench and nearly came to blows with a fan, received a gross misconduct. Red Deer coach Larry Billows, who attempted to intercede between Anderson and the fan, said spectators spat on his team and there was not enough security in the Richmond Arena. The Alberta coach had no real explanation for his team's collapse.
