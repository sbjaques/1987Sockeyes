## Paragraphs

HUMBOLDT The smooth road that the Humboldt Broncos have been following to the Centennial Cup has finally revealed its first bumps. The Broncos were out-worked, out-hit and out-scored Tuesday at the Uniplex, as the Richmond Sockeyes evened their best-of-seven Abbott Cup series at one win apiece. With the teams splitting the first two games, Game Three will be played tonight in Humboldt at 7:30. The loss was Humboldt's first since being upset by Melville Millionaires in the first game of the SAJHL playoffs. After 17 consecutive wins, the loss to the Sockeyes jolted Humboldt coach Bernie Lynch from the party wagon the Broncos have been riding through the playoffs. "To be embarrassed like this in our building and in front of our fans is uncalled for and unnecessary," said Lynch. "Maybe they've forgotten what it's like to be hungry and what it's like to have to work. Somewhere between the parking lot and the arena, they vanished. "Part of the problem is that y we played /rec hockey' against Selkirk, waltzed through Lloydminster, waltzed through Yorkton and waltzed through Melville after the first game. Now they're going to have to earn that respect back." First order of business for the Broncos will be finding a way to stop Richmond's Jason Phillips, who has been a scoring threat on almost every shift. Phillips, who scored once in Monday's opener, scored three goals and added an assist on Tuesday. "I give all the credit to Brian Kozak," said Phillips. "'He set me up all over the ice.
