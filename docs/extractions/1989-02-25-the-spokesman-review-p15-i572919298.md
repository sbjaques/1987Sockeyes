At a glance: The two teams, battling Seattle for the fourth and final playoff berth in the West Division, tangle for the fourth time in a week. Barnes and Kolzig have been outstanding against Spokane. "The thing that's going to make him (Barnes) a great player is that pressure is a motivator to him," Tri-City coach John Olver said. "The more pressure, the better he plays." Kolzig leads the league in goals against average and has probably been the league's best goaltender over the past month. "His progress you look at how he's playing, and he's on a straight line up," Olver said. "'Where he's going to stop, nobody knows." The strong recent play of Barnes and Kolzig has turned them into two of the top prospects for the 1989 National Hockey League entry draft. Green, Spokane's top prospect for the June draft, also has turned in a number of strong performances of late.

KENNEWICK The Spokane Chiefs have - developed quite a magic act. Put them on a they disappear. Spokane tied the club record of 24 road losses and moved within one of the club record of nine straight games without a road win when Tri-City whipped the Chiefs 7-3 Friday night at the Tri-Cities Coliseum. The game was crucial to the playoff hopes of both teams. They meet again tonight at 7:30 at the Spokane Coliseum. Tri-City took over sole possession of fourth place in the West Division of the Western Hockey League when their victory over Spokane was coupled with Seattle's loss at Kamloops.
