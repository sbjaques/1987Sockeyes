### Region 1380,1122 → 2531,2935

by Russ Ullyot An old shootout got the B.C. Junior Hockey League season started at the Chilliwack Coliseum on Friday. But when the smoke cleared, the Chilliwack Eagles fluttered to the ground and the visiting Richmond Sockeyes escaped town with an 8-6 victory. The opening game in the 1987-88 BCJHL season saw two teams of opposite character go at each other. The Sockeyes' skaters had the upper hand over the grinding style of the Eagles. On the bright side, Darryl Anderson notched a hat-trick for the Eagles with other markers coming off the sticks of Greg Anderson, Mike Dutton and Cam Matches. For Richmond, Brian McFarlane had a five-point night, scoring twice and adding three assists. Mario Desjardins notched two goals, with singles to Scott Miller, John Atkinson and Tony Bobbitt. Richmond outshot Chilliwack 43-25. The Sockeyes were decididly out-hit by the Eagles. But Richmond coach Orland Kurtenbach countered, "'We outshot them." Kurtenbach added, "It's not the kind of game you like to see, and you only hope you're on the right end of the score at the end." The Eagles held a 4-3 lead after the first 20 minutes. Darryl Anderson opened the scoring on a three-way passing play from Jim Butterworth and Derby Bognar that gave him a wide open net in which to slip the puck. Richmond scored the only two second-period markers to take a 5-4 lead, and the Sockeyes added a power play marker and a short-handed goal in the third en route to the win. The Eagles were held to two power play opportunities, while giving the Sockeyes three chances with the man advantage. Quill Notes Chilliwack minor hockey was well represented in the opening-day lineup for the Eagles. In uniform for Chilliwack were Bart McAstocker, Jerry Lund, Bryan Padgham and Marc Gaultier. Padgham and Gaultier each picked up one assist in the contest. Padgham also had three minor penalties, while Gaultier handled himself well in the game's only fight late in the third period. Unfortunately for Lund, the game ended abruptly midway through the third period when he went down in a collision at centre ice. Lund will be out two weeks with strained knee ligaments. The Eagles' next games are on home ice Friday and Saturday (Sept. 25 and 26). On Friday, the Eagles will play at the Coliseum against the Summerland Buckaroos before shifting to Vedder Twin Rinks on Saturday to host the Abbotsford Falcons.
