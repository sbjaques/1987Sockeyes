NHL BY THE NUMBERS Hockey today SCOREBOARD San Jose at Edmonton (9:40 p.m. EST). The Sharks need a victory the Northlands Coliseum to avoid the distinction of winless between Thanksgiving and Christmas. STATS The New York Rangers are 6-1-1 in their last eight games. San Jose's Rob Gau dreau has 10 goals in his first 10 NHL games. STREAKS San Jose has lost 12 straight games. The Sharks' last win was 3-2 in overtime at Calgary on Nov. 25. Pittsburgh is 6-0-2 in its last eight meetings with Quebec, which hasn't beaten the Penguins since Feb. 26, 1990. SHOTS ON GOAL Calgary and Edmonton combined for only seven shots in the first period on Monday night. The Oilers, who lost 3-2, finished with just 14 shots, matching the lowest singlegame total of the season; Hartford had 14 in 5 9-3 loss at Buffalo on Dec. 11. ... The New York Rangers had just 17 shots in their 3-0 win at New Jersey, getting only two in the third period. SPECIAL TEAMS Winnipeg's power -play unit was 1-for-11 against San Jose and allowed a pair of shorthanded goals. Despite that, the Jets won 5-4. SENATORS They may be in last place in the NHL, but fans are stil interested in seeing more of the Ottawa Senators. The team says 97 percent of season tickets for next season have been reserved. About 300 will be offered to fans on a 6,000-person waiting list. SLUMPS Rangers defenseman Mark Hardy scored a goal for the first time in more than a year when he connected Monday night in New Jersey. Hardy ended a 56-game goal drought dating to Dec. 2, 1991. SWINGS Pittsburgh's power-play unit, which was 0-for-7 and allowed two shorthanded goals on Saturday against the Islanders, went 3-for-9 in a 7-4 win over Quebec on Monday. STARS Monday - Sean Burke, Whalers, stopped 36 shots in Hartford's 5-2 win at Montreal. John Vanbiesbrouck, Rangers, made 35 saves for his 16th career shutout in the Rangers' 3-0 victory at New Jersey. Alexei Zhamnov, Jets, scored twice in a 5-4 win over San Jose. Week Calgary goaltender Mike Vernon, who had back-to-back road shutouts among his three wins last week, was named the NHL Player of the Week. SIDELINED Rangers defenseman Kevin Lowe missed Monday night's game in New Jersey with a sore neck.
