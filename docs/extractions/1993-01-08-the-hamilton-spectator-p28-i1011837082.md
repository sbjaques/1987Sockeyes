NOTEBOOK Savard wants in on fun MONTREAL - A knee injury left Denis Savard wondering yesterday if he can play when former Montreal Canadiens coach Pat Burns comes back to town with his new team, the Toronto. Maple Leafs, this weekend. The game tomorrow night is getting big hype in local media and Savard, a booster of Montreal's new coach Jacques Demers, wants to be part of it. His sore right knee may make it impossible, however. "It's been feeling better the last three or four days but I've got to be careful with it," said Savard. The Canadiens were four games into a six-game road trip when Savard returned home after suffering a bruised back and aggravating a knee injury during a 5-5 tie in Los Angeles last Saturday. Tests showed no damage to the knee, but Savard stayed off skates until trying a brief spin before practice yesterday. The pain was still there, but he planned to try skating again today. It looked doubtful that he would be ready for tomorrow. "It's no fun being out," said Savard, who will turn 32 on Feb. 4.
