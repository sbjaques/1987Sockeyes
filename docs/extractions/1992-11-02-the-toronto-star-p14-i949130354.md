eased a crowded situation on their back line and added strength up front by trading defenceman Paul Cavallini to the Washington Capitals last night for centre Kevin Miller. Before the trade, the Blues had nine defencemen but only two SLAPSHOTS proven centres. The Blues also expect defenceman Bret Hedican back in two weeks from a pre-season knee injury. "Paul had become accessible," Blues GM Ron Caron said. "And with all the injuries we have up front, we'll certainly welcome Miller. He's a very versatile player." Washington's defence has been hampered by injuries. Sylvain Cote had been out until recently with a broken hand and Jason Woolley is out indefinitely with a broken wrist. Cavallini, 27, had a goal and four assists in 11 games with the Blues. He had been with St. Louis since 1987-88, longer than any other current player. Cavallini had the best plus-minus rating in the league in 1989-90 and played in the NHL All-Star Game in 1990, but had been the subject of trade talk for the past few seasons. "'We needed a good defensive defenceman," Capitals spokesman Lou Corletto said. "He brings very good credentials to the Capitals." Miller, also 27, lasted only 10 games with the Capitals and had three : assists. Washington acquired him from Detroit on June 20 in exchange for Dino Ciccarelli. Miller, who can also play right wing, had 21 goals and 25 assists with the Red Wings last season, including four game-winning goals. St. John's romps: The St. John's Maple Leafs set a franchise record for goals in a game in a 10-2 win over the Hershey Bears in American Hockey League play last night on Rock. Every Leafs forward picked up at least a point in the win, St. John's fifth in a row. Yanic Perrault led the way with three goals, while Dave Tomlinson added a pair. Chipping in with singles were Ken McRae, Eric Lacroix, Guy Lehoux, Todd Hawkins and Rob Cimetta. Leafs coach Marc Crawford said the win shows what the team can do when it gets production from all three lines. "Total effort - it was the key tonight," he said. "If we can get that each and every game, home and away, we'll be on course for the season." St. John's has five days off before travelling to Halifax for a game on Saturday. Vial, on the issues: Who says hockey players are too dumb to follow politics. "Nobody here wants (U.S. presidential candidate Bill) Clinton," American-born Red Wings defenceman Dennis Vial said recently in the Detroit dressing room. "He wants to raise taxes for everybody making over $200,000 a year. That affects everybody in this room. - except me." Vial earns $110,000, well below the league average. Looks good to me: Why do players from the former Soviet Union, now playing in the NHL, like eating at Denny's restaurants? Flyers radio broadcaster Gene Hart explains: "I went with them to Denny's and I found out why they wanted to go there. What's different about Denny's? They have pictures of the food on the menu. So, when the waitress comes up and asks for our order, (rookie Viacheslav) Butsayev points to the picture of a hamburger and holds up two fingers. Everything was going fine until she asked him, 'Onions with that?' He had no idea what she was saying."
