While the New Westminster Bruins are doing all the things a team does when the regular season is over and it hasn't made the playoffs, the Western Hockey League club isn't finished yet. Preparations including fund-raising continue as the Queen's Park Arena crew prepares for its departure to Europe April 10 for the Coupe Beard competition April 14-21 in Switzerland. Tuesday night, though, the club honored some of its players at an awards banquet at Centennial Lodge that was attended by about 150 people. In awards decided by the coaching staff, Burnaby native Stuart Sage was named the most improved player while linemate Scott Rawson, who's from North Vancouver, was named the rookie of the year and top scorer (edging Sage by one point). Shoulder aching ST. LOUIS - A strained shoulder will keep. 35-goal scorer Mark Hunter out of the St. Louis Blues lineup for a minimum of five days. Hunter, who plays right wing, was examined by orthopedic surgeon Dr. Jerome Gilden who said the injury was a strain of the joint between the shoulder and breast bone. A counter punch BOSTON - A lawyer for Marvin Hagler is expected to seek a temporary restraining order tomorrow to block a World Boxing Association decision to strip him of his middleweight title. Hagler's lawyer, Morris Goldings, has a hearing scheduled in the federal Appeals Court before Judge Robert Keeton and is expected to ask for a preliminary injunction.

HOCKEY NOTEBOOK

News Services GAINESVILLE, Fla. - Mike Smith of Kenora, Ont., led the decathlon at the Florida Relays track and field invitational with 4,238 points after the first day of competition yesterday. Smith, competing for the University of Toronto Track Club, set a meet record in winning the 400 metres in 47.63 seconds. He also ran 10.97 seconds in the 100 metres, went 7.25 metres in the long jump, 2.06 metres in the high jump, and threw the shot put 13.72 metres. Knut Gundersen of Mount St. Mary's College in Emmitsburg, Md., stood second with 3,929 points. The competitors complete the final five events today. Stamps ink pair CALGARY - The CFL's Calgary Stampeders have signed Canadian university all-stars Rob Ros and David Pappin to contracts for the 1987 season. Ros, 23, was a leading receiver for UBC Thunderbirds. He earned all-star honors last year, making two touchdown catches in the Vanier Cup including the game winner against Western Ontario Mustangs. Pappin, 23, played linebacker for McMaster Marauders. Big puck awards NEW YORK - Frank Mathers, president and general manager of the Hershey Bears of the American Hockey League, and the late Hobey Baker have been awarded the Lester Patrick Trophy for service to hockey. The trophy is awarded annually by the NHL and the New York Rangers "for outstanding service to hockey in the U.S. in fond memory of a great man in the history of a great sport.'
