### Region 215,955 → 1388,2004

By MONTE STEWART Herald-Tribune staff The acronym for the B.C. Junior Hockey League is BCJHL. It is an abbreviation which can now afford an extra "C' for r champion. Richmond Sockeyes rendered the Peace Cariboo Junior Hockey League still winless in Centennial Cup playoffs by sweeping Quesnel Millionaires in a best-of-three B.C. Tier II championship series last weekend. (If Grande Prairie North Stars had defeated Quesnel in the PCJHL finals, they also would have played for the B.C. championship because the league is sanctioned by the B.C. Amateur Hockey Association.) The Sockeyes drubbed the Millionaires 5-1 in Richmond on Saturday night. Friday, the hosts edged the PCJHL champion Quesnel 4-3. Richmond now advances to meet Red Deer Rustlers in the next round of the Centennial Cup playoffs. The Rustlers locked up the Alberta Junior Hockey League title with an 8-5 triumph over St. Albert Saints on Friday night. Red Deer took the best-of-seven AJHL final 4-2. With the victories over the Millionaires, the Sockeyes remained undefeated in the playoffs. Their post-season mark now stands at 16-0. The BCJHL-AJHL showdown will be a best-of-seven affair. The first two games will be held in Richmond and the remaining games - depending on how many are necessary - will be held in Red Deer. Although the Mills came close Friday, the PCJHL champions haven't won a single game against BCJHL clubs in Centennial Cup playoffs. Penticton Knights blanked Prince George Spruce Kings 2-0 in last year's best-of-three series and went on to win the Centennial Cup.
