SPORTS DIGEST Tackle Jason Phillips named Miami's MVP FROM WIRE AND STAFF REPORTS Jason Phillips, a senior defensive tackle from Detroit, was named Most Valuable Player of the Miami University football team during a banquet Monday at the Shriver Center on the Oxford campus. Other award winners were: Jeff Chabot (nominee, for Mid-American Conference MVP award); Gary Layton (special teams MVP); Johnnie Williams (outstanding junior); Skip Tramontana (senior, scholar athlete); Marcus Stepp (outstanding freshman); and Jeff Cheeks (most valuable walk-on). Bill Atha, Eric Henderson and Deland McCullough captured Redskin Warrior Awards. Phillips, and Chabot won captain's awards. Chabot, a center, was named first-team offense on the All-Mid-American Conference team. Firstteam All-MAC defensive players from Miami were, Dee Osborne, Atha, Phillips, Layton and defensive back David Thomas. McCullough and Williams were named secondteam All-MAC picks, while kicker Chad Seitz received honorable mention. I Liyi Wang, the Wright State University diving, coach, has been named an assistant coach on the U.S. Olympic diving squad. Cedarville College sophomores Michelle Burson and Julianne Pletcher became the first NAIA All-Americans in the Yellow Jackets' women's cross country history at last weekend's NAIA Na-, tional meet in Kenosha, Wis. Burson placed 26th, and Pletcher 27th out of 329 runners. The top 30 finishers are All-Americans. Jennifer Zenner was named an NAIA All-America Scholar-Athlete, which recognizes junior and senior varsity runners who carry a cumulative grade point average of 3.50 or higher. I Darren Heidenreich, a junior at Wright State from Cincinnati, set the Raiders' 1,000-yard freestyle record with a 9:34.92 clocking during a meet at Bowling Green. The old mark of 9:36.35 was set by, Scott Troutwine in 1985. Kristin Cusimano, a senior swimmer at Satellite High School in Indian Harbour Beach, Fla., has, signed a national letter-of-intent to attend WSU. Football: Buckeye tackle honored Ohio State tackle Korey Stringer and Wisconsin defensive back Jeff Messenger were named Big Ten players of the week. Ohio State's Marlon Kerner, was named special teams player of the week. A Virginia man accused of running naked across the field during halftime of the Ohio StateMichigan game Saturday has pleaded innocent in court. Daniel John Walsh, 21, a James Madison University sophomore, is charged with criminal trespass and public indecency. I Christopher Bell, a 6-foot-7, 290-pound offensive guard on the Northmont High School football team, has made a verbal commitment to play at the University of Cincinnati. Auto racing: Pemberton joins Penske The Penske South team of NASCAR driver Rusty Wallace has hired Robin Pemberton as its crew chief for 1995. Pemberton, who held the same post at Roush Racing with driver Ted Musgrave this' year, succeeds Buddy Parrott. Car owner Richard Petty is expected to name a driver for '95 in the next day or two.
