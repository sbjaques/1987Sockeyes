MATT MARTIN Herald Sports Editor Five Broughton pitchers held Lee Senior to only three hits Saturday and Patrick Simpkins cracked a three-run homer as the Caps defeated the Yellow Jackets 6-3 in a non-conference game at Lacy Oldham Field. Rob Masterson, the fifth Broughton pitcher, retired the last six Lee batters in order. and was awarded the victory. Rick Gantt, the second of five pitchers for Lee, took the loss. THE LAST 12 Lee hitters were retired in order, and the Jackets also went down 1-2-3 in the second inning. They sent four men to the plate in the first and third. Lee led 3-1 in the top of the fourth when Brian Pickard reached on an error by 1 Neel Bradham at second, and Steve Cahoon walked. Simpkins, the Caps' designated hitter, sent a Gantt fastball out over the left field fence, make it 4-3. The Caps, 2-1, went ahead 5-3 against Garrett Jones in the fifth on an RBI single by Lance Miller. Singles by Simpkins and Tony Bobbitt, a walk and a passed ball gave Broughton a 6-3 edge in the sixth. Jackets, 1-2, took a 3-1 lead in the third when Jody Marks singled to center, Roger Brown doubled to left center, and Mike Jones walked to load the bases. Allen Davis' roller to short scored pinch runner Rick Womack from third, and Brown scored on a throwing error by Doug Esposito, trying to complete the double play. BROUGHTON, the defending 4A
