Pierre Page is in his rookie year as a head coach in the National Hockey League. The former assistant coach of the Calgary Flames took over the duties as head coach of the Minnesota North Stars In July of this year, replacing Herb Brooks. Interviewed by The Progress following Monday's game he talked about Dave Archibald's role with the Minnesota North Stars. "It's been tough, it's been a struggle. To be honest, (he's) been our biggest project. "Here's a kid that has a lot of talent. A good player in junior and I think he was rushed to the team last year and that didn't help him. I'm not used to rushing kids and I'm not going to in this organization and I think (Archibald) paid a dear price last year. "We don't know where to play him, left wing, centre, right wing. I think he plays better at left wing for us. He's got talent, a great shot, he's strong. But he's really not playing as well as he should because he needs a lot of ice. "At his age, he would better off playing somewhere else, the Canadian Olympic team or playing junior again where he would get a lot of ice time. But we might not do that so in the meantime we've go to get him better and play him more. "How many kids play football, baseball, basketball at 19 years of age the youngest rookie in the NBA (National Basketball :: Association) is 21 years of age. What are we doing rushing kids? It's a detriment to the player. "Dave could be playing 28 or 29 minutes a game somewhere else, he's playing 8 to 10 minutes with our job is to make sure he can play 10, 15, 20, 22 minutes eventually by the second half, or by late in the season. The only way were going to do that is by keep working with him, show him tapes and (assistant coach) George Kingston is really working hard with him. "We like his attitude, he's really coachable.
