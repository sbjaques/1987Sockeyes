## Paragraphs

The door will be open to anyone worried about their drinking or about someone else who might be hitting the bottle. The pilot scheme will be launched on March 17. Outreach worker Norman Claringbull says the "Time Out" centre will offer a casual, drop in service with a sociable and friendly atmosphere. If the response is good the service

will be extended to the day time and Tuesdays between 12.30pm and 3.30pm are already being ringed on the Outreach Service's diary. Mr Claringbull says that for many sectors of the alcohol misusing population spending some time living in a hostel is either impossible or inappropriate. "The Outreach Service is now targetting those sectors of the population where an alternative means of help to that provided by a residential programme is more suitable," he said.
