AROUND THE WORLD I Johnson to miss indoor championships A nagging hamstring injury has forced sprinter Ben Johnson to withdraw from the Canadian indoor track and field championships slated to take place this weekend in Windsor, Ont. Johnson pulled a hamstring in his left leg Feb. 5 at a meet in West Germany and is not expected to return to action before Feb. 26. B.C. hockey coach suspended Dan Sproule, owner, coach and general manager of the Juan de Fuca Whalers, has been suspended from coaching in the B.C. Junior Hokey League until Nov. 30 because of threats he made against an opposing goaltender. The league said Sproule cannot be in an arena where his team is playing for an hour before the game, and cannot enter the building until an hour after the game has ended. Sproule had threatened goaltender Willie Popp, who had been loaned to the Chilliwack Eagles on an emergency basis by the Richmond Sockeyes for a Jan. 30 game against the Whalers in Victoria. Dave Ferguson, a BCJHL vice-president, said Sproule called his office and said: "If Popp starts the game, he won't finish." Popp, 20, of St. Albert, Alta., broke the wrist of his catching hand when checked by a member of the Whalers. He is expected to be out of action for six weeks. Chilliwack had asked for the use of a replacement goaltender when Glen Pencer was injured and two other Eagle affiliate netminders were under suspension. Royals, Fisk at impasse The Kansas City Royals' interest in free agent catcher Carlton Fisk has deteriorated in view of his demand for "way in excess of $1 million," Royals general manager John Schuerholz said Wednesday. "It's very, very, very remote that Carlton will sign with us," Schuerholz told The Kansas City Star at the Royals' spring training site in Florida. Fisk, who signed a one-year contract with the White Sox for $700,000 during the off-season, was asking for a minimum of $1.2 million plus incentives from the Royals, sources close to the talks told the Star. The Royals reportedly offered Fisk $800,000 without incentives. Fisk became a free agent when aribtrator Tom Roberts ruled in January that owners conspired against the 1985 free agents.
