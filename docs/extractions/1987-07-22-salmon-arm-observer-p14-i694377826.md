## Paragraphs

Salmon Arm Sockeyes did well at the highly-competitive Kamloops Aquatic swim meet on the weekend, says assistant coach Bob Tucker. "Considering the extremely high level of competition, with several clubs from the lower mainland, the club did very well in Kamloops. Many of our swimmers made the finals and four finished in the aggregate medals. "Barry and Ricky Say and Sandra Geiger once again finished in the medals (the Says both received silver medals and Sandra Geiger a bronze) and Carmen Lazzarotto continues to maintain her undefeated streak, thus winning once again the gold aggregate," he says. In division one girls, Carmen Lazzarotto took first place in the backstroke, breaststroke, butterfly, and freestyle.

Sockeyes' coach Hamish Tucker holds the record in the 100 metre backstroke in division six (at 1:06.4) and division seven (at 1:07.3). The first place finisher this year in six was more than three division, slower than Tucker's record. In that race Cameron McDougall was sixth. David MacAulay was sixth in the breaststroke, Myles Smith was fourth in the butterfly, and David Say was fourth in the 50 metre freestyle.
