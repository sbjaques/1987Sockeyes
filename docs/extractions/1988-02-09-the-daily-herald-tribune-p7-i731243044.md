## Paragraphs

It's common knowledge that Ken Head is a good coach, but few know he's also a magician - he's pulled a hockey player out of his hat. The Grande Prairie North Star's general manager/coach announced yesterday the signing of Chad Neilsen, an 18-year-old forward who scored 189 points in two seasons with the Fort St. John Huskies. Neilsen played part of this season with the Richmond Sockeyes and Abottsford Falcons of the British Columbia Junior Hockey League. An injury sidelined him and, consequently, set up the series of conversations with Head that led to his signing. "Just like that I'm in Grande Prairie," said the 5-foot-11, 180 lb. centre. A succession of injuries shoulder pull, abdominal pull and ankle sprain - kept him out of the Falcons' lineup before Christmas. He signed with the Falcons after refusing to go to Salmon Arm in a trade while playing for the Sockeyes. Abottsford placed Neilsen on an injury card, the only way they could keep him until the next year since all their playing cards were full. Such a move requires a doctor's certificate of injury - which the Falcons didn't have - and because Neilsen was fit to play the move was entirely illegal by the rules of the British Columbia Amateur Hockey Association. "They were trying to pull a little hanky panky," Head suggested. "*They were just trying to hold him until next year without playing him. "But we got him released - we've been working on this since Christmas time. We've been talking to Chad and his dad (Gary) regularly and he did all the ground work for the deal.' The process of cutting through the red tape has been a difficult one. "It took well over a month to get me released." added Neilsen. *They would have gotten away with it if I hadn't decided to come to Grande Prairie.' " Neilsen will take to the ice for the first time tonight (8:30 at the Johnny MacDonald) against, ironically, his former team.
