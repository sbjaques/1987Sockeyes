By DAN SCHNICK Herald-Tribune staff Ritchie Webb hopes someday to be in Ken Head's shoes. North Stars coach has what Fort St. John coach-general manager Webb considers a luxury with 11 veterans. **It would be nice to have as many returnees as Grande Prairie.' " says Webb as he prepares his 0-10 club for their third meeting of the Peace Cariboo Junior Hockey League season with the Stars. Webb has been busy negotiating with managers from the Richmond Sockeyes, Victoria Cougars and Abbotsford Falcons for players. So far he has traded forward Blaine Moore for defenceman Steve McCuaig from the B.C. Junior Falcons. **We need someone of quality and experience," says Head as he attempts to put an end to the worst start in Huskies history. **I think we're playing well as a team but are lacking that experience and leadership that pulls a team together.' Nevertheless, Head knows their is a battle to be won when the club storms the Fort tomorrow night. **(Webb's) always working," said Head whose club is off to an 8-3 start.
