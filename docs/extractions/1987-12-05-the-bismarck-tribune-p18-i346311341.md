GRAND FORKS - Gino Gasparini can be excused for having mixed feelings Friday night. "You won't win many hockey games playing the way we played tonight, said Gasparini, moments after the University of North Dakota beat Northern Michigan 5-3 in Western Collegiate Hockey Association action. "We were up 3-2 after one period but I was upset. Then in the second period, we'd go hard for three minutes and then drop off But we'll take the win." Five different players scored for the Sioux. Rick Forst, Grant Paranica, Garry Valk, Geoff Smith and Lee Davidson were UND's marksmen. Steve Johnson, who had seven points in two games last weekend, assisted on three goals.

It's the same old story in Class A high school wrestling Bismarck Century wins by a lot. Friday night, at the Mandan high gym, it was 46-15 over Mandan. CHS wrestlers scored a number of easy victories, but coach Bill McCollum was impressed with the close ones. One was at 135 pounds, where Trent Gilliss decisioned Mandan's Dean Schmidt 8-7. Then came a 7-5 victory for Mike McCormick over Roy Ogden at 140. McCollum was also impressed with a loss - a 3-2 setback by Ted Cash to Mandan's heavyweight power Neil Anderson.
