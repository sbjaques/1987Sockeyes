JON CLEVELAND created a splash at the Canadian Olympic swimming trials when he combined with Cam Grant of the Keyano Swim Club to knock world record-holder Victor Davis off the team in the men's 200-metre breaststroke. The 17-year-oid Cleveland, from the University of Calgary Swim Club, impressed everyone with his performance in the Montreal Olympic pool in late May. Now U of C coach Deryk Snelling, an assistant coach with the Canadian team, is predicting he'll be in the Sept. 23 final and has the potential to win a medal. Cleveland is the adopted son of former major league baseball pitcher Reggie Cleveland and lowered his time more than four seconds to two minutes, 16 seconds in Montreal. He advanced from first in the consolation final at the 1986 Swimming Canada summer nationals to third in 1987 before making his big breakthrough in May. If his progress is as rapid during the summer as it was before he qualified for the Olympic team, he's capable of achieving all that Snelling feels is possible. MICHELLE CONN will celebrate her g 25th birthday in a special environment Sept. 17, during the opening ceremonies at the Summer Olympics in Seoul. Bronze medals from the 1986 World Cup for women and 1987 Pan-American Games are arguably her biggest field hockey moments. But they'll pale by comparison in Korea. She hopes to help the Canadian team to a medal beginning with a Sept. 21 match against Australia. Conn was born in Edmonton, is now a science student at the University of Calgary but spends much of her time here training with teammates Deb Covey, Liz Czenczek and Shona Schleppe under U of Alberta Pandas' head coach Dru Marshall, at the Universiade Pavilion. The 5-foot-5 forward has spent much of this year travelling in preparation for the Seoul tournament, with trips to Australia, Europe and back to Australia for the final touches. The Canadians are also grouped with South Korea and West Germany as they seek a berth in the medal round starting Sept. 26.
