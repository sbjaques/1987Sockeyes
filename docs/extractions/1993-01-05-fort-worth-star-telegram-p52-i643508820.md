Fort Worth Fire coach Peter Mahovlich said he won't know until before tonight's Central Hockey League game with the Memphis RiverKings whether he can return to the bench. As of yesterday, CHL commissioner Monte Miron hadn't received the videotape of Thursday's incident in Wichita that resulted in the suspension of Mahovlich for Saturday's home victory against Wichita. Mahovlich said the videotape will show the Thunder had six players on the ice during a fight Thursday when he sent Mike McCormick in to even the numbers. If Miron rules against Mahovlich, the Fire coach could be suspended another four games. "It should be cleared up [today]," Mahovlich said. "I don't know why Wichita hasn't sent the tape yet. I hope they're not thinking about doing something to it. "All I'm asking is to sit down with [Miron] when he watches it." Fort Worth's Ernest Hornak, suspended for allegedly striking a linesman in the same altercation, must miss at least three more games. Miron will be in Fort Worth on Friday to review Hornak's case.
