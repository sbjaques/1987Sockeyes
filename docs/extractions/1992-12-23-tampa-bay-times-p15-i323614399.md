Young got his first start in goal since Dec. 9, when he gave up four goals in 14 shots against the New York Rangers and was pulled in the second period in favor of Pat Jablonski. Young looked fairly sharp despite the six-game layoff and finished with 30 saves. The only goal scored in the third period was the empty-net goal. Tampa Bay had a good chance, b but Rob Zamuner broke his stick on a point-blank shot. The Lightning were badly outplayed and outshot 14-3 for the first 12 minutes of the second period. Young made several good saves, including a glove save while on his back of a rebound shot by Steve Leach early in the period. But Boston capitalized with two goals to take a 4-2 lead. Darrin Kimble - whom the Lightning acquired in a trade from St. Louis and then traded to Boston before training camp for Ken Hodge and Matt Hervey sent a crushing body check to Roman Hamrlik. With Hamrlik out of the play, Ashton got the loose puck and beat Young over his right shoulder at 5:39. It was only Ashton's second goal in 26 games. Six minutes later Donato, waiting at the left point, one-timed a pass from Adam Oates and that clanged off the right post and in for a power-play goal to give Boston a 4-2 edge. Oates, who was playing in his 500th NHL game, has 570 career points. But Tampa Bay came to life and did not allow Boston a shot on goal for the last 8:37 of the period. Bradley pulled the Lightning within 4-3 with 3:38 left in the second period. Rob Ramage's aggressive play kept the puck in the Bruins' zone on a Lightning power play.
