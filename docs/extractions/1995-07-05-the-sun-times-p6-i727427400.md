By JONATHON JACKSON For The Sun Times Growing up in small-town New Brunswick had an insulating effect on Shane Kenny. The big Owen Sound Platers defenceman always dreamed of making it to the National Hockey League but never imagined it was possible until he arrived in the Ontario Hockey League in 1993. He was 16. "I'd heard about it (the OHL) but that was it," Kenny said in a recent interview, adding the Maritime provinces had no major junior outlet at that time.

"Owen Sound's all right. It's a good hockey town," Kenny said. He added he won't mind coming back to the Scenic City for another year or two of junior - and meeting new coach Ric Seiling. "I've heard he's strict. I need a little discipline," Kenny said with a laugh. Seiling toiled for 10 years in the NHL. It won't be Kenny's first time being coached by a former NHLer. His midget coach was Fredericton native Danny Grant, who played parts of 13 NHL seasons with four teams and who scored 50 goals for the 1974/75 Detroit Red Wings. Another former coach, Jerry Harrigan, believes Kenny has the tools to be successful in the pros. Kenny, now 18, is one step closer to his dream. The Oromocto native's ranked 42nd among North American prospects for Saturday's NHL entry draft in Edmonton. "I'd like to be a little higher," Kenny said of his second-round ranking. "But I didn't have that good of a year." The si 230-pound rearguard got off to a slow start this past season and saw his stock drop accordingly. Once a projected first-round he feels he'll even go a bit lower than what Central Scouting has predicted. "I think I'll end up a third-rounder. I have a feeling.' He racked up 13 goals and 32 points in 65 games as a Plater sophomore in 1994/95, up from his four goals and 13 points in 54 games as a rookie. Kenny started skating at age two and began playing competitive hockey at age five. He said he's always been a defenceman, following in the footsteps of his father - an allstar blue liner in the New Brunswick senior leagues. DYNAMOS WIN PORT ELGIN - Brian Dolan scored the winner on penalty kicks Tuesday as the Owen Sound Dynamos nipped the Port Elgin Flyers 3-2 in its first game ! of MidWestern Ontario S Soccer League Competitor Cup play. Ryan Traynor backstopped the victory in the Dynamo net as the Flyers missed their first penalty kick after an Owen Sound goal. Darren Elkerton opened the scoring for the Dynamos, heading a corner from Rob Blundell 10 minutes into the game. Roloff Carstensen knotted the score five minutes later but Blundell headed a throw-in from Sean Dantas before the end of the half, putting Dynamos ahead 2-1. Carstensen got the equalizer on a deflected free kick 25 minutes into the second half. Recently signed Will Valdez, John Walsh and Jan Bohlmark shored up the Dynamo line-up which was short six players. Owen Sound returns to league play this Sunday when it plays host to Hanover at 4 p.m. HOCKEY TRYOUTS Grey Bruce Highlanders triple-A hockey organization Tuesday announced tryouts will begin July 29 in Ayton. The tryouts for the organization's seven squads, minor atom through midget, will cost $20 per player. That guarantees three practices. A registration fee of $529 will apply to all signed players. Executive member Steve Leal anticipates team rosters will be finalized by the Labor Day weekend with the league schedule beginning on Oct. 1. Area residents will get a chance to "hear" what the Grey Bruce Highlanders triple-A hockey organization is all about Thursday. Host Tony Ambrogio and Highlanders executive members Miles McCauley, Leal, Denis Longpre and Bob Stride will be on CFOS Sportsticker at 6 p.m.. The Highlanders are still seeking volunteers, coaches and managers. Interested applicants can write Steve Leal at Box 65, Markdale, NOC 1H0 or fax (519) 924-3073.
