The Royals have lost just once in 40 games so far this season, but Olver says they're averaging only 300 to 400 paying customers. "It's (Royals' attendance) scary because they're an exceptional team in a pretty good league," says Bob Hall, who retired last year after 10 seasons as an NHL referee and who recently became a league vice dent under ailing president Bruce Allison. "I started (refereeing) in Tier 2 all across western Canada and this is the best league," adds Hall, now in sales management with a local fitness equipment company. "I haven't been on the scene long enough to say much now. But realignment will be a priority at upcoming meetings of league owners.' It's possible new ownership will revive Penticton for next season and Prince George, Williams Lake and Quesnel, of the Peace-Cariboo Junior League, will have representatives at the BCJHL meetings. Olver says that Royals' owner Bruce Taylor, the Burnaby businessman who formerly ran Richmond Sockeyes, runs a "class organization but he doesn't like to talk about (financial) losses.'
