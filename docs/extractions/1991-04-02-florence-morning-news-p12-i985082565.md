Scott, Drexler honored NEW YORK Dennis Scott of the Orlando Magic was named rookie of the month Monday in the NBA while Clyde Drexler of the Portland Trail Blazers was selected player of the week. Scott averaged 19.4 points last month while setting a league record for most 3-pointers in a season by a rookie. He broke the record for 3-pointers, set last season by Dana Barros with his 95th. Drexler averaged 23 points, 6.5 rebounds and 6.3 assists while leading the Trail Blazers to a 4-0 record last week. Morris joins Cleveland CLEVELAND - Joe Morris, a two-time Pro Bowl running back with the New York Giants who did not play last season, was among 10 players who signed with the Cleveland Browns on Monday., Morris had three 1,000-yard seasons with the New York Giants and holds franchise records in total yards rushing (5,296) and touchdowns (48). He's also their career in career rushing attempts (1,318) and set a team record with 21 rushing touchdowns in 1985. Morris suffered a broken foot in New York's last preseason game in 1989, was placed on injured reserve and missed the entire season. He rehabilitated the injury but was placed on waivers in September 1990 and went unsigned for the entire season. Students mourn death MANCHESTER, N.H. Students stood silently and the flag at Central High School was lowered to half-staff Monday in honor of Kevin Charbonneau, the Manchester teen-ager killed in a hockey game during the weekend. "I think shock is the best description of this," principal William Burns said of students' reaction. "I mean no one is ready for anything like that. And I think realization doesn't take place for a while.' Charbonneau, 15, died Saturday night at Catholic Medical Center after blocking a shot with his chest during the Manchester Regional Youth Hockey Association Bantam Division championship game. Lady Vols welcomed home KNOXVILLE, Tenn. - Tennessee women's basketball coach Pat Summitt, in her Volunteer orange sweatsuit, strode to the podium Monday and looked up at the 1,500 cheering fans gathered to celebrate the Lady Vols' NCAA basketball title. "Can you believe it?" she said. Summitt has made no secret all year that she and her team wanted to atone for the end of the 1990 season, when they lost in the regional finals to Virginia, in overtime, to miss the Final Four held on their home floor in Knoxville.
