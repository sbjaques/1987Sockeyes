KNIGHTS WIN Meanwhile, Penticton got four goals from Lee Davidson in a 9-4 victory Tuesday night over Merritt Warriors to give the Knights a two-point edge over Kelowna Packers at the top of the BCJHL Interior Division. In the evening's only other game, Coast-Division-leading leading, Richmond Sockeyes lost home to second-place Delta Flyers. In addition to Davidson's four goals, Penticton got singles from Cal Brown, Keven Melrose, David Shields, Chris Anderson and Scott Rawson. Replying for the fifth-place Warriors, who were outshot 64-23, were Kevin Thorlakson, Brad Smiley, Rob Revell and Mike Catchpole. Gord Macinnes scored twice as the Flyers outhustled Richmond, which Delta now trails by 10 points. It was the Sockeyes' first loss to a Coast Division tear, and only their sixth defeat in 33 games. Delta goalie Chris Dickson was a key to the win, as the Flyers won while being outshot 39-30. Delta, which took: a 1-0 edge in the first period and went ahead 5-2 in the second, got singles from Rod Ware, Rich Steniford, Gerry Mumford and Mitch Eva nish.
