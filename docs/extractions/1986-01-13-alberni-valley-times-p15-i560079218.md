## Paragraphs

And the piece de resistance: four of the five Canuck skaters sent off for something outrageous so that Darcy Rota could show off his penalty killing skill against five opponents. He proved he can still skate, even if the wind wasn't quite what it might have been a year ago. Rota collapsed finally, in the Alberni defensive zone, and the Oldtimers went down the ice and hammered away at Bromley gleefully until they finally scored. Also surprising to this fan, who was viewing the Canuck oldsters for the first time, was the sheer size of Orland Kurtenbach. No wonder he was treated with respect in his playing d days. Kurt was a gentle giant on this occasion, however, turning in the odd burst of speed and making nifty passes, but by a and large staying in the background and leaving things to fleeter teammates. Those teammates turned it on at will, as you would expect from expros. They would score a few, let Alberni back into it a little, and then slam the door with a few more goals. Clearly, they could have scored at will, even given Community Arenatype lighting.
