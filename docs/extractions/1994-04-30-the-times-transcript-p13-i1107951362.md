By Dwayne Tingley Sports Editor ST. JOHN'S, Nfld. (Special) - On paper, it looks like a battle of the controlled defensive system of the Moncton Hawks against the overwhelming offence of the St. John's Maple Leafs. Hawks and Maple Leafs begin their best-of-seven American Hockey League Atlantic Division final tonight at cozy St. John's Memorial Stadium and the home team is heavily-favored to win. Maple Leafs led the AHL with 102 points and 360 goals scored during the regular season. They lost only nine home games all year and easily disposed of the Cape Breton Oilers in five games in their Atlantic Division semifinal. Right winger Patrik Augusta scored 53 goals while player/assistant coach Rich Chernomaz and second-year centre Yanic Perrault each had 45. Chris Snell broke all league scoring records for defencemen with 96 points in 75 games. Snell also had a +43 rating and Leafs coach Marc Crawford can't understand why the smooth-skating defenceman isn't in the NHL. Maple Leafs were second in the league with a success rate of 26.3 per cent on the power play and their penalty-killers were the AHL's best with "We a rate a lot 82 per cent., of things right against Saint John, but we'll have to be even better against St. John's," said Hawks coach Rob Laird. Meanwhile, Crawford downplayed his team's dominance of the Atlantic Division this season and said it's too simplistic to portray this series of a battle between Moncton's defence a and the offence of St. John's. "There's no doubt about it - Moncton has an impressive group of defencemen and Stephane Beauregard is a quality goaltender, but there's much more, to their lineup than that," Crawford said. "They have ability to be an explosive hockey team, like we are," Crawford said. "When they got all of their injured players back and they got some guys back from Winnipeg, it became a real potent lineup.'
