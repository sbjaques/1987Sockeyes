## Paragraphs

*One way or another, Richmond Sockeyes are going to the Centennial Cup. If they beat Humboldt Broncos in their best-of-seven western final, the B.C. junior A hockey champion Sockeyes take the western berth into the -team national championship. But the Broncos also happen to be hosts of the Cup and if they win the western final, the Sockeyes will round out the tournament entry. The western final goes tomorrow and Tuesday in Humboldt. If there's a split in the games, a third game will be played Wednesday with Thursday and Friday off for travel days and the best-of-seven resuming Saturday at Richmond Arena. If either team sweeps the first two games, the travel days are Wednesday and Thursday and play opens Friday at Richmond Arena. Richmond advanced to the final Thursday in Red Deer when Dave Tomlinson scored three goals to pace the Sockeyes to a 9-1 thrashing of the Alberta champion Rustlers, allowing the West Coast squad to win the best-of-seven western semifinal 4-3. The teams were tied 0-0 after the first period, but Richmond exploded for five goals in the second. Rob Clarke, Mike Claringbull, Jason Phillips, Jim Gunn, Mike McCormick and Tony Bobbitt also tallied for the Sockeyes. Red Deer's Pete DeGraaf spoiled Jamie Stewart's shut-out bid late in the third period.
