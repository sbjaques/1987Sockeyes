Scoring on their first shot 35 5 seconds into the game and then scoring again on their first shot in the second and third periods, the St. John's Maple Leafs administered a solid all-round lesson in hockey on Moncton, defeating the Hawks 8-2 in an American Hockey League playoff game in front of 5,206 fans at the Coliseum Thursday night. The victory allowed the Maple Leafs to sweep the -seven Atlantic Division finals 4-0. They won the first two games 8-4 and 6-5 at home and then took Game : 3 here 4-2 on Tuesday night. With an opportunity to clinch the series there was no stopping the Maple Leafs as they came out full-throttle, outshooting the Hawks 18-6 in the first period to grab a convincing 4-0 lead. The triumph was St. John's eighth straight in the playoffs. They lost their first playoff game in overtime to the Cape Breton Oilers but have since gone unbeaten. Former Moncton Golden Flame Greg Johnston paced the Maple Leafs offence with a hat trick while Saint John native Andrew McKim and Yanic Perreault each had two assists. Chipping in with a singles for the Maple Leafs were Todd Hawkins, Dave Tomlinson, Ted Crowley, Todd Gilllingham and Kevin Maguire. Tyler Larter and Craig Duncanson connected with Moncton's goals, both coming on the power play. "Leading the series 3-0 the only thing we keyed on tonight was the first period," said St. John's coach Marc Crawford after the game. "I reminded the guys that although we were in a very, very solid position it's still important to try and control how we want to play. "We wanted to come out strong in the first period and everybody did a great job. We forechecked well. We positioned ourselves well. We shot the puck well. We were really going hard on the puck in all zones and that set the tempo for the whole game." Serious was the way Johnston described his team's style.
