While the Whalers were burying the Chilliwack Eagles 11-1 Sunday at the Juan de Fuca Arena to close out the regular season. the swaggering. swashbuckling image of the Juan de Fuca team, and those who run it, was having a go over at league offices. Coach, co- and president Dale Sproule was in Vancouver appealing his league suspension (until Nov. 30) because of threats he allegedly made against Willie Popp, a top goalkeeper who was lent to Chilliwack by leaguerival Richmond Sockeyes for a game against Juan de Fuca in Victoria on Jan. 30. Sproule, incensed at what he saw as collusion by two Lower Mainland teams, is alleged to have phoned the league office and said, "If Popp starts the game, he won't finish.' Popp broke his wrist in that game. Chilliwack and Juan de Fuca were battling it out at the time for the fourth Coastal playoff berth.
