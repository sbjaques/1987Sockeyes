## Paragraphs

Scotty Bowman, Al Arbour, Keith Allen and who knows how many other experienced NHL executives. Watt wasn't the team's first choice to be coach, either. Dave King of the Olympic team accepted the job, but gave it back, all five years on an $800,000 contract. Lorne Henning, an assistant coach under Arbour with New York Islanders before spending a year in the minors as a head coach, could have had the Vancouver job, but turned it down when the Canucks apparently wouldn't match his minor league salary. Now the Canucks have settled on Watt, providing they can persuade the Toronto native and agent Alan Eagleson to sign a contract. Watt will inherit a troubled team from Neale, who joined the Canucks in 1978 and saw Vancouver make the playoffs every season until 1984-85. Vancouver finished 23 points behind fourthplace Los Angeles Kings and had the only losing record in the Smythe Division. The Canucks are getting old in goal with Richard Brodeur, are too young on defence to be effective and didn't have a 40-goal man on the forward lines. Neale, Bill LaForge, Roger Neilson, Orland Kurtenbach, Phil Maloney, Bill McCreary, Vic Stasiuk and Hal Laycoe have coached the Canucks during their first 15 years in the NHL. No Vancouver coach has had a winning record. Watt left the Canucks on good terms in 1981 to become head coach of the Jets. Winnipeg improved by 48 points that winter while the Canucks went to the Stanley Cup final with Neilson after a league suspension to Neale. It was downhill after that for both Watt and the Canucks. Watt eventually was fired by the strong-minded Ferguson after his coach had a run-in with scoring star Dale Hawerchuk. one of the NHL's most gifted players.
