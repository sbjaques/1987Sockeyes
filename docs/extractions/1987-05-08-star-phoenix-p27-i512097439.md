THE MAN who checked in at the registration desk at the downtown Vancouver hotel asked if he could get the Canadiens-Nordiques game on his television set. The clerk replied in the negative. "Well, then maybe I'll take in the RichmondHumboldt game. It should be a heck'uva series," he commented. He knew the teams would be returning to Humboldt to play for the Centennial Cup and hoped to take in at least one of the games. I happened to be on holidays at the time and the next day planned to be in Kelowna. At Arby's, I ran into big Gene McWillie. I didn't realize he was in Kelowna with the Canadian men's national softball team preparing for the Pan-American Games later this summer and the World Softball championship to be held in Saskatoon in 1988. So, as well as taking in the doubleheader that night, I also got a chance for a short interview with the team's manager, Dave Pearce of Regina.
