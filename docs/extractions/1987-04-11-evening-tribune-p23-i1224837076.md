Broncos win SELKIRK, Man. (CP) Humboldt Broncos scored three times in the third period Friday to score a 6-4 victory over Selkirk Steelers and take a 2-0 lead in' the best-of-seven Centennial Cup western semifinal. The home Ice advantage provided by the tiny Selkirk Arena ice surface proved meaningless in both games. "That was a big psyche said Humboldt coach general manager Bernie Lynch. you're given and you play hard." Selkirk, already missing five regulars, lost Kevin Mayo to a game misconduct in the first period and Jeff Trapp to an ankle injury. "They're giving it their best shot," said frustrated Steelers' coach Frank Proco. * "We just don't have enough horses at this point." he said.
