## Paragraphs

By ELLIOTT PAP The weekend dethroning of the Richmond Sockeyes as B.C. Junior Hockey League and Centennial Cup national champions has got head coach Orland Kurtenbach thinking - thinking he wants another crack at tier two hockey's biggest prize. The Sockeyes were eliminated by the Vernon Lakers Saturday, losing the best-of-seven BCJHL final 4-1. "I told Bruce Taylor (Sockeyes' owner) I'd let him know in a month to six weeks whether I'd be back," said Kurtenbach, the former Vancouver Canuck captain and coach. "However, I would say it's a fair assessment on your part if you predicted I'm not ready to stop coaching. I have my insurance business and the driving range, but my S enjoyment is still in hockey." The 51-year-old Kurtenbach was lured to the Sockeyes for the 1986-87 season and won everything in sight as a rookie junior coach. The loss to Vernon was Kurtenbach's first playoff defeat after eight straight series victories, plus last May's Centennial Cup tournament.
