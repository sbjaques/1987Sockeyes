HUMBOLDT, Sask. - Jason Phillips scored three goals to lead the Richmond Sockeyes to the Centennial Cup Tier Two junior A hockey championship Saturday night with a 5-2 win over the Humboldt Broncos. But the Sockeyes had to fall back on an outstanding performance in goal by Frank Romeo, who stood up to 46 Humboldt shots while his teammates managed only 23 at the Broncos net. Romeo was named most valuable player in the four-team, roundrobin series. British Columbia's Sockeyes skated to period leads of 2-0 and 2-1 before i Paul Rutherford scored the winning goal in the third. Dave Tomlinson also scored for Richmond, which took seven of the game's 12 minor penalties. Curtis Chamberlin and Kevin Luke scored for the Saskatchewan champion Broncos. 000 SHERBROOKE Jody Gage notched two goals Saturday to lift the Rochester Americans to a 43 comeback win over the Sherbrooke Canadiens in the second game of the American Hockey League final. Rochester's win evened the series at one game each and ended Canadiens' goaltender Vincent Riendeau's winning streak at home at 23 games. The loss was the Canadiens' first at home since March 29. Rochester scored first at 5:51 of the opening period when Gage tipped a shot past a screened Riendeau.
