## Paragraphs

The professional careers of several Moncton Hawks should end in 25 days. Incapable of playing in the American Hockey League, many players will be released by the NHL parent Winnipeg Jets at the conclusion of the regular season schedule. In most cases, Winnipeg's decisions on who to re-sign are easy ones. High-scoring forwards Randy Gilhen, Peter Douris and Alfie Turcotte and defencemen Todd Flichel and Paul Boutilier are valuable players the Jets want back in the organization next season. What about the rest of the Hawks? With a team as bad as this year's Hawks, the NHL club can't be too enthusiastic about many prospects. In the minor leagues, there are always surprises, but this year it has only meant, one another. disappointment There is one great exception - rookie defenceman Matt Hervey. The California native played Tier II junior last winter in Richmond, B.C. and was recommended to the Jets by coach Orland Kurtenbach. Hervey impressed the Jets so much at training camp that he was assigned to the Hawks with an amateur tryout. It didn't take Hawks coach Rick Bowness long to convince the Jets that Hervey was worth signing to a pro deal. After a few AHL games, Hervey showed he enjoyed the physical play and he was eager to learn. Besides the offensivelyskilled players (Gilhen, Douris and Turcotte), Hervey has been Moncton's steadiest player. He's played the point on the power play, killed penalties and taken extra shifts. At plus 2, Hervey is the only plus player on the Hawks roster. "He's been very consistent," coach Bowness noted. "He's one player r who has been consistent at home and on the road. "We have a lot of players who don't bother showing up on the road. That's why we're in last place. Hervey is one guy who comes to play every night....It doesn't matter if we're at home or not." Bowness also says the 22- year old defenceman is the most improved player on the team. "He wasn't even being considered at training camp," Bowness explained. "His enthusiasm caught our eye. He's played hard and practised hard to improve himself. "He's proven that he can play in this league. We've used him in every situation possible and he's proven himself. He's also got more than 200 minutes in penalties so that tells you something about his character. He is a real competitor." Inviting many players from this year's team back to the Hawks next season would be disasterous. However, Matt Hervey is one player who has earned that right.
