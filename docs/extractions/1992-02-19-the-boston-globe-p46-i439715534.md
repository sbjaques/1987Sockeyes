By Nancy L. Marrapese GLOBE STAFF CALGARY, Alberta - Rick Bowness hasn't ruled out anything in his quest to make the Bruins a better team, and the changes started yesterday. Forwards Barry Pederson and Scott Arniel and defenseman Matt Hervey were recalled from Maine while center Ken Hodge and defenseman Jack Capuano were sent to the Mariners. That was before practice. During practice, Bowness, who had been reluctant to split up center Vladimir Ruzicka and right wing Steve Leach, did just that, putting Leach on a line with Adam Oates and Bob Carpenter. Andy Brickley was put at left wing on Ruzicka's line, with a right wing to be named later, possibly Peter Douris. The coach also moved Brent Ashton from left wing to center on a line with Dave Reid and Lyndon Byers. The fourth line consisted of injured center Dave Poulin between* Lou Crawford and Chris Winnes. But tonight, when the Bruins play the Calgary Flames at the Saddledome, it's anybody's guess who will be playing with whom. "Sometimes changes are made just to get the team going and you have no control over it," said Leach, who added he's not exactly losing anything by playing with Oates. "I'll just communicate with him, find out what he likes to do and try to play off of that," said Leach, who missed Monday's game because of the flu. Bowness said the Ashton switch was an attempt to get him out of a slump. In the past 11 games, Ashton has I goal and 1 assist.
