Bounce Cornwall in opening game of best-of-three set Evening Times Globe CORNWALL, Ont. - The Moncton Hawks did more than just win the opener in the bestof-three Calder Cup semifinal. They made a loud statement. Moncton used a stifling defence, continually breaking up opposition plays in the neutral zone and never allowing much penetration in the slot, en route to a 4-1 road triumph over the Cornwall Aces on Saturday night at the Civic Complex. The Hawks can clinch a berth in the American Hockey League championship series against the Portland Pirates with 8 win tonight at 7:30 o'clock at the Moncton Coliseum. Game 3, if necessary, is on Wednesday at the Moncton Coliseum. "They did a really good job of standing us up at the blueline," said Cornwall right winger Dave Karpa. "They forced us to dump the puck in because we couldn't carry it across the blueline. "They always seemed to have four guys back and we couldn't find a way to break through. We tried to play dumpand-chase, but their forwards did a good job of holding us up (to allow their defence time to clear the zone)." Craig Fisher scored twice to up his Hawks-leading playoff total to 10 with Dave Tomlinson and Brian Straub completing the attack. It was Straub's first of the post-season to go along with eight assists. Mike Hurlburt spoiled the shutout bid of Hawks' goalie Stephane Beauregard. There was plenty of hype surrounding the series opener with the local game-day newspaper featuring five articles and two advertisements. Still, only 2,480 fans filed into the 4,000-seat building. It was Cornwall's biggest playoff crowd and raises its pergame average to a league-low 1,855 in the post Those who came were given white towels to wave in hopes of leaving the opposition in a white -out. But blue - as in the color of the Hawks' road jersey was much more prominent as the visitors led 2-1 after the first period and closed the summary with two more to open the final frame. United Press International TORONTO - The Toronto Maple Leafs finally put an end to the heroic run of the San Jose Sharks Saturday night and set up an allCanadian duel for the NIL's Western Conference title. Wendel Clark had two goals and an assist to boost the Maple Leafs to a 4-2 win over San Jose, a team that surprised the hockey world by winning its first playoff series ever and almost winning another. "We will be back next year," said San Jose coach Kevin Constantine. "I am very proud of my team and my players. We thought we could win Stanley Cup. And I think we proved it wasn't the impossible dream." Toronto will meet Vancouver in the conference finals beginning tonight on the Maple Leafs' home ice. Felix Potvin mado 30 saves for Toronto, including a series of spectacular stops early in the game that kept the Sharks from gaining early momentum. "You got to give the Sharks a lot of credit," said Toronto centre Doug Gilmour. "They played great. Felix Potvin made a lot of big saves. He was the big man, along with Clark. They came up big for us again." The Sharks entered the league just three years ago and won only 11 games in 1992-93, but qualified for the playoffs this season in Constantine's first year as coach. They stunned Detroit in seven games in the first round and had a 3-2 edge over the Maple Leafs before losing the last two games.
