By Larry Mahoney Of the NEWS Staff Five Canadians and a defenseman from Andover, Mass., and Northwood Prep School in New York will join Dewey Wahlin, the top schoolboy hockey scorer in Minnesota this past winter, to comprise the first recruiting class that will be under the direction of new University of Maine Orono Coach Shawn Walsh. There is still some important paperwork, a foreign student financial statement, that must be completed by the Canadians and their parents, but that should be taken care of within the next few weeks. "I'm really pleased that we were able to attract players of their ability level," said Walsh, who added that the program has also attained some quality people in these recruits. There was some concern that Walsh's late appointment to the position vacated by Jack Semler would hamper the recruiting effort, but Walsh said, "This is a recruiting class I'd be happy with even if I had the benefit of an entire recruiting year. "The key is we have immediately upgraded the talent on the team in specific areas,' continued Walsh. "We have added some mobility on defense and the group of forwards will help us in both the finesse and the grinding styles of play." But he also said that he isn't expecting the freshmen to have a tremendous impact immediately, but does expect them to play "significant roles" next season and to play vital roles in the rebuilding of the program over the next four years. The five Canadians include three forwards and two defensemen and four of them live within a 40-minute drive of each other in British Columbia. Stan Czenczek is a 5-foot-11, 190- pound defenseman from Surrey, British Columbia, who was a first-team all-star for the Nanaimo Clippers in the Western Canada Tier II League. Czenczek, who has just recently turned 1 18 years of age, had nine goals and 42 assists for 51 points this past season. Dave Nonis is the other defenseman and he is a 5-11, 183-pounder from Burnaby, British Columbia, who starred for the Burnaby Blue Hawks this past season and, according to Walsh, was voted the top player at the B.C. Junior Olympic camp in 1982. The forwards include Dave Wensley, a 6-0, 175-pounder from North Vancouver, British Columbia, who was another Western Canada Tier II first team All-Star this past season.
