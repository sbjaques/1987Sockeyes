Jones seemed intent on penalizing any body contact in the late stages and wound up handing out 34 minor penalties - 18 to Richmond and game misconducts to Knights' coach Rick Kozuback and defenceman James Burnett. Kozuback was thrown out for entering the ice surface to check on an injured player; Burnett for failing to go to the dressing room after getting a 10-minute misconduct. The Penticton win overshadowed a fine three-goal, one assist performance by Sockeyes' Dan Baker, who, like the rest of his teammates and coaches, was unavailable for comment following the game. Sockeyes' co-owner Bruce Taylor barred reporters from interviews, claiming "we're too upset to talk."

By DAN STINSON If Richmond Sockeyes are to stay alive in their quest for the B.C. Junior Hockey League championship, they must accomplish a feat tonight that no B.C. team has managed in two years. They must win at Penticton's Memorial Arena. Penticton Knights have not lost to a BCJHL team on home ice since 1984. And the defending league champions lead the best-of-seven final series three games to one following their entertaining 8-7 double overtime triumph over the Sockeyes Tuesday night before about 1,- 200 fans at Richmond Arena.
