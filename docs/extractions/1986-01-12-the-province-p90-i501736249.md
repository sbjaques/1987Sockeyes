## Paragraphs

Staff Reporter Erin Ginnell is gone - finally. Ginnell, who left New Westminster Bruins after the Western Hockey League club fired his father Paddy Nov. 24 from his job as coach and general. manager, was traded Friday to Calgary Wranglers. At the time he left the Bruins, Ginnell was the team's leading scorer with 17 goals and 13 assists in 22 games. But because he was determined not to play for the Bruins, New Westminster got something for nothing with the trade. The Bruins obtained Darin Kimble, a 17-year-old -purpose forward, and the rights to highlytouted Jason Talo, a bantam-age centre playing midget in Richmond.
