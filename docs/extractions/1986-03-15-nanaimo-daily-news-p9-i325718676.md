## Paragraphs

Knights, Sockeyes triumph

By The Canadian Press Chris Anderson scored three goals to help Penticton Knights defeat Kelowna Packers 7-2 Friday night in the opening game of their best-of-seven B.C. Junior Hockey League Interior Division final. In the Coast final, Richmond's Kevin Weremy scored with 38 seconds left to put the Sockeyes past Delta Flyers 5-4 and tie the series at one win apiece. At Penticton, Knight coach Rich Kozuback expressed mixed feelings about the victory. "They (the Packers) had intensity coming off their last series but they sure tired easily - our layoff showed ... we were slow. "We'll have to play an awful lot better in their building if we are going to win in their rink. They play so much better at home.' In addition to Anderson's three goals, the Knights, who led 3-1 and 5-1 by periods, got scoring from Cal Brown, Peter deGraaf, David Shields and Aaron Scott. Ward Carlson and Jamie Kurki scored for Kelowna. Penticton goalie Warren Sharples blocked 30 shots while Frank Romeo made 44 saves in the Packers goal. Weremy's winning goal for Richmond came 29 seconds after Delta had tied the game at 4-4 on a goal by Steve Rennie. The game had first been period and deadlocked 3-3 going 2-2 after into the third. The Sockeyes, who had a 46-25 advantage in shots, got two goals each from Dan Baker and Paul Bifano. In addition to. Rennie, the Delta scorers were Richard Steniford, Lance Momotani and Rob Steele. "There's probably no team in our league that capitalizes on mistakes better than they do," said Richmond assistant coach Bill Wilms.
