## Paragraphs

Notes and quotes from the wonderful world of hockey Stan Czenczek, a defenceman with Nanaimo Clippers, is rated as a sixth round pick by Central Scouting Service for the June 9 entry draft in Montreal. Wayne Blanchin, who coached Czenczek last season, says Richmond Sockeye wingers Steve Tuttle and Richard Novak are ranked in the later rounds by the same scouting service. Doug Bodger, a Chemainus product who enjoyed a fine year with Western Hockey League champion Kamloops Junior Oilers, will be one of the first six picks in the amateur lottery. Detroit Red Wings are high on Bodger and have already flown him to the Motor City for a look around. Regina Pats' centre Dale Derkatch, drafted in the late rounds by Edmonton Oilers last year, will likely play next season in Switzerland, joining Kelly Glowa of Brandon Wheat Kings. Glowa played most of the past season in Switzerland before rejoining the Wheaties. With centres Ray Cote and Mare Habscheid in the Oilers' farm system, Derkatch really has no other alternatives, does he? Brian Benning and Ray Podloski of Portland Winter Hawks and Dave Pasin of Prince Albert Raiders all grew up in Edmonton within 10 blocks of one another, beginning minor hockey at the Delwood Community League outdoor rinks. On June 9 at the Montreal Forum, all three players will be chosen in the first round of the NHL draft.
