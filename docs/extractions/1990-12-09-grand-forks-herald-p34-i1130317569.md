Central High School surprised Bismarck Century, the defending Class A state champion, 32-23 Saturday in the championship dual of the annual Grand Forks S Sertoma tournament. "I'm very surprised," said Central's Jim Hoffman, who scored one of the Redskins' two pins in the final. "We haven't won this since 1976. I can't believe it. *And you go in so intimidated. Century's the best. You see somebody from Bismarck and you're thinking like, *Oh, wow. they're good.' But hey, maybe they're just like everybody else." So what did Uhrich have to do with the victory? He drew probably Central's most difficult assignment, meeting Century 1989 state champion Mike McCormick at 189 pounds. - The match never got past the first period, however , C'hrich won by disqualification when McCormick was called for slamming him to the mat. Chrich lay on the mat after the slam and, after being attended tot by a trainer, sat up. But he was unable to return to competition. "That's the trainer's decision," said Vince Ames. Neither Ames nor Central co-coach Duane Hennessy conferred with Uhrich. "We stay out of that," Ames said. Uhrich's default victory gave Central a 29-23 lead and the 7-0 Redskins hung on for the win when heavyweight Branden Gourneau scored a 5-1 victory. "The slam hurt us," Century coach Willie McCollum ; said. "He brought him down too hard. "But Grand Forks deserved to win. They wrestled like they had nothing to lose. There were so many close matches where we got out-wrestled in the third period." Even without the disqualification victory, Central held a 6-5 advantage in individual wins, with one match ending in a draw. In three Central wins as well as the draw, the Redskins were tied or trailed entering the final two minutes. "Our conditioning showed," Hennessy said. "Our kids are physically in condition and ready to wrestle. They don't stop." While Uhrich's victory was the most timely, Jason, Griffis probably had the most important win. The 112-pounder trailed 3-1 after two periods, but, with a relentless style, came back and won with a pin with one second left. "Jason kept after him, pushing it and pushing it, until he got the pin," Ames said. "That got the fire going for us." The Redskins built a 23-11 win after Hoffman scored a pin at 145, only to see the Patriots rally for a tie by winning their next three matches. That set up the heroics by C'hrich and Gourneau. Whether the win diminishes the mystique of the longstanding power base in Bismarck remains to be seen.
