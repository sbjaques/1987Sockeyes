FROM WIRE REPORTS As expected, the news was not good for Charles Barkley and the Philadelphia 76ers yesterday. The 76ers' All-Star forward will be out for at least two weeks with a slightly torn left knee ligament, the club announced. Barkley underwent a magnetic resonance imaging test, which confirmed that suffered a second degree sprain of the medial collateral when he collided with teammate Rick Mahorn in the third quarter of a 110-101 victory over the Cavaliers on Sunday. Team doctor Jack McPhilemy said the fall caused the injury, which will keep Barkley sidelined until at least April 14. The 76ers (40-32) have already clinched a playoff berth. Dallas Mavericks forward Roy Tarpley was charged with driving while intoxicated, and the district attorney's office said it would ask that his probation for a 1989 conviction be revoked. Tarpley, sidelined since Nov. 9. with a knee injury, is serving two years' probation on a DWI conviction in 1989. Dale Ellis, acquired by the Milwaukee Bucks in a Feb. 16 trade with the Seattle SuperSonics, could be out for the rest of the season because of a back injury, Dr. David Haskell, said. The Chicago Bulls suspended reserve Stacey King for one game without pay for missing practice. He will sit out tonight's home game against Orlando. Hockey The proposed North American Hockey League awarded franchises to Hamilton, Ontario, and Oakland-Sacramento. NAHL President Gordon Stenback of Cleveland made the announcement after a conference call involving franchise owners in Cleveland, Miami, Cincinnati, Atlanta and Dallas. An NAHL meeting has been scheduled in Cleveland on May 3-4 to complete plans for a player draft. "All of the owners will begin looking for coaches and to get their staffs on board," said Stenback. "We're stronger now than we were one month ago or one week ago. We're going ahead." The league plans to begin a 70-game schedule in November. Nine months after Bob Murdoch was named coach of the year, he was fired by the Winnipeg Jets, one of five clubs in the 21-team NHL to miss the playoffs. The Chicago Blackhawks said that winger Michel Goulet will be sidelined for 10 days to two weeks because of a sprained right knee.
