## Paragraphs

The NCAA is investigating whether the mother of a University of Kentucky basketball recruit had her transportation or expenses paid by the school during a campus visit, according to the Louisville CourierJournal. The investigation involves signee Shawn Kemp of Elkhart, Ind., and a visit his mother, Barbara Brown, made to campus Oct. 31-Nov. 1. The NCAA has also been investigating allegations that Kentucky assistant coach Dwane Casey sent the father of recruit Chris Mills $1,000. Kemp, who signed with Kentucky on Nov. 11, is considered by some to be the top college basketball prospect in the country. Kemp and Mills are good friends, which figured in their signing with Kentucky.

The state Senate Environment and Natural Resources Committee will hold public hearings in Tacoma and Seattle next week on salmon and steelhead management issues. The Tacoma hearing, on Wednesday, will focus on South Puget Sound salmon fishing and on bottom trawling, a controversial method of commercial fishing which opponents say damages the habitat and hurts salmon and bottomfish angling. The Seattle hearing, on Tuesday, will concern marine mammal predation in 1 Puget Sound and Cedar River sockeye salmon enhancement. Scheduled to testify in Seattle are state and federal officials and a representative of the environmentalist group Greenpeace, in addition to members of the public.
