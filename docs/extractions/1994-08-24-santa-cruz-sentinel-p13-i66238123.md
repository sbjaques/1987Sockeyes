But Kozlov will be stepping into perhaps the best situation he could find anywhere. As a boy, his heroes were right wing Sergei Makarov and center Igor Larionov, two of the top players in the Soviet Union throughout the 1980s, and now he will be skating with them and talking to them every day. "He's a tall kid and he has good hands," said Larionov, who practiced with Kozlov when he came to San Jose in February for a brief visit. "I would say it will be easier for him to adjust to this hockey style in a short period of time when he has some old folks like us around him to make it easier." The Sharks have been high on Kozlov for a long time, so high that they pulled an elaborate draft-day deal last June. Knowing they could get Kozlov even if they traded away their second pick overall, they swapped that pick with Hartford in a package that included: the sixth pick overall, with which they chose Kozlov; a second-round pick with which they selected Czech defenseman Vlastimil Kroupa, an instant success last season; and future considerations, which ended up giving them the rights to Makarov, their leading scorer last season.

San Francisco Chronicle SAN JOSE - More than a year after they drafted him sixth overall in the 1993 draft, the Jose Sharks finally signed left wing Viktor Kozlov on Tuesday, agreeing on four-year that pay the young Russian forward $3.45 million. Kozlov, 19, has spent the summer at Sharks director of scouting Chuck Grillo's hockey camp in Nisswa, Minn., working with Coach Kevin Constantine and others in the organization. He will come to Sharks training camp next month in Minnesota with an excellent chance at making the team. "I think he'll play in San Jose this season, there's no doubt in my mind," said Grillo. "But he still has to go to training camp.' 'The Sharks like their young European prospects to spend some time playing minor-league hockey to help them adjust to life in North America. But they are so excited about Kozlov they probably will not be able to resist plugging him into the lineup right away. At 6-foot-5, 225 pounds, Kozlov has enough size to become a dangerous player in front of the net. For so large a player, he skates well and handles the puck adeptly.
