Grand Forks Red River High School's boys bas-. ketball team received a rude introduction into its season Friday night when host Jamestown charged to a 54-36 Eastern Dakota Conference win. "We just got beat by lack of experience," Red River assistant Coach Tim Delmore said. "We only have one guy with varsity playing experience on the team, and we played one of the powerhouses of the EDC. We made a lot of rookie mistakes." .1 Jamestown, ranked with Fargo South as the top teams in the EDC, was led by all-stater Darin Jensen who had a game-high 22 points. Teammate Jim Kale added 16. Sophomore Craig Larson was Red River's top scorer with 12. "Jamestown probably has the best individual player in the state in Darin Jensen,". Delmore said. Scott Kalbrener collected two goals and an assist as Grafton edged the Grand Forks Red River high school hockey team 3-2 Friday in Grafton. The loss dropped Red River to 4-1, while Grafton upped record to 3-0. "It wasn't a great team effort," Red River assistant Howard Walker said. "Grafton played well' and was beating us to the puck all night. That one line they have really plays well together." That one line consists of Kalbrener, Jim Langowski and Eric Evans. The trio combined for all of Grafton's scoring. : Kalbrener opened the scoring with just 10 seconds left in the first period. But Red River came back to tie it midway through the second period on a goal by Joff Thompson. Grafton then scored two unanswered goals to go ahead 3-1. Red River's Chris Barta scored the final goal of the game with 2:32 remaining. "We had some pretty good shots on net," Walker said. "But their goalie really played well and came, up big when he had to." Spoilers goalie Blaine Estad made 37 saves while Red River goalie Mike Blank had 29 stops.
