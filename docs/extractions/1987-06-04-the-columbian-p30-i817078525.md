Mike Shimensky Del Harris Lim Chun-ae Alysheba is 8-5 favorite; Bucks name Harris coach Alysheba, bidding for the richest prize in thoroughbred racing, today was installed as the 8-5 early favorite over nine rivals for Saturday's 119th Belmont Stakes. The race will be worth $553,600 if all entries go the post. A victory would earn 1 Alysheba $5 million, the guarantee for sweeping the 12th Triple Crown in history. The Kentucky Derby and Preakness winner again will be ridden by Chris McCarron, who also won last year's Belmont. Alysheba will leave from the No. 4 post position. BASKETBALL: The Milwaukee Bucks today named Del Harris as their new head basketball coach to replace Don Nelson, who resigned after a bitter feud with team owner Herb Kohl. Harris, who was an assistant coach under Nelson last season, was given a three-year contract. He becomes only the third head coach in the franchise's history. Larry Costello coached for nine seasons before Nelson took over in the 1976-77 season. Nelson coached for 11 seasons. Mike Shimensky, trainer of the Los Angeles Clippers for the past six seasons, has been named to fill that post with the Trail Blazers, the club announced Wednesday. He succeeds 13-year veteran Ron Culp, who retired at the end of the Blazer season. Shimensky spent more time in the Clippers' operation than any player, coach or front-office worker, joining the club in September 1981 after nearly 10 years of training and sports clinic experience in the Phoenix area. He is a native of Honolulu and a graduate of Central Washington University.
