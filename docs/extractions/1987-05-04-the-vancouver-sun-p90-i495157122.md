By ELLIOTT PAP Sun Sports Reporter HUMBOLDT, Sask. - After two days and three games, the coaches of Centennial Cup co-favorites Richmond and Humboldt are begging to differ. The bone of contention centres around Ontario champion Pembroke Lumber Kings, and whether they too are legitimate threats for the national junior A hockey crown. "I'm impressed with Pembroke. They're a very disciplined team," said Sockeye coach Orland Kurtenbach after watching the Kings drop a close 4-2 decision to Humboldt's Broncos Sunday night. "It looks like this is a three-team tournament now.' Bernie Lynch, coach of the Broncos, couldn't agree less. "I saw nothing out there that will make me change my mind," countered Lynch. "I still think it will be us and Richmond in the final." The only consensus reached on the tourney's opening weekend is that Atlantic champion Dartmouth Fuel Kids are the dregs of this event. Kids were easy 7-3 losers to Richmond yesterday afternoon, the loss following a 7-2 trouncing they received Saturday from Humboldt. Tonight's game pits the 'Eyes against Pembroke with a semi-final berth assured Richmond if it wins. The Sockeyes are still in fine shape, however, with Dartmouth proving to be so hopeless. In the worst case scenario, Richmond could finish the round-robin at 1-2, as could the Fuel(less) Kids.
