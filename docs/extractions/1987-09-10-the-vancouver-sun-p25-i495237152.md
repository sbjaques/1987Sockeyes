SQUAMISH - Anglers taking some good dolly varden in the rivers, Valley Sporting Goods reports. Lots of salmon are appearing in the rivers but fishing for them is still closed. Fishing at area lakes is slow. PEMBERTON - Duffy, Joffrey, Lizzie and Anderson lakes are good, Spud Valley Sporting Goods reports. The sockeye run is now into Birkenhead river and some trout are holding with the salmon. The trout can be taken on single artificial eggs. KAMLOOPS -Fishing in most of the area lakes is starting to pick up, Yosh's Hook and Tackle reports. Dragonfly nymphs, leeches, shrimp, and Water Boatmen are producing for fly fishermen and Perch Scale and black flatfish and gang trolls for trollers. MERRITT -Fishing in the area has picked up generally, Ponderosa Sports reports. PRINCETON - The highway lakes to the north of Princeton, Dry, Round, Allison and Summit, are warming up again, Princeton Sports Ltd. reports. Chain and Link are reported fair. Hans (fly only) is producing a few trout up to five pounds. Creek fishing is tapering off. OKANAGAN - Fishing is still good but the kokanee are starting to turn color. Trout fishing has improved, with Okanagan Lake producing nine to 12-pound rainbows. Anglers are using Apex Hotspots and squid plugs.

SALTWATER VANCOUVER-HOWE SOUND - The Fraser River mouth currently is a hot spot, Berry's Bait and Tackle, Richmond, reports, with good fishing for sockeye at about 90 feet and pinks at 50 feet. Red and pink hoochies and red Hotspots have been the most effective lures. A couple of northern coho have been seen at Gower Point and the arrival of the run appears immiment, Sewell's at Horsehoe Bay reports. Trolling with strip or hoochies has produced most action recently. Fisherman's Cove Esso Bargereportsan improvement in fishing in the past week, with coho, sockeye and pinks appearing off the south side of Passage Island. Trolling at about 65 feet with red hoochies and Apex Hotspots has produced the most success. Castaway Tackle, North Vancouver, reports there is still good fishing for coho andpinks at the mouths of the Capilano, Seymour and Indian rivers. GIBSON'S-Hyack Marine reports a lot of sockeye showing up on the offshore bank. Coho are in close at Roberts Creek and Gower Point. Moochers are getting some coho, with buzz bombs also effective. Spincasters working from shore have taken some coho. A few northern coho have appeared at Lasqueti Island. PENDER HARBOUR -Fishing in the area is fair, Lowe's Resort reports. A few coho have been taken off Bierre Shoals and some nice fish from Mary Island and Seal Reef, off Texada, all on mooched live herring. POWELL RIVER - Westview Fishing Charters reports fishing at Texada island still good, mostly for mooched coho. CAMPBELL RIVER - Still a few big springs being caught, Campbell River Sportsman reports. Rivers are low andrain likely would improve spring activity. A few northern coho are showing up. COURTENAY -Fishing has been spotty, Jasper's Fishing Resort reports. Lots of coho around in the five to sevenpound range, with only one northern taken so far. PARKSVILLE - Good coho have been showing up in 50 feet or less water by the lighthouse on Ballenas, with live bait fishermen getting best results, Beachcomber Marina at Northwest Bay reports. Some springs are starting to show up, including some feeders. Still lots of coho reported off the kelp beds at Sangster, with live bait best. Trollers have been catching some pinks in the area as well. NANAIMO - Still lots of coho in the area, Island Tackle and Billiards, Nanaimo, reports. Live herring, zingers and strikers have been the best lures and Snake and Entrance Islands the best spots. PORT ALBERNI - Island Tackle and Billiards reports 2,220 salmon, with a total weight of 26,500 pounds, were weighed in during the three-day Port Alberni salmon festival.
