The NHL, struggling to salvage its season, was unable to save its AllStar Game. d. a For the first time in its history, the NHL on Thursday canceled its show-case game because of deepening labor problems. The game was - scheduled for Jan. 21 in San Jose, Calif. * •Stephen Solomon, chief operating .officer of the NHL, said the labor .dispute "makes it impossible for us to go forward." Speaking at a news conference in San Jose, Solomon said the All-Star Game will be played in San Jose in 11997. The 1996 game is in Boston. Sharks president Art Savage called the cancellation "absolutely *the best decision to be made under •the circumstances." He said planning could now be aimed at 1997. The All-Star Game was supposed •to be the first NHL telecast by the •Fox network under its new contract with the league. Said Fox: "We fully understand the more pressing issues needed to be resolved that are necessary to ensure the future growth and stability of the game of hockey." Susan Hammer, the San Jose ;mayor, said she was "profoundly disappointed" by the decision. "But it is made better that the All*Star Game will be here in two ¿years," she said. as Solomon said he did not know what effect the cancellation would have on prospects for reaching a settlement and saving the season. * "But, as time goes by, we're certainly getting closer to putting the season in jeopardy," he said. The announcement came with the lockout having completed 69 days and the NHL's board of governors awaiting a critical meeting in New *-York on Monday.
