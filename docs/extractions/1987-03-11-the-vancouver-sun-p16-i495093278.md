Flames score with new line Canadian Press Calgary Flames' coach Bob Johnson 1 put together a line with two rookies and veteran checker Tim Hunter Tuesday night, and it paid unexpected dividends. Unexpected to everyone but Johnson. "I expect great things out of them," said Johnson, after rookies Joe Nieuwendyk and Gary Roberts scored second-period goals to rally the Flames to a 5-3 National Hockey League victory over the Washington Capitals. Nieuwendyk, a 21-year old centre who signed with the Flames last Thursday, was appearing in his first NHL game after playing three seasons at Cornell University. He scored at 10:03 of the second period to tie the score at 2-2. Roberts, playing in his 23rd NHL game, put the Flames ahead at 13:54. "Timmy took out both defencemen, and I just went in and picked the puck up," said Nieuwendyk. "I couldn't ask for a better way to start. After a disappointing season at Cornell, where we didn't make the playoffs, this is a great opportunity.", Goaltender Mike Vernon, still a rookie despite playing in 21 of the Flames' 22 playoff games last spring, posted his 28th victory of the season for Calgary. The Flames equalled a club record with their 18th road victory of the season. The Flames and Edmonton Oilers are the only clubs in the league to have winning road records this season. •New York Islanders received second-period goals from Mikko Makela, Brent Sutter and Duane Sutter to lead the Isles to a 6-3 win over Pittsburgh Penguins. Mike Bossy had a goal, his 37th, and an assist as the Islanders ended Pittsburgh's two-game winning streak. The Penguins have not won more than two consecutive games since opening the season with a team-record seven straight vietories. •Randy Moller put Quebec ahead for good in the second period and Michel Goulet added two goals in the third as the Nordiques ended a five-game winless streak, beating Hartford Whalers 6-4. The win moved Quebec back into fourth place and the final playoff spot in the Adams Division with 59 points, two more than the idle Buffalo Sabres.

Sockeyes take opener in OT Canadian Press They worked overtime in the B.C. Junior Hockey League Tuesday night and both winners came up with 7-6 victories. In Richmond, Mike McCormick scored at 3:19 of the second overtime period to give the Sockeyes the nod over Delta Flyers in the first game of their best-of-seven semi-final series. Game two goes tonight at 7:30, also at Richmond Arena. Goalie Jeff Clark was outstanding for the Flyers with 51 saves.
