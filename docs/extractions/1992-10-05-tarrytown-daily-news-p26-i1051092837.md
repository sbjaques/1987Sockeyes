Rangers ink deals with four The Rangers signed defensemen Joby Messier and Brad Tiley and left HOCKEY wings Craig Duncanson and Dave Thomlinson yesterday. Terms of the contracts were not disclosed. Messier, a cousin of Rangers captain Mark Messier, played four seasons at Michigan State. Selected in the seventh round of the 1989 draft, the 22-year-old had 21 goals and 48 assists in 171 games for Spartans. He had 13 goals and 15 assists in 41 games last season. Tiley, Duncanson and Thomlinson were free agents. Bruins acquire Kimble: The Boston Bruins acquired right wing Darin Kimble from the expansion Tampa Bay Lightning for center Ken Hodge and defenseman Matt Hervey. Kimble, 23, has played four seasons in the NHL with Quebec and St. Louis. The 6-foot-2, 205-pounder had a goal with three assists in 46 games for the Blues last season. In 177 NHL games, Kimble has 12 goals, 15 assists and 747 penalty minutes. Olajuwon threat Hakeem Olajuwon's agent has BASKETBALL threatened legal action against the Houston Rockets unless his client is traded and the team retracts statements it made him during an injury controversy, according to a published report. Rockets general manager Steve Patterson confirmed Thursday he received the letter from agent Leonard Armato. Jazz sign Crotty: The Utah Jazz signed former Virginia guard John Crotty to a one-year contract. Crotty, 23, played for the Jazz in the Rocky Mountain Revue in Salt Lake City and in the California S Summer League, where he averaged 15.0 points and 11.6 assists. The 6-foot-1 Crotty averaged 20.3 points and leagueleading 6.1 assists for Greenville of the Global Basketball League last season.
