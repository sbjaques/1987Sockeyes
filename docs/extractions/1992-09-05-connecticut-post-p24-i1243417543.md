CONNECTICUT POST Saturday, BRIEFLY A wire staff, report MacAdam named Senators coach Don MacAdam, who coached the Cape Breton Oilers to three playoff appearances in the American Hockey League, was named head coach of the AHL's New Haven Senators Friday. MacAdam, 41, had two second- and a third-place finish in his three seasons with the Oilers. He was an assistant coach with the Detroit Red Wings for three seasons before heading to Cape Breton. BOXING/Great day for Danes Jimmi and Johnny Bredahl of Denmark made pro boxing history Friday night as they won World Boxing Organization titles in successive bouts. The brothers, both undefeated European champions, also became Denmark's first pro boxing champions in modern times. Johnny Bredahl scored a unanimous 12-round decision over WBO junior bantamweight champ Jose Quirino of Mexico, and less than two hours later, his older brother Jimmi outpointed veteran Daniel Londas of France after 12 rounds for the WBO junior lightweight championship. HOCKEY/Bruins deal Hodge The Boston Bruins acquired right wing Darin Kimble from the expansion Tampa Bay Lightning for center Ken Hodge and defenseman Matt Hervey. • The New York Rangers signed defensemen Joby Messier and Brad Tiley and left wings Craig Duncanson and Dave Thomlinson. Messier, a distant cousin of Rangers captain Mark Messier, played four seasons at Michigan State and had 13 goals and 15 assists in 41 games last season. The Winnipeg Jets have been served with a lawsuit launched by Russian recruit Alexei Zhamnov. Zhamnov wants damages out of his recently signed contract with the Jets, which guarantees him a reported $1.25 million over three years. He apparently grew dissatisfied with the deal after the Jets signed Finnish prospect Teemu Selanne for a reported $2.7 million, including a $1.5-million signing bonus. BASKETBALL/Jazz ink Crotty The Utah Jazz signed former Virginia guard John Crotty to a one-year contract. Crotty set a Virginia record for career assists with 683. He averaged 15.5 points as a senior. Hakeem Olajuwon's agent demanded that the Houston Rockets trade his client and retract statements the team made about Olajuwon during an injury controversy. Speculation surfaced that Olajuwon was faking the hamstring injury to gain leverage for a contract extension.
