## Paragraphs

Sabres: The club signed center Dale Hawerchuk, 29, to a multiyear contract that will pay him $1.2-million per season. Terms were not released. Jets: The club has been served with a lawsuit by Russian recruit Alexei Zhamnov, who wants damages out of his recently signed contract, which guarantees him a reported $1.25-million over three years. Zhamnov apparently grew unhappy with the deal after the Jets signed Finnish prospect Teemu Selanne for a reported $2.7-million, including a $1.5-million signing bonus. Rangers: The club signed defensemen Joby Messier and Brad Tiley and left wings Craig Duncanson and Dave Thomlinson. Messier, a distant cousin of Rangers captain Mark Messier, played four seasons at Michigan State. - Information from the Associated Press was used in this report.

TAMPA The Lakeland Civic Center will look like Grand Central Station during the Tampa Bay Lightning's training camp, which begins Sept. 12. The Lightning has invited 72 players to camp. Only the Ottawa Senators, the other expansion team, will have a larger camp with 82 players. "It's going to be competitive, that's for sure," Lightning coach Terry Crisp said. "It will be hectic for a while." The Lightning plans to carry 22 or 23 players. Twenty are allowed in uniform for each game. Not attending training camp will be veteran defenseman Bob McGill, who was placed on waivers, and right winger Darin Kimble, who was traded Friday to Boston for center Ken Hodge and defenseman Matt Hervey.
