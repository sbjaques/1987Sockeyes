MONCTON - The message worked. With his club trailing 4-0 after the first period, Saint John Alpines' head coach Jamie Kennedy had a stern chat with his players in the intermission of last night's Maritime Junior A Hockey League game. Saint John rebounded to upset the Moncton 'Gagnon Beavers 5-4 in front of 822 fans at the J. Louis Levesque Arena. Tied 4-4 following 40 minutes, Kortney McAllister potted the winner with 10:13 remaining. It was an unassisted effort, a slapshot from the slot high to goaltender Tony Gallant's glove side. Moncton, the defending champion, won the first three games in lopsided fashion against first-year Saint John. Saint John has won the last two meetings. "I just said, 'Boys, this is a joke," said Kennedy, réferring to his first intermission speech. "I said, 'Boys, we're not hitting anyone. We're playing disgraceful.' "Down 4-0, it would've been easy for us to roll over, but we didn't. The guys deserve a lot of credit. They came up big in the final 40 minutes. I can't say enough about the character they showed in making this comeback." Moncton, 19-9-0, remains in first place overall, two points in front of the Amherst Mooseheads, 18-9-1. The Beavers lead the Roger Division by 16 points over the runner Charlottetown Moosehead Abbies, 10- 16-2. The Summerside Western Capitals, 10-17-1, are one point behind Charlottetown and five in front of last -place Saint John, 8-19-1. Scott Adams, Chris Bartlett, Seth Bowan and Jamie Topshee also scored for Saint John. Serge Bourgeois, Germain LeBlanc, Kevin Cameron and Mark Seamone countered for Moncton.

The National Hockey League yesterday named Scott Bowman of the Detroit Red Wings and Doug MacLean of the Florida Panthers as the head coaches for next month's All-Star Game. Bowman will coach the Western Conference and MacLean will handle the Eastern Conference for the All-Star Game, which will be played Saturday, Jan. 20, at the new FleetCentre in Boston. Under new rules in effect for this season, the coaches with the best records in each conference through games of Dec. 27 will head the All-Star teams. Bowman's Red Wings lead the Western Conference with 52 points. MacLean's Panthers are tied with the New York Rangers for the Eastern Conference lead with 52 points but MacLean was selected over New York's Colin Campbell on the basis of more victories. Marc Crawford of the Colorado Avalanche will serve as Bowman's assistant and Ed Johnston of the Pittsburgh Penguins will assist MacLean on the Eastern Conference bench. 12 NEW YORK, United Press Internationale

PHILADELPHIA, United Press International Blues obtain Matteau The New York Rangers yesterday traded left wing Stephane Matteau to the St. Louis Blues for centre Ian Laperriere. The 26-year-old Matteau played with New York for the past two seasons after spending three years with the Chicago Blackhawks and one with the Calgary Flames. Matteau, a 6-foot-3, 210-pounder, had four goals, two assists and 22 penalty minutes this season. Matteau scored one of the most memorable goals in Rangers' history in the second overtime of Game 7 against New Jersey two seasons ago for the Eastern Conference Championship. It was a heartbreaking loss for the Harrison Trimble High Trojans of Moncton during high school hockey action yesterday at the 28th annual Saint John Irving Oil Christmas Hockey Tournament. Tied at 2-2 with 30 seconds on the clock, one of the Trojans accidently popped the puck over his own goalie and into the net, delivering the Kennebecasis Valley High School Crusaders of Quispamsis a 3-2 win after three rousing periods of hockey. Tyler McDonald started the scoring for KVHS in the first frame with Dan Curry responding for HT. Pat Murray sparked the Trojans to a 2-1 lead in the second with a marker only to have Crusader Chris Haines tie it up with five minutes to go in the third. The final goal goes to KVHS's Ceri Gillies, the last opposing player to touch the puck before the Trojans slipped it past their own posts for the loss. Top scorers in yesterday's action with three goals apiece include peewee A players Nathan Harvey of Florenceville, Justin Libby of Bangor and Steve Noble of Campbellton. Saint John bantam A player Mike McCormick and Brad McHugh of the St. Malachy's Memorial High Saints also had hat tricks while bantam AAA sharp shooter Adam Colpitts of Quispamsis netted a total of four, including a hat trick in a 5-0 win over Carlton York- Victoria.
