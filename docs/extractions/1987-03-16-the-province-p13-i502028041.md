The Packers beat the Sockeyes 4-3 in Richmond and 7-4 in Kelowna but that was before Richmond made extensive changes to its roster, loading up with players like goaltender Frank Romeo and defencemen Matt Hervey and Mike Claringbull (both 20 and with major junior experience). "I would say we're whatever you want us to be,' said Swail when asked about his team. That ability to do it all resulted in a league-best 45-5-2 regular season record, a momentum that could be severely tested by the Sockeyes.

More than the B.C. Junior Hockey League championship is at stake when the Richmond Sockeyes and the Kelowna Packers take to the ice tomorrow night at 7:30. The best-of-nine series between the Coastal Division champion Sockeyes and the Interior Division title-holding Packers also provides the teams with the chance theoretically to make some money. Play in the series, which continues Wednesday at Richmond Arena before heading off to Kelowna for games Friday and Saturday, really means something and as such attracts far more fans than regular season contests. That consideration also means the number of games played in each community is of key importance and will determine the series schedule. If it's tied after four games, two more games will be played in Richmond, the next two in Kelowna Memorial Arena and then the last game back in Richmond.
