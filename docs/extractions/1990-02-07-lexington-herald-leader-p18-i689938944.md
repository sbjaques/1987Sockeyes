## Paragraphs

Charles A. (Chuck) Mahan, 50, a resident of 180 Peachtree Dr., died Tues. at St. Joseph Hospital in Lexington. He was a manufacturing supervisor for Texas Instruments and a veteran of the U.S. Army. Survivors include his wfie, Mary Delila Mahan, Versailles; three daughters, Mrs. Dwayne (Lisa) Brown, Versailles, Mrs. James (Linda) Morris and Mrs. Thomas (Shari) Day, both of Nicholasville; three sons, Richard Allen and David Bruce Mahan, both of Lexington, and James Todd Mahan of Nicholasville; a sister, Mrs. Bradley (Sherri) Abney, Lexington; a brother, Steven Mahan, Lexington; and three grandchildren, Joshua Dwayne Brown, James Luther Morris Jr., and Mary Elizabeth Day. Funeral services will be 1 p.m. Thurs. at the Duell-Clark Funeral Chapel conducted by the Rev. Gary E. Black. Burial in Sunset Memorial Gardens. Casketbearers will be Dwayne Brown, James Morris, Thomas Day, Tony Bobbitt, Steve Castle, Brad Abney, Jimmy Mahan, and Brandon Bobbitt. Friends may call at the funeral chapel from 2-4 and 6-8 p.m. today.
