It was rather sad to see Phil Niekro end his Major League Baseball career, earlier this week. Rod Hardy and I had the honor of seeing old 'Knucksy' in the Toronto Blue Jays' clubhouse during their late August road trip to Seattle. There he was, sitting at his stall in his tee-shirt and underwear, puffing on a cigarette and staring at the floor. He had a Bass fishing hat on and he looked more like a weary sailor than a pro ball player. I almost felt sorry for Niekro as the rest of the youthful Jays gobbled down lasagna and watched a Yankees' game on TV. My sorrow turried to happiness when I realized Niekro had broken into the Majors when I was seven-years-old. And here he was trying to help the Blue Jays win the American League East pennant 23 years later. He had' a fine and long career, playing a game he • loved and dedicated •himself to playing. No need to feel sorry for Knucksy. He was one of the best.
