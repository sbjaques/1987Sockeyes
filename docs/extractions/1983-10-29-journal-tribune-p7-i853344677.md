BIDDEFORD - For the past two years, the University of Maine hockey team has had problems establishing themselves as a team to be reckoned with in Division I hockey. Last year, the team finished with a 5-25 record, a record which was just barely improved from the year before. Some spectators say program has grow too fast, contending that it should still be in Division II as it was when the program began. Some even say that hockey is just a fad and it, like most fads, will pass.

By CHARLOTTE McATEE Staff Writer BIDDEFORD When Babson College faces off against the University of Maine in a preseason hockey game at the Biddeford Arena Saturday night, there will be a very familiar figure on the ice for Babson. Wearing number three for the Division II power will be center Jim Gunn, who played ice hockey for Biddeford High School before becoming a vital part of the Babson offense. A senior marketing major, Gunn was a sohopmore in high school when Biddeford instituted varsity hockey and was part of an undefeated team. The winning has continued at Babson, as last year the Beavers finished second in the ECAC Division II and fourth in the nation.
