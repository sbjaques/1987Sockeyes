By Howie Stalwick Staff writer Spokane's top hockey teams have hired a proven college champion to try to turn them junior champions for the first time. Dale Zeman, who played for the NCAA champions of Denver University in 1968 and 1969, has been named assistant coach of the major junior Spokane Chiefs and head coach of the Junior B Spokane Braves. The decision to combine the jobs is part of a new, closer, working relationship between Chiefs and Braves. The Chiefs have agreed to provide the local-oriented Braves with up to five out-of-town players this season. Bob Strumm, general manager of the Chiefs, said his club might also add a part-time assistant coach sometime after training camp opens next Friday at the Spokane Coliseum.
