Fraser River good for sockeye, Fisherman's Cove Esso reports. Point Grey bellbuoy to North Arm ietty, Separation Marker to ietty, plus ietty back-eddy producing lots of limits. Troll with red lures. Berry's Bait and Tackle, Richmond, suggests depths of 40-60 feet for pink, 70-100 for sockeye at North Arm mouth. Fisherman's Cove reports coho at Cowan Point, Seal Rocks, south end of Passage Island. Sewell's Landing, Horseshoe Bay, says chances fair at Passage Island channel marker, mooching or trolling at 60 feet for pink, sockeye, chinook. Try bright orange Hot Spot. Slow for coho. Morning tide changes triggering bite. Vancouver Fish-On Guiding reports mouth of Capilano River good for coho, drift mooching with cut plug or live bait just off the bottom. Best bite in first four hours of flood tide. Good beachcasting for coho at Cap mouth reports Castaway Tackle, North Vancouver. Mouth of Seymour River good for coho, pink. Hyack Marine, Gibsons, reports fair chances for coho (mooch live herring) and pink (troll red hoochie) at Cape Roger Curtis, The Gap, Camp Byng. PENDER HARBOR - Fair to good for coho at Bierre Shoals, Thormanby Island, southern tips of Lasqueti Island, Texada Island, reports Lowe's Resort. Spotty for chinook. POWELL RIVER - Good to excellent for coho to 10 pounds, fair for chinook, reports Westview Fishing Charters. Mooch live. Best locations: top end of Texada, Harwood Island, Grant Reefs, Mystery Reef, Rebecca's Reef. CAMPBELL RIVER - Good for large chinook from Discovery Pier to Orange Point, plus Cape Mudge Lighthouse, River Sportsman reports. Trolled plugs working best. Good for pink and sockeye in Seymour Narrows. I Fair for coho at Francisco Point. COURTENAY Y - -Fair for coho (herring strip or Hot Spot), chinook (Buzz- Bomb or Zinger) a quarter-mile off Bates Beach, reports Jasper's Fishing F Resort. PARKSVILLE - Fair for coho at Ballenas Island, good at Sangster Island, reports Beachcomber Resort, Northwest Bay. Chin• ook pounds at Mistaken Island. NANAIMO- - Excellent for coho at Entrance Island, Snake Island, Thrasher Rock, reports Island Tackle and Billiards. Jigging, mooching effective. Fair for chinook to 25 pounds at Entrance, Five Finger Island, Neck Point. PORT ALBERNI - Good for chinook to 40 pounds, plus sockeye, in Alberni Inlet, from Franklin River to Home Island, Harbour Sports reports. Barkley Sound fair, at Cape Beale, Whittlestone, for coho and chinook. FRESHWATER LOWER MAINLAND - Fraser River bars slow for chinook, according to Berry's, and to Fred's Custom Tackle, Vedder Crossing. Cutthroat prospects improving in Stave River, Nicomen Slough, Harrison River. PEMBERTON Spud Valley Sporting Goods suggests these lakes: Lizzie, Joffre, Birkenhead (troll deep with plug for kokanee), Anderson, Blackwater, Duffey (Wedding Band and small troll). HOPE - Coquihalla River slow for steelhead (fly fishing only, catch and release), reports Cheyenne Sports. Cheyenne reports Skagit River slow for rainbow. Kawkawa Lake slow for kokanee. MERRITT-PRINCETON LAKES - Lakes remain too warm for consistent fishing. Ponderosa Sports, Merritt, reports Stump good for kokanee and rainbow. Higher elevation lakes recommended, fish close to bottom. Aspen Grove Store reports Corbett the most productive lake in area. Evenings best. Princeton Sports Ltd. rates prospects fair throughout. Similkameen River rated good in evening. KAMLOOPS LAKES - Fair throughout, Yosh's Hook and Tackle reports. Water levels exceptionally low in many lakes, fish sluggish. Ruddick's Fly Shop, Burnaby, reports Roche good using green Carey. OKANAGAN LAKES -Okanagan good for limits of kokanee says Clem's Sporting Good Sales, Kelowna. Upper lakes, McCullough, Beaver, Postill (brook trout and rainbow) rated fair.
