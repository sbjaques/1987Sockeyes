"You can't expect to beat teams with 15-20 NHL draft choices with a team that has three NHL draft choices," said Walsh. "I'm somewhat disappointed because I think we've gotten as much out of our players as we can, and we've been in 15 of our 17 games well into the third period. To have only five wins is a bit of a disappointment, but not unexpected." Walsh feels the building blocks for the future have also been put into place in the form of discipline, academic emphasis, and the hockey systems that have been implemented this season. "And the second layer (of the foundation) may be even stronger because of the tremendous work done by our two assistant coaches in recruiting," said Walsh. "I wouldn't trade the verbal commitments we have now (for next season) with anybody's in the country. What we've accomplished since April is satisfying.' Transfer Mike Golden, a secondround draft choice of the Edmonton Oilers; Jack Capuano, a fifth-round choice of the Toronto Maple Leafs; Chris Cambio, a standout for Mount St. Charles (R.I.) and Shawn Anderson, a multi talented 16-year-old playing for Quebec Development AAA Hockey League (midget) power Lac St. Louis, have already made verbal commitments to attend Maine next fall. The Bears have been playing seven freshmen on a regular basis and six of them are among the team's top 10 scorers: Dave Wensley (2nd with 6-6- 12), Dewey Wahlin (5th, 9-1-10), Dave Nonis (2-7-9, 6th), Todd Studnicka (4-3- 7, 7th), Stan Czenczek (0-7-7, 9th) and Scott Drevitch (1-5-6, 10th). Sophomore Kevin Mann (2-11-13) is the leading scorer with seniors Ray Jacques (5-7-12) and Pete Maher (6-5- 11) and junior John McDonald (3-4-7) also among the top 10. Czenczek, Nonis, and Drevitch are defensemen, and the only freshman not among the top 10, Paul Lelievre (2-1-3), is a defensive specialist who has effectively shadowed some of the nation's best players. "I like the way our freshmen are coming along and that was very evident in the way they played Saturday night (in Maine's 8-6 loss to Wisconsin)," said Walsh.
