## Paragraphs

Defending Centennial Cup champion Richmond Sockeyes make their first of four B.C. Junior Hockey League visits to Civic Arena tonight (8 o'clock), hoping to gain revenge for last week's loss to Nanaimo Clippers. The Sockeyes, guided by former Vancouver Canuck captain and coach Orland Kurtenbach, boast perhaps the most exciting player in the league in shifty five-foot-nine centre Mario Desjardins of Terrace. Desjardins, a three-year Western Hockey League veteran who played with Kamloops Blazers last year, leads the BCJHL with 31 points. He joins 65-goal scorer Rob Clarke and speedy returnee Tony Bobbitt on a Sockeye team expected to finish second in the Coastal Division. "We're about as far from the Centennial Cup as we were at this point last season," said Kurtenbach. "We'll do the same thing as we did last year and go by process of elimination and wait for someone to emerge."
