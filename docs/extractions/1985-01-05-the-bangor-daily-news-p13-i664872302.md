"I asked her if Stan had made his decision, which is something I never do. 1 always deal directly to the player. She said he had and that he was going to Maine." Walsh was stunned and overjoyed and his persistence has been rewarded. Czenczek was a busy man on Friday as he was one of just four defensemen getting a regular shift. "I enjoy playing four D (defensemen) because its gets you more involved, in the game," said Czenczek.

Czenczek nearly opted for Denver

By Larry Mahoney Of the NEWS Staff There was at least one University of Maine-Orono player who was no stranger to the University of Denver on Friday night when the two teams faced off in the first game of their two-game series. Black Bear freshman defenseman Stan Czenczek, by his own admission, came "pretty close" to attending Denver. "Denver recruited me and I visited the school," said Czenczek, who was Maine's -rated defenseman over the first half of the season, according to Bear Coach Shawn Walsh. "The day I got home from my visit to Denver, Shawn offered me a scholarship. I spent the next few weeks making up my mind." The offers, according to Czenczek, were similar. "But Shawn was a lot more persistent and seemed to want me a lot more (than Denver Coach Ralph Backstrom said Czenczek. "And some of the guys on the Denver team didn't look, up to coaching staff very much. They seemed to be more concerned about playing for themselves (rather than the team). I wanted to go to the school where I could learn the most hockey and I felt Shawn would teach me a lot more." *Czenczek also said Backstrom offered full scholarships to a couple of players who played in the same league he did last year (the British Columbia Junior Hockey League) and "I didn't think they were very- good players. That turned me off.'
