I JASON PHILLIPS: a positive attitude is showing up in WHL scoring statistics Alex Barta photo

By Jeff Rud Times-Colonist staff Jason Phillips has waited five years, but it's finally happened. confidence level has caught up to his talent and the combination is paying big dividends for the Victoria Cougars this season. For the first time since he made his initial stab at the Western Hockey League five seasons ago, Phillips is scoring consistently. He has 21 goals and 15 assists in 23 games and is the leading scorer for coach Wayne Naka's Cougars. "Confidence is the big difference," Phillips said this week. "I have the ability to play in this league and Wayne has brought it out. He puts a lot of confidence in me." Actually, Phillips' surge of confidence began late last November. It came after one of the bleakest periods of his young life when he quit the Seattle Thunderbirds and considered giving up on hockey altogether. He had been bounced around from Kamloops, to Brandon, to Seattle and had never had a real shot at playing regularly in the WHL. "My confidence was at it's worst when I quit Seattle," recalled the 20-year-old Coquitlam native. "I felt like I wasn't getting on with life - that I was falling behind." Then, all of a sudden, things started going his way. He got a call from Bruce Taylor, the owner of the B.C. Junior Hockey League's Richmond Sockeyes, asking if he'd like to finish out the season there under coach Orland Kurtenbach. He took the offer, and it led to a key role in the Sockeyes' Centennial Cup championship drive. At Richmond, Phillips scored 29 goals in just 34 games. He also graduated from high school. McNabb to coach Clippers The Canadian Press NANAIMO - Larry McNabb has returned to coach the Nanaimo Clippers of the British Columbia Junior Hockey League, team president Vern Benedictson said Tuesday. McNabb, 48, became the Clippers' winningest coach when he was behind the bench between 1974 and 1982, capturing three BCJHL titles. The Clippers are tied for second place with a record of 12-11-1 in the Coastal Division and have been without a coach since Rich Dutka was fired Nov. 18. McNabb left the Clippers in 1982 when new ownership moved the team to Esquimalt.
