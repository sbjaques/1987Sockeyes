By Dwayne Tingley Sports Editor Centre Dave Tomlinson, one of several Moncton Hawks who have struggled offensively during their opening round playoffs series, says it's time to put up or shut up. Saint John Flames lead the Hawks 3-2 in the best-of-seven American Hockey League's Atlantic Division semifinal. Game 6 is set for tonight at 7:30 p.m. at the Coliseum and if a seventh game is needed it will be played Wednesday at Harbour Station. Flames are leading the series largely because they've shut down most of the Hawks top scorers, including Tomlinson, John LeBlanc, Harijs Vitolinsh and Russ Romaniuk. "Personally, I've been quite happy with my two-way play, but I'm also expected to contribute more and score some goals," Tomlinson said after practice Sunday. "I can't worry about what's happened in the past games," added Tomlinson, who scored 23 goals in 39 regular season games with the Hawks. "All I can say is that I'm due and it couldn't come at a better time. Coach Rob Laird said he was confident the team's top guns won't Things will start going our way pretty soon." be silenced for much longer. "I don't expect all of them to break loose all at one time, but I do expect them to dig a little deeper and find something extra in their games,' Laird said. "I'm looking for one or two of these guys to find the net soon," the coach continued. "All of these guys are going to be given every opportunity players. to break loose and see what they can do. I believe in these "I can tell that they want to make things happen and with that kind of a work ethic and commitment, I know they will make positive things happen." Laird said an offensive outburst would be a big boost to the Hawks, but noted "it takes a team effort to win, not just one or two players." "Only a goaltender can win a game all by himself and the goaltending on both teams has been outstanding this series," Laird said. "Stephane Beauregard has stepped forward and given us solid goaltending. Now, we have to score some goals for him." Tomlinson said the Hawks are looking forward to tonight's game and the opportunity to play a seventh and deciding game back at Harbour Station.

"The series has been a close one, just like everyone expected it would be," said Tomlinson, who spent almost half the season in the NHL with - the parent Winnipeg Jets. - "There's been a lot of intensity and it's hard-fought all of the way. It's playoff hockey and if you're any sort of hockey player at all this is what you live and die for." Laird said he sensed a positive mood on the team at practices during the weekend. "We talked about all the excitement that a seventh game would bring, but we agreed that under no means will we prepare for that game until we work hard and win Game 6," Laird said. "We'll have to play a smart game because we know that Saint John is going to be coming at us hard. They don't want to extend this series to a seventh game and they're looking to wrap it up (tonight). "For us, it means hard work and playing with poise. We've got to execute and not over-react to anything that happens during the game. It's physical hockey with discipline and poise." . Tomlinson, a third-year pro, said the Hawks are disappointed, but not discouraged by their showing so far in the series. "We're certainly not in the best situation, but we're not down and out by any means," Tomlinson said. "Our focus is on (tonight's) game and that's all. We have to work on making this our best effort of the series. If we can win this one, we'll worry about crossing the next bridge."
