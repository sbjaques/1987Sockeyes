Hawks got in trouble early. Winger Al Stewart took a questionable five-minute high-sticking penalty just 2:53 into the game and the Maple Leafs scored on the power play. Second-year left winger Joe Sacco scored three goals, his first three of the series, for the Maple Leafs. Other scorers were: Guy Larose, Matt Martin, Ken McRae and Rob Cimetta. Hawks will have meetings with Laird in the next couple of days before the coach and management of the NHL parent Winnipeg Jets begin plans for next season. Only nine of the 23 players who finished the season with the Hawks have contracts for next year. Meanwhile, Jean Yves-Roy scored the winning goal 11:15 into overtime as the Binghamton Rangers rallied to defeat the Baltimore Skipjacks 4-3 Monday night. The victory gives the Rangers a 3-2 lead in the opening round of the best-of-seven Calder Cup series. Game six is scheduled for Wednesday night at Binghamton. If a seventh game is necessary, it will be played Friday at Binghamton. Several league observers are predicting a St. John's-Binghamton final. Yves-Roy, a former University of Maine star, took a feed from Chris Cichocki and blasted a slap shot from the top of the left faceoff circle that sailed over the right shoulder of goaltender Duane Dersken.

HALIFAX (Special) St. John's Maple Leafs can go back to The Rock and the Moncton Hawks can go. on vacation. Maple Leafs scored four goals in the third period Monday night and overpowered the Hawks 8-2 in an American Hockey League playoff game before 2,783 spectators at the Halifax Metro Centre. Maple Leafs won the best-of-seven Atlantic Division semifinal 4-1 and will face the Cape Breton Oilers in the next round, beginning Thursday night in St. John's. A four-month-old municipal workers strike has ended in St. John's and the Maple Leafs voted after the game to play the remainder of their playoff home games at Municipal Stadium in the Newfoundland capital. Left winger Todd Hawkins said the Leafs were looking forward to playing their home games "in front c of the best fans in the league." "I don't think we had any other choice," said Leafs goalie Damian Rhodes, who played every game against the Hawks. "It wasn't the fans who caused us to leave, but they've been tremendously supportive, even when the were away," he added. "And after all, it says St. John's Maple Leafs on our jerseys." Meanwhile, the Hawks have several months to contemplate what might have been.
