(Herald staff writer) Vernon Lakers have never lost more than one game at a time this season. Can Calgary Canucks do the impossible and shatter that monument of consistency? The Canucks beat the Lakers 7-3 Tuesday night in the first game of a Centennial Cup quarterfinal. Game 2 goes tonight, also at Max Bell Arena. The Canucks, 51-9 in the Alberta Junior Hockey League's regular season, are trying to break the B.C. Junior League's two-year domination of the Centennial Cup, the symbol of Canadian Tier II supremacy. Richmond Sockeyes won last season while Penticton Knights took the cup the year before. No Calgary team has won the Centennial Cup. With the victory, the Canucks kept alive a streak of their own. They have yet to lose a series opener in this season's playoffs. Brandy Semchuk led the underdog Canucks with two thirdperiod goals. Dave Loewen, Dean Larson, Tim Kollman, Mike Bertamini (shorthanded) and Dennis Fleming supplied single markers. Farrell Goodwin (power play), Mark Greyeyes and Wes Elliott replied for the Lakers, who outshot Calgary 30-28. The first period was tied 1-1, but the Canucks led 4-3 after the second. Dean Larson gave Calgary a 2-1 lead with a breakaway goal at 6:46 of the second. The goal came courtesy a steal by Bertamini. Tim Kollman ripped a slapshot along the ice at 7:22, providing the Canucks with a 3-1 lead.
