HOCKEY: Lightning acquires Hodge Bruins acquired right wing oM The Boston Darin Kimble from the expansion Tampa Bay Lightning yesterday for center Ken Hodge and defenseman Matt Hervey. 8T. Kimble, 23, has played four seasons in the ea NHL with Quebec and St. Louis. The 6-foot2, 205-pounder had a goal with three assists in 46 games for the Blues last season. In 177 NHL games, Kimble has 12 goals, 15 assists and 747 penalty minutes. Hodge, 26, was named to the NHL's allrookie team following the 1990-91 season. He split last season between the Bruins and their AHL affiliate in Maine. He had six goals and 11 assists in 42 games with Boston and six goals and 11 assists in 19 games for Maine after being sent down on Feb. 18. He's the son of former Bruins star Ken Hodge Sr. Hodge Sr. played with Lightning executive Phil Esposito for Stanley Cup champion Bruins teams in 1970 and 1972. BOXING: Dane wins title Johnny Bredahl became Denmark's first world professional boxing champion in modern times yesterday when he scored a unanimous 12-round decision over World Boxing Organization junior bantamweight champion Jose Quirino of Mexico in Copenhagen, Denmark. Bredahl, 24, the European champion, extended his winning streak to 15. The three judges scored the fight 118-111, 119-110 and 118-110. The three first rounds were about even. Then the Dane, supported by a partisan crowd at the Parken, Copenhagen's new soccer stadium, took command. "I expected a hard fight, but I had an easy payday," Bredahl said.
