## Paragraphs

Quesnel crushed Grande Prairie four games to one to claim the Peace Cariboo Junior Hockey League championship on the weekend. The Millionaires thus became the seventh PCJHL team to go after the Mouat Cup for the provincial junior A championship. But the PCJHL, a combination of teams from the Peace Junior Hockey League and the Cariboo that began in 1975, has never captured the provincial title against B.C. Junior Hockey League representatives. "'We haven't won a game yet," admitted PCJHL commissioner Bob Leer. In fact, the closest a PCJHL team came to winning was three years ago in Prince George when the Spruce Kings lost in double overtime to the Langley Eagles. The Millionaires take on the veteran-loaded Richmond Sockeyes at 7:30 p.m. tomorrow at Minoru Arena. The best-of-three series continues at 7:30 p.m. Saturday and again Sunday, if necessary. The Sockeyes are undefeated in the playoffs, winning 13 consecutive games to sweep three series. Richmond coach Orland Kurtenbach hadn't seen any film or heard many reports on Quesnel earlier this week but plans to get some more data on what he knows to be a young team. He expects the Sockeyes to play their own game, a physical, defensive one that is also capable of exploding for goals when needed.
