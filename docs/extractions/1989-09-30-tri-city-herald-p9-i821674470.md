Sunday, 6 p.m. The team- record goal vs. Seattle spurt from 6:37 to 7:06 of the third by Kalvin Knibbs, Bill Lindsay and Scott Levins was not really the result of new strategy but frustration from trailing the entire game, said Steve McNutt. "It was basically pride in our team and our fans." said McNutt, who picked up two assists and twice brought the crowd to its feet with two first-period goals. But the first huge ovation came during the pre-game introduction of Barnes, who returned to the Tri-Cities after a prolonged tryout with the Winnipeg Jets of the National Hockey League. While Barnes may have worn himself out in his first game back, he had plenty of skill left over despite playing in even-strength. power-play and penalty -killing situations. "Up there (in the NHL) I skated about every fourth shift." said Barnes. "Tonight I was dying out there. I was really sucking wind and just trying to get used to being out there so much.' With 12:54 left in the game, he picked the pocket of Spokane's Milan Dragicevic and casually beat goalie Shawn Dietrich to give the Americans a 9-7 lead. He capped his six-point performance with slick pass to Devin Derksen for the Americans' 10th score, a power-play goal at 18:52. "I'm getting about five times more shifts than I got last year." said Derksen, after his four-point night. "If you keep working hard, then you will play. The fourth-line guys hardly ever played last year, and we had problems in the playoffs when (Sean) LeBrun and (Steve) Jaques went out because of guys who weren't used to playing. Offensively the Americans showed plenty of spunk. But it was the expense of defense which may force Tri-City Coach Rick Kozuback to reexamine his strategy. You aren't going to play Kamloops, allow nine goals and win," said Kozuback, exhausted after his WHL debut and still weakened by the injuries he suffered in a car accident last week. •We need to become a lot more defensive minded to make things easier for our goaltenders. The furious scoring pace forced both teams to reach for their backup goaltenders. The Americans' starter Steve Passmore lasted until 15:14 of the second. giving way to Kevin Courchesne. Dietrich relieved John Colvin after Levins' marker at 7:06. And Dietrich mostly stemmed the tide.
