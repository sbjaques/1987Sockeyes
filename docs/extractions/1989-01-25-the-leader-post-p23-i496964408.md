BRANDON - Steve Jaques is a player. In hockey terminology, that means he comes to play. Every night. At home or on the road. So when he saw Mike Modano, the dipsy-doodle danderoo from Detroit, come motoring over the blue line Tuesday night, Jaques lowered the boom. It didn't matter to him that this was the Western Hockey League's all-star game, an East versus West tilt that sometimes has as much body contact as a game of solitaire. What mattered to Jaques was that this was hockey. And Modano was infringing on his turf. Modano was the first player selected in the National Hockey League's 1988 draft - by the Minnesota North Stars. Jaques, 19, has been through the draft twice and has yet to hear his name called. Modano, the offensive leader of the Prince Albert Raiders, has a four-year contract in his pocket, a contract said to be worth as much as $1 million. Jaques, from the Tri-City Americans, is fighting for his hockey life. Modano had 127 points last season and has 105 this winter. Jaques, listed at 5-foot-11 and a rock-hard 175 pounds, had 15 goals, 46 points and 426 penalty minutes last season. This time around, he's got 13 goals, 35 points and 201 penalty minutes. He is a tough kid who plays a tough game. The scouts who have been around for awhile say he's a throwback. He plays the game the way Baun and Stanley and Gadsby played it. He gives no quarter, nor does he ask any. He plays for keeps. And he makes no apologies. It may have been an all-star game but they were keeping score. On top of that, there were scouts, and lots of them, in attendance. Therefore, Steve Jaques, from Surrey, B.C., came to play. "I was nervous and I wanted to get a big hit in early," Jaques said.
