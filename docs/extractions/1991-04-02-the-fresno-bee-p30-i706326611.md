HOCKEY National Hockey League

MISSION, Texas - Most people can only dream of winning $1 million, and most golfers can only fantasize about hitting a hole-in-one, but both dreams came true at the same time for Ray Wilkerson of Harlingen, Texas. The 41-year-old made the shot during the Pizza Hut Million Dollar Hole-in-One Contest Sunday afternoon in the South Texas Health Care Foundation Golf Classic. He parlayed an eight-iron tee shot 150 yards, 2 feet, into the No. 5 cup to win the big money. The National Hole-in-One Insurance Association will pay Wilkerson, a route salesman for Rainbo Baking Co., $25,000 a year for the next 40 years, said Lee Singletary, tournament director. Hockey Nine months after Bob Murdoch was named coach of the year, he was fired by the Winnipeg Jets, one of only five clubs in the 21- team National Hockey League to miss the playoffs. The Jets fell to fifth place in the Smythe Division with just 27 wins and were eliminated from the Stanley Cup playoffs with a 3-2 overtime loss in Vancouver on Thursday. The Chicago Blackhawks said that wing Michel Goulet will be sidelined for 10 days to two weeks because of a sprained right knee. Goulet was hurt Sunday in a pileup into the boards during Chicago's 5-1 win over Detroit. Tennis Gabriela Sabatini and Jennifer Capriati, both ranked in the top 10 in the world, teamed up to eliminate Helen Kelesi and Katerina Maleeva 6-4, 6-4 in the opening round of doubles play at the $500,000 Family Circle Magazine Cup at Hilton Head, S.C. Football Minnesota Vikings kicker Donald Igwebuike went to trial alone on drug smuggling charges after a second co-defendant in the case agreed to plead guilty to importing $1 million worth of heroin. General The Nevada Assembly gave final legislative approval to a bill that would require the NCAA to give due process to coaches and athletes. The lower house action sent the bill to Gov. Bob Miller for his signature.
