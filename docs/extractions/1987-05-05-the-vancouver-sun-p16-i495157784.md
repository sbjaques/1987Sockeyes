Sockeyes the men in black in Centennial Cup tourney

By ELLIOTT PAP Sun Sports Reporter HUMBOLDT, Sask. - Dressed in their black uniforms, Richmond Sockeyes look every bit the villains they are turning out to be in this year's Centennial Cup tournament. The fans in Humboldt cheer against them and the referees don't seem to appreciate their somewhat rugged style. So all coach Orland Kurtenbach's boys do is win, win, win. The 'Eyes polished off Ontario champion Pembroke Lumber Kings 4-1 Monday night to go 2-0 in the round-robin and clinch a semifinal berth in the national junior A hockey championships. Richmond has today o off and will meet the host Humboldt Broncos, who are also 2-0, Wednesday. At stake will be a bye into Saturday's final, while the loser will meet either Pembroke or Atlantic champion Dartmouth in Thursday's semi-final. Tonight's match sees 0-2 Pembroke and 0-2 Dartmouth squaring off for the remaining playoff spot. The Sockeyes were too much for the Lumber Kings last night despite spending half the game in the penalty box. Referee Wes Smith of Saskatoon called all the borderline stuff - with Richmond committing most of it and that left Kurten- bach in a state of near apoplexy.
