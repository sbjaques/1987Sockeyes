Sporting summary HOCKEY.

• Cardigan rugby player Sharon Gibby from Clunderwen who will represent Wales at rugby on Sunday having already gained international honours in hockey. Picture: Huw R.D. Thomas

FEW people can boast to have represented Wales in two sports, but that's exactly what Sharon Gibby will be doing at Neath on Sunday when she wins her first Welsh ladies rugby cap at the Gnoll (kick off 3.00p.m.) Prior to playing rugby Sharon, 22, was very successful hockey player and has represented her country on 20 occasions in this sport at under 18, under 21 and senior level. Although she still plays hockey now and again for Kilgetty, former Ysgol y Preseli pupil Sharon now devotes most of her time to playing rugby for Cardigan. A very strong tackling player she has played most of her club rugby at full back, but it will be in the right wing position that she will make her Welsh debut. A natural footballer with a particularly powerful kick, she has been drafted into the side to try and combat the electrifying pace of England on the wings. She will be marking Richmond flyer Debbie Francis a former junior sprint champion, but Sharon is no slouch' and can make a real name for herself. Although she is winning her first cap, Sharon will be the No.1 goal-kicker, while Wales have also some handy moves up their sleeves involving Gibby.
