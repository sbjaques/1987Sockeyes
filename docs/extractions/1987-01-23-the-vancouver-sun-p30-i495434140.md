## Paragraphs

MERRITT - The Coastal Division came out on top of the annual B.C. Junior Hockey League all-star game Thursday night, but had withstand a comeback by the Interior squad to secure the 5-4 victory. After jumping to a 4-0 lead after one period, the Coast saw its lead shrink to 5-3 after 40 minutes. Brad Smiley of the Abbotsford Falcons, who scored one goal, was named the Coast's most valuable player. Goaltender Lucien Carignan of the Kelowna Packers got the nod as the Interior's most valuable player, shutting out the Coastal snipers after he came on for the game's final 30 minutes. Other Coastal goals were scored by Tony Bobbitt of the Richmond Sockeyes, Darren Biggs and Mike Knapton of the Nanaimo Clippers and Aaron Nosky of the Delta Flyers. The Interior scoring was provided by Pat Mangold and Craig Bedard of the Kelowna Packers and Darryl Kirkpatrick and Steve Manuel of the host Merritt Warriors. The Interior outshot the Coast 42-32.
