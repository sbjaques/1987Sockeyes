Best fishing in North Idaho for perch has been at Hauser, Fernan and Pend Oreille lakes. Dave Nall of the West Side Resort at Hauser said anglers have been keeping lots of 7- to 10- inch perch, plus a few 14- to 17-inch rainbows. Most fishermen have been drilling holes on the east side near the public access area and out from the Rainbow Inn. Ice fishermen have been catching perch at several places around Lake Pend Oreille, but the biggest have been coming from the Sunnyside area. A few anglers have been able to catch their 25- fish limits of the 8- to 10-inch landlocked sockeye salmon. Round Lake has been producing good numbers of 12- to 16-inch rainbows. Best steelhead fishing last weekend was in the Snake just above and below the mouth of the Grande Ronde River. Washington fisheries biologist Glen Mendel said that anglers averaged about 11 hours per steelhead in that stretch. An average of less than 15 hours per fish indicates excellent fishing. Mendel said the Ronde is clear and some anglers have been fishing the lower part of the stream. Steelhead fishing between the confluence of the Clearwater and Snake rivers and the Asotin area was slow last weekend, with anglers averaging 39 hours per steelhead, according to Idaho fisheries biologist Ron Lindland of Lewiston. The Clearwater below Potlatch Creek was muddy last weekend as the result of rain and warm temperatures. Steelhead fishermen had fair luck between the mouth of Potlatch Creek and Orofino, averaging 25 hours per steelhead. The Clearwater should be clear again this weekend.

An all-star list of instructors, including Basketball Hall of Fame member Oscar Robertson, will speak at free clinics for basketball, baseball, hockey and cycling next Wednesday in Spokane. The baseball clinic involves former major league players Rocky Bridges and Steve Lubratich, plus Community Colleges of Spokane coach Keith Snyder. Bridges manages Pittsburgh's top minor league affiliate in Buffalo, and Lubratich is the new manager of the Spokane Indians. Former National Hockey League stars Butch Goring and Dennis Hull will be joined by Spokane Chiefs goaltender Troy Gamble and former Spokane Jets and Flyers defenseman George Talotti at the hockey clinic. Goring now coaches the Chiefs, and Talotti is a local youth hockey coach. The cycling clinic will be conducted by champion road racer Katrin Tobin and local cycling enthusiasts Sue Bradley and Mike Palmer. Robertson, one of the all-time greats in pro and college basketball, will be joined at the basketball clinic by Gonzaga University standout Tammy Tibbles, GU women's coach Mike Peterson and CCS women's assistant coach Bruce Johnson.
