The 24-year-old son of former NFL quarterback and coach Bart Starr was found dead Thursday in a Tampa, Fla., house leased by his father, police said. Bart Starr summoned police after he arrived at the home of his son, businessman Bret Michael Starr, discovered a foul smell and couldn't get in, said Hillsborough Capt. Gary Terry. Police said the body was badly decomposed. There were no obvious wounds and no signs of a struggle, Terry said. Authorities said they would have to await an autopsy report from the Hillsborough County Medical Examiner's office to find out how Starr died. BASKETBALL: Roy Williams, an assistant coach at North Carolina, was named head coach at the University of Kansas today to replace Larry Brown, who left for the NBA after leading the Jayhawks to the national championship last spring. •The Louisiana Superdome will hold its third Final Four tournament, beating out Seattle's Kingdome for the 1993 event, the National Collegiate Athletic Association's basketball committee announced. New Orleans was host for the 1982 and 1987 championships. Seattle was the site of the 1984 Final Four and will be host city again in 1989. AUTO RACING: Bobby Allison left his hospital room in a wheelchair Thursday for the first time since his June 19 racing accident. Ed Gossage, spokesman for Allison's sponsor, Miller Racing, said Allison began eating solid foods early this week. Gossage said Allison, 51, who suffered a concussion and several broken bones in the first-lap accident in the NASCAR Miller 500 at Pocono International Raceway in Long Pond, remains in guarded condition at Lehigh Valley Hospital Center.
