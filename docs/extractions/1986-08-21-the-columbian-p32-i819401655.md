Salem beat Hazel Dell-Metro of Vancouver for the regional championship and the berth in the national tournament. • The San Diego Padres say they no longer will offer multiyear contracts to their players because baseball clubs are barred from including drug-testing clauses in players' contracts. Team President Ballard Smith and owner Joan Kroc said Tuesday the decision is in direct response to a July 30 ruling by arbitrator Tom Roberts that drug-testing clauses in the contracts of major league players were unenforceable. BASKETBALL: A former assistant women's coach at the University of Oregon was sentenced Wednesday to 15 years in prison for the attempted murder of her female lover and a man who tried to prevent the attack. Debra Ann Weston, 24, a high school All-America pick from Tacoma, who played four years at Seattle University, was found guilty June 24 of two attempted murder counts in a non-jury trial in Eugene. Maryland coach Lefty Driesell appeared Wednesday before a grand jury investigating the death of Len Bias, but refused to discuss his testimony. BOWLING: Glenn Allison clipped the seven pin off the 7-10 split on his final ball of the championship game to edge top-seeded Bus Oswalt 201-200 and capture the $115,000 Professional Bowlers Association Kessler Seniors Championship at Canton, Ohio. The victory was worth $16,000 and ended a 22-year dry spell for Allison, a 56-year-old from Whittier, Calif., who had won five previous PBA tournaments, but none since 1964. CYCLING: Italian Moreno Argentin battled rain and numbing cold to win the Estes Park (Colo.) Circuit Race of the Coors International Bicycle Classic. Frenchman Bernard Hinault, fivetime Tour de France winner, kept the overall lead. In the women's race Inga Benedict of the 7-Eleven team, won, leaving her i in second place but inching toward the lead. The win brought Benedict, Reno, Nev., within 1:16 of race leader Jeannie Longo of France.
