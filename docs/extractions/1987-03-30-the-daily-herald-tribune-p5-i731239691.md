By MONTE STEWART Herald-Tribune staff QUESNEL - Tom Marsh paused momentarily, looked around the dressing room and, eyeing Millionaires' netminder Scooter Henson, asked rhetorically: "Hey, Scoot, it is a little different, eh?" The Quesnel Millionaires had just defeated Grande Prairie North Stars 4-2 in the decisive game of the Peace Cariboo Junior Hockey League championship series Saturday night. The victory before a packed house at the Civic Arena culminated a rags to riches story for the Millionaires, who only a year ago were the doormats of the PCJHL. The Millionaires clinched the best-of-seven PCJHL championship series in five games. Friday, they thrashed the North Stars 8-2, setting the stage for Saturday's finale. "These guys have worked so hard all year long," said Marsh, the 26-year-old coach who, in his first full season of coaching at any level, lifted a team that won only six games last year out of the gutter and into the caviar set. Quesnel will now face B.C. Junior Hockey League champion Richmond Sockeyes in first round Centennial Cup play.
