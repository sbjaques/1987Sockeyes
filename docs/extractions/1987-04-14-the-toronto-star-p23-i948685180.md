Fighting fans threaten series between juniors RED DEER, Alta. (CP) - The junior hockey series between the Red Deer Rustlers and the Richmond Sockeyes could be cancelled fans don't behave, the Canadian Amateur Hockey Association says. The CAHA said in a statement yesterday that unruly behavior of fans in both cities could result in the series being cancelled or moved to another city. One man was arrested during Sunday's game here, won 7-1 by the Rustlers. The best-of-seven between the Alberta and British Columbia junior champions is tied 2-2. Sunday's game was delayed 30 minutes as fights broke out on and off the ice. Last week in Richmond, two Red Deer players were suspended for becoming involved in a postgame altercation with fans and the Sockeyes were fined $500 by the CAHA for failing to provide proper security.
