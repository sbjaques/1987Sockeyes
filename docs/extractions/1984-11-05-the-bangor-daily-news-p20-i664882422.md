## Paragraphs

HOUGHTON, Mich. - It'll go down in the books as a 10-2 loss for the Uni-' versity of Maine-Orono's Black Bears against host Michigan Tech on Saturday night, but the score wasn't indicative of the game and the circumstances surrounding the outcome did not sit well with University of Maine-Orono hockey coach Shawn Walsh. Walsh, whose Bears trailed just 3-1 late in the second period, said the officiating left a lot to be desired, particularly when the game was still in doubt. "There was no way we were going to get out of there with two wins (as far as the referees were concerned)," implied Walsh, whose Bears had beaten Tech 3-2 on Dewey Wahlin's overtime goal Friday night. . "'We played 30 great minutes of hockey and had begun dominating the game in the second period when they (the referees) decided to take complete control of it." One of the referees and the linesman were from Houghton, Walsh explained. The Bears were assessed three minor penalties within a span of 44 seconds and the Huskies got their all-important fourth goal when Donnie Porter scored on a power play at the 17:52 mark of the period. The first penalty occurred when freshman center Paul Lelievre, attempting to spit a mouthful of water onto the ice, accidentally hit the referee with the spray. That earned the Bears a bench minor. Just 44 seconds later, Stan Czenczek was assessed an interference penalty, giving Tech a two-man advantage for 1:16. As the Czenczek penalty was being assessed, a disgusted Walsh waved a white towel (representing surrender) and that resulted in another bench minor.
