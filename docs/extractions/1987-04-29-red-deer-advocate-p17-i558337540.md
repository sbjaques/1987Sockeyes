Humboldt gets even RICHMOND, B.C. (CP) Mike Gaber scored at 3:31 of overtime Tuesday to give the Humboldt Broncos a 4-3 victory over the Richmond Sockeyes and new life in their Centennial Cup playoff series. The victory forced a seventh and deciding game in the Western Canada final here tonight. Bill McDougall led the Broncos with two goals and Curtis Chamberlin was the other marksman for Humboldt which trailed 1-0 at the end of the first period and was tied 2-2 entering the third period. Paul Rutherford led the Sockeyes with two goals while Dave Tomlinson added the other goal for Richmond which was outshot 46-45. Humboldt got spectacular netminding from Bruce Hoffort, who made 42 saves. Punch resigns Bruins' post NEW WESTMINSTER, B.C. (CP) - Ernie (Punch) McLean has resigned as head coach of the New Westminster Bruins of the Western Hockey League. McLean needs to spend more time with his mining interests in northern British Columbia, general manager John Olver said, adding McLean will remain with the club as a director. Olver said no decision has been made on a replacement. McLean took the Bruins to four consecutive Memorial Cup tournaments starting in 1975 and New Westminster won the Canadian title in 1977 and 1978. After selling his interest in the WHL team five years ago, McLean returned as coach in November, 1985. New Westminster finished fifth in the Western Division this season and missed the playoffs.
