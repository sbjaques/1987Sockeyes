By NEIL HODGE Telegraph Journal MONCTON - The regularseason champs looked like chumps on Sunday evening at the Moncton Coliseum. The Moncton Hawks were in total command, embarrassing the St. John's Maple Leafs 5-0 to take a 3-2 lead in the bestof -seven Atlantic Division final in American Hockey League playoff action. Moncton can complete a series upset on Tuesday night in St. John's. Game 7, if necessary, is on Thursday and that, too, would be in Newfoundland. Craig Fisher and Ken Gernander, each with a pair, and Dave Tomlinson paced the Hawks to intermission leads of 2-0 and 5-0. Goaltender Stephane Beauregard stretched his shutout streak to 130 minutes and 21 seconds, a span that dates back to the first period of Game 4. A crowd of 3,829 watched what looked like grown men playing against young boys just learning the sport. The Hawks capitalized on both their power-play opportunities in the first period with Fisher scoring the winner after 9:15 expired.
