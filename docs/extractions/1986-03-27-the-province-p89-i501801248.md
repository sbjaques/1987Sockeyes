## Paragraphs

Sockeyes facing bad odds Staff Reporter Richmond Sockeyes didn't have the odds in their favor last night in Penticton. After dropping an 8-7 decision to the Knights in the second overtime period Tuesday night in Richmond, the Sockeyes fell behind 3-1 in the best-of-seven B.C. Junior Hockey League championships. Making the situation even more serious for Richmond was the fact the Knights are nearly unbeatable at home. In the past two season, they have lost just one game - a playoff contest with Red Deer last year. And Penticton went on to win that series. The BCJHL champion advances to the provincial championship Mowat Cup against Prince George Spruce Kings beginning tomorrow.
