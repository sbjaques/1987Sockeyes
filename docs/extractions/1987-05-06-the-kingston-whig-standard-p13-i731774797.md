NOTEBOOK: OK: Brophy rewarded From The Whig- Standard wire services A year ago, coach Dan Maloney took the underdog Toronto Maple Leafs to the deciding game of the Norris Division final. Leaf owner Harold Ballard was initially impressed by his team's performance, but then offered his coach a new contract without a substantial raise. Maloney considered the incident an insult, so he left Toronto and signed on to coach the Winnipeg Jets. This year, new Toronto coach John Brophy once again brought the Leafs to within one game of the National Hockey League semifinal playoffs. And this time, just two days after the Leafs were eliminated by the Detroit Red Wings, Ballard gave his man a new three-year contract with what Brophy described as a "substantial" raise. Assistant coach Garry Lariviere of Gananoque also got a new three-year pact. 0 Pembroke Lumber Kings clinched a spot in the semifinal game of the Centennial Cup junior A hockey championship tournament last night with an 8-4 victory over the Dartmouth Fuel Kids. The Ontario champions will meet the loser of today's final round-robin game between Humboldt Broncos and Richmond Sockeyes, who both have 2-0 records. O Longueuil Chevaliers scored three goals in 47 seconds in the first period en route to a 10-4 triumph over Chicoutimi Sagueneens to win the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League championship last night. The Chevaliers, who won the best-of-seven series 4-1, will represent Quebec at the Memorial Cup tournament in Oshawa, which opens on Saturday.
