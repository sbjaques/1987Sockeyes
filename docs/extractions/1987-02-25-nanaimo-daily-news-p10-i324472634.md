FLINT, Mich. (AP) - Frank Perkins, a player and assistant coach for the International Hockey League's Flint Spirits, has died from what apparently were natural causes. He was 27. Perkins, who came to the team this season, was pronounced dead Tuesday night at St. Joseph Hospital. He was discovered at his home by his fiancee, Cathy Stoker, at about 6 p.m. Tuesday. An autopsy was planned for today. Team members would make a decision about whether they'd take the ice tonight in Flint in an IHL game against Salt Lake City. Perkins, who played junior hockey briefly in Kitchener, Ont., in 1977-78 before moving on to the Sudbury Wolves, appeared in 53 games for the Spirits this season. He scored eight goals and had 13 assists. Spirits coach Rick Dudley said Perkins had a back problem that he didn't talk about. "He would get bumped and come back for more, even though you knew he was hurting," Dudley said. "That kind of desire rubbed off on the entire team and that's one of the reason we have been successful."
