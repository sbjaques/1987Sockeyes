The Bruins made some preliminary cuts yesterday, assigning 19 players to Providence. They are: goaltenders Matt DelGuidice and John Blue; defensemen Jeff Ricciardi, Sean Gorman, Steve Jaques, Christian Lariviere, Peter Laviolette and Darryl Olsen; and forwards Sergei Zholtok, Grigori Pantaleyev, Clark Donatelli, Bill Huard, Eugene Pavlov, Chris Crombie, Chris Winnes, Andrew McKim, Jim Vesey and Matt Robbins. Mark Kumpel will serve as player/assistant coach in Providence.

By Nancy L. Marrapese GLOBE STAFF LANDOVER, , Md. - He and his I teammates kidded team owner George Brett OrEBo® about how much tougher hockey players were than baseball players, and there's no doubt Scott Bailey and his colleagues gave the future baseball Hall of Famer a few pointers on the game. But during the past two weeks, Spokane has been the furthest thing from Bailey's mind. The 20-year-old goaltender, who spent the past couple of seasons with the Western Hockey League team, is trying to win a job with the Bruins. Bailey was Boston's third pick in the June draft and went from being a lifelong Montreal fan to a Bruins fan in the time it took to announce his name. In the past two weeks, he's been trying to make an impression. Last night he and veteran Reggie Lemelin split the netminding chores in the team's exhibition game here against the Washington Capitals. Bailey took his lumps, surrendering four goals - three on the power play - as he played the second half of a 6-4 loss. Before that indoctrination, he was savoring his opportunity.
