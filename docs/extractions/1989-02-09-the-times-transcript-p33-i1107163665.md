- WINNIPEG (CP) Don't blame the players for the firing of head coach Dan Maloney, says Winnipeg Jets' captain Dale - Hawerchuk. "I don't think we can be blamed," Hawerchuk said after learning Maloney had been axed Tuesday, less than four months after general manager John Ferguson was fired. "We do the hiring of personnel, or the firing of managet ment. I really don't see how you can blame the players. I didn't see anyone quit."' GLENS FALLS, N.Y. (CP-Special) - Led by captain Paul Boutilier, members of the Moncton Hawks called a players-only meeting prior to Wednesday night's American Hockey League game against Adirondack Red Wings.... Boutilier, who says he's seen it all in seven years of professional hockey, the players they must "keep going," despite the loss of coach Rick Bowness. Bowness was named coach of the NHL parent Winnipeg Jets Tuesday after a season and a half with the Hawks. Veteran Ron Wilson is continuing to play and fill in as coach until a full-time replacement can be found. "I think the meeting that the players had must have helped a said Wilson Wednesday night, shortly after the Hawks surprised the hometown Red Wings 5- 2 before 3,402 spectators at the Glens Falls Civic Centre. "Paul told them that I'm the boss now and I've got a job to do," Wilson added. "They've handled the situation very professionally. Sure, they're going to miss Rick a helluva lot, but they've got the rest of the season to worry about. "Most of the guys here believe they can win a championship and they want to go for it." Second-year centre Scott Schneider scored two goals to help the Hawks defeat the Red Wings. The win pushed the Hawks into a three tie for second place in the AHL's Northern Division. However, they also have two games in hand on both New Haven Nighthawks and Halifax Citadels. The Wings' loss came in Bill Dineen's 1,000th regular season game as a professional coach. Schneider scored the first and last goals for the Hawks, 29-20-5. He had the only goal of the first period on a power play at 19:35.
