HOCKEY National Hockey League Wales Conference PATRICK DIVISION

Nine high school seniors from District 5AAA were selected to play for the South in the 40th Shrine All-Star football game Aug. 8 at Civic Stadium in Portland. South Eugene and North Eugene landed two players each - linebacker Mark Koenig and fullback Geno Zaharie of South and linemen John Niemela and Mike Davenport of North. Zaharie and Niemela both have indicated they will sign letters of intent with Oregon. Quarterbacks Andy Johnson, the league most valuable player from Marshfield, and Andy Gray of Willamette both could end up as defensive players as Jeff Barry of South Medford and Rich Ekman of Bend also were selected as quarterbacks. The other players selected from 5AAA were offensive guard Bryan Pinney of Sheldon, running back Javan Yriarte of Churchill, and offensive tackle George Racette of Thurston. State-playoff runner-up Corvallis will be represented by three players - Dan Blus, Nick Todorovich and Jason Phillips. Pendleton, Lebanon and Bend also had three players each. Three all-state selections from state-champion Glencoe - running backs Brett Collins and Kyle Kozak and linebacker Todd Sahlfeld topped the North team team. Jason Palumbis of Lakeridge, the all-state quarterback bound for Stanford, was the lone quarterback chosen to play for the North.
