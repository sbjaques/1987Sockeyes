"He has great sense with the puck," said Walsh. "He moves the puck well. If anything, he's too unselfish with the puck." "He's capable of putting the puck in the net, he's scored some big goals for us," said senior center Dave Wensley. Scremin scored his first collegiate goal in Maine's 7-5 win over Boston University on Nov. 14, slipping into the slot from the right point, taking a pass from teammate and close friend Christian Lalonde and snapping a 30- footer into the far corner. The two hooked up on an almost identical play at Boston University a few weeks later in Maine's 7-1 win. He picked up his third of the year in last Friday's 12-5 win over Denver, capitalizing on a four-on-three power play with a 30-foot drive from the top of the slot. He spied the daylight and skated to it from the point. Growing up the youngest of four children raised by Florio and Silvana Scremin, who emigrated to British Columbia from their native Italy soon after they married, Scremin played a lot of sports as a youngster. His parents were very supportive of his endeavors. Eventually, hockey became his No. 1 sport and he helped lead the Richmond Sockeyes to the BCJHL Coastal Division title. He had 8-35- 43 and was a plus-40 during that 1986-87 = season. Scremin has been pleased with his season to 03 date but is not going to stand pat. "I just want to keep on working hard and learning from each game," said Scremin. "In every game, there are different situations (you can learn from)." He has proven to be a quick learner.
