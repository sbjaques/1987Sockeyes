By JACK WILSON of The Advocate The Alberta-B.C. junior hockey championship could be cancelled or moved unless fans behave, the Canadian Amateur Hockey Association says. The association said in an announcement today that unruly behavior of fans at the Arena has cast a shadow over the series between the Red Deer Rustlers and the Richmond Sockeyes.

He was arrested and released following the game which was won 7-1 by Red Deer to even the best-of-seven series at two games each. Sgt. Williams said three policemen were at the Arena. He said the policemen were not assigned to the game but patrolled the building as part of their normal patrol zone. Arena manager Albert Mason said his staff didn't call the police to help quell the secondperiod disturbance. The Rustlers have between 10-12 security members. The game was delayed 30 minutes by four on-ice battles, as well as a fracas behind the Richmond Sockeyes players bench that was instigated by a group of disorderly fans who threw debris at the Richmond players, including eggs. Red Deer fans have thrown debris after every Rustler goal, including eggs, tomatoes, and fish. One player on the Richmond bench was hit in the face by an egg. The series has been punctuated by fan disturbances. Last week in Richmond two Red Deer players recieved suspensions of one and three games each for a post-game altercation with fans. The Richmond club was fined $500 by the CAHA for failing to provide proper security at that game.
