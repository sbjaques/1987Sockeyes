Knights 8 Sockeyes 7

By FRANK LUBA Staff Reporter No matter who you were cheering for, it was a thriller. When Richmond's Kevin Weremy scored his second goal of the game just 10 seconds into the 10-minute overtime period it looked like the Sockeyes were going to hang on for a win over Penticton Knights that would tie the best-of-seven B.C. Junior Hockey League final at two *games apiece. But a power-play goal by Penticton's Rod Summers at 6:06 kept the Knights alive. At the the end of the 10-minutes, the teams headed for their dressing rooms as the ice was cleared for the ensuing 20-minute, sudden-death second overtime.

Then Kevin Melrose scored 47 seconds into the final overtime period to give the Knights an 8-7 victory and a 3-1 lead in the series. The Sockeyes had rallied from a 5-3 second period-deficit to tie the game twice in the final period. The first overtime period was marred by several incidents.
