## Paragraphs

By Gerard McLaughlin Sports Reporter Talk about a team going hot'and cold. That was the Moncton Hawks on the weekend. Winless in five games, Hawks came out with fire in their eyes in Halifax on Saturday night and scorched the rival Citadels 4-1 in front of 4,107 fans at the Metro Centre. But, it was a much different story at the Coliseum Sunday. Although Moncton scored first, visiting Newmarket Saints then took complete control, triggering seven straight goals, defeating the Hawks 7-3 in front of 2,713 fans. Saints 7, at Hawks 3 Mike Stevens powered the Saints attack with three goals, two of them shorthanded. Mike Miller added two goals and two assists while Greg Johnston and Bob Reynolds each had goals. Moncton, which outshot the Saints 40-31, got goals from Simon Wheeldon, Todd Flichel and Matt Hervey. The Saints, now unbeated in their past five games (4-0-1) improved their overall record to 12 wins, 12 losses and three ties while Moncton's record dropped Moncton's record to 11-12-8.
