; By BRENT WORONOFF •DAYTONA BEACH - Mainland's T.T. Toliver may have thought he was making an innocuous appearance on the punt team. But he turned out to be the center of attention. Toliver, playing blocking back on a punt less than four minutes into the game, was tackled by three Spruce Creek players after the ball was snapped to the : punter. Mainland coach Doug Stanley, incensed by the play for which Creek was penalized for a late hit, dropped his "low key" game plan, and the Bucs went on to trample the struggling Hawks 56-3. Mainland improved to 6-2, 4-0 in District 5-5A, while Creek fell to 0-8, 0-4." "It was crazy for them to do that because we had no plans for this score getting anywhere near this," Stanley said. Toliver, nursing a, sore shoulder, was not scheduled to play much, and not at all at quarterback. But he wound up passing for 89 yards and two touchdowns and catching a 21-yard touchdown pass. "I figured if they wanted him that bad, I'd give him to them," Stanley said. Stanley said Creek coach Todd Rutz apologized to "*him after the game. Stanley said he accepted the apology. Rutz said it was simply a case of his young players becoming overzealous. "They put (Toliver) back" there (on thepunt). We knew they did that to fake," said Rutz. "It's not against the rules to go after him, but we hit him late. It was a legitimate penalty. We were going to rush through him, but our kids got a little overaggressive." The penalty kept the Bucs' first drive alive and Toliver finished it with a 12-yard touchdown pass to Jermel Terry. 1. Joe Giddens, who started at quarterback for the Bucs, passed for 89 yards and a touchdown and scored on a five-yard run. Freshman Tony Bobbitt, playing most of the way at QB in the second half, went 3-for-4 for 82 yards. Mainland added 212 yards rushing. Al Sermon caught five passes for 89 yards, including two touchdowns. : Creek, which scored in the fourth gaurter on a 29-yard field goal by Scott Biernacki, had 49 yards on offense, including just one yard in the first half. . *
