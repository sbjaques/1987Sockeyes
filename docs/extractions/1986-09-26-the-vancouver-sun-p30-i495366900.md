## Paragraphs

Canadian Press Darren Schwartz jammed in a rebound at 15:23 of the third period to give the visiting Kelowna Packers a comeback 5-4 win over the Richmond Sockeyes in B.C. Junior Hockey League play Thursday night. In the only other BCJHL contest, the Nanaimo Clippers led throughout the game as they defeated the Buckaroos 8-5 in Summerland. In Richmond, the Sockeyes led 4-2 after two periods but Kelowna came back with goals from Kevin Harder and Jay Luknowsky to tie the game. Kerry Russell and Kevin Taillefer scored the earlier Packer goals. The Sockeyes built their lead on goals by Jeff Nottingham, Dave Tomlinson, Peter Holmes and Mario Desjardins, before coming up empty in the third period. The Sockeyes outshot the Packers 35-32. It was the third staight win for Kelowna and the first loss of the year for Richmond, now 2-1. In Summerland, Mike Knapton scored twice for the Clippers, while Darrin Biggs, Corkey Parks, Brett Mayor, Todd Demchuk, Craig Frankford and Joey Barszczewski added singles. Nanaimo outshot Summerland 52-35. Summerland goals came from Steve Fredrich, Darrin Harrold, Mark Valley, Tim Roux and Kevin Hoey.
