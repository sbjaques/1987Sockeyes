Football The Atlanta Falcons signed their fourth-round draft pick and two free agents. The free agents are quarterback Bob Gagliano, 35, who has played eight NFL seasons and two in the USFL, and wide receiver Jason Phillips. Gagliano joined the Falcons last Oct. 7, but did not play in a game. Phillips, a five-year player, has played in 56 games with 49 catches for 578 yards and two touchdowns. He joined Atlanta in 1991 after two seasons in Detroit. The rookie is quarterback Perry Klein, a 6-2, 218-pounder who set NCAA Division II passing records with 38 touchdown passes and 3,757 yards at C.W. Post. Chesnokov is the highest-seeded player remaining in the event. Hockey - Defenseman Jyrki Lumme has signed a multiyear contract with the Vancouver Canucks. Lumme, 27, had 13 goals and 42 assists last season. He also accounted for 13 points - two goals and 11 assists - in the playoffs. The native of Finland has played for Vancouver for four seasons after being acquired from the Montreal Canadiens in March 1990. Details of the contract were not disclosed. Glen Sather said he's too busy to decide who will take over as the coach of the Edmonton Oilers and he doesn't expect to name his replacement until next month. Sather added the role of coach to his duties as general manager and president last November when he fired Ted Green. He said from the start he would relinquish the coaching duties after the season. There were reports he would name his replacement yesterday, but Sather said he's going fishing next week and wants to wait for owner Peter Pocklington to return from Europe before he makes an announcement.
