Maple Leafs

TORONTO STAR The speculation couldn't help: but heat up again. Moments before last night's: Leafs/Sharks game, Edmonton Oilers general manager Glen Sather made a surprise appear-: ance in Gardens pressbox asking: "Where does Cliff sit?" It might simply have been Sather's sense of humor, but the fact he was looking for Cliff: Fletcher, Toronto's top man, had reporters buzzing. That he had Don Meehan, Wendel Clark's: agent, in tow only sweetened the proposition. Before Christmas, it was widely reported that Sather had almost. landed Clark, the Leafs' captain, and winger Rob Pearson in a trade that would have seen Edmonton holdout Joe Murphy and veteran winger Esa Tikkanen shipped to Toronto. Sather apparently balked at the last minute, asking for a substantial amount of cash, as well. That killed the deal temporarily. "We're talking,' Sather told The Star's Frank Orr, when asked if he was still looking to swing a deal with the Leafs. "I- don't know if anything is going to happen, but we're talking. We're talking to a lot of people." Fletcher, though, told a differ-. ent story. "We haven't talked about *a trade in weeks and weeks and weeks," the Leafs general man-: ager said of Sather's pressbox: cameo. "He's here with his friends. I didn't even know he was coming." But are you still interested in: Murphy? "Less and less, obviously," Fletcher said. "This is another month he hasn't played. I think anyone making a trade for Joe: Murphy at this point is making a: trade for next year, not this year: It will take him a long time to get ready. Everyone else has been playing since Sept. 6." Still, Sather's presence seemed a little odd considering the Oilers played last night in and face off tonight in Washington. Flights between those cities usually don't stop in Toronto. Meanwhile, Fletcher also indi-: cated h he expected injured Leafs centre Peter Zezel to sit out another two games with the sprained wrist suffered Wednesday against Vancouver. He said that when Zezel does return, replacement Dave Tomlinson: wouldn't necessarily be returned* to Toronto's farm team in St. John's. "It depends on how he plays," Fletcher said.
