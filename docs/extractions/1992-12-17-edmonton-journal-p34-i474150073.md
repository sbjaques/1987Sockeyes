## Paragraphs

Edmonton's Liz Czenczek is making a comeback with thoughts of seeking a position on the Canadian women's field hockey team. Czenczek, 28, retired after the 1988 Seoul Olympics to concentrate on her studies at the University of Alberta. She has resumed training under Dru Marshall, coach of the U of A Pandas and the Canadian junior team, and is among six Edmonton athletes invited to an upcoming Canadian team training camp. Heather Jones and Tara Croxford, who played in Barcelona before returning to help the Pandas earn bronze medals at the Canadian Interuniversity Athletic Union championships, will also attend the national-team camp. • Women will compete for the first time when the World Cup of wrestling is staged in Edmonton in 1994. The Edmonton committee is already busy planning for the prestigious meet.
