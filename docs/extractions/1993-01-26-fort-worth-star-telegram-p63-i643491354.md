DALLAS - Players and coaches of the Fort Worth Fire can't believe the Oklahoma City Blazers are 18 games better than them, as the Central Hockey League standings indicate. The difference may be * only a matter of minutes. "Every night they come out and give a full effort," Fire forward Pat Penner said. "Everybody plays hard the whole game. Of course, they have a lot of good players, but they never have a lapse or a breakdown." As for the Fire... "It's up to us to maintain that type of play for 60 minutes," Fire coach Peter Mahovlich said. "Too often we play well for only 55 minutes." Fort Worth will test its resolve tonight against the CHL leaders in Oklahoma City. Faceoff is 7:30 tonight at the State Fair Arena. In one of seven meetings this season, the Fire actually handed the Blazers one of their eight losses. Other contests have been close with the Blazers winning three times by one goal. In the last three, however, Oklahoma City holds a 26-9 edge in scoring. "They don't necessarily get more scoring chances than anyone else," Penner said. "They're very good at finishing. We have to take advantage of our scoring chances because they have a good goaltender who smothers the puck, and defensemen who force you wide."
