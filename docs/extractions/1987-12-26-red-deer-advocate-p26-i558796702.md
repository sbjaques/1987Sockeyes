General manager Graham Parsons made it clear that his choice to fill the position would be either former Rustler bench boss Wynne Dempster, or Larry Billows, a veteran coach of three tier two leagues. Later in the month, the Red Deer Central Lions Speed Skating Club played host to the Western Canada Cup, Dempster withdrew from the Rustler coaching sweepstakes, and Donna Nowochin's earned a berth to the provincial women's curling championship. February barely started when Billows made his debut as Rustler coach in a 6-5 overtime loss to Sherwood Park. As the month progressed, Jim Richardson and Irene Wardill won the Red Deer Farmers and Farmerettes bonspiels; the Flying Fathers appeared at the Arena; Nowochin was eliminated from the provincials; and the Red Deer College Queens and Kings locked up the North Division titles in the ACAC volleyball conference. March provided one highlight after another. Kurt Browning of Caroline headed to Cincinatti to compete in the world figure skating championships, the RDC Kings and Queens captured the ACAC volleyball crowns and then grabbed bronze medals at the Canadian finals, and the Kings hockey club was ousted by NAIT in the ACAC final. In addition, Bev Watson of Red Deer became the world women's, para-skiing Chiefs champion, Deer Moose laid claim to their second consecutive major bantam provincial hockey title, and the Rustlers won gut-wrenching seven-games series with the .Calgary Spurs and heavily-favored Calgary Canucks to win the AJHL South title. Rustlers created headlines in April by downing St. Albert Saints 4-2 in the AJHL final, and then taking the eventual Canadian champion Richmond Sockeyes to seven games in the Western Centennial Cup semifinal. The contests at the Arena with the Sockeyes were particularly memorable, as Red Deer fans (?) pelted the ice (and the Richmond players) with debris in the first two games before settling down when the CAHA threatened to move the series. Rustlers led 3-2 at one point, but eventually succumbed to the bigger and more talented Richmond crew. Other highlights in April. Volleyball and basketball players Bob Bragg and Mary Lynn Lang were named the RDC male and female athletes of the year, the Moose Chiefs placed third at the Western Canada bantam finals, and Tom Eirikson of Longview was crowned allaround champ of the Red Deer Westerner Silver Buckle Rodeo. As April turned into May, Rimbey native Rod Palm continued his winning ways in the Advocate Road Race, in which saw Sandy Last of Calgary placed first in the women's division. A week later, with Red Deer Riggers preparing for the start of the Foothills Baseball League season, playing-manager Terry Hendley accepted the Advocate award as Red Deer's Athlete of the Year. Dr. Bob Carter was also honored, winning the Alberta Energy Company-AdvocateAlberta Sports Council coach of the year laurels. Red Deer's native were featured in June.
