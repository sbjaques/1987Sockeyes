Rough ride puts Broncos in final The Canadian Press HUMBOLDT. Sask. - The Humboldt Broncos won a place in the final of the Centennial Cup junior A hockey championship tournament last night with a 6-1 victory over the Richmond Sockeyes. The losers, with a round-robin record of 2-1, will meet the Pembroke Lumber Kings in tonight's semifinal. The final will be played Saturday night. Bill McDougall and Garnett Kazuik led Humboldt with two goals apiece, while Duncon Ryhorchuk and Curtis Chamberlin had singles. The win gave Humboldt, from Saskatchewan, a 3-0 record in the round-robin portion of the tournament. Stan Czenczek scored the Richmond goal. The two teams were tied 1-1 after the first period but Humboldt jumped ahead to 4-1 after the second. The Broncos outshot the British Columbia champion Sockeyes 37-19 in a scrappy game that saw Humboldt take 15 of 27 minors. Both teams took two five-minute majors and two game misconducts. Humbolt also picked up two 10-minute misconducts. The Lumber Kings, from Ontario, finished with a 1-2 record in the round-robin section of the tournament. The fourth team involved, the Dartmouth Fuel Kids from Nova Scotia, lost all three of its games.
