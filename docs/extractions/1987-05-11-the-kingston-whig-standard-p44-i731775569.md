ATLANTIC CITY, N..J./ The Canadian Press Michael Olajide is ready to take on Thomas Hearns for the International Boxing Federation middleweight crown, but the trainer of his latest victim says the Canadian got lucky. "Michael Olajide got a Mothers Day gift," said Angelo Dundee after his fighter, Troy Darrell, lost to Olajide yesterday in a unanimous, 10-round decision. Olajide, of Vancouver, 23-0 and ranked No. 2 by the IBF, admitted the fight wasn't his best, and Dundee was quick to agree. "I'm amazed my guy lost," said Dundee. "Olajide definitely got licked." Judge Al De Vito scored it 96-92 for Olajide, while Rocky Castellani's scorecard was 97-94 and William Kostrub's read 95-94. The victory means Olajide will be negotiating today at IBF headquarters in Newark with Hearns for a battle for the vacant middleweight title. Olajide will fight either Hearns, the No. 1 contender, or the next leading available contender. Olajide, who has moved to New York City to train, floored Darrell twice in the opening round with quick flurries to the head but couldn't finish the job. Darrell, from New Rochelle, N.Y., bolstered by surviving the round, then waged an inspired inside fight. Richmond Sockeyes win Centennial Cup HUMBOLDT, Sask./ /The Canadian Press Jason Phillips scored three goals to lead the Richmond Sockeyes to the Centennial Cup junior A hockey championship Saturday night with a 5-2 win over the Humboldt Broncos. But the Sockeyes had to fall back on an outstanding performance in goal by Frank Romeo, who stood up to 46 Humboldt shots while his teammates managed only 23 at the Broncos net. Romeo was named most valuable player in the four-team, roundrobin series.
