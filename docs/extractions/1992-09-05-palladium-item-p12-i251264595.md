Hockey Buffalo Sabres center Dale Hawerchuk signed a multi-year contract that will pay him $1.2 million per season, the team announced Friday. The length of the contract was not released. The agreement makes the 29-year-old Hawerchuk the second-highest paid Sabre behind Pat LaFontaine, who earns $1.5 million per year plus bonuses that can push his salary to $1.8 million. The Winnipeg Jets have been served with a lawsuit launched by Russian recruit Alexei Zhamnov. Zhamnov wants damages out of his recently signed contract with the Jets, which guarantees him a reported $1.25 million over three years. The New York Rangers signed defensemen Joby Messier and Brad Tiley and left wings Craig Duncanson and Dave Thomlinson on Friday. The Boston Bruins acquired right wing Darin Kimble from the expansion Tampa Bay Lightning on Friday for center Ken Hodge and defenseman Matt Hervey.
