By Mike G. Morreale It isn't often that a team can refer to a regular-season tie as a clutch effort in a difficult spot. But with 10 days remaining before the cutoff date for the state ice hockey tournaments, Pingry, HOCKEY No.- 16 in The Star-Ledger Top 20, can't afford many more setbacks. The Somerset County school was certainly in survival mode yesterday when it battled perennial state power Christian Brothers Academy to a 3-3 standoff at the American Hockey Center in Wall Township. Pingry (6-8-2) could have as many as four games remaining before the Feb. 12 cutoff, including matches with Roxbury on Saturday, Princeton Day, the 1999 Star-Ledger Trophy recipient, on Monday, and No. 3 Delbarton on Wednesday. If Pingry eliminates Princeton Day (15-3) in the prep B Division semifinal round on Monday, it would advance to the championship game on Feb. 12. If Pingry doesn't obtain the requisite .500 record for an automatic tournament bid, it could also gain admittance into the NJSIAA/New Jersey Devils parochial schools tournament as an at-large participant. In order to be considered for an at-large bid, Pingry. must not be more than four games below .500. "We're not counting on an atlarge bid, we're just focusing on playing good hockey down the stretch," said Pingry goalie Andrew Houston, who made 34 saves yesterday. "If the committee sees fit to give us the atlarge bid, we would gratefully accept it and be real excited." Pingry took a 3-1 lead just 57 seconds into the third period when Dan Armstrong scored his second goal of the game on a power play. But moments later, No. 5 CBA (9-4-3) received goals from Alex Soloway and John Perrotta in a span of 32 seconds to even the score. - • Houston was forced to wear r skates a half-size bigger than he is ac- customed to after leaving his behind at school.
