By ELLIOTT PAP The 'Eyes still seem to have it. Richmond Sockeyes, who won everything in tier two junior hockey last season, are rolling along in the 1988 B.C. Junior League playoffs despite icing almost an entirely new team. Only three members of the '87 national champions - Rob Clarke, Tony Bobbitt and Jason Talo - rejoined coach Orland Kurtenbach to defend their title. "Yeah, we're a little different this year," said Kurtenbach, after the Sockeyes knocked off Delta Flyers 6-3 at Richmond Arena Tuesday night to take a 1-0 lead in the best-of-seven Coastal Division final. Game two goes tonight, 7:30, at Richmond. In the Interior Division, Vernon Lakers thumped Penticton Knights 8-4 to lead that best-of-seven 3-0. The Sockeyes aren't nearly the amazing powerhouse of a year ago when they didn't lose a playoff game until their fifth series (17 straight wins). They needed six games to dispose of Juan de Fuca Whalers in the first round and Kurtenbach predicts a tough battle against Delta.
