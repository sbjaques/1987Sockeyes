## Paragraphs

HUMBOLDT - The green, cardboard step chart in the dressing room of the Humboldt Broncos says it all. Game-by-game the Broncos have been charting their playoff success, marking each win on their climb to the prize at the top of the ladder: the Centennial Cup. They moved one level higher on Monday, pounding Selkirk Steelers 9-1 to sweep the Centennial Cup Western se in four games and win the Anavet Cup given to the winner of the Saskatchewan-Manitoba championship series. But a quick glance at the step chart shows that the Broncos have seven more wins t to reach their goal. "I know this isn't too much," said defenceman Al Novakowski, who won a Centennial Cup championship as a member of the Penticton Knights last year. "It's something, but there's something more down the road. Lots of kids here have never won anything never a midget title, never a bantam title. For them, winning the league title was great and winning this was great. And it just keeps getting sweeter." This victory was, however, especially sweet for Humboldt coach Bernie Lynch and the Bronco veterans, who remembered the bitter seven-game semi-final series ' which they lost to Winnipeg South Blues last year. When the Broncos skated a victory lap around the Uniplex, hoisting the SAJHL championship trophy over their heads (Winnipeg failed to turn in the Anavet Cup in time for it to be transported to Humboldt), Lynch said the memory of last year's defeat was erased. tastes a lot better than most people think it does," said Lynch. "There was a lot of bitterness over last t year's series. But this is just like burying the hatchet." Bill McDougall led the Broncos with three goals and two assists, raising his playoff point total to 40. Rod Kavanagh, with his first two goals of the playoffs, Corey Belitski, Morgan Watts, Dave Shyiak and Brad Bergen also scored for Humboldt. Jeff Smith had Selkirk's only goal. "In close games we're able to take the man out of the play," said Bergen, who had the honor of putting the latest mark on the Broncos' chart. "That's something we didn't always do last year. Sometimes that might have cost us some goals." The Broncos led 2-0 after one period, but forced the Steelers to melt under the heat of intense forechecking. Humboldt scored four goals in a span of 3:34 in the second and led 6-1 when the period ended. "We're an aggressive hockey team," said Lynch. "If we win 5- 1, it means the other team is doing a good job of keeping the puck out of the net. That aggressiveness is a reflection of our style. Our players don't quit. They never want to quit.' Next rung on the ladder for the Broncos will mean stepping over the winner of the Richmond Sockeyes-Red Deer Rustlers series, which is tied 2-2, in the Western final.
