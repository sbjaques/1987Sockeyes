MONTE STEWART Herald-Tribune staff FORT ST. JOHN - The golf clubs are still in the closet. The Grande Prairie North Stars came up smelling like a four-leafed clover on St. Patricks Day. The reason: they defeated Fort St. John Huskies 7-5 in the seventh and deciding game of their Peace Cariboo Junior Hockey League semifinal here last night. The most prevalent color on the Stars' uniforms is green, but they didn't want to be seeing another type of green - the one you putt on. "Like (former coach Ray) Prefontaine said, 'it's pretty hard to golf in the snow, said Grande Prairie defenceman Morgan Hall, relieved after the Stars had come out alive in the do-or-die contest. North Stars are now just a chip shot away from a PCJHL championship. They meet Quesnel Millionaires in the best-of-seven final (starting date and site still to be announced). Backed by two busloads of raucous supporters, the Stars trailed just once in the contest played before a near-capacity crowd, 1-0 early in the first period. The Green Machine put the game virtually out of reach before the end of the second. "Grande Prairie, you have to give them credit, they were intense for whole 60 minutes," said Fort St. John coach Ritchie Webb, sporting the green sweater and green tie that he had worn when his club came back to tie the series 3-3 Sunday with a 6-5 double-overtime victory. "No, I'm superstitious," said Webb when asked if he was Irish. "Really, to go seven games in a series, a good game like this, hey, you can't win 'em all."
