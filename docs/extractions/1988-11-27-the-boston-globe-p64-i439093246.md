Freshman Ed Peterson scored 23 points, including a crucial three-pointer late in the second half, to propel Yale to a 77-68 victory over George Washington in Washington. Peterson made two free throws with 5:57 remaining to break a 57-57 tie and give Yale the lead for good. Then, with Yale nursing a 63-61 lead, he hit a three-pointer from the top of the key for a 66-61 advantage with 3:03 left. George Washington, which scored only two baskets in the final 6:18, never cut the gap below 4 points the rest of the way. Mike Ryan and Travis McCready each scored 11 for Yale, which made 32 free throws, including 9 in the final two minutes. UMass-Boston got 29 points from Stephen Donnelly and captured the Harbor Invitational with a 98- 83 win over Clark in Dorchester. Clark held a 27-26 lead when UMass-Boston reeled off 21 straight points en route to a 53-32 halftime lead. Vaughn Ames had 25 points and 15 rebounds to lead Old Westbury over Emerson, 87-60, in the consolation game. David Brown showed the way with 18 points and Anton Brown added 15 as Massachusetts crushed Southern Connecticut, 84-61, in Amherst. Jim Barton had 34 points and John McKay added 24 as Dartmouth nipped Kent State, 88-86, in Charlottesville, Va. Ed Cirane's game-high 24 points enabled Suffolk break away from a 41-41 halftime tie for an 85-73 to decision over Framingham State in Cambridge. Leonard Bass had 24 points as Westfield State took a 92-89 overtime decision over Southeastern Mass. in Westfield despite 36 points by Waldemar Sender. Matt Hancock had 21 points and Tom Dorion added 17 to boost Colby over Roanoke in Salem, Va. Gary Bestman and Bill Shepard each had 18 points to lift Western Connecticut over Fitchburg State, 86-67, in Danbury, Conn. Jay Fraser led the way with 16 points and Dave Tomlinson came off the bench to score 14 as MIT cruised to a 69-58 triumph over Gordon in Cambridge. John Schmuck had 24 points and Jim Ferrer 23 as Bentley topped New Hampshire College, 88-76, in Manchester, N.H. Orlando Vandross scored 32 points and four other AIC players were in double figures in a 95-77 romp over host Lowell. Lowell's Leo Parent led all scorers with 39. Amherst routed host Worcester State, 91-55, behind Scott Saft's 22 points and 21 by Yram Groff. Pace nipped Merrimack, 54-52, in New York with only Matt Baldini in double figures with 13 points. Steve Scott led Merrimack with 15.
