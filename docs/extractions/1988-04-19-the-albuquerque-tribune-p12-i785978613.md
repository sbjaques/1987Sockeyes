ALBUQUERQUE'S UNSUNG HEROES Karl Hale School: University of New Mexico Age: 20 Residence: Albuquerque Sport: Tennis Accomplishment: Hale, who is the No. 1 player on the men's tennis team at the University of New Mexico, recently returned from Davis Cup competition in Jamaica where he defeated Nicholas Perea, the No. 157 ranked player in the world. Hale has compiled a 3-1 Davis Cup record and is 15-15 at UNM in single play. The university studies major is a contender to qualify for the NCAA tennis championships, said Steven Willoughby, assistant tennis coach at UNM. By TI PIPER Special to The Tribune Anglers looking to hook a kokanee salmon need to go north - not to Alaska, but to northern New Mexico. Heron Lake, north of Santa Fe, is considered prime water to catch kokanee, which are sockeye salmon that live in fresh water. But fishing for kokanee requires a boat and a deep-water fishing technique. Prime salmon habitat is 20 to 60 feet down from the lake surface where zooplankton flourish, the menu kokanee consume. Yet kokanee readily strike lures, especially hot pink, fluorescent ones. So the trick is to get the lure down to where the fish feed.
