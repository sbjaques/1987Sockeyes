Fir clubbers Hockey pros pay visit to youngsters

"They're as good as the Stars," said Stephen Hunter, 10. "I really wanted to meet these guys. Tim Story, 9, also was notably impressed. "It's a lot different; they're good," Story said. "Look at the passes they make; it's pretty cool. They're a lot better than what I've seen here." Last year, the National Hockey League's Dallas Stars donated about $1,000 worth of hockey equipment to establish a StreetStars youth program at the Boys & Girls Clubs of Arlington. It was the first StreetStars league in Tarrant County. Frederick said 110 children participated last year and said he expects 150 to sign up this year. The league begins in late March. The coach and players capped the visit by autographing Fire programs for every participant. Harrison said hockey is gaining new fans and participants all the time. "It's really expanded," he added. "At some of these clinics the kids show up in RollerBlades and with full equipment. They might come out to watch us play, but it also helps the kids. It's terrific.'

BY ERIC ZARATE Fort Worth Star- Telegram Professional hockey Clubs of has come to the Arlington. The Fort Worth Fire, two players and the coach, anyway, stopped in Thursday at the club's main branch for a street hockey clinic and some "floor" time in the gymnasium. "Thank goodness we only told the kids a week in advance; they were so excited," said Mike Frederick, the club's street hockey coordinator. The Fire is a member of the Central Hockey League. It was the team's first visit to a Arlington youth club. Center Sean Rowe, left wing Mike McCormick and coach Steve Harrison put about 40 players through shooting, stick-handling and passing drills. A buddy of Rowe, former minor-leaguer Chris DeLeon, also helped. "I feel, and so do the players, that you have to give something back," Harrison said. "It's a game and you see the players enjoying it as well as the kids. I think the players feed off it." Goalies Justin Ward, 10, and Gavin Martin, 11, willingly hurled their padded and helmeted bodies in front of incoming pucks- fired by club members and pro players alike. "*The goalies did a good job," Harrison said. "One puck hit me here [thigh] and the other here [face mask]," said a pelted but proud Ward. "I'd say these guys shoot about 90 percent harder than any of us." Maybe even harder.
