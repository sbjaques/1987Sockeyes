## Paragraphs

CHILLIWACK - The Juan de Fuca Whalers dropped a 6-3 B.C. Junior Hockey League decision to the Chilliwack Eagles on Saturday night but they won't have to wait long for a chance to get even. The Eagles and the Whalers square off in a rematch today at Juan de Fuca Recreation Centre beginning at 1 p.m. Mike VanSickle, Ian Doak and Randy Zulinik scored for Juan de Fuca in Saturday's loss, which dropped the Whalers' record to 15-13-1 and left them tied for third place in the BCJHL's Coastal Division with the Nanaimo Clippers. The Whalers, however, have a game in hand over both the Clippers and the second-place Richmond Sockeyes, whom they trail by two points. Cam Matches, Jim Tobin, Darby Bognar, Jim Butterworth, Darrell Anderson and Kevin Jorgenson scored for the Eagles, who led 1-0 and 5-1 by periods. Glenn Pencer made 30 saves to record the victory in the Chilliwack net while Byron Dafoe stopped 35 shots in the loss for Juan de Fuca. Whaler vice- Allan Sproule said neither team was able to get untracked in Saturday's game, slowed by numerous penalties. "I know it wasn't a fun game t to watch," Sproule said.
