Door open for rookie to shine By BRIAN SMILEY Sun Staff Writer BLOOMINGTON, Minn. - Winnipeg Jets have two pre-season games remaining - Friday at Oakland against Pittsburgh Penguins and Sunday at San Diego versus Los Angeles. Grant Richison, Shawn Cronin and Matt Hervey have two games to prove they belong on the final roster of Winnipeg Jets. The loss of third-year veteran Brad Berry for three weeks with a shoulder seperation opens the door for one of these young men to take a spot, albeit a brief stay until Berry's return. "We all know there's a job," said Richison yesterday. "But you can't try too much out there. "I'll just keep continue on playing the way I have been. You know, it's too bad for Brad, and I know he's got a spot on the team, but I'll just keep playing and getting more experience." Of the three, Richison has demonstrated he's the best puck handler. The free-agent signee from University of Calgary has been one of the biggest surprises to emerge from training camp. It was first thought he would merely be another body to fill out camp rosters. The 22-year-old now has a chance for a job, if not with the Jets then American League affiliate Moncton Hawks. Hervey is fundamentally sound, but prone to crucial giveaways in his zone. "I think I've kind of fallen into Brad's footsteps," said Hervey. "We've got a lot of offensive defencemen here. I'm just looking to provide good defence clear the front of the net and moving the puck out of our zone consistently." Cronin likely has the inside track due to the all mighty intimidating factor. At 6-foot-2, 210- pounds, Cronin the Barbarian makes forwards think twice before they plant themselves around the Jets' net. "Brad's injury is unfortunate," said Jet coach Bob Murdoch. "You can look at it and say Berry and (Peter) Taglianetti out that means we lack experience and don't have depth. "But on the other hand you can say some of your kids are going to get added game situations and develop a little quicker. "In the long run it probably help the Winnipeg Jets. In the short run, you have to question what will happen."
