Sabatini slump continues ROME - Not even Gabriela Sabatini's favorite tournament can change her recent results. The No. 3 seed, seeking her first title since winning here two years ago, was upset in her first match in the Italian Open, losing 6-4, 6-3 to little-known Irina Spirlea of Romania. Sabatini has won the Italian Open four times and long considered the Foro Italico her home. But she never got into the match lost badly to a player who is ranked 58th and who had never beaten someone in the top 10. Another hockey 'miracle' MILAN, Italy It wasn't quite the "Miracle on Ice." Still, the United States' 3-1 victory over the remnants of the Big Red Russian machine rekindled memories of the 1980 Olympics and one of hockey's most startling upsets. The win was the first time in 14 tries since those Olympics that the United States had beaten the Russians or their Soviet predecessors. It propelled the Americans into Saturday's semifinals of the World Championships.
