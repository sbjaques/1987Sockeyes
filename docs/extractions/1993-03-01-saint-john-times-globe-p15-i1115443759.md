The St. John's Maple Leafs won't be back to Saint John this week. The team has decided to play two American Hockey League games against the Moncton Hawks at Charlottetown Friday and Saturday. The team has been without : a home since December when a strike closed down Memorial Stadium. The Leaf's played games in Saint John Friday and Saturday against the Fredericton Canadiens and fans expressed hope the Leafs would return. Oleg Petrov, Steve Larouche and Lindsay Vallis scored two goals apiece as the Canadiens defeated St. John's 8-2 Saturday. It was the second of two games between the same teams here at the Lord Beaverbrook Rink. Both games attracted capacity crowds of better than 3,500. Donald Brashear and Gerry Fleming also scored for the Canadiens, who scored five of their goals on the power play. Dave Tomlinson and Eric Lacroix responded for the Maple Leafs, who scored the first goal of the game three minutes into the first period. Then, the Baby Habs responded with three fast power play goals for a 3-1 lead and added another before the period ended. Fredericton scored three unanswered markers in the middle period while the teams exhanged a goal apiece in the final frame. The Canadiens outshot St. John's 41-21. Fredericton coach Paulin Bordeleau said he liked "the way our power play found itself tonight." St. John's captain Kevin McClelland said that "considering everything that's happened, maybe we were due for a bad game. But not that bad. They got their first glimpse of the regular-season product in : almost 15 years over the weekend and there were few negative comments, despite a blowout Saturday. The fans really didn't care. They were there to get a taste of the AHL and, by and large, they loved it. The weekend games pitted the homeless St. John's Maple Leafs against the Fredericton Canadiens. The Lord Beaverbrook Rink was sold out for both games. Clearly, the Maple Leafs were the fan-favorites simply because the Toronto Maple Leafs have long been the favorites of Saint John fans. Just as soon as the blue jerseys with the Maple Leaf showed up on the ice, electricity filled the air. The boos were reserved for the Canadiens, but only until little Oleg Petrov wound up with the puck and headed for enemy territory. Fans at both games were given a treat by the Russian speedster.
