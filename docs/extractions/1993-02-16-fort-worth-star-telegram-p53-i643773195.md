From Wire Reports Slumping goalie Kelly Hrudey had 43 saves and Jari Kurri triggered a three-goal third period yesterday as Los Angeles ended a seven-game winless streak with a 3- 0 NHL victory against Vancouver in Inglewood, Calif. The National Hockey League's second-most productive offense was blanked for the first time since losing consecutive shutouts in December 1991 at Edmonton and Quebec. It was the second shutout by Hrudey this season and the 15th of his career, and it snapped a personal five-game winless streak. The Kings had allowed 29 goals in their past three games.

BY DAVID BOCLAIR Special to the Star-Telegram DALLAS- It is commonplace to hear players and coaches complain about officiating in any sport. In the Central Hockey League, however, those complaints land on a sympathetic ear. "We've had trouble finding good officials," CHL president Ray Miron said. "We got the best; we were able to get. They're doing the best they can trying to administer discipline." Among the referees employed by the CHL are Scott Zelkin and Kevin Hall, both members of the National Hockey League's training program. Fifteen referees are in the program and work all levels of minor-league hockey; the best do substitute duty in the NHL. Hall officiated the Fire's 5-4 victory in Tulsa on Sunday and didn't receive rave reviews from Fort Worth coach Peter Mahovlich.
