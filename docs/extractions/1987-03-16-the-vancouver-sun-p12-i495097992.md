Canadian Press CALGARY - Mervyn (Red) Dutton, former president of the National Hockey League and a member of the Hockey Hall of Fame, died here Sunday following a lengthy illness. He was 89. Born Jan. 3, 1898 in Russell, Man., Dutton moved to Calgary in 1922 and played for four years with the Calgary Tigers of the old professional Western Canada Hockey League. When that league was disbanded, Dutton played in the NHL for 10 years, the first four with the Montreal Maroons. He was traded in 1930 to the New York Americans for Lionel Conacher, Canada's athlete of the halfcentury, and spent the six years there. Canadian Press SEATTLE - Dean Sexsmith and Shawn Chambers each had two goals to help Seattle Thunderbirds gain a 7-7 overtime tie against New Westminster Bruins in a Western Hockey League game Sunday night. Stuart Sage scored three goals to lead the Bruins, who trailed 3-2 after the first period but had a 6-5 lead after 40 minutes. Chris Joseph, Mike Schwengler and Trevor Sim completed the Seattle scoring while Glen Goodall had three assists. Scott Rawson, Sean LeBrun, Cal Zankowski and Mario Desjardins, who fired the equalizing goal on a power play with 7:36 remaining in the game, were the other Bruin snipers. Goalie Drago Adam blocked 34 shots for the Bruins while Danny Lorenz made 35 saves for the Thunderbirds.
