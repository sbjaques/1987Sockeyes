Dino Ciccarelli: returned to action Steelhawk facing surgery HAMILTON STEELHAWKS' forward Alain Laforge suffered a severe gash on one of his fingers and will need plastic surgery to repair it, Hawks' trainer Conrad Lackten said. Laforge - no relation to the team's coach - was injured in the third period of last night's Ontario Hockey League game during a fight against Jamie Allan of Windsor Spitfires. The finger cut was caused by a skate blade. In addition, 18-year-old Laforge : suffered another cut in the same third-period altercation. His forehead hit the ice and 10 stitches were needed to close the wound. Said Lackten: "His head hit the ice and when he was down somebody stepped on the ring finger (which was bare) of his left hand with a skate. It made a cut from the last knuckle to underneath the nail. "It's not as serious as we first thought. We thought the tendon might have been severed. But there's no tendon damage ... he was lucky." Acquired as part of a six-player trade with Sudbury Wolves Nov. 30, Laforge lost a lot of blood in the accident. He was taken to hospital in Windsor and returned to Hamilton last night on the team bus with a patch on his forehead and a heavily bandaged finger. "It will take a week or two to get the movement back in his finger," Lackten said. Laforge will be examined by Hawks' team physician today. 000 FORMER HAMILTON Steelhawks' goaltender Willie Popp suffered a broken left wrist last Saturday while playing for Chilliwack Eagles of the British Columbia Junior Hockey League. Popp is in the middle of a controversy, because he was on emergency loan at the time from Richmond Sockeyes. Apparently, the Chilliwack club didn't receive proper permission to use Popp. Popp has the best goals against average in the division - 3.08. 000 ELSEWHERE IN the OHL last night, Cornwall Royals trounced Toronto Marlboros 7-1 and Peterborough Petes defeated Oshawa Generals 5-2. In Cornwall, the Marlboros' Sean Davidson scored after 17 seconds of the opening period but the Royals bounced back for a victory. Steve Bisson recorded the first three-goal game of his three OHL career, while Cornwall's leading-goal scorer Larry Mitchell added two goals. Jason Cirone and Mark Evans had singles for Royals. Cornwall outshot Toronto 43-22 before a crowd of 1,834 and moved into sole possession of second place in the Leyden Division one point ahead of the Ottawa 67s. In Peterborough, Petes jumped into a quick 2-0 lead and coasted to a win over Generals in a fight-filled contest. Referee Dave Lynch called 192 minutes in penalties including 22 fighting majors. Generals were penalized 99 minutes and the Petes got 93. Jamey Hicks, Corey Foster, Paul Mitton, Mark Freer and Jody Hull scored for Petes. Brian Hunt and Marc Laniel replied for Generals.
