So what did Saint John fans think of the calibre of the American Hockey League? They got their first glimpse of the regular-season product in almost 15 years over the weekend and there were few negative comments, despite a blowout Saturday. The fans really didn't care. They were there to get a taste of the AHL and, by and large, they loved it. The weekend games pitted the homeless St. John's Maple Leafs against the Fredericton Canadiens. The Lord Beaverbrook Rink was sold out for both games. Clearly, the Maple Leafs were the fan-favorites simply because the Toronto Maple Leafs have long been the favorites of Saint John fans. Just as soon as the blue jerseys with the Maple Leaf showed up on the ice, electricity filled the air. The boos were reserved for the Canadiens, but only until little Oleg Petrov wound up with the puck and headed for enemy territory. Fans at both games were given a treat by the Russian speedster. He was, indeed, the most popular player on the ice in both games. The weekend games showcased the All. for Saint John's often hard-to-please fans, for the fans who will be supporting our city's AlL franchise next season.
