Odell Lake in 1984. THE UMPQUA IS SUPPOSED to have yielded an 83-pound chinook in 1910 which stood as a world record for hook and line until 1959. World mark for chinook now belongs to the 97-pound, 4-ounce beast caught three years ago in Alaska's home of giants, the Kenai. Other salmon records for the state belong to a 25- pound, 5-ounce coho from Siltcoos Lake, a 19-pound chum from the Kilchis, and a 3½-pound kokanee (sockeye) from Paulina Lake. The 64½-pound striped bass which Nevada fly fisher Beryl Bliss took from the Umpqua in 1973 remains a world record for fly rod. Other state leaders include a 33½-pound flathead catfish from Brownlee Reservoir on the Snake; 17- pound, 9.4 ounce walleye from the Columbia; 7-pound, 1-ounce striped-white bass hybrid from North Tenmile; 6-pound, 13-ounce smallmouth bass from Brownlee; 4¾- pound shad from the Willamette, and 4-pound whitefish from the McKenzie. Sturgeon aren't listed because of the six-foot max for keepers. Nothing entered for golden trout, Atlantic salmon or redear sunfish, so go for it! If you catch your Atlantic in Hosmer Lake, though, better be ready to explain how you were able to weigh, measure and mug your fish before releasing it unharmed as required on that lake.

By The Associated Press LEXINGTON, Ky. - The NCAA is investigating another possible recruiting violation by the University of Kentucky basketball program - whether transportation or expenses were provided for a recruit's mother to visit the campus last fall. The Courier-Journal reported the investigation in a story in Saturday's editions. Kentucky also is being investigated to determine whether assistant coach Dwane Casey sent the father of recruit Chris Mills $1,000. The other investigation involves signee Shawn Kemp of Elkhart, Ind., and a visit his mother, Barbara Brown, made to the campus Oct. 31-Nov. 1. According to correspondence between UK and the NCAA obtained by The Courier-Journal under an openrecords request, Brown paid cash for her airline ticket and for a night at the Radisson Plaza Hotel.
