WENATCHEE - The Lake Wenatchee sport sockeye salmon fishery, promising to become nearly as popular as the often-closed Lake Washington season, opened Saturday and produced excellent fishing over the weekend. State Fisheries Department personnel checked an average of nearly three fish per boat, according to a spokesman at Cougar Inn on the lake's upper end. Lavonne Dobson at Telma Resort said she talked to 15 or 20 anglers on Saturday, only two of which hadn't caught at least one sockeye. "A lot of people were taking limits (three per day; and almost everybody had one or two," she said. Morning fishing was best, she added, at a depth of 50 to 70 feet. Later in the day it was necessary to go as deep as 100 or 110 feet. Fish were scattered in various parts of the lake, both contacts reported. The season runs through Labor Day, and a salmon stamp and card are required. EWU coach apologizes for 'embarrassment' CHENEY - Fired Eastern Washington University basketball Coach Joe Folda issued a brief apology to the school and its athletic department but chose not to tell his side of the scandal that cost him his job. The third-year coach was fired last Friday after admitting that violations of National Collegiate Athletic Association rules were more serious than previously announced. The NCAA is investigating, several admitted rules violations i in the Eagles' basketball program. "Right now I have one comment," Folda said Monday. "As I look back, I've made some very poor decisions during my stay at Eastern. I'm not prepared to justify anything, 1 just want to say that I'm extremely sorry for the embarrassment to the athletic department and to Eastern Washington." North Korea unhappy with latest IOC proposal TOKYO - North Korea said today the International Olympic Committee's latest proposal falls far short of meeting its demand to join South Korea as host of the 1988 Summer Olympics, and called for another meeting on the issue before Sept. 17. A North Korean statement said the problem is South Korea's intention "to monopolize the 'Seoul Olympics' planned originally and singlehandedly stage the Games for its political purposes." The North has claimed that Seoul, chosen by the IOC as the 1988 Olympic host, would be unfit for that role but that making Seoul and Pyongyang co-hosts would contribute to reunification of the Korean peninsula. High school athlete collapses on field, dies later ASHEVILLE, N.C. - An Asheville High School football player collapsed on the field during practice Monday evening and died about an hour later at St. Joseph's Hospital, authorities said. Michael Cutshaw, 16, suffered "full cardi-c arrest" during practice and rescue personnel worked for almost an hour to revive him, according to assistant coach Gene Hammonds and a spokesman at the hospital. "The kid fell down while he was running," Hammonds said. "That's about all I know about it."
