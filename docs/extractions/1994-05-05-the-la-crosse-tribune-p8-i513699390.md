Alcala leads DuPont WILMINGTON, Del. - Defending champion Raul Alcala of Mexico rode to a cautious, but steady prologue victory in the dreary and dangerous start to the Tour DuPont. Alcala, 30, the last of 112 starters, completed the 2.98-mile course in steady rain and strong winds in 6 minutes, 25.718 seconds, an average speed of 27.81 mph. Greg LeMond of Edina, Minn., a three-time Tour de France champion, was fifth in 6:32.514. Reigning world road champion Lance Armstrong of Austin, Texas, the pre-race favorite, finished a disappointing 25th in 6:43.999. Lemieux might be back PITTSBURGH - Mario Lemieux, who talked openly of retirement following the Pittsburgh Penguins' playoff collapse, almost certainly will return next season, according to Penguins chairman Howard Baldwin. Weary of fighting with back pain and the stress related to his 1993 cancer treatments, Lemieux said last week he would decide by Sept. 1 if he would retire. Lemieux, 28, remains disappointed with the Penguins' second early-1 playoff defeat in as many seasons, but is more upbeat and is already talking of starting an extensive offseason conditioning g program, Baldwin said. RED WINGS: Sergei Fedorov, who had 56 goals and 64 assists this season for the Detroit Red Wings, has been named NHL player of the year by The Sporting News. Sabatini slump continues ROME - Not even Gabriela Sabatini's favorite tournament can change her recent results. The No. 3 seed, seeking her first title since winning here two years ago, was upset in her first match in the Italian Open, losing 6-4, 6-3 to little-known Irina Spirlea of Romania. Sabatini has won the Italian Open four times and long considered the Foro Italico her home. But she never got into the match and lost badly to a player who is ranked 58th and who had never beaten someone in the top 10. Another hockey 'miracle' MILAN, Italy - It wasn't quite the "Miracle on Ice." Still, the United States' 3-1 victory over the remnants of the Big Red Russian machine rekindled memories of the 1980 Olympics and one of hockey's most startling upsets. The win was the first time in 14 tries since those Olympics that the United States had beaten the Russians or their Soviet predecessors. It propelled the Americans into Saturday's semifinals of the World Championships. Elsewhere • HELLO, GOODBYE: The San Francisco 49ers re-signed running back Dexter Carter and waived running back Amp Lee as they continued roster maneuvering under the NFL salary cap. The two former Florida State stars were backups to Ricky Watters last season. • HORSE HALL: Jimmy Croll, trainer of Kentucky Derby favorite Holy Bull, and Steve Cauthen, who rode to Derby glory on Affirmed in 1978, will be inducted into the Racing Hall of Fame. Also honored were horses Arts and Letters, Ta Wee, Eight Thirty and the jumper Flatterer.
