FORT WORTH - With the closest team in either direction slumping, the Fort Worth Fire finally has a chance to make a significant push in the Central Hockey League standings. Fort Worth starts the week solidly in fourth place, two points ahead of Memphis and five back of Dallas. Three road games in four days - beginning at 7:30 tonight in Memphis, Tenn. - could conceivably conclude with the Fire in third place or at least closer to third than fifth. It was nine days ago that Fort Worth took a one-point lead over the RiverKings. Memphis has not been able to pull even, losing seven in succession since the start of the new year. A Fire victory tonight would create a cushion between fourth and fifth place that hasn't existed since Jan. 4 when the RiverKings were five points up on Fort Worth. "If we can win [tonight] it really gives us breathing room," Fire coach Peter Mahovlich said. "Now all of the sudden, too, we're five points behind Dallas." The Freeze has dropped four in succession overall and three consecutive to Fort Worth. The Fire's week wraps up Friday with a game in Dallas.
