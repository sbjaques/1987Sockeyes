Hawks coach Dave Farrish also had "good feelings" about his roster and he analyzed it, position-byposition. At goal, the Hawks will have rookie Rick Tabaracci and thirdyear veteran Bob Essensa. "These two have great futures in the game, but they have to play well here,' Farrish said. "The team should be confident with these two goaltenders." Hawks are currently carrying seven defencemen and all "are big, physical and they move the puck well." "It would seem like there's a little more depth at defence this year," the coach said. "There's no one offensive force like a Paul Boutilier, but there's great balance among the seven.' Moncton defencemen are: Chris Norton, Matt Hervey, Guy Gosselin, Todd Flichel, Dallas Eakins, Bryan Marchment and Grant Richison. The Hawks be lacking a little depth at forward, but another player or two could soon be reassigned to Moncton from the NHL parent Winnipeg Jets. The Schendier-Hughes-Kulak line will be as good as any in the league, but the team's other lines could be lacking scoring punch. Other Moncton forwards are: player/assistant coach Ron Wilson, Guy Larose, Luciano Borsato, Neil Meadmore, Larry Bernard, Danton Cole and Brian Hunt.
