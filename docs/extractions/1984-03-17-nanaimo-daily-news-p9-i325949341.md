Clippers 6, Sockeyes 5 (OT) By KEVIN MITCHELL Staff Writer Given one wish, there's no way Wayne Bianchin could have asked for a better end to Nanaimo Clippers' quarter-final playoff series with Richmond Sockeyes Friday night at frenzied Frank Crane Arena. Len Evans scored at 7:09 of a second s suddendeath overtime period to give the Clippers thrilling 6-5 win over Richmond before close to 1,200 B.C. Junior Hockey League fans. The Clippers voted early this morning to begin the Coastal Division best-of-seven final series against Langley Eagles Sunday night (7:30) at Frank Crane Arena. Langley can't get weekend ice time so the series will shift to the Fraser Valley for games two and three Monday and Tuesday. "Even as a pro, there's nothing bigger than tonight," said a joyous Bianchin, in his second year as Clipper coach. "You put away all the frustrations of the season for phe magic moment.' It was without a doubt the best junior hockey game played in Nanaimo in five years if not ever. The Clippers looked like the New York Islanders for the first 10 minutes of the game, but came up empty due to about four goalpost shots and some sensational goaltending by rookie Chris Dickson. As strange as it seemed, Richmond shouldn't have held period leads of 2-1 and 3-1. The Clippers were the stronger team on this night, but until defenceman Stan Czenczek rushed the length of the ice to narrow the margin to 5-4 late in the third period, couldn't get a break. Nanaimo held an overwhelming 19-8 shot advantage in the opening session, but had to settie for Gord Simpson's eighth goal of the series to offset a powerplay goal by David Street and a shorthanded effort by Eric Catchpole. Richmond took a 5-2 lead at 11:36 of the third frame when Dave Phipps' shot struck the end boards, hit Clipper goalie Ken Trousdall in the back of the leg, and trickled in the net. That shot by Phipps, his second tally of the night, stung the Clippers like a rope burn in what was a highly intense tug-of-war. However, Evans scored 33 seconds later and Czenczek followed five minutes later with a rush which resulted in a marker which would rate goalof-the-week in any league. Simpson then brought the house down when he dove at a loose puck in front of Dickson, connecting enough to send the puck over the line with just 49 second left in regulation time. That goal came with Czenczek in the penalty box for an all too obvious trip in front of referee Dave Dodson. Both clubs enjoyed great scoring chances in the first 10-minute overtime session, but Trousdell and Dickson wouldn't give an inch. On the winning goal, Evans stole the puck off Sockeye defenceman Peter Gill just inside the Richmond blueline. Al Johnston then beat Richmond forward Richard Novak to the puck and fed Evans in front of the net.

Clippers bring back memories As far as entertainment goes, you can't beat the thrills and spills of junior jockey. I learned that when as a youngster my dad used to take me to old Civic Arena in Vernon. I'll never forget the excitement I got watching the likes of Bob Gassoff, Eddie Johnstone, Wayne Dye, Ernie Gare, John Price and numerous others. Years and thousands of junior hockey games later, I still get the same excitement out of junior hockey. Especially at playoff time. There's nothing like playoff hockey to get one's spine tingling. I can still spend an hour telling whoever will listen how I enjoyed a Centennial Cup western final series between Kelowna Buckaroos and Selkirk, Man. Steelers or another one between Vernon Essos and Weyburn Red Wings of Saskatchewan back in the very early 1970s. Friday night's game between Nanaimo Clippers and Richmond Sockeyes must have injected a ton of enthusiasm into anyone who is fond of the game of hockey. Since it's been four years since this city's junior team has been in a playoff game at Frank Crane Arena, the Clippers' 6-5 win in doubleovertime was just what the doctor ordered. Coach Wayne Blanchin described it as a rink "magic felt happy moment" as and they I'm stood sure and everybody watched in the Clippers mob one another in a a a a a a a a a a victory celebration. As one of thousands of Canadians who never realized a dream of playing for the Leafs of Canadiens, I have never found myself envious of the junior or pro hockey player. Instead, I have found joy in seeing them achieve their goals, finding happiness in a big victory. Watching the beaming smiles in the dressing room and at a post-game paryy made. me feel good as a hockey fan. I realized they had gone through a long and often frustrating season and had surely deserved this moment of joy. For the local young hockey fan, the likes of Len Evans, Gord Simpson and Ken Trousdell will long be remembered as their heroes, if not for Friday's thrilling win, but for what may happen in the next series. One can only hope the best is yet to come, but if it isn't, the Clippers have something very special to be proud of nonetheless. It was a great seventh and deciding game, as good as you're going to see in any hockey league. And it will some day be a nice memory.
