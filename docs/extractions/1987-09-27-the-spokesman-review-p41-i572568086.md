Dale Ward, a statistician for Washington's Department of Fisheries, said commercial fishermen last year received nearly $60 million for their catch of chinook, coho, pink, sockeye and chum - the five principal North American species - in the Columbia and Washington coastal fisheries. In 1985, he said, there was a total of 686,333 sportcaught salmon in the state. When all the numbers for 1986 are tallied, he said, the more recent year will be bigger. Ward estimates each salmon hooked by a sport fisherman is worth at least $56, because of a variety of expenses, so the 1985 fishery value approached $40 million. The numbers covered non-Indian fishermen. Indians, under century-old treaties, are entitled to about one-half of the region's harvestable salmon and steelhead. Most of the Indian-caught fish end up in the commercial market.
