Nissan-Jeep final - the University of Alaska Anchorage against British Columbia - should rekindle some memories of a junior team trying to upend UBC, something the Paper Kings never quite managed. "There were quite a few fights when we played them," said Tobin, smiling. "Both times we played them there were at least two or three scraps." Everybody agrees the exhibitions were close, but nobody agrees on exactly how close the Paper Kings ever came to winning. "They beat us 9-7," says Pankewicz, a junior wing. "It was pretty close. I'd like to play them again." "We played them twice," says Tobin, a sophomore center. "We lost by one goal both times. I can't remember the scores. Just that we lost by one." Forget the scores. Tonight's will be a lot more important anyway. The Seawolves are the defending champions of the Nissan-Jeep, even it that's hard to remember, since that was two years ago. Tonight's championship game also will reunite the three Seawolves with one of their former Paper King teammates, Scott Frizzell, a sophomore forward for UBC. Actually, Frizzell sounded more enthused about playing against his former juniors teammates than they did. "I told the (UAA) guys last night I hope we both get in the finals just to see how it goes," Frizzell said. "I wanted to play the guys. That's what I was looking forward to when we came up here. "Me and Trent were the oldest guys on the team," Frizzell says. "We were both leaders. Panky had over 100 points, I had over 100 points and we both led the league in penalty minutes, just about." That should make tonight interesting. But UAA coaches had actually expressed more interest in meeting Merrimack in the final, since that game would have counted on their NCAA record, while a game with a Canadian foe won't. Also, Merrimack was an Independent rival until joining Hockey East, so there remains somewhat of a rivalry with the Seawolves. "We don't care who we play," said UAA assistant coach Mark Ostapina early Saturday. "But Merrimack, being an Independent, that would add flavor to the whole matchup.' But this UAA-UBC matchup is fine with the band of former Paper Kings, along with four UAA players - Doug Spooner, Brown, Rod Trenholm and Jeff Batters - who are from British Columbia. The Seawolves' berth in the final was secured with a victory over Dartmouth on Friday, so Saturday they kicked back at Sullivan Arena and watched the two other semifinalists go at it. "It'd be nice to play UBC," said Pankewicz Saturday. It was early in the second period and UBC led Merrimack 5-2. The Thunderbirds would end up winning 6-4. "It's always fun seeing them," Frizzell says of his former juniors teammates. "But when you're on the ice, it's all serious. There's no friends on the ice." TIMES SPORTS WRITER The University of British Columbia proved Saturday night that simply completing 60 minutes of victorious hockey is no guarantee that the evening will be considered a success afterward. UBC advanced to the championship game of the Nissan-Jeep Classic with a 6-4 victory over Merrimack before 4,328 at Sullivan Arena, but not before stalwart defenseman Henry Czenczek was assessed a costly game disqualification for fighting Merrimack's Howie Rosenblatt after the final buzzer.
