Yet for Western Washington University football coach Paul Hansen, today is just another day. He looks warily at another day - May 8 - when the school's Athletic and Recreation Committee makes its final recommendations as to the budget allotment football will receive. That is a big step forward from the state of the game on the Belling;ham campus earlier this year. As noted here in January, the Vikings' football program was on the brink of being terminated. Eight wins in Hansen's four-year tenure spread very thin with t the school's board of trustees, faculty and students. The program was deemed a considerable financial drain on the rest of the athletic body, and the machinery was in place to make Western football extinct. Since January, however, football has received a vote of confidence. A review board has doubled the WWU coaching staff; it allotted funds for the hiring of an assistant coach. "It's going to be an upgrade to a certain extent in that we're going to get an assistant coach," said Hansen. "Now it's just a matter of what the rest is going to be. "Yeah, it's sort of victory. It's a definite upgrade." Matters unsettled include a budget and "talent awards."
