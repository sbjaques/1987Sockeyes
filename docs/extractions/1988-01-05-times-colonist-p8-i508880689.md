Sawtell pleased Whaler brass to no end Monday, bringing his goal total to 21 with the dream game. "That's probably my biggest game ever, in junior hockey anyway," Sawtell said of the eight-point effort. "Everything I touched tonight seemed to go right for me." The game wasn't all roses for the Whalers, who trailed 2-0 early in the game and were down 4-3 five minutes into the second period. But four unanswered goals gave Juan de Fuca a 7-4 lead after two periods and the Whalers romped to six more goals in the final frame. Todd Demchuk also added a pair of goals for the Whalers while Ian Doak, Rod Trenholm, Paul Ritchie, Randy Zulinik and Jamie Fowler had singles. Dean Methorst scored three times for the Falcons. Monday's win improved the Whalers' record to 19-16-1 and moved them to within four points of the second-place Richmond Sockeyes, a team they will face in a home-andhome series this week; Wednesday in Richmond and Saturday at home.
