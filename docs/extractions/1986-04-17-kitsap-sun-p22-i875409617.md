By Ed Friedrich Sun Sports Writer TACOMA - The Ralph Black-Chico Borja feud was rekindled last night when Borja planted an elbow to Black's chops. Black's fat lip soon will heal. The Wings' plight may not. Borja's ill-timed blow could cost Wichita a trip to the Western Division finals as the Wings were leading 4-3 with just 4:06 to go in regulation when he let if fly. It's impossible to speculate the outcome had both teams remained at full strength, but the Wings were in control and it appeared on their way to a 2-1 series lead. Had d they sat on their lead, they would've at least forced a final game back in Wichita, if not sewn things up tomorrow night at the T-Dome. As it now stands, the Stars must win just one of two games, the first played tomorrow night before as many as 20,000 fans. During an 1 officials' time out, Black and Borja were fueling their continuing verbal battle when Borja spun the Stars' 22-year-old all-star around with a sharp blast to the kisser in full view of the officials, who sentenced Borja to two minutes in the penalty box for pushing. He could've easily been red-carded and sequestered for five minutes, but in the words of Stars assistant coach Ray Evans, "With those refs, we were lucky to even get the two minutes." With 26 seconds remaining in the power play, Tacoma coach Alan Hinton called time out and inserted Steve Zungul as a sixth attacker, giving the Stars a 6-on-4 advantage. And though the Wings killed the penalty, Borja couldn't catch up with the play before Gary Heale scored the tying goal at the left post. The Stars wound up winning 5-4 in overtime. "It's a war. That's the second time he's cheapshotted me," said Black, recalling the first meeting of the season when Borja deliberately kicked him. Later in that game, Dale Mitchell slammed Borja up against the boards and Borja attacked, getting ejected from the game. "He did the exact same thing to me," Black said of the first tangle. "I was running back and he was behind me and everybody was going forward and last time he just kicked me. "I haven't forgot it. I've played fair against him. I don't know if any of you seen it, but he elbowed me for no reason. I was saying something to him but that's all right, it's only words. Words can't hurt no one. "He just wound up and pegged me right here, right on the lip. You can see it a little bit. It's not major, but ... "Then I just went ah ... it cost him two minutes, we got a goal from it. My first reaction was to retaliate, but then I thought, 'Why retaliate? It's gonna hurt the team,' and I kinda held it."
