Eric Brule has scored four goals since joining the Fire on Jan. 2. Each has been the Fire's final tally of the game, all have been in the closing minutes and two have been game-winners. In his first contest he netted the decisive shot in a shootout victory against Dallas. On Jan. 26, he capped the six-goal third-period rally against Oklahoma City with four seconds remaining in the game. Then Saturday night he beat Tulsa with a short-handed goal with three seconds tc play. His earliest, and only goal in a losing effort, was Jan. 29 in Memphis. He scored with 2:51 remaining pulling the Fire within 5-4, a score that ultimately stood up. Old foes It was no surprise to Mike McCormick when Tulsa's Doug Lawrence - no favorite of the Fort Worth fans - stepped right in front of an official to take a shot at Ron Aubrey on Saturday night. "I played on a line with that kid in Greensboro," McCormick said. "He was always starting stuff that I had to finish." Briefly Going head-to-head on Friday and Saturday nights, the Fire outdrew the Dallas Freeze both games. The total turnstile count for the weekend: Fort Worth 13,929, Dallas 8,472. Jason Brousseau, who scored three goals in two games, was chosen as the CHL player of the week yesterday. Fort Worth was the only team in the league that didn't lose last week.

FORT WORTH - The Fort Worth Fire plans to install corporate boxes in the Fort Worth-Tarrant County Convention Center next season, general manager George Branum says. Preliminary plans call for the boxes to be located on the north side of the arena. "They will be for corporate entertainment," Branum said. "It's purely for the guys who want to play the high-roller." Branum envisions leather arm chairs - six to a box. A wait staff will serve cocktails and food to those in attendance. "The corporate entities have really given us a boost in the community," Branum said. "In five months we've gone from nobody knowing what the Fire was to an actual entity. A lot of that is because of corporate people talking about us outside their involvement with us. "Every other sport has corporate boxes; hockey may as well have some too."
