April 15, 1980: Verbal exchanges between some Medicine Hat Tigers and assorted Pats fans almost lured players into the Agridome stands during a Western Hockey League post-season matchup. The Tigers hurled sticks, gloves and helmets everything but themselves toward the spectators, who responded with a barrage of debris. The affair followed a line brawl which erupted when Tigers goaltender Kelly Hrudey charged out of his net to fight Regina's Bill Ansell. Moments later, Medicine Hat's Bruce Gordon left the bench to attack Ansell. "I threw him on," said Tigers coach Pat Ginnell. "I'm not going to stand around and watch our goalie take a beating." The Thunderbirds won the game, the first of a best-of-nine West Division semi-final, 9-2. The teams played Game 4 last night in the TriCities Coliseum with the Americans winning 4-0. The Thunderbirds lead the series 3-1. The suspension issued to Kozuback is a three-pronged affair: He's already missed two games and won't coach any games in Seattle for the remainder of this series (that could be one, two or three more games, for a maximum of five games); Should the Americans advance, he won't coach any road games during the West Division final (that could be another five games); and, he has been suspended from coaching any games in Seattle next season (that, too, could be five games). Kozuback will also be on probation for all of the 1990-91 season. As well, the Americans were fined $2,000 and told to post a $5,000 bond which they will lose if there are any more problems. The league will hold the bond through next season. The Thunderbirds were fined $1,000 for failing to control their crowd. And three Tri-City defencemen were suspended. Steve Jaques and Terry Virtue got one game each and have already served those suspensions. Jeff Fancy was hit with a five-game suspension and is eligible to return for Game 7 should the series last that long.
