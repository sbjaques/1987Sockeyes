the Brandon Wheat Kings is about to sign a contract with the New York Islanders. Maneluk, 20, has agreed to terms on what he is calling a modified three-way contract. The contract is structured so that if he plays 20 American Hockey League games it becomes a two-way deal, meaning the International Hockey League would be out of the picture . It was in December when Gord Kruppke, a defenceman with the Prince Albert Raiders, suffered a ruptured spleen while playing the Warriors in Moose Jaw. Kruppke, who underwent emergency surgery in Moose Jaw, couldn't, and still can't, remember when he was injured. Now Kruppke's out again, this time with ligament damage to his left knee suffered last Friday against the Blades in Saskatoon. And guess what? He can't remember it happening. Centre Neil Pogany, 20, hasn't played since joining Brandon from the Warriors. He's bothered by a tender knee. Centre Mario Desjardins, who has bounced around the WHL over the past couple of seasons, is leading the B.C. Junior Hockey League in scoring. Going into this week, he had 29 points for the Richmond Sockeyes . . . Moose Jaw defenceman Pat Beauchesne is on the shelf with a cracked bone in an ankle, a souvenir he brought back from the Washington Capitals' training camp . Speaking of the Warriors, head coach Jim Harrison has Theoren Fleury skating between Trevor Jobe and Bob Foglietta and that could turn the opposition's end of the ice into a real red-light district. They totalled 146 goals last season. Could they hit 200 this time around? Saskatoon centre Kevin Kaminski is out with a bruised shoulder, while defenceman Tony Twist is still sidelined with an elbow problem. Both are expected back on Friday when the Swift Current Broncos visit Saskatoon. Right-winger Dwight Mullins missed one game with a swollen knee but is likely to play tonight against the Tigers in Medicine Hat. Brandon defenceman Steve Wienke banged up a knee last night against the Broncos and will likely miss games tonight in Swift Current and at home Monday against the Regina Pats.
