## Paragraphs

By Gregg Drinnan L-P Assistant Sports Editor Right-winger Dwight Mullins will enter the Western Hockey League record book tonight when his Saskatoon Blades meet the visiting Medicine Hat Tigers. Mullins, 20, is suiting up for his 329th WHL game, tying the record established last season by Rick Herbert. Mullins has played with the Lethbridge Broncos, Calgary Wranglers and Saskatoon. He'll break the record on Wednesday night when the Blades visit the Brandon Wheat Kings. Herbert, a 20-year-old defenceman, could play in the WHL this season but so far has chosen to play with the Richmond Sockeyes of the B.C.J Junior Hockey League. Four other active players Craig Endean of the Regina Pats, Todd Sceviour of the Swift Current Broncos, Lee Trim of the Moose Jaw Warriors and Greg Hawgood of the Kamloops Blazers - will also pass Herbert in the not too distant future. Meanwhile, two of the WHL's career offensive records could fall this season. Only Ron Chipperfield's goal-scoring mark - he scored 261 goals in four seasons with Brandon - isn't in jeopardy. Hawgood, with 295 assists, is closing in on the record of 343 held by Kamloops centre Rob Brown. Hawgood needs 48 assists in the Blazers' 27 remaining games to tie the record. And Endean, with 470 points, has a chance to better the record of 522 held by Brown. Endean is 52 points away from tying Brown and the Pats have 28 games remaining.
