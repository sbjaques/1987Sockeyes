## Paragraphs

Sockeyes assured of Cup play Richmond Sockeyes, champions of the B.C. Junior r Hockey League, opened with a loss to Humboldt Broncos in the best-of-seven Western Canada Centennial Cup championship, but win or lose, will be playing in the national four-team final. Humboldt, the Saskatchewan champs, scored twice in a 20-minute overtime period Monday night to earn a 5-3 victory in the opening game of the best-of-seven western final. However, as Humboldt will play host to the Canadian final - which also features a team from Ontario and one from the Atlantic provinces both Sockeyes and Broncos are guaranteed spots as the two Western representatives. The teams met again last night (Thuesday) in Humboldt. If the Broncos won, the series shifts to Richmond, with game three scheduled Friday night. If the Sockeyes won, game three will" be in Humboldt tonight (Wednesday) before the move to Richmond. Sockeyes, who rapped Red Deer Rustlers 9-1 in the seventh and deciding game of a wild and wooly B.C.-Alberta playoff, got goals Monday from Bryon Moller, Matt Hervey and Jason Phillips.
