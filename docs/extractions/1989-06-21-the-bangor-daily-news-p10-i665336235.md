By Pete Warner Of the NEWS Staff : Ellsworth's Jim Card, who helped spark the Eagles to an Eastern Maine Class B runnerup finish, has been selected as the Most Valuable Player in Penobscot Valley Conference Class B baseball. Card is one of five Ellsworth play. ers named to the squad, which was drawn from the five schools representing PVC 'B' - Ellsworth, Bucksport, Hermon, Mount Desert Island and Orono. Ellsworth Coach Jack Scott, who guided the Eagles to a 16-3 record, was named the conference Coach of the Year in balloting of the league's coaches. Bucksport placed four players on the PVC All-Star squad, while MDI accounted for three, Orono two and Hermon one. Ellsworth's other representatives are senior catcher Chad Sattler, junior outfielders Jason' Clarke and Mat Brown, and senior pitcher Joe Domagala. Bucksport's All-Star contingent is made up of senior third baseman Matt Leathers, senior shortstop Todd Gilley, senior second baseman Rick Jones and senior pitcher Mark Smith. Senior pitcher Dave Tomlinson, senior catcher Chris Linscott (utility) and sophomore designated hitter Chip Applegate were MDI's honorees. Senior first baseman Casey Garland and sophomore third baseman Chad Eddy (utility) of Orono and Hermon senior outfielder David Hannan also earned the praise •of league coaches for their efforts. Card edged out Bucksport's Leathers in the MVP balloting by virtue of his consistency in the field and at the plate. The 6-foot-1, 170-pounder led the conference with a .564 batting average, including four home runs and 19 runs batted in.
