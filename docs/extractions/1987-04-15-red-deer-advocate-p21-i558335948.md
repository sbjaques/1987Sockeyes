By GREG MEACHEM of The Advocate With close to 1,700 fans roaring at the top of their lungs, Richmond Sockeyes' forward Jason Phillips must have felt the heat. Certainly, Red Deer Rustler goaltender Ken Weiss did. Fortunately for Weiss and the Rustlers, Phillips' thermostat was turned up little higher. The Sockeyes' ace sniper came up empty in a one-on-one duel with Weiss late in a 10- minute overtime period Tuesday at the Arena. Rustlers held on to their one-goal cushion, added an empty net marker 14 seconds later and scored a 7-5 win, taking a 3-2 lead in the best-seven Western Centennial Cup semifinal series. The sixth game is tonight at 7:30 p.m. at the Arena. With 24 seconds left in the overtime stanza, Richmond was awarded a penalty shot after referee Cliff Stauble ruled a Red Deer player had intentionally dislodged the Rustler goal. While Phillips stood at center ice, sizing up the opportunity, Rustler winger Rich Anderson persuaded the crowd to turn up the volume. Phillips skated in on Weiss and shot low to the corner, but instead of finding the mesh, it was blocked by the goaltender's pad. "I thought he was going to try to deke me," said a relieved Weiss, who turned in his fourth straight glittering performance, stopping 46 shots. "But he kept holding the puck to the outside. My heart was pounding away, I went into a butterfly and it hit me. "I felt confident I could stop it, but it was a lot tougher for him than it was for me. . If he makes it, they're still alive. If he doesn't, he's the goat." The first two periods of the game had all the intensity of a taffy-pull. In the most lacklustre contest of the series to date, Rustlers led 3-2 after one period and 4-3 after two, before being outscored 2-1 in the third. Dixon Ward notched the winner just 59 seconds into overtime and the Rustlers moved to within one win of a date with Humboldt Broncos in the Western final, and an automatic berth in the Centennial Cup (Canadian championship) slated for next month in Humboldt. The best-of-seven Western final starts Monday in Humboldt. "We sort of moonwalked through the first two periods," said Rustler coach Larry Billows. "We had more life in the third, but maybe that's because the fans finally started to get excited." With •Big Brother' the Canadian Amateur Hockey Association monitoring the situation. the Red Deer crowd was less lively Tuesday. The CAHA warned that the series would be either cancelled or moved to a neutral location if the fans didn't stop harassing the Sockeyes and throwing debris into their bench. "That's the way hockey should be played (without fan interference)." noted Sockeye general manager-coach Orland Kurtenbach. •What bothered me the first two games here (Saturday and Sunday) was not just the fans, but the refereeing.
