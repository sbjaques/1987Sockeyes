Olympia Airport to host races for sports car Amateur sports car races, ranging from showroom stock to Formula Fords, will be held this weekend at the Olympia Airport. Gates open Saturday and Sunday at 8 a.m. and tickets are $5 for adults and children under 12 are free. Qualifying races start at 9 a.m. and main heats are at 1 p.m. The track is 1.3 miles and there will be 12 different classes. 6-foot-and-under cagers to work out at Pavilion A 6-foot-and-under pro basketball team from Calgary will practice at Saint Martin's College Monday and Tuesday. Practice Monday will be held from 5- 6:30 p.m. On Tuesday the team will turn out twice, going 11 a.m. to noon and again at 5:30 p.m. Mike Thibault, a former Saint Martin's assistant coach, is the Calgary coach. The team is preparing for the playoffs which will be held in Los Angeles. The season began in May. Sockeye season to end on Lake Washington SEATTLE - The sockeye fishing season will close next Monday on Lake Washington because of record catches, state fishery officials say. "We credit the new bare-hook technology. The catch just exploded after word got out about it about a week ago,' Tony Floor, Department of Fisheries information officer, said Wednesday. The sports catch hit a record 96,000 this season, doubling the previous high caught in 1977. Pryor's neck injury puts comeback on hold ROCHESTER, N.Y. - Former world junior welterweight champion Aaron Pryor will have to postpone his latest comeback attempt until at least November because of a serious neck injury, fight promoters said Thursday. His injury also prompted promoters to postpone the entire boxing card scheduled for Sept. 23, which included an International Boxing Federation championship fight featuring Simon Brown at the War Memorial. Pryor, 36-1 and making his third attempt at a comeback, suffered torn ligaments in his neck during a recent sparring session, spokesman Jim Cassidy said. He is wearing a neck brace and won't be able to resume training for three or four weeks, Cassidy said. Cowboys plan to honor Rooney in Steeler game PITTSBURGH - The Dallas Cowboys, in what is believed to be a unprecedented gesture by a pro sports team, will wear the initials of late Pittsburgh Steelers' founder Art Rooney Sr. on their helmets when the two teams meet Sunday. The Steelers will wear patches containing Rooney's initials, AJR, on their jerseys this season. Sports teams have commonly worn black armbands or commemorative emblems to honor a deceased executive or teammate. But NFL officials are unaware of any previous case where a team has honored an opposing club official.
