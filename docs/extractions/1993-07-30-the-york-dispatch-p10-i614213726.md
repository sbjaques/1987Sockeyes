## Paragraphs

Fall physical examinations for Central York School District will be held Aug. 10 & 12 at Central High School. Those who should report Aug. 10 are those athletes in cross country and golf (7-7:30 a.m.), field hockey (7:30-8:15), girls' volleyball (8:15-9) and varsity/jayvee football (9-10). Those reporting Aug. 12 include: frosh football (3-3:45 p.m.), soccer (3:45-4:15) and cheerleading (4:15-5). Volleyball camp The York Volleyball Camp has openings in the Youth Evening Volleyball camp, which is held at York College under the direction of Bruce and Barb Koller. The youth camp will run from 6:30-9:30 p.m. Aug. 2-5 and is designed for boys and girls in grades 4-9. It is geared towards introducing the players to proper techniques and understanding the game. Fee is $65 per player and includes a t-shirt and snacks. For more information, call Barb Koller at 757-2547.

Physicals The West York Area School District will have physical examinations for its fall sports at 12:30 p.m. both Aug. 5 and 12. The examinations will be administered by school district physican at the senior high nurse's office. All participants in football (grades 7-12), golf, soccer and cross country (grades 9-12) should report Aug. 5, while those participating in girls* field hockey, girls* tennis, girls' volleyball (grades 9-12) and football cheerleaders (grades 7-12) should attend the Aug. 12 examination.

I GOLF TYSKA RETAINS LEAD: Jason Tyska of Connellsville shot a 2-over-par 72 to retain a two-shot lead after the second round of the 80th Pennsylania Men's State Amateur Championship in Lancaster. Combined with the 5- under 65 that he shot in the first round, Tyska's total was 3-under 137. Mechanicsburg's Chip Richter, who shot a 71, for a combined 139. TIGER WOODS ADVANCES: Two-time defending champion Tiger Woods of Cypress, Calif., advanced to the third round of the U.S. Junior Championships. Woods, 17, beat Ryan Lavoie of Pasco, Wash., 6 and 5 and Chad Wright of Ventura, Calif., 4 and 2. BOWLING DICKINSON WINS SHOWBOAT: Gary Dickinson won a semifinal match over Jim Long by one pin and then beat Gene Stus 246-203 to win the $150,000 PBA Showboat Senior Invitational Tournament. Long left a 4-7-10 split in the 10th frame of the semifinal match to allow Dickinson to pull out a 212-211 victory. Dickinson, a PBA and ABC Hall of Fame member, then beat top-seeded Stus for his first senior victory and earn $24,000. AULBY TOPS WICHITA OPEN: Mike Aulby averaged 262 over six games to take a commanding lead over Park Bohn Ill in the $140,000 Wichita Open. Aulby, an Indianapolis native who has won 21 PBA tournaments, has an 18-game pinfall of 4,358 and a 137-pin lead over Bohn of Freehold, NJ., who has nine PBA titles to his credit. FOOTBALL PENN STATE GETS OSTROSKY: Doug Ostrosky, a Fox Chapel High tight end and the son of a Pitt booster, is the second Pennsylvania high school football player to make an early commitment to Penn State. Earlier, all-state wide receiver. Joe Nastasi of Northern Bedford committed to the Nittany Lions. The 6-foot-7, 240-pound Ostrosky is rated by some scouting services as one of the nation's top five prep tight ends. I TENNIS STEVEN UPSETS SAMPRAS: Brett Steven of New Zealand, ranked 45th in the world, beat Wimbledon champion and world No. 1 Pete Sampras 7-6 (8-6), 6-3 in the third round of the Canadian Open. Mikael Pernfors of Sweden shocked second-ranked Jim Courier 6-3, 6-2, 13th-seeded Todd Martin upset fourth-ranked Boris Becker of Germany 7-5, 7-6 (7-3) and 11th-seeded Andre Agassi beat fifth-seeded Michael Chang 7-6 (7-3), 6-3. NASCAR SPEED TAKES ALLISON'S SPOT: Lake Speed will take over for the late Davey Allison at Watkins Glen on Aug. 8 and likely complete the rest of the NASCAR season in the No. 28 Ford Thunderbird, team owner Robert Yates said. Speed started 11 races this season in his own Thunderbird, but his sponsorship ran out after last week's race at Talladega Superspeedway, Allison died July 14 after sustaining head injuries in a helicopter crash at Talledega.
