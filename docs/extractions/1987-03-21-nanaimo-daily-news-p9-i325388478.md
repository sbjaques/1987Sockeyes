KELOWNA, B.C. (CP) - Richmond Sockeyes jumped to a 4-0 lead in the opening period and relied on intensity and rugged play en route to a 7-5 B.C. Junior Hockey League playoff victory over Kelowna Packers. Richmond holds a commanding 3-0 margin in the best-of-nine league championship series. Dean Rutledge, Dave Tomlinson and Jason Philips each scored twice for Sockeyes while Mike McCormick added a single. D Kelowna was led by Jason Ruff and Pat Mangold, each with two goals, while Jamie Kurki rounded out the scoring. Richmond coach Orland Kurtenbach said "the key to any hockey game is if you've got people who are intense and taking the body. That's what it takes to win.' His counterpart, Kelowna coach Bill Hogaboam, conceded his team was dominated. "They outbumped us, they were all over top of us and bang, it ended up 4-0 (after one period). We only played 40 minutes of hockey tonight. Richmond is too disciplined to fall four goals behind." Richmond goaltender Frank Romeo made 26 saves while Packers Dusty Imoo and Lucien Carignan combined to stop 26 shots. NCAA powers triumph By The Canadian Press The defending-champion Michigan State Spartans and the Harvard Crimson, finalists in the U.S. college hockey championship game one year ago, are pining for another confrontation this spring. Both posted easy victories in quarter-final games Friday night - the Spartans getting a 33-save performance from goalie Norm Foster of Vancouver, downing the Maine Black Bears 6-2 at East Lansing, Mich., and the Crimson defeating Bowling Green Falcons 7-1 at Cambridge, Mass. The Minnesota Gophers beat the Boston College Eagles 4-1 in Boston and the No. 1-ranked North Dakota Fighting Sioux defeated the St. Lawrence Saints 3-1 in Grand Forks, N.D., in other quarter-final games. Guy Perron scored his second goal to move Maine within 3-2 early in the third, but goals in the final 12 minutes by Mitch Messier of Regina, Brian McReynolds of Penetanguishene, Ont., and Kevin Miller of Lansing, Mich., ensured victory. While Foster made 33 saves, Maine's Al Loring stopped 26 shots. In Boston, Dave Snuggerud and Tom Chorske each scored a pair of goals and goalie John Blue kicked out 42 shots to lead Minnesota over Boston College, which got its only goal from Ken Hodge of Windsor, Ont. In Grand Forks, forward Tony Hrkac assisted on goals by Lee Davidson, Steve Johnson and Bob Joyce of Saint John, N.B., as North Dakota improved its record to 37-8. St. L Lawrence fell to 24-10. Canada wins semi-final VICTORIA (CP) - Tradition and revenge will be on Douglas Dryburgh's side today in the final of the Uniroyal world junior men's curling championship. The smugly confident Scottish skip will meet Hugh McFadyen's Canadian foursome from Winnipeg. The two, who went 8-1 through the round-robin portion of the 10-country event, won semifinal games Friday to advance to the title game at the Esquimalt Sports Centre. Dryburgh counted one in an extra end to beat Markus Eggler of Switzerland 8-7, while McFadyen eliminated Tore Torvbraten of Norway 11-5.
