SYDNEY, N.S. (CP) Cape Breton is back to normal following a Canada Winter Games that was a gold mine for Quebec and a reason to boast for organizers. The Games held every two years for developing athletes across Canada ended Saturday with Quebec topping the medal list and officials gushing over packed houses. Quebec, represented by 250 athletes over the two period, went on a tear in the final six days, winning 32 gold to wind up with 47. Ontario finished second with 20 gold from its 252 competitors. The news wasn't all bad for Ontario, however, which edged out Quebec 211-210 for the overall points championship and the Canada Games' Flag. It was the fourth straight time Ontario has captured the title and the eighth time in the 11 Games held. Competition ended Saturday with a schedule heavy on emotion and controversy. In New Waterford, the Nova Scotia men's basketball team delighted a partisan crowd of 1,200 with a 91-76 victory over Quebec. The win appeased a rather poor overall showing by Nova Scotia, which won only five gold and 12 medals in total. The basketball gold capped a week of lopsided wins for Nova Scotia, whose closest margin of victory was 12 points against Ontario. Its widest was a 67-point bulge over P.E.I. It appeared to tie the game 14 seconds later on a shot by defenceman Larry Van Hersle - just as referee Charlie Banfield was assessing a penalty.
