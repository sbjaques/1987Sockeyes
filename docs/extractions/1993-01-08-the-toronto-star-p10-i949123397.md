By Paul Hunter TORONTO STAR The Maple Leafs have some fresh meat to throw to the Sharks. Dave Tomlinson, who leads the St. John's Leafs in scoring, has been recalled by the big club and will be in Toronto's lineup against San Jose at the Gardens tonight. The 24-year-old centre, known for skilful play making and a deft touch around the net, has scored 21 goals and added 30 assists for 51 points in 37 games while playing the entire season with the baby Leafs. Team president Cliff Fletcher recently said Tomlinson has consistently been the best player on Toronto's American Hockey League farm team. He will likely replace Peter Zezel, who sprained his right wrist Wednesday night against Vancouver. Zezel suffered the injury when he was stepped on by Canuck defenceman Jiri Slegr after the Leaf had fallen to the ice in the second period. According to X-rays taken yesterday, Zezel's wrist isn't fractured but the left winger/centre is listed as doubtful by the club. Earlier in that same game, Toronto also lost Doug Gilmour, its leading scorer, with a mild concussion when his head was driven into the boards on a check by Trevor Linden. Although Gilmour didn't practise yesterday, team athletic Chris Broadhurst said the slick centre is expected to dress for tonight's game. Defenceman Todd Gill, who has four points in the last two games, also sat out yesterday's practice. "My back stiffened up a little," he said afterward. The Leafs play the Canadiens in Montreal tomorrow night. Meanwhile, Nikolai Borschevsky is expected to make his return against the Sharks after sitting out three games with a stiff neck. Broadhurst said they were particularly cautious with the rookie right winger because two years in Russia he suffered a concussion that put him out of action for six weeks. I
