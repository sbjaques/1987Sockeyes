At a glance: The Chiefs split a pair of preseason games with the Americans, losing 9-5 and winning 9-3 at home. In the win, the Chiefs out-shot Tri-City by an impressive 52-27 margin. Tonight's game should mark the return to the Chiefs' lineup of center Travis Green, last year's leading scorer who spent the preseason in camp with the New York Islanders. In Green's absence, Whitney and Falloon have sparkled. As the preseason progressed, it became apparent that the Chiefs were not to be expected to surrender nine goals as they did in the first meeting with the Americans. Spokane never gave up more than six goals in any contest after that initial defeat. While the Chiefs will enjoy the return of Green, the Americans will certainly be buoyed by the appearance of Stu Barnes. Barnes, the fourth player chosen in the latest NHL Entry Draft, has been playing in the training camp of the Winnipeg Blue Bombers and was set to return to Kennewick Thursday. The Chiefs are 0-4 in season openers. Chiefs coach Bryan Maxwell would not announce Thursday whether John Colvin or Shawn Dietrich would start in goal. Maxwell said he is still shuffling lines, as well and couldn't say exactly who would play with whom. Mike Barlage, Tye Cameron and Shane Massie have been released, while goalie Trevor Lyons has been placed with the Humboldt Broncos of the Saskatchewan Tier I| Junior League.
