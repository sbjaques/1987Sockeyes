## Paragraphs

If they didn't realize it before, the Selkirk Steelers will now recognize that they have to find a way to stop Humboldt Bronco centre Bill McDougall. McDougall, who set SAJHL records for goals and points this season, scored three goals and assisted on the other four as Humboldt downed Selkirk 7-4 in the opening game of their best-of-seven Centennial Cup Western semifinal in Selkirk on Thursday. Mike Gaber, with two, Duncan Ryhorchuk and Kevin Luke also scored for the Broncos, while Kevin Lajeunesse, Jeff Beaudin, Kevin Mayo and Jim Bartley scored for the Steelers. The teams were tied 2-2 after one period and Humboldt led 5-4 after two. Broncos coach Bernie Lynch was not satisfied with his team's performance, despite the win. "We showed the effects of our eight-day layoff," said Lynch. "They should never have scored four goals; we gift three of them. We were really rusty in the first and second, but we played a solid third period and I'm happy with that. "But the key to the game was that they played 20 playoff games and we've played 12. They were really sharp with the puck and passing. "I didn't really feel that we were in control tonight. We'll try to be more dominant (tonight)." Game two of the series will be played tonight in Selkirk. The following three games will be played in Humboldt, beginning Sunday at 7:30 p.m.
