"We did things tonight that we didn't do all year long - bad things," lamented Vernon coach Ernie Gare, whose team rolled to the regular-season Interior title and had nine days rest after dumping Merritt 4-1. The rest didn't seem to help much and Gare suggested his team might have suffered from stage fright. "It's hard to explain," he said. **We just couldn't get anything going.' For New Westminster, everything and everyone - particularly Geldart was going. The 20-year-old from Edmonton joined New Westminster in early January after deciding to leave the University of North Dakota and his scholarship. "I just wasn't happy with things were going," said Geldart, the eighth-round draft pick of the Vancouver Canucks last season. Kevin Hoffman, Jeff McLean and Frank Diller added two goals each for New Westminster, which held a 35-29 edge in shots. Trevor Renkers and Tony Bobbitt scored one each.
