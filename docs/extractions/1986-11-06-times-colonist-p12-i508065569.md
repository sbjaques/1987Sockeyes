Sockeyes sock it to Whalers

Times-Colonist news services Butch Goring has been fired as head coach of the Boston Bruins, the National Hockey League team announced Wednesday night in Buffalo. "Goring has been dismissed and we have no further comment at this time as to who will be his replacement," said Nate Greenberg, a team spokesman. Greenberg said Goring received notice from general manager Harry Sinden immediately after the Bruins lost 8-3 to the Buffalo Sabres. Goring, in his second season with the team, left Buffalo Memorial Auditorium without talking to the media. Goring, 37, was named coach of the Bruins on May 6, 1985, after a a a a a a a a a a 16-year playing career with the Los Angeles Kings, New York Islanders and the Bruins. Boston claimed Goring on waivers from the Islanders on Jan. 8, 1985, and named him player-assistant coach. Goring compiled 375 goals and 888 points in 1,107 NHL games. He was traded by the Kings to the Islanders on March 10, 1980, and led them to their first of four straight Stanley Cups two months later. Under Goring's guidance, the Bruins finished third in the tough Adams Division last season but were swept in the first round the playoffs by the Montreal Canadiens, who went on to win the Stanley Cup. The Bruins were tied for third place in the Adams Division this season, but had struggled recently with just a tie in their last four outings. Goring was the 16th head coach in the history of the Boston franchise and was well-liked by his players. The Boston dressing room was gloomy after the game. Charlie Simmer, who played with Goring in Los Angeles, said it was "very unfortunate." "It's so sad," he said. "We've had so many injuries and tonight we tried to be too cautious.
