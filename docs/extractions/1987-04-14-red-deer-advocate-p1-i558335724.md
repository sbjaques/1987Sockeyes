## Paragraphs

By JACK WILSON of The Advocate Security will be tight tonight for the pivotal fifth game in the British Columbia-Alberta junior hockey series at the Red Deer Arena. Sgt. Bob Williams of city RCMP says recreation department officials met with police Monday to work out a plan to deal with any fights, on or off the ice, for the 7:30 contest between Richmond Sockeyes and Red Deer Rustlers.

However, he said he'd have a better idea this afternoon when general public advance tickets go on sale. The last two games in Red Deer between the Rustlers and Richmond Sockeyes have attracted about 1,650 people. In their Alberta championship series against St. Albert, home crowds hit the 2,290-capacity several times. Rustler general manager Graham Parsons has appealed to Red Deer fans to refrain from throwing debris onto the ice and at the Richmond players' bench.
