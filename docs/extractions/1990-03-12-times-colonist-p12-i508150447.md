TOP ROOKIE Petr Nedved, from Czechslovakia, dominated this category, a fact which National Hockey League scouts would probably agree with. He is considered a top prospect for this summer's draft and could go as high as No. 1. Kamloops continues to bring top talent into the league as evidenced by Scott Niedermayers' No. 2 ranking. Last season, the defenceman was playing bantam hockey in Cranbrook.

Len Barrie has made an impact in the Western Hockey League this season, dominating the scoring race. He has also recently signed a free-agent contract with the National Hockey League's Philadelphia Flyers. It is a season in which he has blossomed. Last year, he did not appear in the top 19 in this category. This year, he leads it.

This is the second annual Best of the West poll, with the focus on Western Hockey League West Division teams, conducted by Times-Colonist sports editor Dave Senick. It brings together the opinions of players, coaches, management and front-office personnel from the Kamloops Blazers, the Seattle Thunderbirds, the Tri-Cities Americans, the Spokane Chiefs, the Portland Winter Hawks and the Victoria Cougars. Each club was sent forms and asked to fill in their top four choices in the respective categories but not to vote for anyone from their organization. This was done to prevent 'ballot stuffing.' The poll is conducted to offer the views of those actually involved in the WHL on a day-to-day basis. Points were awarded on a descending basis on a 4-3-2-1 system for first to fourth-place votes. Some clubs submitted more forms than others and, in those cases, the point-totals were handicapped. That was done so the clubs would not offer more voting points for other players than its own players could receive. All West Division teams took part but the Kamloops Blazers had the most unique and probably most informative way of filling out the forms. Among the four forms they returned, there was a consensus form in which all the team's players got together on a bus trip to vote and decide upon their choices. It should also be noted that Travis Green received a number of votes even though he plays for the Medicine Hat Tigers. Those votes were cast before Green was traded from the Spokane Chiefs. There are a few other players, like Brian Sakic of the Americans who was traded from the East Division Swift Current Broncos, who may not have been seen enough by their counterparts to make a full impression at the time of voting.
