Hockey

Central skiers win title BESSEMER, Mich. Central High School's Maria Schilling won the girls slalom and tied for the overall title to lead the Red Raiders to the girls team championship Tuesday in the 22-team Rhinelander Invitational at Blackjack Ski Resort. Schilling was also third in the girls giant slalom as Central finished with 83 team points, 14 ahead of Ironwood, Mich. Logan was sixth with 310 points. Central's Roxanne Ostreng was fifth in the slalom and eighth in the giant slalom, and teammate Anne Linnert was sixth in the slalom and 10th in the giant slalom. Logan's Mike McCormick finished second in the boys slalom and sixth in the giant slalom to place second overall. Central's Jason Austin was sixth in the slalom and seventh in the giant slalom, Logan's Kyle Caspersen was eighth the slalom and Chris Lorenz of Central took 10th in giant slalom. Central finished third in the boys team standings with 160 points and Logan was fourth with 175. Ironwood won the team championship with 40 points.

Ski results in Names and Numbers Viroqua skaters hold off Flames The Viroqua High School hockey team scored three goals in the first period and held on to edge La Crosse, 5-4, in a Wisconsin-Minnesota league game at Green Island Tuesday night. Chad Knutson scored three goals to lead the Blackhawks, now 3-4-1 in the conference and 9-6-2 overall. Cory Candahl scored two goals for the Flames, 4-5 and 8-18.
