Sockeyes top Flyers in OT By The Canadian Press Mike McCormick proved to be a one-man wrecking crew for Richmond Sockeyes in the B.C. Junior Hockey League Saturday night. McCormick scored the final three goals of the game as the Sockeyes nipped the visiting Delta Flyers 8-7 in overtime. In other games, Kelowna Packers outlasted Vernon Lakers 11-9, Penticton Knights swamped Merritt Warriors 7-1 and Abbotsford Falcons downed Summerland Buckaroos 10-4. In Richmond, McCormick scored at 13:47 of the third period to bring his club to within a goal, tied the game at 18:40 and then fired the winner 1:59 into overtime. Dave Tomlinson also scored three goals for the Sockeyes but lacked his teammate's sense of drama. Trevor Dickie and Tony Bobbitt were the other Sockeye snipers. Doug McDonald also had' a three-goal performance for the Flyers as Delta got its other goals from Todd Hilditch, Mike Bafaro, Lance Momotani and Derry Menard. Goalie Chris Dickson made 50 saves for the losers while Dave Rawson blocked 39 for the Sockeyes. In Merritt, the Knights scored five unanswered third-period goals to beat the Warriors who outshot them 39-33. Ivan Moore led the Knights with two goals. Dowell Frantz, Brad Priest, Shawn Howard, Morris Karpuk and Dave. McColm scored the other Penticton goals. Kevin Horne counted for Merritt with 44 seconds left in the game. Kelowna got off to a fast start in Vernon, leading 3-0 by 3:24 of the first period.
