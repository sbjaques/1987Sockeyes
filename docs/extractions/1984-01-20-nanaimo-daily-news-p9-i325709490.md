## Paragraphs

Although he was on the losing side, Nanaimo Clippers' goaltender Ken Trousdell called Thursday night's B.C. Junior Hockey League all-star game in Richmond "an experience he'll never forget." Trousdell's Coastal Division club was beaten 8-6 by a Interior Division strong, played the first half of the contest, stopping 23 shots and giving up five "The game was pretty wide open and the interior team came at us hard and caught us off-guard," Trousdell said this morning. "Most of my saves were hard ones. I'm not disappointed that we lost because it was a lot of fun being here. All the players here are good ones and had hard shots.' He was joined by teammates Carey Coroy, Rod Jamieson, Darrel Pederson and Stan Czenczek. He said the Clipper players acquitted themselves well and put forward solid efforts. by Brett Hull, Dave Two performances Wensley and Brent Demerais paced the Interior Division to the victory. Hull plays for Penticton Knights and is the league's leading scorer, while Wensley plays for Vernon Lakers and Desmerais for the Merritt Centennials. The Interior led 3-2 at the end of the first period and jumped to a 5-2 lead early in the second period, but the Coast scored three unanswered goals midway through the second period to tie the game at 5-5 at the end of the second period. Randy Douville of Abbotsford Flyers scored two goals and added an assist for the Coastal Division. Singles were added by Dave Gourlie and Nell Reimer of Langley Eagles, Dave Robson of Cowichan Valley Capitals and Rick Dietrich of Burnaby Bluehawks.
