## Paragraphs

Juan De Fuca Whalers stopped their losing streak at six games Saturday with a 6-5 victory over Vernon Lakers in one of five B.C. Junior Hockey League games played. In the other games, Richmond crushed Abbotsford 15-2, Merritt defeated Summerland 7-4, and Penticton downed Kelowna 6-3. In Vernon, Randy Zulinick led the Juan de Fuca attack with a goal and three assists. Ron Bhala scored twice for the Whalers, while Ian Doak, Dal Gans and Rob Coultish added singles. Sean Hartnell, Frank Biller, Wes Gentles, Kevin Scott and Doug Spooner scored for Vernon. The Lakers, who had won their first four games before losing twice this weekend, trailed 2-1 at the end of the first and 5-3 after the second. In Summerland in a battle of winless clubs, the Merritt Warriors beat the Summerland Buckaroos 7-4. With the Buckaroos up 4-2 in the second period, Merritt scored four straight goals to turn the game around. Summerland got out to an early lead with goals by Mark Valley, David Balzer, Gord Paulsen and Tom Cross. Then Merritt goalie Pasquale Valana shut the the Buckaroos down for the rest of the game. The Buckaroos have now lost six straight games. In Penticton, Jackson Penney scored two goals and added two assists to lead the Penticton' Knights to a 6-3 win over the Kelowna Packers. The loss for Kelowna was its first of the season after winning seven straight. Penticton now has a re-. cord of 4-2. In Abbotsford, the Richmond Sockeyes pounded the Abbotsford Falcons 15-2. The Sockeyes took advantage of manpower differences to score six power-play goals and two shorthanded markers.

"We knew that they'd (Nanaimo) be tired from their game last night (Friday in Langley)," said Fricker, who along with assistant coach Gary Scott joined the team last week.
