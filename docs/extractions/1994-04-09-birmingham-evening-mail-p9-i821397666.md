AN uncertain outlook is forecast for a wind-blown weathercock which took flight from his perch on the spire of a Birmingham church. Canon Denis Claringbull, vicar of St Paul's in the Jewellery Quarter, is finding out whether it would be better to repair the wounded but historic old bird or replace him with a new rooster. The vicar, churchwarden Robert Ball and Beadle Alan Sandilands mounted a search of St Paul's Square after a passer-by

"The old cock, made of hollow copper, is in bits and is pretty badly dented and beaten up. Most of the gilt has gone from when it was taken down and refurbished only ten years ago." The weathercock has flown over St Paul's Square since the spire was added to the church 170 years ago and this long service could swing a decision on the future in his favour, ex• plained Canon Claringbull. "I do hope the experts think he is worth saving," he said.
