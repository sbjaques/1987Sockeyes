SWIFT CURRENT (CP) - The Swift Current Broncos had their highest one-game goal output of the year as they crushed the Brandon Wheat Kings 10-2 in Western Hockey League action Thursday. The Broncos were led by Joe Sakic and Peter Soberlak who both had four-point nights. Sakic scored his 48th, 49th and 50th goals of the year to become the first rookie to reach the 50-goal plateau this season. Soberlak had four assists. Others came from Tim Tisdale with two, Erin Ginnell, Clark Polglase, Ian Herbers, Todd Sceviour and Tracy Egelund. It was the first goal of the year for Poglase who has been with the team all season. Dale Marquette and Curtis Bateman replied for the Wheat Kings. The Broncos fired 48 shots at Brandon goalies George Manelukand Derek Babe, while Swift Current's Trevor Kruger stopped 22 of 24 drives. The game was originally scheduled for Jan. 2 but had to be delayed because of the Swift Current bus accident. It also marked the first time the Broncs have won back-to-back games since the accident. They defeated Saskatoon Blades 4-3 in overtime Tuesday.

The B.C. Hockey Association won't let former Pat Bryan Wells play with the Richmond Sockeyes of the B.C.. Junior Hockey League. Wells joined the Sockeyes Jan. 29 but didn't play. He was back home in Moosomin Wednesday after realizing that repeated attempts to allow him to play have failed. Wells was suspended Jan. 21 for the remainder of the WHL season because of a match penalty he received last Nov. 29 for cross-checking Moose Jaw Warriors defenceman Lyle Odelein in the mouth. Sockeyes head coach Orland Kurtenbach said yesterday that even though the WHL had permitted Wells to play in any other league, the BCHA has decided to abide by the suspension and so has the Canadian Amateur Hockey Association.
