Saint John The first-year Flames have sold about 2,300 season tickets and appear to be well on their way to being a success story in Saint John. The team will be relying on the goaltending of second-year pro Andrei Trefilov to carry them through some growing pains. He'll do just that. Trefilov had a 3.19 goals against average with Calgary's unsuccessful IHL affiliate in Salt Lake City last season then the Russian was named the top goaltender at the world championships. Veterans Len Esau and Peter Ahola lead the young defensive corps. Flames will be one of the toughest teams in the league. Mike Stevens can score, but he also had 240 penalty minutes in Binghamton last year. Veteran AHLer Dale Kushner also loves to crash around. Left winger Alex Nikolic racked up 133 penalty minutes in 33 games with Salt Lake last year. Offence will be led by former Cape Breton Oiler David Haas, Mark Freer, who played in the NHL with Ottawa last season, and former top picks Niklas Sunblad and Cory Stillman. Cape Breton Oilers Following is a brief rundown on each of the Atlantic's six teams. St. John's Maple Leafs finished first last season despite playing the last two months, of the regular season on the when an ugly labor

dispute kept them locked out of their own arena. Third-year coach Marc Crawford kept his players motivated and was rewarded with the AHL coach of the year honor. This year's edition of the Maple Leafs will emphasize speed and skill on its forward lines. This team will be able to score in bunches, but there are question marks about defence and goaltending. Forwards like Yanic Perreault (49 goals last year), Patrik Augusta, Ken McRae, Guy Larose, Chris Govedaris and new assistant coach Rich Chernomaz will make things difficult for opposing goaltenders. Also, Terry Chitaroni, Grant Marshall and Eric Lacroix will make sure the opposition keeps their heads up. Veteran bruiser Curtis Hunt leads the thin defensive corps. Chris Snell was signed as a free agent and he'll quarterback the power play. In Rochester last year, Snell I scored 71 points to lead all AHL defencemen. IHL veteran Bruce Racine is the goaltender with the graduation of both Damian Rhodes and Felix Potvin to Toronto. Moncton Hawks made some brilliant offseason signings to improve the team's offence, defence and toughness. John LeBlanc, who scored 48 goals last year, was re-signed to a two-year contract and he'll be the trigger man on the power play. Hawks also signed veterans Andy Brickley (51 points in 38 games) and Dave Tomlinson (84 points in 70 games with the Maple Leafs). Veteran enforcer Kevin McClelland is one of the toughest players in the league and the Hawks will need him in this rugged division. Returnee Ken Gernander and 27-year-old rookie John Fritsche, who played the last seven years in Europe, should also contribute offensively. Teenager Michal Grosek is considered one of Winnipeg's top prospects and he can also help a bit offensively. The defence is a good mix of prospects and veterans.
