Mark Kumpel says the Moncton Hawks "were tired of being embarrassed." "The fans have been booing us and giving us a hard time, but we deserved it," he explained. "We're professionals and we have to respect the fans. Afterall, they pay their bucks to get in to see the games. If they want to boo, they have that right. "For us, it means we have to work harder. If we're tired of being embarrassed, we've got to change the boos into cheers." Kumpel, a 27-year-old veteran of six pro seasons, received the loudest cheers from the 4,478 spectators at the Coliseum Saturday night. He scored two goals, including the winner with just 18 seconds remaining in overtime, to give the Hawks a dramatic 6-5 American Hockey League victory over the Sherbrooke Canadiens. The Hawks had trailed 5-1 late in the second period. The victory ended the Hawks four-game losing streak, including two lop-sided losses in a row at home. Tonight, the Hawks play host to rival Halifax Citadels at 7:30 p.m. The Hawks and Canadiens face-off again Tuesday night at the Coliseum. Following a practice Sunday morning, Kumpel said he could sense a "fresh attitude" among his teammates. "One win has meant a. lot to this team," Kumpel said. "We've regained our confidence and we're positive that we can beat any team in the league again. "We showed some character and we believed in ourselves. "It was a game that brought the whole team together," he continued. "One example was Matt Hervey. He had a rough time in the second period and he didn't see much action in the third, but he was the team's biggest cheerleader on the bench. Seeing the guys work hard for each other is what makes it fun. "We want to win for each other…for the guy next to you. Players like Ronnie Wilson, Paul Boutilier and Robin Bartel played so well. They force everybody else to play better, too. "Ronnie works so hard. Nobody wanted this win more than him. He's a great inspiration for the team.'
