HUMBOLDT, Sask. (CP) - Tony Bobbitt's goal at 1:04 of a 10-minute overtime period gave the Richmond Sockeyes a 4-3 win over the Humboldt Broncos in a Centennial Cup junior hockey playoff game Wednesday night. The victory gave the Sockeyes a 2-1 lead in the best-of-seven Tier 2 western final series. The fourth game will be played in Richmond Saturday. Dave Tomlinson, Mike Claringbull and Rob Clarke scored the other goals for the Sockeyes, who were outshot 39-23 and trailed 1-0 after the first period and 2-1 after the second. BillMcDougall had two goals and Curtis Chamberlin one for the Saskatchewan champion Broncos. Richmond, the British Columbia champions, took 13 of 25 minor penalties and the game's only major. NBA awards franchises NEW YORK (AP) - Charlotte, N.C., Minneapolis, Orlando, Fla., and Miami were awarded expansion franchises on Wednesday by the National Basketball Association board of governors after a four-hour meeting., Charlotte and Miami will be admitted for the 1988-89 season. Minneapolis and Orlando enter the following year. Each expansion franchise will pay an entry fee of $32.5million US. Bids from Toronto and Orange County, Calif., were rejected. The Toronto bid was spearheaded by businessman David Fingold. The other principals were Bill Ballard, son of Toronto Maple Leafs owner Harold Ballard; Dusty Cohl; Michael Cohl; Robert Cohl and former NBA great Wilt Chamberlain. The Toronto group had planned to use Maple Leaf Gardens as the team's home base.
