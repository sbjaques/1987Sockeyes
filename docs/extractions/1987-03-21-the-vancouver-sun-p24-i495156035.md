Sockeyes roll over Kelowna Canadian Press KELOWNA - Richmond Sockeyes jumped to a 4-0 lead in the opening period Friday night and relied on intensity and rugged play en route to a 7-5 B.C. Junior Hockey League playoff victory over Kelowna Packers. Richmond holds a comfortable 3-0 margin in the best-of-nine league championship series. Game four goes here tonight with game five back in Richmond Tuesday. Richmond Orland Kurtenbach said "the key to any hockey game is if you've got people who are intense and taking the body. That's what it takes to win.' His counterpart, Kelowna coach Bill Hogaboam, conceded his team was dominated. "They outbumped us, they were all over top of us ... and bang, it ended up 4-0 (after one period). We only played 40 minutes of hockey tonight. Richmond is disciplined to fall four goals behind.' Dean Rutledge, Dave Tomlinson and Jason Philips each scored twice for the Sockeyes while Mike McCormick added a single. Kelowna was led by Jason Ruff and Pat Mangold, each with two goals, while Jamie Kurki rounded out the scoring. Richmond goaltender Frank Romeo made 26 saves while Packers Dusty Imoo and Lucien Carignan combined to stop 26 shots.
