## Paragraphs

The Packers, who outshot the Blazers 55-47, also got goals from Jamie Kurki and Rick Viens. Tony Bobbitt, Richard Van Ry and Jeff Kentel were the Shuswap scorers. Saturday's close game was in marked contrast to the last meeting between the two teams, a 13-2 Kelowna victory last Sunday. Nanaimo's Mike Knapton scored the only goal of the third period 28 seconds into the frame and it stood up as the winner. Kent Lewis notched a pair for the Clippers, who trailed 3-2 after the first period before making it 5-5 in the second. Nanaimo had a 42-36 advantage in shots en route to its first victory in five games against Delta this season. Singles for the Clippers came from Sean Stotts, Mark Schwager and Corky Parkes. The Flyer goals were by Todd Decker, Lance Momotani, Richard Steniford, Rick Graham and Stan Marple. A second-period brawl resulted in the eviction of five Clippers and six Flyers, including Delta starting goalie Jason Palmer. Darrin Matias and Micah Aivazoff scored two goals each and Keith Fair added a single for the Capitals, who outshot the Falcons 38-23. Jeff Thachuk scored the lone Falcon goal. The Flyers outscored the Capitals 4- 1 in the second period to wrap up the victory in Sidney.
