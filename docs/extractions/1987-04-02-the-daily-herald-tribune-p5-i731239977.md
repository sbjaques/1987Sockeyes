Quesnel Millionaires have gone back to life in the gutter. By dumping Grande Prairie North Stars 4-1 in the best-of-seven championship series, the Mills moved into the aristocracy of the Peace Cariboo Junior Hockey League. They were considered the favorites after eliminating the four-time PCJHL champion Prince George Spruce Kings in the semifinals and proved their worth by soundly defeating the Stars in the final. But, now, the Millionaires have to go against an even bigger fish - the Richmond Sockeyes. Tomorrow night, the Millionaires begin a best-ofthree series in Richmond to determine the B.C. representative in the Centennial Cup playoffs. The Sockeyes went undefeated en route to claiming the B.C. Junior Hockey League championship. No PCJHL club has ever won a single game in these playoffs. Coupled with the fact the Sockeyes went undefeated in the BCJHL playoffs, the Millionaires have reverted to their traditional role of underdogs. *We have absolutely nothing to lose and everything to gain whereas they have everything to lose,' says Tom Marsh, the coach who engineered Quesnel's first ever PCJHL title as a Junior A club. Marsh watched Richmond wrap up the BCJHL title in the fifth and deciding game of its best of nine championship series with Kelowna Packers. The 26- year-old bench boss thinks he has figured out a way to reel in the Sockeyes.
