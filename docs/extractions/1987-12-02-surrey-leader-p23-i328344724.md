## Paragraphs

With nine games left prior to the Christmas break, Hopper's counting the days. "I wouldn't mind (a break)," he said. "Our guys are really tired, expecialy with the flu going around." Ben Biagioni, Aaron Nosky, Doug Macdonald and Shane Henry - a call up from the West Coast Junior Hockey League's Grandview Steelers - each scored once for the Flyers. Rob Clarke, Keith Fair, Steve Rutledge, Barry Cress, Tony Bobbitt and Brad Dickson notched singles for the Sockeyes. The victory was the first for the Sockeyes in four meetings against Delta this year. The Flyers defeated Richmond 7-3 on November, and scored 8-3 and 6-4 wins in their previous two meetings. The loss was only the fifth of the season for the Flyers, who have : 21 wins. The Sockeyes moved ahead on Nanaimo Clippers and into second spot with the triumph but are still 13 points behind the league leaders. Delta met Chilliwack Eagles on Tuesday (after The Leader press deadline) at Sungod Arena, and have a rematch with the Sockeyes in Richmond tonight (Wednesday). Delta returns home Saturday to host Nanaimo. Game time is 8 p.m.
