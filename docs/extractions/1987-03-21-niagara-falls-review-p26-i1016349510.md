McMillan, former president of Biltmore Hats Ltd. in Guelph, Ont., who was active in the Ontario Hockey Association, died Friday of cancer at his home in Guelph. He was 79. McMillan started in the stockroom at Biltmore in 1926. He served as president from 1956 until 1974 and was a director and major shareholder. From 1967 u until 1982 he was chairman of the board. Under his leadership company became the largest hat manufacturer in Canada, taking, over Buckley Hat Manufacturing, Brill Hat and Cap and, finally, its largest competitor, John B. Stetson Co. Canada The company also expanded into the United States and its products became known throughout North America. The company is now known as Biltmore-Stetson (Canada) Ltd. McMillan was also a hockey and baseball player and was a member of the Guelph Maple Leafs baseball team when it won the intercounty championship in 1928. Later he coached a hockey team at the Ontario Veterinary College at Guelph and was an 1 executive with Guelph Biltmore Madhatters when the team won the Memorial Cup in 1952. He leaves his wife, Dorothy, a daughter, Norma, a son, Michael, and six grandchildren.
