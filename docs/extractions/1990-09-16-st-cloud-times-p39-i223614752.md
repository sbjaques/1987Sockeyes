Dahl hopes to pair two junior league recruits together - center Brett Stewart and wing Fred Knipscheer. Stewart played for the Humboldt Broncos of the Saskatchewan Junior League last season, scoring 50 goals and 66 assists for 116 points. Knipscheer was the third-leading scorer in the United States Hockey League last season with the Omaha Lancers, andhe finished the season with 55 goals and 59 assists for 114 points. Other newcomers will be given a long look to pick up the productivity, including wings Brett Lievers (51 points at Wayzata High School last season), Tony Gruba (39 points, Hill-Murray) and Jeff Schmidt (60 points, Rochester Mayo). By MIKE KILLEEN Times Assistant Sports Editor The goal is to score more goals. That's the not-so-simple taskfacing the St. Cloud State hockey team, which opens practice Monday at National Hockey Center. While the Huskies prepare for their first season in the Western Collegiate Hockey Association - their first game is Oct. 12 against Minnesota at National Hockey Center - they also hope to improve an offense that was one-dimensional last season. The Huskies scored 161 goals in 38 games last season. If you plot that total against the eight WCHA teams, the Huskies ranked seventh in scoring - only Colorado College (152) and Michigan Tech (150) scored fewer goals. Worse yet, 68 of the 161 goals (42 percent) were scored by one line - center Jeff Saterdalen (24) and wings Tim Hanus (22) and Chris Scheid (22). "The area we need to improve upon is goal-scoring," St. Cloud State coach Craig Dahl said. "The players are aware of it, and they know we need to improve it.' But there's a flip side to all of this - the Huskies' defense. St. Cloud State allowed 152 goals last season. Again, if you plot that against WCHA schools from last season, that would have tied Minnesota-Duluth for fewest goals allowed. "If we can keep our shots-against and goals-against average down and pick upour offense, I think we could surprise a few people this season," Dahl said.
