SEATTLE - The state Department of Fisheries was scheduled to open Lake Washington south of the Evergreen Point floating bridge today for a sockeye salmon season. Anglers will be able to take two adult salmon, 20 inches or longer, per day. Counts at the Ballard locks indicated the sockeye run is at least at the pre-season forecast of 400,000 fish. About 165,000 sockeye have entered the lake as of today, which exceeds any count during the previous 16 years. Test fisheries in the Strait of Juan de Fuca also confirm a large run, the department said. - From local and wire reports
