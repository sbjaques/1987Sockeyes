By Mike Kiley Chicago Tribune BLOOMINGTON, Minn.-Minnesota, home of the North Stars and the site of the Blackhawks' second preseason game Tuesday night, is one of the National Hockey League's most intriguing contradictions. The Stars play in a state that has 2,255 registered amateur hockey teams, more than any other state. At last June's NHL draft, 17 picks coming out of high school or the junior-A level were from Minnesota, more than any other state produced in the draft. There were 146 high school hockey teams in Minnesota last year, more than 300 fewer than basketball teams. But fans generated $576,241 in hockey tournament admission compared to revenues of $388,793 for basketball, which exceeded football revenues. Minnesota people are sold on hockey, just not on the Stars. The team's average paid attendance of 8,000 a game last season was the worst in the NHL.
