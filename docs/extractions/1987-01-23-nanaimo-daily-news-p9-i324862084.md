## Paragraphs

Nanaimo Clippers' Darren Biggs and Mike Knapton each scored a goal as the Coastal Division came out on top of the annual B.C. Junior Hockey League all-star game Thursday y night in Merritt before a packed house of about 700. The Coastal team had to withstand a comeback by the Interior squad to secure the 5-4 victory. After jumping to a 4-0 lead after one period, the Coast saw its lead shrink to 5-3 after 40 minutes. Brad Smiley of Abbotsford Falcons, who scored one goal, was named the Coast's most valuable player. Goaltender Lucien Carignan of Kelowna Packers got the nod as the Interior's most valuable player, shutting out the Coastal snipers after he came on for the game's final 30 minutes. Other Coastal goals were scored by Tony Bobbitt of Richmond Sockeyes, Aaron of the Delta Flyers. The Interior scoring was provided by, Pat Mangold and Craig Bedard of Kelowna Packers and Darryl Kirkpatrick and Steve Manuel of the host Merritt Warriors. The Interior outshot the Coast 42-32. "I thought it was a very exciting hockey game," said Coast coach Orland Kurtenbach from Richmond. "We won the first and third periods, but both teams played very well.'
