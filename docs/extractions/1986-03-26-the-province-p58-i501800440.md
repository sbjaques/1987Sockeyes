Sockeyes live to play again Richmond Sockeyes rebounded from a couple of devastating weekend losses to defeat Penticton Knights 3-0 Monday behind the outstanding goaltending of Ray Woodley. Richmond's win narrowed the best-of-seven B.C. Junior Hockey League final series to 2-1 with last night's fourth game slated for the Richmond Arena. In the first two games, the defending champion Knights shocked Richmond 7-1 and 12-7 at Penticton. The 19-year-old Woodley made 37 saves in recording the shutout. The fifth game of the series will be played tonight in Penticton. The series winner will play Prince George Spruce Kings, winner of the Peace-Cariboo title.
