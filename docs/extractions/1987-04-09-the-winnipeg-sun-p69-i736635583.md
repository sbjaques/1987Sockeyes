By DARRON HARGREAVES Sun Staff Writer When the Selkirk Steelers face off against the Humboldt Broncos tonight, they'll be facing a team similar in many ways to the South Winnipeg Blues, who pushed the Steelers to seven games in the Manitoba Junior Hockey League final. The Broncos, says coach and general manager Bernie Lynch, "are a fast-skating team, a good passing team, small on the forward lines, but big on the blueline." The Blues didn't have size anywhere, though they were certainly fast and often artistic. Steeler coach Frank Porco also notes the physical makeup of the Broncos is the opposite of his club, which has the big forwards and a smaller defence. Like the Blues' trio of Craig Oster, Jerry Chumola and Todd Bohonos, the Broncos have one big line that does most of the damage. Look for left winger Mike Gaber, centre Bill McDougall and right winger Dave Shyiak to cause the Steelers a few problems.
