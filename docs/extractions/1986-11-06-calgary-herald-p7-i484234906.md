"I can think of hundreds of players who didn't have a sniff and I have two of these." * * Orland Kurtenach is back in hockey coaching at the Tier Two junior level, far from the pressure-packed NHL. The former NHL player and coach of Vancouver Canucks for two years is behind the bench of Richmond Sockeyes in the British Columbia Junior League. He finds the days hectic between his regular job and coaching fuzzy-cheeked teenagers. The man who became the first member of the NHL Canucks in 1970 - he captained and later coached the team wears three hats. His average working day is 8 a.m. until midnight. Now 50 and only five pounds over his playing weight, Kurtenbach is building his own general insurance business, developing a golf driving range and coaching his favorite game.
