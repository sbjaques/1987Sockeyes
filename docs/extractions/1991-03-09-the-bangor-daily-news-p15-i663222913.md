## Paragraphs

Boston University beat Providence 7-5 in the Hockey East semifinals at Boston Garden Friday night. BC had just regained a 4-3 lead with 2:17 left in the middle period on an Ed Ronan goal, but Amonte's goal really took the steam out of the Friars, who had rallied from 2-0 and 3-2 deficits to tie it 3-2. Peter Ahola took a drive from the point that sailed wide of Providence goalie Brad Mullahy. It deflected off the boards, came out the other side and Amonte jammed the puck in. Dave Tomlinson's power play goal at the 4:50 mark of the third period made it 6-3 and the Friars couldn't close to within two goals the rest of the way. Rob Gaudreau and Shaun Kane sandwiched Friar goals around one by BU's Chris McCann to close out the scoring. BU, now 24-10-2, will play the winner of the Maine-Northeastern game in Sunday's 5 p.m. final. Providence finished at 21-10- 2. BU had bolted to a 2-0 lead in the first period on goals by Ahola and Dave Tomlinson. Providence rallied to tie it in the second period on goals by Brian McCarthy and Gaudreau before Keith Tkachuk regained the lead for BU and Larry Rooney equalized for Providence. Ed Ronan regained the lead for • BU with 2:17 left in the middle period as he was alone in the slot, took a pass from Petteri Koskimaki and fired a 20-foot slap shot past Mullahy's blocker side. The Friars were still very much alive, but then came the Amonte goal.
