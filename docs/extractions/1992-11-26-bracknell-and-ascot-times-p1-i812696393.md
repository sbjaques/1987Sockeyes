Last week the row between sandwich seller Dave Tomlinson and Princess Square came to a head when police were called. Mr Tomlinson, who runs George's Sandwich Service, said the centre management asked him to leave the square when he tried to deliver last week. He claims this means he is being denied the right ! to compete fairly with stores i in square which sell sandwiches. But centre manager Valerie Nowroz says the management can exclude "hawkers" from the square. She said: "Any trading activity the within discretion Princess Square is at of the landlord. "Although not obliged to do so I have attempted to accommodate Mr Tomlinson as I felt he might provide a service. "The facility of delivering in the normal manner through the service entrance was offered to him but he declined this offer." She said she considered taking legal action as it was not the policy of the management to allow "unauthorised hawkers to trade in a manner which is contrary to the interests of tenants".
