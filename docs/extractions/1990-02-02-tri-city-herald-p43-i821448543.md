Wednesday night, Woolf was a minus-7, meaning he was on the ice for eight TriCity goals but only one Spokane tally. Kamloops, Lethbridge and Seattle continue to hold down the top three spots in the Canadian Hockey League poll released Thursday. The votes are cast by a panel of professional scouts. The Blazers have been the top-ranked team for the past 12 weeks. Daryl Sydor of Kamloops leads CHL defensemen in scoring this season with 70 points. Dean Holoien of Saskatoon ranked sixth, while Steve Jaques of the Tri-Cities was 10th to round out WHL representatives among the top 10 spots. Through Jan. 28, WHL scorers led the CHL in power-play goals with Kyle Reeves of the Tri-Cities in third place. Petr Nedved of Seattle sat atop the list with 25 goals, followed by Kamloops' Len Barrie, Reeves, Glen Goodall of Seattle and Kelly Ens from Lethbridge. Seattle's 5-3 victory over Kamloops last Wednesday in the Seattle Center Arena was the first weekday sellout in franchise history and the 15th of the season.

Victoria Coach Gary Cunningham didn't make good on his promise to guarantee a win Wednesday at home against the Spokane Chiefs. Instead the Cougars dropped a 5-3 decision and entered their name in the Western Hockey League record book for most consecutive losses with 26 straight defeats. Next in line for Victoria is the Canadian Hockey League record for successive flunking, held by the Kingston Canadians who lost 28 games to end the 1988 season. On Jan. 26. Victoria heard the same old song 17 times in the Seattle Center Arena when the Thunderbirds drubbed the toothless Cougars 17-3, as the T-birds theme song, Gary Glitter's Rock 'n Roll Part II, was played after each Seattle score.

Last year four Tri-City Americans were drafted by the National Hockey League: Stu Barnes, Olaf Kolzig, Greg Spenrath and Steve Jaques. According to the latest Central Scouting Bureau ratings, four more Americans probably will have their names called at the NHL draft this June in Vancouver, British Columbia. The CSB, which charts the process of prospects, projects Terran Sandwith, Brian Sakic, Bill Lindsay and Scott Levins will be drafted by NHL teams. Sandwith, a 6-foot-4 defenseman, is penciled in as the 15th player to be taken in the first round. Sakic, 18, named to the West All- -Star team, is ranked 82nd, which would be somewhere in the fourth round. Lindsay, a rookie, is rated No. 100 and in the fifth round. Levins, a 19-year-old rugged 6-3 winger from Spokane, was listed at No. 196. That would make him a 10th-round selection. Petr Nedved of Seattle is listed to be taken third overall, behind top-ranked Mike Ricci of Peterborough and No. 2-rated Owen Nolan of Cornwall, both in the Ontario Hockey League.
