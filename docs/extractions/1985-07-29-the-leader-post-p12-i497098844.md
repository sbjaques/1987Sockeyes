Mike Wong Shui of Ottawa earned a silver medal in the St. George's match rifle competition as the annual Bisley Shoot came to a close Sunday. Shui, who earlier won the Fletcher competition, scored 148 of a possible 150 total points, shooting at 300, 600 and 900 yards. The winner was Roger Mundy of Britain, who scored 149. Bill Baldwin of Calgary placed fourth with 147 points. John Bloomfield of Brit-. ain won the final major competition, the Queen's Prize, with a combined score of 290 of a possible 300. Baldwin was top Canadian with 285 for ninth place and Jim Bullock of Mississauga, Ont., the 1984 Grand Aggregate winer, placed 32nd with 281 points. The United States, won the Palma Match team competition, scor-' ing 3,403 of a possible 3,600 points over distances of 800, 900 and 1,000 yards. Britain finished second among the 10 teams with 3,377, while Australia was third with 3,358 and Canada fourth with 3,341 . . . Jason Talo scored the final three goals of the game to lift Vancouver to a 5-2 win over Vancouver Island in the major bantam final of the Mac's international summer hockey tournament at Delta, B.C. Vancouver also won the midget final, getting two goals and one assist from Lance Momotani in a 7-2 win over Saskatchewan. Andy Toljander of Duncan scored two goals, including the winner, as Vancouver Island defeated Saskatchewan 5-4 in the pee wee division. Winnipeg won the minor bantam division with an 8-3 victory over Vancouver . . . World champion Han Jian of China deposed of Morten Frot of Denmark as the No. 1 badminton player in the world by winning the men's singles title Sunday at the Indonesian Open. Han's 15-3, 15-3 victory over compatriot Xu Biao earned him 280 International Grand Prix points to take him to the top of the standings with 1,300 points. The 29-year-old all-England champion now is 55 points ahead of Frost, who skipped the Jakarta tournament. Cyclist Wayne Phillips of Richmond B.C., struck by a car during an endurance race, is in critical condition in an Amarillo, Tex., hospital with a spinal cord injury and a ruptured spleen, a hospital spokesman said. Phillips, 36, was hit by a car Thursday near Tucumcari, N.M., while he was competing in the Race Across America, a 5,000-kilometre race from Los Angeles to New York. The driver of the car that hit Phillips left the scene of the accident. Leonard Nitz of Sacramento, Calif., edged ahead of a pack of more than a 100 cyclists sprinting to the finish line Sunday to win an 80-kilometre Grand Prix cycling race in Toronto. Nitz completed the criterium in one hour 38 minutes and 51 seconds, narrowly defeating Bruce Donaghy of Allentown, Pa.
