Of his 15 wins this season, only four have been by decision. "It's kind of scary as a coach," said NDSU's Bucky Maughan. "You don't ever know if he's a better wrestler if he pins him so quick. It's a dangerous style of wrestling. But Mike is on a roll." McCormick said he doesn't need pins to win. He's prepared to rely on what calls a good leg shoot, which he has seldom used this season. He's prepared to endure a long match, even though the ripped hamstring that bulges out in his left leg is only 15 percent healthy. "It cramps up when I'm low on fluids," McCormick said. "I would like to have more stretch back there." Especially for an all day tournament like Sunday's NCC meet, which the Bison have won 12 of the last 13 years. A pin or two might come in handy. "On a good day, Mike McCormick will pin his way to nationals," Maughan said. "But on a bad day, he might not win a match." McCormick figures his bad days are over.
