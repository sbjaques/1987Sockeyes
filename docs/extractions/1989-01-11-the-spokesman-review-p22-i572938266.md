Spokane: G Jeff Ferguson (5.46), LW Darcy Loewen (14-26), C Travis Green (24-56), C Rob Friesen (20-46), D Milan Dragicevic (7-19), RW Mike Hawes (22-42). At a glance: These two teams are tied for fifth place in the West. Division and are evenly matched, but quite different. Tri-City has ranked at or near the bottom of the league all year in goals for, and likewise for Spokane in goals against. The first three games between the clubs produced just 17 goals - 10 by Spokane - and only Spokane has scored as many as four (twice). Some of the Americans' scoring woes likely will be solved if a rumored trade for Brandon star Troy Kennedy goes through. Tuesday was the deadline for most personnel moves involving active Western Hockey League players, and the Chiefs assigned goaltender Jeff Little to Tier Two Penticton, British Columbia. Barnes and Green are two of the top-rated prospects for the 1989 National Hockey League draft. Barnes has scored 16 points in the last four games, including an eight-point ourburst against Kamloops. "He's in a groove unbelievable, Tri-City coach John Olver said. The Americans' power play has often short-circuited, but Spokane can take no one lightly opponents have scored on 12 of 21 power plays the last three games. Ferguson, who was obtained in a trade with Lethbridge Friday, will make his first start in goal for the Chiefs. ALASKA PACIFIC AT WHITWORTH
