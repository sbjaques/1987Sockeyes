By The Canadian Press New Westminster Royals appear to be rolling over just about everyone in the B.C. Junior Hockey League. The Royals skinned the hometown Richmond Sockeyes 13-3 Sunday night in a one-sided game where they jumped to a 4-1 lead in the first period and an 8-3 margin in the second. "The boys played well, they came to play, said Royals coach Eddie 1 Johnstone, who noted the teams are now tied 3-3 in season play. In other action, Merritt beat Vernon 8-4 and Salmon Arm defeated Penticton 6-4. The Royals, who outshot Richmond 54-43, received two goal performances from Jeff McLean, Frankie Biller, Kevin Hoffman and Blaine Moore. Singles came form Lehner, Don Dwyer, Trevor Renkers, Perry Neufeld and Tony Bobbitt. For Richmond, Mark Kaufmann, Brad Dickson and Gunnar Hendrickson scored singles.
