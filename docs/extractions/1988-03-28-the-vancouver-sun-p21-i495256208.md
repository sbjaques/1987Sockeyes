## Paragraphs

Lakers romp to BCJHL title Canadian Press VERNON - The Vernon Lakers blew a four-goal lead but recovered in the late stages of the final period to defeat the Richmond Sockeyes 9-6 Saturday and win the B.C. Junior Hockey League championship. The Lakers took the best-of-seven series in five games. "The character came through on this team," said Vernon coach Ernie Gare. "A lot of teams would have folded after blowing a four-goal lead but we hung in there." The Lakers led 5-1 in the second period before the Sockeyes rallied, pulling into a 5-5 tie by 7:13 of the third. But the Lakers scored the next two goals - defenceman Phil Soukoroff getting the goal that stood up as the winner - and pulled away to the victory. Mike Barkley, Mike Paller and Jamie Steer each scored twice for the Lakers, who also got goals from Ian Alger and Trevor Pohl. Rick Rozecki and Keith Fair scored twice for Richmond, with Barry Kress and Rick Herbert adding singles. The Lakers now meet the winner of the Peace Cariboo Junior Hockey League championship series. Grande Prairie stopped Prince George 6-4 Sunday to take a 2-1 lead in the best-of-seven series.
