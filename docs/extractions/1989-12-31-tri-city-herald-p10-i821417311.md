Two Tri-City Americans selected in first round of NHL draft: Stu Barnes and Olaf Kolzig put the Tri-Cities on the world hockey map when the two Americans stars were selected in the first round of the National Hockey League entry draft. Both players were on hand June 17 when their selections were announced at the Met Center in Bloomington, Minn. Barnes, the Western Hockey League's most valuable player for 1988-89, was the fourth taken overall.. His high selection by the Winnipeg Jets came as no shock. However the Washington Capitals stunned the crowd when they took Kolzig. The 19-year-old made history as the first South African-born player ever taken in the NHL draft and just the fifth goaltender ever selected in the first round. The New York Rangers took forward Greg Spenrath in the eighth round, while Steve Jaques was taken in the 11th round by the Los Angeles Kings. 2. Kennewick beats No. 1 Richland: The Kennewick High football team won two of the most dramatic games in school history by beating Gonzaga Prep and Richland on back-to-back Saturdays during the postseason. The Lions edged previously un*beaten Gonzaga Prep 22-21 by stag"Ing a furious second-half rally. Kennewick, which trailed 21-0 at halftime, capped the comeback with a last-second field goal by Phil

Boal. One week later the Lions shocked top-ranked Richland 23-21. The game's big plays were an 82-yard touchdown run by Darral Moore and a safety by Kennewick defenders Ryan Rettig and Eric Orange, who tackled Richland quarterback Nate Holdren in the end zone. The victory avenged a 28-27 overtime loss to the Bombers during the regular season. The Lions' dream season ended in the semifinals of the state playoffs with a 43-19 loss to Kentwood. 3. Hanford girls win state soccer crown: The Hanford girls soccer team took the state by storm this fall, winning all 18 of its games to become Class AA-A state champion. Hanford became the first school from Eastern Wasington to win a state soccer title in either Class AAA or AA-A since girls prep soccer began in 1981. Led by sophomores Kelly Keve and Jenny Peterson, the Falcons outscored their opponents 107-5. Keve's swift offensive moves produced 32 goals, while Peterson's reflexes provided a goals-against average of about 0.25. The rest of the team frustrated opponents with a blanket defense and ball-control offense. In the state semifinals, Hanford beat Lindbergh 1-0 in a shootout Nov. 17 in Seattle. The next day, Julie Hunter scored a pair of Keveassisted goals as the Falcons blanked Mountlake Terrace 2-0 in the title game at Renton Stadium. 4. Tri-Citians embrace new hockey team: The Tri-City Americans captured the attention of the community and clinched a spot in the Western Hockey League playoffs on the 71st game of the 72-game regular season. The 6,000-seat Tri-Cities Coliseum featured 20 sellouts in 36 regular season games. Fans camped out overnight and waited to buy playoff tickets.
