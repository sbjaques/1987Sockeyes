The future The latest issue of The Hockey News features the top prospects of National Hockey League teams. The Washington Capitals were ranked No. 1 for the second time in the last three years. The Capitas were the only NHL team to be given an grade of A in the rankings of NHL organizations. Buffalo, the parent club of the defending Calder Cup champion Rochester Americans, were second with a B-plus. Edmonton, Colorado and Phoenix got B-ratings. Of the top 50 prospects rated by The Hockey News, the Capitals had five players among the top 20. Svejkovsky was the Caps' highestranked prospect at No. 6. Center Jan Bulis was 10th, followed by Witt and left wing Alexander Volchkov, ranked 11th and 12th, respectively. Baumgartner was ranked 19th overall. Bulis also plays for the Colts and had 37 goals through 57 games. In a listing of the top 10 prospects in each organization, the Capitals top 10 were: Svejkovsky, Volchkov, Witt, Baumgartner, Elomo, Bulis, Dwayne Hay (a left winger for Guelph in the OHL), Zednik, Church and Boileau.
