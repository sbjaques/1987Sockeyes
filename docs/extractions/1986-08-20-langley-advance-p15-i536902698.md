Educated at Edina High School in Minneapolis, MacPherson completed one year at the University of North Dakota. The Langley Eagles B.C. Junior A hockey club has released its 1986-87 g game schedule. The Coastal Division team will play 53 games this season under new coach and g.m. Murray (Muzz) MacPherson, who assumed the coaching reins last month from Larry Romanchych. The• team's season opener is on Thursday, Sept. •18th, against Merritt, followed by back-to-back games against Penticton and Summerland on Friday, Sept. 19th and Saturday, Sept. 20th. The last regular season game is set for Feb. 21st against the Abbotsford Falcons, formerly the Flyers. The Eagles, and all Coastal Division sides, are again expected to face stiff competition from the Interior Division Penticton Knights this season. George Evens, of the Evens Group, has been hired as a marketing, public relations representative for the Eagles. he will work alongside MacPherson, who formerly coached the Burnaby Bluehawks and Richmond Sockeyes. Born in London, MacPherson was influenced greatly as a youth by his father Stewart Miles, who was once A CBC radio hockey broadcaster and later a war correspondent and compere of the Royal Command Variety Show. MacPherson started playing hockey in 1956 as a goalie for the Winnipeg Rangers junior A team and later played for the Flin Flon Bombers and Fort Wayne, Indiana, Komets.
