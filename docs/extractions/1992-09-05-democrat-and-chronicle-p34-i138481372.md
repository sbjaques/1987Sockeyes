Munich massacre remembered Lessons in tolerance are needed to quell the rightist violence gripping Germany, an official said during a ceremony marking the 20th anniversary of the massacre at the Munich Olympics. The 11 Israelis killed by Arab terrorists were remembered by relatives, representatives of the Israeli Embassy and German officials. The ceremony was held in front of Connollystrasse 31, the building that housed the Israeli athletes during the Munich Olympics. During the commemoration, Deputy Mayor Christian Ude said vigilance still was necessary to "resist anti-Semitism and hate of foreigners." Great night for Danish boxers Jimmi and Johnny Bredahl of Denmark made pro boxing history as they won World Boxing Organization titles in Copenhagen, Denmark. The brothers, both European champions and undefeated, also became Denmark's first pro boxing champions in modern times. Johnny Bredahl scored a unanimous 12-round decision over WB0 junior bantamweight champ Jose Quirino of Mexico. It was his 15th consecutive win. Less than two hours later, his older brother Jimmi outpointed veteran Daniel Londas of France after 12 rounds for the WBO junior lightweight championship. It was the southpaw's 13th straight victory.
