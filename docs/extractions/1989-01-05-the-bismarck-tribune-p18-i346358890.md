NBA BASKETBALL BUCKS 110, CLIPPERS 102: Terry Cummings scored 27 points and the Milwaukee Bucks fought off a fourth-quarter rally to defeat the Los Angeles Clippers. Cummings scored eight straight points to cap a 15-2 third-quarter run by the Bucks that opened a 78-54 lead with 5:15 left in the period. The Bucks led 87-68 after three quarters before Los Angeles put together a 14-4 spurt and cut the the deficit to 91-82 with 7:50 left in the game. 5 LAKERS 133, TRAIL BLAZERS 120: Mychal Thompson scored 10 of his season-high 27 points in the third quarter and James Worthy had 25 as the Los Angeles Lakers remained the only unbeaten home team in the NBA with a 1 victory over the Portland Trail Blazers. The two-time defending league champions, who have lost their last seven road games, have won all 11 games at The Forum this season and 18 regular-season games in a row, dating back to a one-point defeat against Denver last March 25. PACERS 116, HAWKS 113: Wayman Tisdale scored 24 points, including eight in the finalperiod run that gave the Indiana the lead for good, as the Pacers snapped a nine-game losing streak with a victory over the Atlanta Hawks. WARRIORS 109, HEAT 100:Chris Mullin scored 26 points and the Golden State Warriors blew most of a 23-point lead before defeating the expansion Miami Heat. SUNS 106, CELTICS 104: Phoenix outscored Boston 10-3 in the last 2½ minutes and the Suns, led by Tom Chambers' 33 points, broke a nine-season Boston Garden losing streak with a victory over the Celtics. By snapping their n Garden streak, the Suns have won seven of their last eight games. Boston is 1-5 in its last six. I KNICKS 105, NETS 96: Trent Tucker's 3- point shot capped a 20-0 fourth-quarter spurt that boosted the New York Knicks over the New Jersey Nets. The Knicks, who overcame a 17-point deficit with the run, held the Nets scoreless for 7:04 in the spurt that was two points short of the club record set in 1974 against Philadelphia. The victory ended the Knicks' five-game road losing streak and was their first in the Brendan Byrne Arena after eight straight losses. BULLETS 109, HORNETS 86: Terry Catledge scored 21 points to lead Washington as the Bullets crushed the cold-shooting Charlotte Hornets. Charlotte missed its first six field goal attempts, shot 33 percent in the first quarter and made just 5-of19 attempts in the second period as the Hornets trailed 59-30 at the half. NHL HOCKEY RED WINGS 4, BRUINS 2: Gerard Gallant scored three goals and netminder Greg Stefan stopped 33 shots Wednesday night as the Detroit Red Wings snapped a four-game losing streak by beating the St. Louis Blues.
