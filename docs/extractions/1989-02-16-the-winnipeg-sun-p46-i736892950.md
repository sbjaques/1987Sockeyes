Flames punish hapless Jets

FLAMES 6 JETS 1

JET STREAM: Lemay, recalled from Moncton yesterday, suited up for the Jets and wore No. 34 . . . The three stars were 1. Theoren Fleury, 2. Gary Suter and 3. Joe Mullen Not dressed for the Flames last night were Jiri Hrdina, Perry Berezan, Rob Ramage (suspended) and Dave Reierson. Winnipeg sitters were Doug Smail (fractured orbital bone), Teppo Numminen and Daniel Berthiaume .. . Jets are now 0-3-1 versus the Flames this season and 17-36-10 lifetime

Minor Jet flashes of brilliance were overshadowed by extended periods of Flame dominance in this one, as Calgary opened up a 4-0 lead and led 6-1 after 40 minutes. And while the Flames - winning a club record eighth straight - deserve an A-plus for their performance, the Jets' would rate a big, fat "F." The Winnipegs have now gone eight games without a win (one tie, seven losses). "It was disappointing, but there's still a long way to go yet," offered a sullen Rick Bowness afterward. "The easy goals were the most disappointing - I hate beating ourselves. And when you turn the puck over like we did tonight, you end up beating yourself." The Flames, who outshot the Jets 16-6 in the first period, scored their first goal just 3:45 into the game. Jets' Gord Donnelly received a major and game misconduct for high-sticking Lanny McDonald and in the ensuing power play, Gary Suter's point blast found its way through traffic for a 1-0 lead. Jamie Macoun put Calgary ahead 2-zip 12 minutes later, overpowering Jets' goaltender Pokey Reddick from high in the slot. Russell native Theoren Fleury and Joe Mullen then fired consecutive goals 14 seconds apart early in the second period to drive the knife deep into the Jets' hearts. Dale Hawerchuk brought 13,221 fans to their feet midway through the middle frame when he beat Flames' Rick Wamsley, but any hopes of a possible comeback were crushed when the Flames' Colin Patterson and Hakan Loob red-lighted in the last two minutes of the second period.
