Lake (Alderwood Manor) is putting out 'bows to 14 inches now, but trollers using blades and worms are getting most of them, particilarly early and late in the day. Those without boats might try Echo Lake in the Maltby area, since Sarvey recently checked three anglers baitfishing from the bank at the access area with eight trout to 14 inches. Silver, Lost and Stickney have all been slow. Good early-season fishing for searun cutthroat on the lower Snohomish River has fallen off, the agent said. Some 21 anglers checked there last week had one 14-inch cutt and one 20- inch Dolly Varden. Rimrock Lake in upper Yakima County is currently a hot spot for kokanee. It's so good in fact, that the Game Department has raised the already. daily limit from 16 to 25 of the landlocked sockeye. Department biologist Jim Cummins estimates the reservoir's kokanee population at better than six million fish, and predicts that this summer's sport take will exceed last season's 250,000 fish. Jameson remains the top prospect in the Columbia Basin, although rainbow fishermen must go deeper now. A perch-pattern Hot Shot trolled behind leaded line has been the best producer for trout running to 2 or 3 pounds. Anglers on Potholes Reservoir are hitting largemouth bass, spawning in the sand dunes area; smallmouth along the rocky face of O'Sullivan Dam; walleye in the Crab Creek channel and in nearby Soda Lake; and perch in a number of spots. • . . Philadelphia 76ers general manager Pat Williams will leave one of the most successful teams in the NBA to join a Central Florida group with dreams of being that successful some day, the Orlando Sentinel reported.
