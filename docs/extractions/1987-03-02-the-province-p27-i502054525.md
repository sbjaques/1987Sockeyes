The B.C. Junior Hockey League playoffs

Canadian Press Nanaimo Clippers, Merritt Warriors and Penticton Knights all had something in common Saturday night. All three teams know what it is like to be drubbed 4-0 in their B.C. Junior Hockey League best-of-seven division semifinal series. In Nanaimo, the pain was that much sharper for the Clippers. The Richmond Sockeyes won the last game in the Coastal Division series by a 7-0 romp. Mike McCormick led Richmond with four goals while goalie Frank Romeo earned the shutout with 31 saves. The Sockeyes now wait for the winner of the Delta-Langley series, which continues tonight.

SPORTS SHORTS Huskies on way to final News Services WINNIPEG The Saskatchewan Huskies earned a spot in the CWUAA hockey final with a 5-1 victory yesterday over the Manitoba Bisons. Five players scored for Saskatchewan, which captured the best-of-three semifinal series 2-1, and goaltender Ross McKay kicked out 28 shots to preserve the win. Saskatchewan will face the University of Calgary Dinosaurs in the best-of-three conference final in Calgary next weekend. Mason perseveres NAIROBI, Kenya Carl Mason of England won the Kenya Open golf tournament in a three-hole, sudden-death playoff against countryman Roger Chapman. Mason and Chapman, along with Englishmen Gordon Brand and Martin Poxon, were tied at 275 after four rounds and remained tied after the first extra hole. Poxon and Brand were eliminated on the second playoff hole before Mason clinched the title when Chapman's tee shot went into long grass. The victory was worth $15,500 from a total purse of $100,000. Marathon record set NAGOYA, Japan - Carla Beurskens of The Netherlands, running into head winds and sleet, won the Nagoya International women's marathon in record time, beating Japan's Kazue Kojima by six minutes and 32 seconds. The 35-year-old Beurskens, winner of the 1986 Honolulu Marathon and -up in the 1986 Boston Marathon, took the lead after 16 kilometres and finished in 2:28.27, breaking the meet record of East German Katrin Doerre by 1:06. Rain dogs Pirates BRADENTON, Fla. Rain chased the Pittsburgh Pirates indoors for the second half of = practice at the Pirate City complex. Rain and heavy winds hit the Pirates midway through practice. The team battled the elements for almost an hour, then went under cover to finish the workout using two indoor batting cages.
