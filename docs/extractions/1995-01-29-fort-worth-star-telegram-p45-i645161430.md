He's the player personnel director, responsible for filling a roster. So far, Harrison's off-season work has been rewarded. The 21- 15-4 Fire is within sight of its first Central Hockey League playoff berth, thanks to a sevenpoint improvement from a year ago at this time. Nine new players recruited by Harrison have played major roles in the move up the standings. Last year, Harrison was hired as coach less than two months before the start of training camp, which didn't leave him much time to recruit. This past year, he had the entire off-season. "He deserves a lot of credit for putting that team together," Dallas Freeze coach Ron Flockhart said. "The guys are playing [hard] for him." One of those players is Scott Allen, who joined the Fire after playing for Utica, N.Y., in the Colonial Hockey League last season. Allen, a gritty center, was the type of player Harrison sought. Allen said he was swayed by Harrison's commitment to winning - plus the climate and enthusiasm among CHL fans. At Utica, he played before tiny crowds and endured long bus trips to Michigan and Ontario in frigid temperatures. Allen said Harrison "was eager to turn things around and produce a winning team. I knew about the work he was doing trying to build a winner. I felt they would have a winning team." BY BRIAN FISHMAN Special to the Star- Telegram FORT WORTH - Perhaps the single imperfection of the Fort Worth Fire's 8-4 victory against the Oklahoma City Blazers last night was the crowd response to Alex Kholomeyev's hat-trick goal. Only two of the 5,633 fans at the Fort Worth-Tarrant County Convention Center tossed a hat on the ice after Kholomeyev batted home a close-in rebound at 11:53 of the third period. "We've only had two hat tricks this season, so maybe the fans don't understand what they're supposed to do," kidded Fire coach Steve Harrison. It was that kind of night for Fort Worth (21-15-4), which converted twice in the opening two minutes, part of a five-goal first period onslaught. The victory was the Fire's ninth consecutive at home, one short of the Central Hockey League record. Chad Johnson scored twice and Mike McCormick, Francois Bourdeau and Troy Frederick one each for Fort Worth. Sean Rowe and Oklahoma City's Michel St. Jacques had four assists each. The Fire's early work in putting away the Blazers (20-15-6) was the result of an effective forecheck and some mediocre play by Oklahoma City goalie Alan Perry. Thirty-nine seconds into the game, McCormick accepted a pass from Rowe at the left-wing boards, skated into the Blazers' end and blasted a 35-footer past Perry's stick and inside the right post. And at 1:24, Jeff Massey stole the puck to the left of Perry and sent a pass in front to Frederick, who scored his 24th goal of the season. "That was the key for us, to set the tempo and play our game. We took the body early on and when we did that, we were in excellent shape," Harrison said. To get leads on potential players, Harrison relies on scouts and friends in and out of hockey circles. Most of his work was conducted over the phone. One contact stands out. Longtime friend Dan Saevig, alumni director at the University of Toledo and an expert on minorleague personnel, is in touch with Harrison regularly concerning player movement.
