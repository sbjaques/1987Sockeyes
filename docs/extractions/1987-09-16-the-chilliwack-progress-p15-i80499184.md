by Russ Ullyot Chilliwack Eagles rebounded from losing their first two exhibition games by posting three straight victories last week. The Eagles left their nest on the weekend, travelling to Richmond's Seafair Arena to take on the Richmond Sockeyes on Friday. They continued to travel, with games in Logan Lake on Saturday and Sunday with the Merritt Centennials. Chilliwack actually, began the week's play last Tuesday (Sept. 8) in Delta, where the Flyers handed the Eagles a 6-5 loss. Confusion was the key word in Richmond Friday night. The two teams showed up to play, but no officals were on hand to do the game. After waiting for more than 30 minutes, the two teams started the game using players from both squads as referees and linesmen. The Eagles squeaked out a 4-3 victory, with Kevin Arnold scoring the winning goal midway through the third period, snapping a 3-3 deadlock.
