THE PANTHERS: Excepting a 6-1 blowout in New Jersey on March 4, the Panthers are playing well. They have a twogame win streak and are 3-2-1 since ending that 2-5 two-week homestand. G John Vanbiesbrouck (three shutouts since Feb. 9) is on a four-game roll and will start. C Stu Barnes has a winning goal and the top assist of the season in his three games back from an eye injury. The Panthers are 2-0 against the Whalers, winning 2-1 on Jan. 28 and 4-3 on Feb. 11. In the first meeting, the Panthers gave up a team record-low 14 shots. D Randy Moller (sprained left knee) is out. ABOUT THE WHALERS: Hartford is 9-12-3 but playing much better. Chris Pronger has the potential to be one of the NHL's top young defensemen. C Darren Turcotte leads the Whalers in scoring with 20 points and is tied with LW Geoff Sanderson with 10 goals. G Sean Burke, back after missing two games because of a groin strain, is having a standout season. He's 8-8-2 with a 2.33 goalsagainst average. D Glen Wesley was picked u up from Boston in the offseason for three first-round picks. C Ted Drury (back strain) is day-to-day, RW Kelly Chase (back spasms) is day-to-day) andD Alexander Godynyuk, an ex-Panther, is out with a groin injury.
