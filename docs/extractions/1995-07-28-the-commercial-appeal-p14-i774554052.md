## Paragraphs

MARY DAGASTINO BOBBITT, 66, of Memphis, former day care worker for Colonial Park Methodist Church, died of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis Thursday at her daughter's home in Memphis. Mass will be said at 10 a.m. Saturday at Holy Rosary Catholic Church, where she was a communicant, with burial in Memorial Park. Memorial Park Funeral Home has charge. Mrs. Bobbitt, the widow of Charles D. Bobbitt, leaves a daughter, Teresa Xiques of Memphis; four sons, Tony Bobbitt of Hartsville, S.C., and Mike Bobbitt, Ernie Bobbitt and Kenneth Bobbitt, all of Memphis; two sisters, Christine Joyce and Irene Watkins, both of Memphis; three brothers, Domenick Dagastino and Ernest Dagastino, both of Memphis, and Joe D'Agostino of Huntsville, Ala., and six grandchildren. The family requests that any memorials be sent to Methodist Hospice. KEPLER BRAEUER, infant son of Harry and Bernadette Braeuer of Memphis, died Tuesday at Baptist Memorial Hospital East. Memorial Park Funeral Home has charge. He also leaves his grandparents, Margaret Ella Staincliffe and Charles Pious O'Donnell, both of England, and Harry Lee Braeuer and June Frances Weinheimer, both of Texas, and his great-grandparents, Mattie Roberts of Texas and Ella Morton of England. LEE THELMA BRENT, 66, of Covington, Tenn., homemaker, died Sunday of heart disease in Southaven. Services will be at 11 a.m. Saturday at Smith Grove Baptist Church in Covington, where she had been president of the Mother Board for 22 years, with burial in Townsend Cemetery there.
