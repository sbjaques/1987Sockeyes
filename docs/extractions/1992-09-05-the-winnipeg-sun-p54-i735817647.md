By ED WILLES Sun Staff Writer Jeremy Stevenson, the Winnipeg Jets thirdround pick in the National Hockey League's summer draft, sustained severed tendons in his right hand in an auto accident and will not be able to suit up until until December. Stevenson, a member of the OHL's Newmarket Royals, suffered the injury while returning home from the U.S. junior team tryout in Lake Placid, N.Y. two weeks ago. He was scheduled to attend the Jets training camp prior to the accident. "We expect him to fully recover," Royals general manager William Wise said. "I've talked to him twice and he seems to be in good spirits." Jets doctors will examine Stevenson in the near future.
