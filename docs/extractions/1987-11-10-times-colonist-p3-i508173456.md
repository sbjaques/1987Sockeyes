Like saying attempted murder should go unpunished because the thug couldn't shoot straight." The question has been asked many times - what is wrong with hockey? The answer is- relatively easy- the Don Cherrys of the game is what is wrong. The macho set, the Rambo/Rocky lovers. Fortunately the NHL saw it differently than Cherry as Dave Brown was handed a 15-game suspension for his cross-check attack on a New York player. It was the third such suspension handed out for hooligan behavior this season, but of course, the suspension was hardly strong enough. It should have been for the full season. Naturally, the hooliganism is not restricted to the NHL. All North American leagues are permeated by the goon set. The Western Canada Junior League, which spawned Brown, is guilty and the B.C. Junior hockey league Juan de Fuca Whalers and Richmond Sockeyes covered themselves in shame with a bench-clearing brawl here Saturday night, a brawl that saw no less than six players (?) pick up double-game misconduct penalties. Those six players should be suspended for the balance of the season, but the league, naturally, won't be any
