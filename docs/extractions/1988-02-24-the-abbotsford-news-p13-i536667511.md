"It feels like the season should just be starting the way the club has been playing the last few games." - Jim Frost. by Alan Watson Their B.C. Junior Hockey League season is over, and even though they thought they had finally climbed out of the Coastal Division cellar, that's where the Abbotsford Falcons finished the 1987-88 season. For Falcon coach Jim Frost, who guided the team during the final fourfifths of the season with Dave Cherniwchan, Saturday night's 6-5 seasonending g overtime loss to Richmond Sockeyes came too soon. Too soon for the coaches and the hard-working Falcons perhaps, who never quit, but not too soon for the rest of the league. Because the Falcons, who had a dismal 1-9-1 start, finished the season with four wins in their final five games. And in their final game, came as close as they could to knocking off the Sockeyes, defending Centennial Cup champions, who emerged on top of the pack in the Coastal Division. "Again, it goes back to the start of the season," noted Frost. "We got off to a bad start, with a young hockey team. It was experience over youth the last few months.' "Pride - and no pressure," said Cherniwchan, explaining the Falcons' rush at the end of the season, which propelled them to an 18-33-1 finish, an improvement of five wins over last season. "The guys were loose. They wanted to go out with some pride. And, it was a matter of who wanted their jobs back. "As far as I know, from talking to all the guys individually, they all want a crack at next year. Nineteen of our 20 guys are eligible, so we should have a pretty fair hockey team next year."
