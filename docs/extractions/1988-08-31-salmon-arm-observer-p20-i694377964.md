Sockeyes win medals at B.C.'s

The provincial championships mark the end of the season for the Sockeyes, who will now take the winter off before beginning training again in May. The team's coaches are confident that the excellent performance of this year's team is a hint of things to come. Several young swimmers will be moving into contention next year, and some older swimmers who did not qualify for the provincials this year are due for a good season. The Sockeyes placed ninth in the province, up from 17th in 1987 and 36th in 1986. The girls' team was sixth in team points and the boys were 13th. This finish ranks Salmon Arm as a major power in summer swimming, on par with larger clubs like Burnaby, Haney, Mission and the North Shore swim club.

Head coach Hamish Tucker was more than pleased with the results. "With our young swimmers, there's no reason why we can't be top five in the province within a few years," Tucker: stated. Salmon Arm is a very strong team in the younger age groups. Sockeye swimmers were prominent in the results from divisions one to three. As these swimmers grow older and more good young, swimmers join the club, Salmon Arm will become a dominant force. "It's only a matter of time before we begin winning the region and supplant Kamloops as the interior's strongest club," says Bob Tucker, assistant coach.

The Salmon Arm Sockeyes sent 31 swimmers to the provincial championships in Victoria on August 19, 20 and 21, and this team brought home the best Salmon Arm finish ever in the meet.
