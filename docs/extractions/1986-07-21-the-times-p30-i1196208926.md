## Paragraphs

A hockey strike? Please, say it's SO After taking our shot at pro football (upper left hand corner of page), let's do hockey now. I used to be a big hockey fan, used to live at the old Garden in the early 1960s, where the Rangers usually stunk up the place, but they usually beat up on folks. Guys like Vie Hadfield and Reg Fleming, Arnie Brown and Orland Kurtenbach. Boy, could they fight. Then I found out that wasn't the way you were supposed to play hockey. Then I learned the right way. the way the Russians do it, which usually proves embarassing to the NHL teams that chase their passes around the rink. All of which brings us to Alan Eagleson, leader of the NHL players' union. Today, hockey negotiators return to the bargaining table with Eagleson, "hopeful but not confident" of averting a September strike. You know what's coming next here, don't you? Go out on strike. Don't play your 80 games for a whole season at an average of only about $2,000 per game, plus per diem. All of you, go out on strike. See if I care, 'cause I won't.
