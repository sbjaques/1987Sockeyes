That in itself says a lot about a small community's efforts to bring the hockey world together for a brief five days. In the end, the University of Alberta Golden Bears will win the tournament. It won't be easy since the Czechoslovakian National under-18 team isn't any pushover, and the Bears are playing without several of their regulars because of injuries and a 24- year-old age limit. But, the fact remains the Bears are older and have a half dozen National Hockey League draft choices in their lineup. They have eight players 23 years of age and only their pickups from the Fort Saskatchewan Traders are under 20. Any they only play briefly. Red Deer and Camrose colleges are younger and less experienced, and the Europeans are younger yet. But don't blame the Bears. They were invited as a last minute replacement for the Richmond Sockeyes. Sockeyes dropped out after they were invited late in 1987 when the Soviets decided to send their National under-18 team. Still, this tournament would have been a real dogfight if the Bears and Arizona which had no business being here at all were not involved. In that event, the four teams in the playoffs would have been the Soviets and Kings on one side and Swedes and Czechs on the other. Four well-matched teams making for a great finish. However, the tournament has still been entertaining, even with some of the lopsided games. International matches have a habit of bringing that out if nothing else. Two of the more entertaining teams have been the Czechs and Soviets. But, you expect them to be. Both teams have been handpicked by their respective countries to build a base for future world junior and national teams. The Soviets are as an exciting team as you want to see. They are quick, skate as well as any team anywhere, and move the puck at high speed. And, you as a Red Deer hockey fan will get an opportunity to see them twice in the next eight days - Friday against the RDC Kings and Jan. 13 against the Red Deer Rustlers. I had the opportunity to see the Kings-Soviet game Sunday, and it was as good a match as there has been at this tournament. Kings lost 4-1, but it was 1-0 for the Russians after 40 minutes, and Kings' coach Al Ferchuk says his squad can beat them. "We need to refine a couple of things," he claims. But, it won't be easy. The Soviets have 16 players off their national under-17 team, which waltzed its way though Canada last year, and it's expected at least half of this team will graduate to the National junior team next year.
