## Paragraphs

Helen Claringbull refuses to allow bogus caller, Aron McMahon into her house. A bogus conman! FIFTEEN-year-und Helen Claringbull knew exactly what to do when a bogus caller came to her door. She kept the door securely on the chain and demanded to see the caller's identification before letting him into her home. If she had not, who knows what night have happened... In reality, Helen and the bogus caller, Aron McMahon, aged 14, are both pupils at Abbeylands School in Addlestone. Last Thursday, they were showing elderly visitors at the Eileen Tozer Day Centre, in Station Road, Addlestone, what to look for and what to do when a stranger knocks on the door. Pupils peformed series of little plays, which they had written themselves, designed to show old people how not to fall prey to door-to-door conmen. Supt. Ian Pedrick, of Addlestone police, said: "It is a sad fact of life that one of the most vulnerable sections of society is the elderly, who are so trusting of people who come to their door." He added: "People do take advantage of this very trusting section of society.'
