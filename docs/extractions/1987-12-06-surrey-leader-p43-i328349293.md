## Paragraphs

by Larry Pruner Suddenly, the Richmond Sockeyes are playing darn good hockey. And on Wednesday night in Richmond, they did it at the Delta Flyers' expense. The defending Centennial Cup champions broke open a 2-2 tie in the second period with five unanswered goals and breezed to a 7-2 thrashing of the Flyers in B.C. Junior Hockey League play. The loss dropped the Flyers' first-place record to 23-6 on the season, while the Sockeyes moved into second spot 11 points behind the coastal division leaders. "They beat us handily," conceded Flyers' general manager and coach Rick Hopper. "They have a whole new line-up. There's not going to be another easy game against the Richmond Sockeyes."
