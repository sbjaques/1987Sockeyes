IN HOCKEY, the Boston Bruins on Friday acquired right wing Darin Kimble from the expansion Tampa Bay Lightning for center Ken Hodge and defenseman Matt Hervey. J Kimble, 23, has played four seasons in the NHL since Quebec selected him 66th overall in the 1988 draft. He played two-plus seasons for the Nordiques before being traded Feb. 4. 1991, to St. Louis. Kimble. 6-foot-2, 205 pounds, had one goal with three assists in 46 games for the Blues last season. In 177 NHL games, Kimble has 12 goals with 15 assists and 747 penalty minutes. Hodge, 26. named to the NHL's all-rookie team following the 1990-91 season. He split last season between the Bruins and their AHL affiliate in Maine. Hodge, 6-foot-1, 200 pounds, had six goals and 11 assists in 42 NHL games and another six goals and 11 assists in 19 games for Maine after being sent down on Feb. 18. Hodge is the son of former Bruins great Ken Hodge Sr. Hervey, 26, spent one season with the Bruins after signing as a free agent in August 1991. He played in 16 games, with one assist and 55 penalty minutes. In 36 games for Maine, Hervey had a goal and seven assists with 47 penalty minutes. Hervey, 5-foot-11, 195 pounds, signed with Boston after spending the two previous seasons with AHL Moncton. He split 1988-89 be tween Moncton and Winnipeg, where be played two games with no points. IN BASEBALL, the city of San Francisco yesterday sued to block the proposed move of the Giants, claiming club owner Bob Lurie, the team and a Florida investment group violated stadium lease provisions. The suit was filed in San Francisco Superior Court. It seeks an injunction to halt any further action that would lead to approval of the proposed sale and move to St. Petersburg, Fla. In announcing the suit, City Attorney Louise Renne said it was based on a clause in the lease between the city and the Giants for the use of Candlestick Park. The clause states that no action can be taken by Lurie or the Giants while the lease is in effect. Renne said the lease is in full force and effect through at least Dec. 31 and that Lurie breached the lease when he opened negotiations for the sale with out-of-town buyers. "In our view, this provision precludes the Giants from negotiating, signing contracts or seeking the approval of major league baseball the sale and relocation of the team.' said. Lurie announced Aug. 7 that be signed an exclusive agreement to sell Giants for $111 million to a group plans to move the team to the Florida Suncoast Dome. The sale must be proved by baseball's owners.
