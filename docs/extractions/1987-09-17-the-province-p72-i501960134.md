## Paragraphs

Deft deal completed by Bruins Staff Reporter At this point, the New Westminster Bruins' latest trade looks like the perfect deal. The Western Hockey League club yesterday acquired defenceman Mark Kuntz from the Medicine Hat Tigers for Swedish rookie Patrick Backlund, a goaltender. Backlund was a standout for the Humboldt Broncos last season in the Saskatchewan Junior Hockey League but didn't see much action once the club advanced to interprovincial play, eventually losing the Centennial Cup final to Richmond Sockeyes. He was rated behind New Westminster veteran Ray Woodley, currently at the New York Rangers' training camp, and highly-touted rookie Dusty Imoo. So, for a player who didn't figure in their plans, the Bruins get defensive insurance. **We were a little bit concerned with (defenceman) Jayson More being kept in New York," explained New Westminster coach John Van Horlick of the Rangers' first-round draft.
