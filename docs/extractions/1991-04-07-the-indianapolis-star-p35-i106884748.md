The Indianapolis Star's "Old Pro," with some outside input and opinions, arrived at a deadlock between Sean Williams, the team's leading scorer, and Brian Noonan in the MVP race. Other annual awards go to Jim Johannson, Mike Dagenais and Warren Rychel. For the winners there is no material reward. There's but a pat on the back and an "attaboy." Williams went into the last three games of the regular season needing but three goals to pass Ron Handy as the franchise's leading goal-scorer. Handy, who owns the overall Indianapolis hockey franchise record of 55, and Brent Sapergia set the Ice mark of 43 three years ago. Williams' previous high as a pro was 32 goals with the former Saginaw franchise two seasons ago. He had 27 here last year. With three games left. Williams' totals were 41 goals, 48 assists for 89 points in 78 games. Noonan. who joined the club late this season from the parent Chicago Blackhawks, trailed Williams by two points heading into the final weekend of action. But Noonan was expected to watch as the Ice finished up. He suffered a separated shoulder at Muskegon a week ago and has been listed as "day to day" since In 57 games, Noonan was 36-51-87 and was threatening the franchise assist mark of 57, also owned by Handy. Noonan started the week leading the squad in plus/minus with a plus-35. Williams was third at plus-28, a point behind Mike Stapleton.
