This is a lucky ground for us. Macc's first after only seven minutes was an Easter gift, courtesy of Fisher's Jimmy Quinn. The midfildler's attempted back-leader to keeper Paul Bastock was intercepted in the area by Astey who cleverly robbed in from 12 yards. Four minutes later, Fisher's Paul Collins almost managed a spectacular won-goal. Trying to clear Dave Tomlinsons free-kick from the right edge of the box, the big defender's full-length diving header rebounded to safety off the post. Only Askey's goal separated the teams at half-time, although Macc could have had at least three more. Burr, put through by Askey, blasted over from a good position after 34 minutes, and soon after, he was too high again after Bastock could only parry Paul Kendall's powerful leader from Tomlinson's corner. And a minute later, with the home defence straded, Steve Hanlon's angler effort was turned round the post by Bastock. Hanlon and Kendall both went close after the break, but it was Burr, ironically following Fisher's best spell, who got No2 in the 62nd minute.
