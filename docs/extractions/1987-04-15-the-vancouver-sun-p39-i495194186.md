Sun News Services RED DEER, Alta. - Red Deer Rustlers beat the Richmond Sockeyes 7-5 in overtime Tuesday in a Centennial Cup playoff hockey game that saw no repeat of the unruly fan behavior that marred Sunday's match. The victory enabled the Rustlers to take a 3-2 lead in the best-of-seven tier two junior western semi-final series. The sixth game goes here tonight. "There wasn't a bit of trouble," said Sockeye coach Orland Kurtenbach. "The management asked the fans to refrain from throwing anything on the ice and there was no problem." Dixon Ward's goal 59 seconds into the 10-minute overtime period was the winner. Geoff Gosselin added an insurance marker at 9:50 into an empty net just after Sockeye Jason Phillips missed a penalty shot. The Sockyes were led by Brian Kozak, who had two goals, while Paul Rutherford, Mike Claringbull and Phillips contributed singles. The Rustlers held period leads of 3-2 and 4-3, fell behind 5-4 in the third period but managed to pull even at 13:16 to force the overtime. Richard Anderson and Brian Puhalsky each scored twice for Red Deer and Greg Pulliam added a single.

Mitchell is a hockey idealist. He played four years at University of Waterloo and then five years of Intermediate A. When pressed, he would get involved in fisticuffs because that is the way the game has been accepted. But when he thought about it, he concluded, "What the hell is fighting doing in hockey anyway?
