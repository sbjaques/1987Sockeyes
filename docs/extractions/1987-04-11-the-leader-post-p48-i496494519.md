SELKIRK, Man. (CP) - Humboldt Broncos scored three times in the third period Friday to score a 6-4 victory over Selkirk Steelers and take a 2-0 lead in the best-of-seven Centennial Cup western semi-final. The home ice advantage provided by the tiny Selkirk Arena ice surface proved meaningless in both games. "That was a big psyche-out," said Humboldt coach-general manager Bernie Lynch. "You take what you're given and you play hard." Selkirk, already missing five regulars, lost Kevin Mayo to a game misconduct in the first period and Jeff Trapp to an ankle injury.
