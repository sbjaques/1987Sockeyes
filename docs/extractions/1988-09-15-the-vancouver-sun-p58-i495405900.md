## Paragraphs

David McKay, 28, Coquitiam. Freestyle (68 kg). 5' 6", 149 pounds. McKay's career has spanned 10 years. In addition to competing, he is an assistant coach al the national training centre at Simon Fraser University. Results in 1988: 1st, Canadian championships; 3rd, U.S. championships with record of 6-1; 10th, 1987 World Championships. Medal prospects: Fair.

fair. Sewell's Landing, Horseshoe Bay, suggests trolling live herring or green or white hoochie at 15-35 feet for coho at Seal Rocks. Fisherman's Cove Esso reports Capilano River mouth slowing down for coho; a fast bite on low slack tide fishing on bottom outside the bay. Fisherman's says north arm of Fraser River (as seine opening for sockeye there Wednesday) best at 150 feet, trolling from outer marker directly into north arm. Hyack Marine, Gibsons, reports McCall Bank excellent for coho, a few sockeye and pink. Roberts Creek good for coho, well offshore in 100 feet of water. Castaway Tackle, North Vancouver, says Cap mouth fair for beachcasters with fly fishermen using sinking line doing best. Upper Howe Sound, closed to salmon, good for rock and ling cod says Valley Sporting Goods, Squamish. POWELL RIVER - Good mooching live herring for coho to 12 pounds, plus chinook 5-8 pounds at Harwood, Mystery Reef says Westview Fishing Charters.
