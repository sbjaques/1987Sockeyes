Dale Zeman, who played for the NCAA champions of Denver University and 1969, has been named assistant coach of the major junior Spokane Chiefs and head coach of the Junior B Spokane Braves. The decision to combine the jobs is part of a new, closer working relationship between the Chiefs and Braves. The Chiefs have agreed to provide the local-oriented Braves with up to five out-of-town players this season. Bob Strumm, general manager of the Chiefs, said his club might also add a part-time assistant coach sometime after training camp opens next Friday at the Spokane Coliseum. Zeman replaces Mike Kouwenhoven as assistant coach of the Chiefs, and Buddy Bodman as head coach of the Braves. Strumm said the time demands Kouwenhoven's schooling and Bodman's job made it impossible for either man to handle both coaching jobs. Zeman, 40, has been a youth hockey coach in the Denver area. Born and raised in Saskatoon, Saskatchewan, he played two years of major junior hockey in Saskatoon before joining Denver University in the days prior to the NCAA's ban on players with major junior, experience. Strumm said Zeman comes strongly recommended by former NHL coach and player Keith nuson (one of Zeman's fellow defensemen on Denver's NCAA champions in '68 and '69), ex-DU coach Murray Armstrong and current DU coach Ralph Backstrom.
