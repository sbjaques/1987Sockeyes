## Paragraphs

Herald Tribune Staff If you were going to build a successful junior hockey team how would you do it? Well, let's start from the ground up. You'd get an owner that r pumps lots of money into team to get top prospects. You'd hire a coaching staff that sincerely cares about the success of the team and has Tier I junior and NHL experience. And you'd get players. a couple of 50-goal scorers and the top point getter in the league would be nice. A big, fast, bruising defence and a checking line that gives most teams fits would do well. Oh, and you'd want all-star goaltending. You have just described the British Columbia Junior Hockey League champion Vernon Lakers. And it just so happens that the Lakers are in town this weekend to play 1 the Grande Prairie North Stars for the Mowatt Cup, emblematic of the British Columbia Tier I1 championship. "I think we have a pretty well-rounded club," says Lakers coach Ernie Gare, the older brother of former NHL'er Danny Gare. "We've played pretty well in the playoffs." And not bad in the regular season either. The Lakers ran their record to 42-10 after what Gare calls a slow start of 6-4. They have eliminated three teams thus far - the Merrit Centennials. Penticton Knights and Richmond Sockeyes - and have taken only 13 games to do it. Their playoff record is 12-1. "Richmond has a good team," says Gare of the 1987 Centennial Cup champions. "But we out-skated them and out-scored them. They couldn't quite keep up to us. Despite his team's success, Gare isn't taking the Stars lightly. "I think they have a real good skating club and certainly a club with character after coming back from a 3-1 deficit in games. With a -three on home ice we have to be careful. We're going to have to be ready.' Joining Gare in preparing the team is Eddie Johnstone, a retired NHL'er who played 13 years, and George Fargher, a teammate of Gare's on the last Vernon Lakers squad to win the British Columbia championship in 1971-72. The man behind the scenes is owner and general manager Mel Lis who helped build the team that has nine players guaranteed scholarships with United States colleges next year. "We have a pretty balanced team," says Gare, who kids that he is older than his brother but 'has more hair'. **There are five guys from last year. We have four 20 year olds, three 17 year olds and the rest 18 and 19 year olds.' Here's a look at some of the players that promise to give the North Stars a handful come 8 p.m. Friday night at the Johnny Macdonald.
