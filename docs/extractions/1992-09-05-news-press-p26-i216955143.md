SCORECARD Basketball Hakeem Olajuwon's agent has threatened legal action against the Houston Rockets unless his client is traded and the team retracts statements it made about him during an injury controversy, according to The Houston Post. Rockets general manager Steve Patterson confirmed Thursday he received the letter from agent Leonard Armato a couple of months ago. Olajuwon was out for five games this spring after the center said he could not play because of a left hamstring injury. After club medical officials evaluated Olajuwon, Rockets management concluded Olajuwon was physically able to play and suspended him for failing to render services under his contract. Hockey The Boston Bruins acquired right wing Darin Kimble from the expansion Tampa Bay Lightning for center Ken Hodge: and defenseman Matt Hervey. Kimble, 23, has played four seasons in the NHL with Quebec and St. Louis. The 6-foot-2, 205-pounder had a goal with three assists in 46 games for the Blues last season. Motorsports Mark Martin broke his own track qualifying record to earn the pole position for today's Gatorade 200 E Busch Grand National stock-car race. Martin, driving a Ford, turned a lap of 159.652 mph, eclipsing the mark of 169.068 he set on the 1.633-mile › Darlington (S.C.) Speedway oval in the spring. The NASCAR Winston Cup star was followed by three Grand National regulars, Todd Bodine at 158.586, Jeff Gordon at 158.153 and Jeff Burton at 157.904.
