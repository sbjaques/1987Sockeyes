Clippers 4 Sockeyes 7 By AL CAMERON Staff Writer When they want to be, Richmond Sockeyes can be a very effective team in the/B.C. Junior Hockey League. They showed that Friday night at Civic Arena in a 7-4 decision over Nanaimo Clippers before a season high The "Sockeyes, showing more depth than they have all season, controlled the game from the outset and perhaps sent a message to the league in the process. "We're definitely happy with the two points, said Sockeye coach Orland Kurtenbach. "We have four lines that we can go with now and we're pretty evenly balanced there'll be no more checking on (Mark) Kaufmann and (Jason) Jennings. If the other team wants to put guys on them, we'll come back with another scoring line." The Clippers had managed to keep the Sockeye snipers in check for the most part this season, but Kaufmann shrugged off the tough checking of Clippers' Mark Fabbro to trigger a goal and three assists while Jennings added a goal and one assist. "Some days you just play with more intensity," said Kaufmann. "Sometimes you get away from the checkers and sometimes you don't. It'll be close all season between us, but we have to win the rest of our games against Nanaimo." "Fabbro didn't do a bad job on Kaufmann," added Clipper assistant coach Ian Ramsay. "But when they get free, they're pretty key guys for their team and the rest of them are grinders and aggravators." Kurtenbach pointed to the addition of veterans Ron Bhala and Jeff Crossley as a key in the Sockeyes' improved play. Bhala cemented his reputation as the least popular player among Nanaimo fans by devastating Clipper rookie Brad Knight in a third period tussle. It was Knight's first fight in the regular season. "They'ved helped because we have such a young club : . . they give us stability," he said. "We've got a couple 17-year-olds on defence and a pack of them up front and that's the route we've decided to go." The Sockeye youth corps looked like veterans in the face of heavy pressure from the Clippers. When the Clippers were putting on pressure to get back in the game, the Sockeyes were able to get goals against the run of the play and take the steam out of Nanaimo's sails. "It was a funny game," said Ramsay. "At times we seemed to be on the verge of getting back in the game, then we break down somewhere and we're behind two goals again." Sascha Pogor had a pair of goals for the Sockeyes, who led 3-2 after one period and 6-3 after two. Jason Talo, Dan Walton and Garett MacDonald scored the other Richmond markers.
