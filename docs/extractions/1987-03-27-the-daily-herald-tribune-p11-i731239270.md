By MONTE STEWART Herald-Tribune staff QUESNEL, B.C. - What's good for the gander is good for the goose, right? After suffering back to back double-overtime losses to the Quesnel Millionaires last weekend, the Grande Prairie North Stars looked like they had their goose cooked only seconds before Colin Clements' goal gave them a 9-8 double-overtime triumph over the Millionaires here last night. The victory gives the Stars an opportunity to tie the best- of Peace-Cariboo Junior Hockey League championship series tonight. Quesnel presently leads 2-1. "It finally went our way," said Clements about his second goal of the night, which came on a breakaway at 11:05 of sudden-death. It was the third straight double-overtime game of the series. Quesnel won the first two. Both were played at Johnny Macdonal Arena. **(Quesnel goaltender Scooter Henson's) Legs were this far apart," said Clements spreading his arms widely.
