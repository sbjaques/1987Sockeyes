"Actually, I can see myelf being a coach down the road at the minor league level," Maurice said. "To tell you the truth, it's more nerve-racking than playing. - can see where ulcers develop.' "I think Paul's handled the transition very well," said Webster. "'He was very concerned about what it would be like going from player to coach but I think he's adapting to the change." Maurice was drafted by the Spitfires in the third round (34th overall) of the 1984 midget priority draft and became captain of the team in 1985. Noted as a disciplined, stay-at-home defenceman, Maurice contributed eight goals, 40 points and 261 penalty minutes during the 189 games. At the end of last season, the Spitfires made a commitment to invite Maurice back as an overage player, an offer the six-footthree, 192-pounder gladly accepted. The team was left with Maurice and Gorley as their two overagers but then Jablonski returned, meaning one of the three would have to be released or traded by the league deadline. Jablonski seemed to fill a void in goal that the Spits felt needed strengthening if they were to make a run at the Memorial Cup.
