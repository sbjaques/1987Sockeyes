Clippers 6, Whalers 4 By KEVIN MITCHELL Sports Editor Nanaimo Clippers' victory Tuesday night will cost head coach Bill Bestwick money, but he's not complaining. "I. told Ian (assistant coach Ramsay) I'd buy him and his girlfriend dinner anywhere he wants if we won tonight," said Bestwick, who viewed the Clippers' 6-4 triumph over Cowichan Valley Capitals from the press box. Ramsay was naturally all smiles as the Clippers snapped a fourgame B.C. Junior Hockey League losing streak with a strong team effort. "I had fun out there," said Ramsay. "It's just one game though and have to build on it.' Grant McDonald, who quietly goes out and gets a few points every game, netted three powerplay goals, including a rolling 65-footer into an empty net with eight seconds remaining to spark Nanaimo. "Grant does a hell of a job on the powerplay, said Ramsay. "'He picks up the loose pucks and takes a lot of punishment in front of the net. You don't notice him out there until you look at the scoresheet.' "Did McDonald get three goals? Gee, Id didn't even notice him, said Cowichan assistant coach Ian Doak, who felt his club ) simply blew a great chance to surpass the •fifth place Clippers. "We were tired," he said. "That was our fifth game in six nights and it showed. It's the same old story with these guys (Clippers). We had them down and out, but we can't put them away. They got the big goals when they needed them.' Nanaimo received a fine goaltending performance from, Corey Jones, who stopped 20 shots, including a pair of game-savers from 'gimme range' in the third period. "Jonesy came up big when we needed him and to win hockey games, you need that," said Ramsay. "We haven't been getting that lately." Cowichan netminder Jason Bilous was a crowd non-pleaser all night as he kept piling snow on his goalline after the linesmen kicked the snow away.
