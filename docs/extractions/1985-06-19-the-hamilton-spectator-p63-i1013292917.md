Now the Canucks have settled onWatt, providing they can convince the Toronto native and agent Alan Eagleson to sign a contract. Watt will inherit a troubled team from Neale, who joined the Canucks in 1978 and saw Vancouver make the playoffs every season until 1984-85. Vancouver finished 23 points behind fourth-place Los Angeles Kings and had the only losing record in the Smythe Division. The Canucks are getting old in goal with Richard Brodeur, are too young on defence to be effective and didn't have a 40-goal man on the forward lines. Neale, Bill LaForge, Roger Neilson, Orland Kurtenbach, Phil Maloney, Bill McCreary, Vic Stasiuk and Hal Laycoe have coached the Canucks during their first 15 years in the NHL. Watt will attempt to be successful where they have failed; no Vancouver coach has had a winning record, although Neilson came close at 51-61-21.
