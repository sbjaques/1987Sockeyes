Whistler's Rob Boyd, a potential catalyst for a rebirth of the Crazy Canucks. Boyd won the second men's World Cup downhill skiing event of his career, pacing a solid day for Canada at Val Gardena, Italy, on Dec. 12. Ontario's Brian Stemmle was third and Castlegar's Felix Belczyk 10th. Their rise, coupled with recent strong showings by the women's team, raises optimism for the rest of the World Cup series as well as for the Calgary Olympics. AND WINNING came to be a habit for the Orland Kurtenbach-coached Richmond Sockeyes last season. Behind the leadership of goaltender Frank Romeo and forward Jason Phillips, they went all the way to the Canadian junior A hockey championship and came home with the Centennial Cup.
