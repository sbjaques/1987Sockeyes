PAUL - Bob Bougher scored the winning goal in the 16th round of the shootout to lead the Cincinnati Cyclones to a 6-5 win over the Minnesota Moose in the International Hockey League. The Moose led 5-3 entering the final period before Cincinnati (30- 14-5) rallied with two goals by Paul Lawless to tie the score at the end of regulation. Minnesota (17-20-9) got goals from Yvon Corriveau, Dave Hakstol, Larry Olimb, Dan Currie - all in the first period - and John Young in the second. Minnesota goalie Tom Draper stopped 17 of 22 shots in regulation. Cincinnati's Frederic Chabot stopped seven of the 10 shots that he faced in the first period. Danny Lorenz replaced him and stopped 13 of 15 shots the rest of the way.

By Dale McCabe The Forum GRAND FORKS, N.D. - Goalie Taras Lendzyk made 37 saves Saturday night - 17 in the third period - leading Minnesota Duluth to a 6-3 Western Collegiate Hockey Association victory over North Dakota before 4,811 fans at Englestad Arena. With the victory, the Bulldogs completed a series sweep and avenged a pair of losses to UND earlier this season. "They deserved two wins this weekend," said Sioux coach Dean Blais. "They were opportunistic and we just didn't have much puck luck." UMD, which has gone 7-1-2 in last 10 games overall, also joined the hunt for home ice advantage in the WCHA playoffs for the first time this season. "We were down but never out of it," said Bulldogs' coach Mike Sertich, whose team remained in eighth place but climbed within two points of fifth. "We've been inching our way up, but it takes a sweep to gain any real ground." UMD (8-11-4), hadn't gained a WCHA road sweep in nearly six years while the Sioux (10-11-1), missed a chance to move up further into the first division. Instead, they're now in sixth place just one point ahead of Duluth. UND jumped out to a 2-0 first period lead on hard-working goals by wingers Keith Murphy and Brett Hryniuk.
