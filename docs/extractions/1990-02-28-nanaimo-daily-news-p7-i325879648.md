## Paragraphs

RICHMOND, B.C. (CP) - The Richmond Sockeyes of the B.C. Junior Hockey League are moving te Chilliwack, team owner Al Brew said Tuesday. Brew said the club will move at the end of this season because of a lack of fan support in this Vancouver suburb. The move still needs league approval, expected in June when league governors meet in Chilliwack, about 100 kilometres east of Vancouver. Chilliwack has not had a team in the league since the 1988-89 season, when the Eagles moved to Ladner. Brew said Richmond coach Orland Kurtenbach will become team president next season and assistant coach Mike 0'Brian will become head coach. The Sockeyes are fifth in the sixteam Coastal Division. Meanwhile, Mike Barrie scored at 2:15 of overtime to lift the Kelowna Spartans to a 7-6 win over the Vernon Lakers in a B.C. Junior Hockey League game Tuesday night. The win capped a comeback for the Spartans who trailed 6-2 after the second period. In other games, it was: New Westminster Royals 4, Richmond Sockeyes 3 and Powell River Paper Kings 4, Cowichan Valley Warriors 3 (OT). "I thought the pass was coming out front so I just hit it," Barrie said of his first BCJHL goal. The 15-year-old was playing only his second league game since being called up from a bantam squad.
