## Paragraphs

The club was close, so dangerously close, to becoming just another grim statistic. Why? It's easy to point fingers and claim mismanagement on behalf of the former owners. Perhaps they shouldn't have sold season tickets for the low price of two for : $129. Perhaps there were too many promotions that allowed fans into the building for simply buying this or that product. But that's all hindsight. The owners truly believed Red Deer fans would flock back to the Arena this season. Especially after last spring's Cinderella story that saw the Rustlers win the AJHL title and extend the powerful Richmond Sockeyes to seven games in the Western Centennial Cup semifinal. They truly believed that success on the ice would breed success at the box office. They were wrong. Five months into the season, the club was $30,000 in debt and the owners, staring at a projected overall loss of $60,000, had to make a move. Their proposal to slash honorariums to $25 per month, per player, was unanimously rejected. Feeling they had no choice in the matter, the directors announced the club had folded.
