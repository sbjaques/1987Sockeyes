The real name of the Centennial Cup, emblematic of Junior “A” hockey oe in
Canada, is the Manitoba Centennial Cup. It was presented to the Cana’ ian Amateur
Hockey Association in 1970 to celebrate the centennial of the Province.

It was first contested in the 1970-71 season after a major shake-up of the structure
of Junior hockey in Canada at the CAHA meeting in Newfoundland in 1970. The first win-
ners of the Centennial Cup were the Red Deer Rustlers who are one of only three clubs
to have won the coveted trophy more than once. The Rustlers who have made three club
appearances won their second Centennial Cup in 1980. The Rustlers were one win away
this year from making their 4th appearance in the National Championships when they
dropped a decisive 7th game, 9-1 to the Richmond Sockeyes, in the B.C.-Alberta finals.

The first final series ever played was somewhat of a rarity. An American team, Detroit
Junior Wings, represented Eastern Canada against Red Deer and eventually lost 4 games
to 2. Guelph, Ontario, under two names, the CMC’s and the Platers, have been in five
finals and also have two Centennial Cup Championships to their name.

The most successful team in Junior A history, by far, was the Prince Albert Raiders.
P.A. moved to the Western Hockey League in the 1982-83 season after defeating Guelph
in four straight games for the Centennial Cup. Among P.A. players who dominated Junior

A hockey at the turn of the decade were three members of Canada’s 1984 Olympic team,
Robin Bartel, Dave Tippet and James Patrick.

The Raiders are the only team to win back-to-back Cups (’81 and ’82
to be in three straight finals (1977-79). In five appearances the Raiders
under coach Terry Simpson, who led Canada to the Worl

Under this system all 4 teams play each other once in the round robin event. Thursday,

winner lays claim to the Centennial Cup.

The Humboldt Broncos won the right to host the Centennial Cup in May 1986 at the
annual CAHA meetings which were held in Montreal. The bid to host committee includ-
ed Bronco President Brian Thomson, Team Marketing Manager Brent Stebbings, Coach
Bernie Lynch, and local businessmen and professionals Dr. Gerald Rooney, Reg Wentz,
Clair Isert, Bob Watt and Glen Frehlich.

A full year of planning has gone into the 1987 Centennial Cup which all residents of
Humboldt and area feel will be the most successful Junior A Hockey tournament ever.

Current committee members of the Centennial Cup include Thomson (Chairman,
Finance), Stebbings (Promotions), Rooney and Wentz (Games), along with Aaron Lukan
(Travel and Accommodations), Lorne Johnson and Ray Gehlen (Banquets and Awards),
John Scott (Finance), Dr. Terry Henning (Health and Welfare), Gerry Ehalt (Trade Fair),
Doug Nasipayko, Dave Eberle, and lan MacDonnell.

In addition to committee members over 100 additional supporters of the Broncos and
the Centennial Cup have contributed to the events’ success in one capacity or another.

Guelph Platers (77-78) Penticton Knights (85-86)

will emerge as Centennial Champions for 1987. For 69 other Junior A teams across the

1987 CENTENNIAL CUP

Game 4, Monday, May 4 - Richmond Sockeyes vs Pembroke Lumber Kings

Yesterday all 4 teams saw action in Centennial Cup play. Last night the host Humboldt
Broncos became the first team to clinch a playoff spot in the tournament, with a 4-2 win
over the Pembroke Lumber Kings.

Earlier, in the afternoon, the Richmond Sockeyes, playing their first game, handed the
Dartmouth Fuel Kids their second consecutive loss (7-3). The Sockeyes were led to vic-
tory by winger Jason Phillips who notched a hat trick. Bryon Moller, Steve Jacques, Dave
Tomlinson, and Matt Hervey scored the other goals for Richmond.

The Fuel Kids got a two goal effort from Jerry Scott. Brian King added the other Dart-
mouth marker.

The Broncos in winning their second straight game, got single goals from four players

including Kevin Luke, Rob Rice, Curtis Chamberlin and Duncan Ryhorchuk. Mike Eastwood
supplied both of the Lumber Kings’ goals.

Game Star winners yesterday were as follows: Richmond Sockeyes - Jason Phillips,
Dartmouth Fuel Kids - Jerry Scott, Humboldt Broncos - Duncan Ryhorchuk, and Pem-
broke Lumber Kings - Mike Eastwood.

Tonight’s game between the Sockeyes and Lumber Kings will even everything up as
far as games played are concerned. All 4 teams will have played 2 games after this.

A Richmond victory tonight will ensure either a first or second place finish for both
the Humboldt Broncos and the Sockeyes. A Pembroke victory on the other hand would
still leave 3 teams in the hunt for first place.

Regardless of tonight’s outcome the Dartmouth Fuel Kids need a victory tomorrow
against Pembroke to avoid being eliminated from the Centennial Cup Tournament.
