By Larry Mahoney Of the NEWS Staff DURHAM, N.H. - Senior goalie Bruce Gillies, the latest in a long line of outstanding netminders to play at the University of New Hampshire, rejected 33 shots here Tuesday night to thwart the University of MaineOrono's Black Bears 4-2. Gillies was simply immense in the nets, sliding his 6-foot-3, 215-pound frame from post to post to ruin the Hockey East debut of the Black Bears. He protected a 3-2 lead late in the third period by coming out of his crease to rob Stan Czenczek, who was set up perfectly just 30 feet in front of Gillies. Gillies got his left pad on Czenczek's wrist shot. "I probably should have tried to go around him because I had the time to,' said Czenczek. A few minutes later, Bruce Hegland cleverly wheeled in front of the net only to have his seven-foot wrist shot, which was labeled for the far post, deflected wide by the glove of Gillies. "He was the first star tonight," said Maine Coach Shawn Walsh. "We would have won by three goals if it wasn't for him," said senior winger Ray Jacques. "We outplayed them and outshot them, but he (Gillies) was the difference," said senior winger Ron Hellen. Senior center Hegland also felt the Bears outplayed the Wildcats and predicted that "things will start going our way." Walsh was equally pleased with the performance of his Bears. "We walked into this arena to do a job, and I felt we did the job," said Walsh, who added that his team improved in 'several areas.
