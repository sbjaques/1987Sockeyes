## Paragraphs

Belle Buttons present skit at lodge in JC JUNCTION CITY - The Eagles Lodge No. 830, Junction City, honored a group of Herington Centennial members Saturday evening. A Centennial Belles group, the Belle Buttons, put on a skit. They sang their theme song and rang their bells, singing the "Kansas Waltz," composed by Teen Donahue. Those participating in the skit were Rosie Girton, Georgia Robison, Karen Donahue, Mary Ann Brown, Bobbi Ann Johnson, Beulah Vanderslice, Clyde Shamburg and Ross Girton. Bob Roberts, dressed as Uncle Sam, distributed centennial flyers and Harry Robison distributed song sheets. Acting mayor, Vance Donahue, presented a Herington Centennial Cup to the president of the Eagles Lodge, Clint Gfeller, and invited Junction Citians to attend the Centennial celebration. Others attending from Herington were Estelle Baker, Verna Warneke, Mr. and Mrs. Glen Fudge. Out-of-town people with the Herington group were Mr. and Mrs. Bill (Sybil Schlesener) Rock, of Milford; Estelle Baker's family, Mr. and Mrs. C.J. Baker of Salina, Mr. and Mrs. Bob Thompson and Barbara Muller of Wichita; Mr. and Mrs. Earl Higgins of Abilene; Mr. and Mrs. Herman McNeil, Mr. and Mrs. George Trow, all of Salina, Carroll Gates and Clyde Shamburg, both of Glasco.
