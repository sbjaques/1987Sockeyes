HOCKEY N.Y. Rangers sign Healy -From wire service reports Glenn Healy, the star of the New York Islanders' surprising playoff success last spring, signed a contact with the New York Rangers on Tuesday. Healy's contract was worth a reported $3.5 million over four years. New York Rangers general manager Neil Smith said he expects Corey Hirsch, 21, the team's goalie of the future, to remain with Binghamton of the AHL this season. Healy took a circuitous route from Long Island to Manhattan. After leading the Islanders to the Wales Conference finals, Healy was left unprotected in the expansion draft and was selected by Anaheim. The next day, though, Tampa Bay -took him in the second phase of the expansion draft. The Lightning traded Healy's rights to the Rangers for a third-round pick. Healy, 31, is entering his seventh NHL season. He played two seasons for Los Angeles before signing with the Islanders in 1989 as a free agent. Last year, Healy was 22-20-6 with a 3.30 goalsagainst average, then was sensational in the playoffs. He started all 18 games and posted a 3.19 average and an .887 save percentage. Dunham loses in net New Jersey Devils signee Mike Dunham was in goal for the U.S. national hockey team's 3-1 loss night to Sweden's Vastra Frolunda club at Gothenburg, Sweden. Dunham, of Endwell, made 32 saves for the U.S., which is 5-3 on its European tour. The team's next action is in the Epson Cup on Thursday and Friday at Dusseldorf, Germany. The U.S. tied Vastra Frolunda, 1-1, midway through the second period when Jim Storm of Milford, Mich., picked up the rebound of David Sacco's slapshot and sent it past goalie Mikael Sandberg. Vastra Frolunda broke the tie late in the second period. Sandberg stopped 31 shots.
