Fergie's smiling these days John Ferguson says the Winnipeg Jets have never had so much depth in their organization. In fact; the Jets general manager says this year's club will be forced to send several NHLcalibre players to the American Hockey League's Moncton Hawks. "We don't have room for everyone," Ferguson explained during a telephone interview earlier this week. "There are some talented players who are good enough for the NHL who must go to Moncton. That's going to make our team in Moncton at least 50 per cent better than it was last year. "It also strengthens our organization and makes us contenders in the Smythe Division (of the NHL)." When the Jets begin training camp at the Moncton Coliseum in less than two months, Ferguson n and his coaching staff headed by Dan Maloney will have to make some difficult decisions. Only superstar centre Dale Hawerchuk and goaltenders Daniel Berthiaume and Pokey Reddick appear to have job security. The rest of the jobs are ° open. Ferguson reviewed the team's depth chart. He says the Jets will have 11 "NHL" defencemen at training camp and the team will only keep seven or eight. "I doubt if we can keep eight defencemen," he noted. "I'd like to have all my players playing. It's no good keeping him in the press box in Winnipeg if he can be playing in Moncton. "I might keep one extra defenceman around in case of injury. Other than that, they should be in Moncton."' Randy Carlyle, Dave Ellett, Mario Marois, Peter Taglianetti, Frederic Olausson, Teppo Nummenin and Jim Kyte are almost certain to be rewarded with full-time jobs with the Jets. However, Olausson may be moved to forward, where he played steadily during the playoffs. Ferguson also wants to give 19-year-old Bryan Marchment, last year's first round draft pick, a chance.
