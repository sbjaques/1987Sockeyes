KEY MATCH: The opening 152- pound match featured Arrowhead junior Zach Le Poidevin and KM senior Mike McCormick - LePoidevin with a 23-3 record and McCormick 20-4 entering the match. LePoidevin had the first takedown but McCormick came back with two takedowns and led 5-3 after the first period. An escape and takedown by LePoidevin gave him a 6-5 advantage after two periods and a stalling point was the only scoring in the final period for a 7- 5 decision. REACTION: "That was a huge match for us," Arrowhead coach John Mesenbrink said. "But based on how he (LePoidevin) has competed and performed this year, he can.go with anybody in the state."

TOWN OF MERTON - Whitney Abene always wanted to play hockey but her mother would not give her the opportunity. Kathleen Abene had seen just how tough the sport is and how much of a beating players took. * Kathleen had a first hand perspective. Kathleen's brother. Bob Hunt, now the girls hockey coach at Arrowhead High School. played the sport throughout his youth and came home battered and bruised. But after years of watching Whitney, now an Arrowhead senior, get knocked around playing soccer, Kathleen softened her stance on hockey when Whitney was a sophomore. "My mom always thought that I would get hurt playing hockey," Abene said. "But after watching how much I was getting knocked around playing soccer, she finally let me try hockey." From having a uncle who was a hockey coach and a female cousin who also played hockey, Abene had the basic concepts of what the sport was about before she took to the ice. The hard part was learning how to skate. "Learning how to skate was not the easiest thing in the world to do," Abene said. "Halfway through my sophomore year I finally started to feel comfortable on the ice." Hunt helped Abene. "I would go out with Whitney and try to teach her how to skate," Hunt said. "She also took some skating clinics after her sophomore year."
