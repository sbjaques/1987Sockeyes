Other winners included: Dave Tomlinson, third place, geraniums; Drumm, third place, terrariums; Mark Cramer, third place, hospital arrangements; and Tina' Hynicker, fifth place, fruit/vegetable baskets. Dan Helwig, who teaches floral greenhouse landscape technology at Mount Joy, said about half of his students are from IU-13. IU students, he said, accounted for nearly half of the 17 awards garnered by his class. The horticulture course, he said, is a useful program for students who have difficulties in a standard academic track. "A disability isn't going to get in their way," he explained. "You can turn out a beautiful floral design and, if you have a calculator, you can figure out the price.... And there are enough jobs in the horticulture fields.'
