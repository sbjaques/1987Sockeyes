In researching the Kennewick development, Dixon secured more than 3,000 season ticket pledges - for either an existing or expansion Western Hockey League team- - at $20 per pledge. His company is now in the process of collecting 50 per cent of the balance owed for the season tickets, approximately $50-$70. "We actually had 3,600 people put down deposits and now we are trying to see how many of them are for real,' explained Dixon. "I don't know how we're doing yet because we've just started." Asked why he would go to all this trouble ifhe was planning to keep the Bruins in New Westminster, and what would happen to the money if another team moved into the Kennewick rink, Dixon replied: "The money is in trust." "It's too early to tell what will happen," he added. "Part of the reason I amin hockey is personal enjoyment. I don't really relish the idea of watching a team 300 miles away." JUNIOR NOTES - The Bruinsare in Kamloops Wednesday and return to Queens Park Friday to face onetime Bruin assistant coach Doug Sauter and the Regina Pats.. .. Richmond Sockeyes of the B.C. Junior League have lost leading scorer Mario Desjardins to Seattle of the WHL. "Mario was doing some work for our owner and he said he didn't want to work, he just wanted to play hockey," said Sockeye coach Orland Kurtenbach. "He leaves a hole in our team." Desjardins has had previous WHL stops in Seattle, Kamloops and New Westminster.
