Staff Writer If the story sounds a bit familiar, don't be surprised. For two periods, Nanaimo Clippers matched Richmond Sockeyes evenly. The locals hit, skated and scored as well as the Coastal Division regu ular season champions. ½ But, as was the case in games one and two in Richmond, the Sockeyes showed their experience and pulled out a 7-3 win over Nanaimo in game three of their B.C. Junior Hockey League best of seven semi-final series Friday at Civic Arena before a season high 1,415 fans. The win gives Richmond a 3-0 lead, with game four scheduled for tonight at Civic Arena at 8. The Clippers held a 3-2 lead going into the third period on the strength of a last-second goal by Corky Parkes. Richmond disputed the goal, resulting in captain Trevor Dickie's ejection from the game. The goal stood, and it seemed the marker would give Nanaimo the momentum together a good third period® together and finally beat the Sockeyes. However Rob Clarke's first goal of the series at 1:43 of the third tied the score with the Sockeyes enjoying a man advantage. A little over a minute later, Jason Phillips, with his second of the game, put the Sockeyes up for good, breaking in alone on Flaherty and deking him out to score into a wide open net. Dave Tomlinson, Mike McCormick and Dean Rutledge with an empty-netter broke the game open in the third. Sockeye coach Orland Kurtenbach says his team was able to withstand pressure of Parkes' late goal in the second. "I just told the guys that we had been having everything work against us," said Kurtenbach. "We had to keep our cool. Once we got the tying goal, we just started to roll. Clipper captain Sean Stotts says third-period letdowns are nothing new for Nanaimo. "It's been happening that way all year long," said Stotts. "We're just not coming out mentally prepared for the third period. We have to play the same way we do in the first two."
