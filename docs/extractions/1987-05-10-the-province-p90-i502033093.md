HUMBOLDT, Sask. - Named to the Centennial Cup hockey tournament all-star team, which was selected before semifinal play, were three Humboldt players, two from Richmond and one from Pembroke. Humboldt's Bill McDougall, who tied for the round-robin scoring lead with four goals and three assists in three games, was the choice at centre along with teammates Rob Rice, a burly defenceman who hails from Prince Edward Island, and right wing Duncan Ryhorchuk. Getting the nod from Richmond were left wing Jason Phillips, second in round-robin scoring with three goals and three, assists, and defenceman Matt Hervey. By FRANK LUBA Sports Reporter HUMBOLDT, Sask. - Since the Richmond Sockeyes and the Humboldt Broncos first began tangling, all the talk has been of how the two junior A hockey clubs are so evenly matched. Now, suddenly, they're both underdogs - against each other in the Centennial Cup Canadian championship. Confusing? You bet.
