## Paragraphs

There appears to be a healthy amount of mutual respect between the Richmond Sockeyes and the Humboldt Broncos. Richmond, the B.C. junior A hockey champions, held a 2-1 edge in the best-of-seven Abbott Cup Western Canadian championship with Saskatchewan's title-holding Humboldt as play resumed last night at Richmond Arena. Both squads are loaded with 20- -olds and their corresponding experience. But discipline and control are the key words as both teams advance to the Canadian championship Centennial Cup next weekend in Humboldt no matter what happens in this series. *Any team that wants to act like idiots is going to pay the price," said Humboldt coach Bernie Linch. He's expecting the Richmond portion of the series to be much like the Humboldt games: "lots of skating, lots of hard work." All that has been accompanied by close checking but play has not been as physical as the Sockeyes' Western semifinal with the rowdy Red Deer Rustlers.
