Everyone has an opinion regarding the players, coaches and even referees they watch 1 through a season. Some are considered good and others bad. st sports editor Dave Senick has put the opinions of Western Hockey League players and officials together, with the result Best of the West survey. Teams in the WHiL's West Division were sent survey sheets with the request that at least three per team be filled out by players and management. Some teams filed more, others less, but all six clubs within the division - Portland Winter Hawks, Victoria Cougars, Kamloops Blazers, Tri-Cities Americans, Seattle Thunderbirds and * Spokane Chiefs - did respond. Point totals were awarded on a descending basis. A first-place vote drew four points, second place got three points, third place two points and fourth place received one point. Since those replying to the surveys were asked not to vote for anyone within their organization, some team votes were handicapped with the 4-3-2-1 ratio in mind. That was done so that clubs returning more surveys would not be penalized by offering more voting points for opposition players than their own players could receive. The survey was to be conducted strictly among West Division teams and concern personnel from that division. However, the Brandon Wheat Kings, the Moose Jaw Warriors and the Swift Current Broncos of the East Division did obtain copies of the survey that were sent via the WHL's inter-club communication network. Moose Jaw and Swift Current voted on players from both divisions so those results were not part of the final total. However, Brandon voted strictly on West Division personnel so its survey was taken into account when calculating votes. Following are the categories and the complete results.
