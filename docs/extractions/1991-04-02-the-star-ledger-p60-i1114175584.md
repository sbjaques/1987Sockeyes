Saying it briefly From The Star-Ledger wire services HOCKEY: Wayne Gretzky won his ninth Art Ross Trophy, Ed Belfour captured his first William Jennings Trophy and the Chicago Blackhawks took their first President's Trophy as the National Hockey League's regular season came to a close. vyv BASKETBALL: The Chicago Bulls suspended reserve Stacey King for one game without pay for missing practice yesterday. He will sit out tonight's home game against the Orlando Magic. Because starters were exhausted after Sunday's 135-132 double overtime loss at Boston, they were excused from yesterday's regular practice, which followed a meeting about NBA policy on drugs and other matters. King, a forward-center who didn't play Sunday, left with the starters after the meeting, giving no reason for skipping practice. ... FOOTBALL: Minnesota Vikings kicker Donald Igwebuike went to trial alone on drug smuggling , charges yesterday after a second co-defendant in the case agreed to plead guilty to importing $1 million worth of heroin. Unlike Ibezim Ofedu, who pleaded guilty last month to conspiring with Igwebuike, Maduwuba Ibekwe will not be asked to testify for the government under a plea agreement that awaits court approval today. Meanwhile, jury selection began for Igwebuike, 31, who faces up to 120 years in prison and $6 million in fines if convicted of smuggling conspiracy, conspiracy to possess with intent to distribute and importation of heroin.. vu SOCCER: Soccer star Diego Maradona wants to return to his native Argentina from Italy to escape the furor over his testing positive for cocaine, his personal manager said yesterday. "He needs to relax, to have a change of scene," Marcos Franchi told a crowd of journalists gathered outside Maradona's villa in Naples, where he plays in Italy's professional soccer league. Maradona, who inspired Argentina's 1986 World Cup victory, had originally planned to leave Italy on Sunday with his wife Claudia and their two daughters, Franchi said. But they left without him, arriving in Buenos Aires yesterday. Although he remained in virtual seclusion at his villa yesterday, Maradona was visited by several of his Napoli teammates, many of whom earlier offered sympathy and support for their embattled captain. vyv MISCELLANEOUS: The Nevada Assembly yesterday gave final legislative approval to a bill that would require the NCAA to give due process to coaches and athletes. The lower house action yesterday sent the bill to Gov. Bob Miller for his signature two days after UNLV's 79-77 loss to Duke in the NCAA semifinals. There was little comment on the bill or Saturday's game as the Assembly concurred in Senate amendments to the bill. But after the vote, Assemblyman Gene Porter, D-Las Vegas, griped briefly about officiating at Saturday's game. Porter, one of several lawmakers who attended the game in Indianapolis, said that after the game he found his car had been towed by police.
