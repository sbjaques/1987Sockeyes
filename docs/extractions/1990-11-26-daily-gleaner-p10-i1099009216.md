Amateur Hockey Roundup Hockey Kats On Prowl

Winning the consolation title at the second annual Coca-Cola Radio One Challenge Cup tournament was small consolation to the Fredericton Boldon's Canadiens. Oh, they're not about to refuse the handsome piece of hardware that comes as the consolation prize in their eight team tournament, and truth be told, it might have been as much as they were expecting coming in against some pretty high calibre hockey clubs. But on further review, the Canadiens were in one of those 'so near and yet so far' situations. They finished the tournament with an overall record of 4-1, the same as the eventual champions from Halifax, and their only loss in the five day tournament that began Wednesday came at the hands of the McDonalds, who needed two third period goals to post a 7-5 victory. Canadiens missed qualifying for the championship half of the tournament by mere percentage points, the losers in a complicated tiebreaking formula that saw them beat the Gloucester County Kings 10-3, only to come up a goal short under the system that saw goals for plus goals against divided into goals for. Canadiens edged Saint John Pepsis 3-2 Saturday to get another crack at the Gloucester County Kings, who beat their Ontario namesakes, the Rangers, 6-2 in their consolation semifinal. Canadiens rolled to a 9-2 victory in the consolation contest. "I'm very happy with the way the guys played," said coach Danny Grant afterward. "If someone had said before the tournament that you're going to win four out of five games but you're not gonna be in the gold medal round, you would say 'hey, there's something wrong.' "But I think our team has improved at an unbelievable rate...I'd say we're two months ahead of schedule right now. We've developed some character and we're starting to learn how to win, to want to win and to come to play every game. We won four of five games and I think we played well in all five of them...we might just as easily be 5-0. But there are so many other good things that happened that I'm really excited about it." Fredericton Huskies captured the gold medal in their division at the Saint John Early Bird hockey tournament, going through the tourney with a perfect 5-0 record. They completed round robin play with a perfect 3-0 record based on a 4-0 victory over Sussex, a 2-1 win over Lancaster and a 4-1 win over Saint John. They eliminated Lancaster in cross over semifinals with a 3-2 victory and then rolled to an 8-1 win over Sussex in the final. Mike Bishop, Richie Clair, Craig Dixon, Ricky Potter, Adam Sprague, Steve Stewart, Denis Belliveau and Shawn Davis all had single goals for the winners while Ron Arthurs replied for Sussex in the final game. Chris Billings and Rob Messer shared the goaltending chores for the winners.

McDonald's emerged from the second annual Coca-Cola Radio One Challenge Cup 1 tournament with the Challenge Cup itself yesterday, taking advantage of an undisciplined Flyers team to record a come from behind 7-4 victory in the championship game of the eight team tournament yesterday. Flyers winger Troy Bassett drew a match penalty for spearing goaltender Rob McLean in a goalmouth scramble at 17:23 of the first period and while the McDonald's scored but once on the accompanying five minute power play, coach Rusty James suggested that one and another incident in which McLean was run in the second period provided the McDonald's with all the inspiration they needed against the Flyers. Riled Up Players "They had kind of run our goalie once and another guy came in and ran our goalie again...We could have retaliated, but our guys didn't...they were more determined to play the game and take advantage of it. It seemed to fire our guys up...it just made us more mad and more determined to beat these guys. We wanted to play and at times, I'm not sure they did...they tried to intimidate us and that just made our guys madder." All the anger translated into five second period goals as Troy Welsh and Jamie Fraser each punched home a pair of goals to go with singles off the sticks of Casey Walsh, Jeff Gray and Scott Snyder. Don Mills got goals from Mario Marchese, Matt McLean, Steve Potvin and Mike McCormick - this before, he too was asked to leave by referee John O'toole via a match penalty after spitting on goaltender McLean at 14:36 of the third period and with the proverbial fat lady at least warming up. "We definitely didn't play our game," said coach Randy Ward of the Flyers, although, judging from the way they weilded the lumber all tournament long, it didn't seem a dramatic departure from form. "There's no question we didn't come here to play hockey today." said Ward. "I think both teams are pretty equal and if we would have stayed on the ice and played hockey...we lost our composure. And let's give the other team credit...they played very well. They were determined to beat us today." Flyers "took a couple of careless penalties," said Ward, "but we just didn't play well. Our game is a real skating game and we've got a real offensive team. We can score...normally w'e average five and six goals a game, and today we only scored four." While Grant saw a lot of things he liked this weekend, he saw some things he didn't - or rather he didn't see enough of what he would have liked, in terms of fan support. Fan support was down even from the first version a year ago, when the Challenge Cup shared billing with both university teams and the Fredericton High School tournament that was scheduled the same weekend a year ago. Even as the only show in town this time, the Aitken Centre was bathed in bright, blinding orange much of the time. Canadiens drew 365 for their opening night victory against the Flyers and their numbers on succeeding nights didn't approach those. For his part though, Grant says the tournament will survive. "Financially it concerns me," he said. "I don't think there's any question we're going to lose money on this tournament and that's a concern. I understand that there's a lot of hockey in the city and only so many dollars to go around, but I would like people to realize that this is for the kids. It's a real necessity...the Maritimes and the Atlantic region has lacked a tournament such as this one for a long time and it has the potential to rank with all the top tournaments right across the country. All the teams that come here think it's one of the best they've ever been to and the scouts think it's just great that they get to look at the Atlantic kids... we've had 30 scouts here all week long. It's something that we want keep going and we will regardless. But it would really be nice for the local kids to get a little more support from the community."
