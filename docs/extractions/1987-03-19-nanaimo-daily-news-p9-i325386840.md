By The Canadian Press The race for second place in the Western Hockey League's East Division tightened up Wednesday as the Saskatoon Blades defeated the Prince Albert Raiders. In other games, Brandon downed Regina 8-7, Swift Current defeated Calgary 4-3 and Portland edged Spokane 6-5. Blades 5 Raiders 3 The Blades need only a tie Friday to clinch a spot in the second round of the playoffs. Darryl Daignault fired two third-period goals to snap a 3-3 tie and move Saskatoon within one point of the second-place Raiders. Jack Bowkus, Shaun Van Allen and Blaine Chrest also scored for the Blades. Steve Gotaas, Mike Modano and Wayde Bucsis replied for Prince Albert. "It was intense right from the start," said the Daignault, Blades who was picked up by as an over-age player in late January. Hawks 6 Chiefs 5 The Portland Winter Hawks scored two quick goals midway through the third period to beat the Spokane Chiefs. Trevor Pohl and Dennis Holland each had two goals for Portland, with singles from Jim Latos and Jamie Nicolls. Mike Rickert scored twice for Spokane while Mike Berger, Rob Friesen and Gregg Delcourt added singles. Portland held a 36-34 edge in shots over Spokane.

Clippers 1, Richmond 9 BY MICHAEL RHODE For The Daily Free Press His Nanaimo Midget A Clippers, had performed so well in Island playoffs and in their stirst three provincial final games, ran into a sizzling hot hockey team in day four of the five day provincial Midget AAA hockey championships Wednesday at Frank Crane Arena. Richmond at 3-0 Nanaimo 2- 0-1 were the only two teams who still had a chance of claiming the provincial title, so in effect, Wednesday night's game was the championship final. Richmond, Greater Vancouver champions, proved too much for the Island champions in coasting to 9-1 victory to clinch the B.C. title, and earn a spot inte B.C./Alberta series to decide the Pacific representative in the Air Canada Cup. In other games Wednesday, Coquitlam beat Kamloops 7-3 and Prince George beat Nelson by the same score. "I'm elated," said Richmond coach Wayne Tulloch. "If some one had said to me at the start of the year that we would win the B.C. championship probably would have laughed. But we have progressed through the year with hard work and determination. I'm really happy for the guys." It wasn't so much the final score that hurt. It was the fact that the game for all intents and purposes was over very early. It was just :12 into the game that Richmond took a 1-0 lead and 1:06 when the winner was scored. The combination of Brad Costa and Kelly Matsuo did heavy damage early as they were in on Richmond's first three goals. Costa made it 1-0 as he picked up a rebound in front of Clipper goalie Kevin Romando and tucked it in the left side, and just 54 seconds later Matsuo, the Richmond captain, got the winner as his point shot trickled in past Romando. Matsuo made it 3-0 at 5:14 with a shot past Clipper goaltending replacement Dan Mattice.
