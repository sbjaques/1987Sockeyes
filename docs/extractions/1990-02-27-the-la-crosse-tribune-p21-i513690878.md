D-3 La Crosse Tribune, Tuesday, February 27, 1990 Schilling wins ski title No. 3 for Central girls WAUSAU, Wis. - Central's Maria Schilling became only the second skier ever to win three straight Wisconsin State High School Skiing Championships girls titles Monday when she won the overall title in Monday's competition at Rib Mountain. Schilling, who joins former Central skier Chris Storlie as a three-time overall champion, earned six overall place points for the title by finishing first in the super giant and giant slalom events and fourth in the slalom. "The thing was that the slalom was the first thing and she did the worst in that and people were thinking it might be a jinx and she couldn't do it (come back to win the title)," said Central- Schilling Logan Coach Cindy Patza. "She came back with a vengeance and blew them away." Schilling finished the super giant slalom in a time of 24.25 seconds, and the giant slalom in 27.42. The Central girls team finished in a first-place tie with Brookfield Central-East, with both teams earning five team points. Wausau East finished third with nine points. The tie broke over a decade of dominance by the Red Raiders girls. Brookfield Central and East teams combined to form one team for the first time this year, and that helped Brookfield make such a strong showing, according to Patza. In the boys meet, defending overall champion Mike McCormick of Logan finished second to Ashland-Washburn-Bayfield's Brad Marcouillier, who collected six place points. McCormick, who finished third in both the super giant and giant slaloms, finished with 13 points. Brookfield Central-East won the boys team title with eight points. Central was fourth with 12, Logan was sixth with 19 and Onalaska was seventh with 22. Schilling, McCormick and Jason Austin of Central were named to the Wisconsin state team which will compete in the Eastern High School Championships in New Hampshire March 9 and 10. Waukesha, fouls end WWTC's year WAUKESHA, Wis. Foul trouble took its toll on the Western Wisconsin Technical College basketball team Monday night. The University of Wisconsin-Waukesha took advantage of the situation and defeated the Cavaliers, 95-84, in a Wisconsin Junior College Athletic Association tournament game. The loss eliminated the Cavaliers from the tournament. The Cavaliers were coming back from a 44-28 halftime deficit when the fouls started coming in bunches, according to WWTC Coach John Becker. "We didn't play well in the first half, but the kids regrouped, came back and played their hearts out," Becker said. "We even had the lead one time, with nobody in foul trouble. The next thing I know, four of our starters each have four fouls." But the fouls didn't hamper the play of Scott Indahl, who scored a game-high 38 points. Jerry Nelson added 18 for the Cavaliers, 6-18, while Greg Wieman netted 12. UW-Waukesha was led by Tom Szewezk's 31 points.
