By Ken Juba of the Star-Phoenix Humboldt 9 Selkirk 3 HUMBOLDT Next stop for Dave Shyiak is Northern Michigan University. But for the rest of this season -as the Humboldt Broncos take their run at a Centennial Cup championship - the 20-yearold native of Brandon can be found planted squarely in front of the opposition's net, stirring up trouble and slashing at rebounds. Shyiak steamrolled his way to three goals to lead the Broncos to a 9-3 win over Selkirk Steelers at the Uniplex on Sunday, giving Humboldt a 3-0 lead in the best-ofseven western semi "He's a very intense player," Broncos coach Bernie Lynch said of the NCAAbound Shyiak. "If all the players in Canada had half his heart, we'd never lose an international game. He'll play all game if you want him to." Shyiak, who scored 30 power-play goals during the regular season, continued to be the trigger man for Humboldt's extrastrength unit, scoring twice while the Broncos held the man advantage against Selkirk. The second-year Bronco opened the scoring on a power play, 6:05 into the game, banged in a rebound for his second power-play marker in the second period, and completed his hat trick at 5:27 of the third, converting a pass from Bill McDougall, before earning a game misconduct for a third-period fight. Humboldt and Selkirk each picked up three fighting majors and three game misconducts. The Broncos, who outshot Selkirk 49-17, took 14 of 26 minor penalties. The Broncos can wrap up the series tonight in Humboldt. Game time is 7:30 at the Uniplex. "Too many good players injured, too many tough series to get here, and not enough left," is how Steelers coach Frank Porco assessed his team's performance against Humboldt to date.
