For New Westminster, everything and everyone particularly Geldart - was going. The 20-year-old from Edmonton joined New Westminster just after the new year after deciding to leave the University of North Dakota and his scholarship. "I just wasn't happy with the way things were going, said Geldart, the eight-round draft pick of the Vancouver Canucks last season. He's happy now. Kevin Hoffman, Jeff McLean and Frank Diller added two goals each for New Westminster, which held a 35-29 edge in shots. Trevor Renkers and Tony Bobbitt scored once each. The teams continue the best-ofseven series for the Nat Bailey Cup tonight in Queen's Park. No one is expecting another walkover. "They're certainly not going to roll over and play dead," said New Westminster coach Dennis Pisiak of his opposition. "I expect them to come out with all guns blazing.'
