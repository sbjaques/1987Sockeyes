THE UNITED STATES BASKETBALL LEAGUE plans to set up a southern division with a team in Memphis, a former assistant coach with the United States Football League says. Ed Emory, a former USFL coach and former head football coach at East Carolina Universty, said at a news conference he has been named commissioner of the new division. The division will have six to eight teams, Emory said. The 2-year-old USBL has a summer playing season, and plans call for the southern division to be staging games by May 1987. Emory said the USBL is looking for financial backers for the southern teams. Memphis will be headquarters for the new division, he said. USBL teams may be set up also in Kentucky, North Carolina, South Carolina, Mississippi, Arkansas and Virginia, the league announced at the news conference. Emory was an assistant coach with the Memphis Showboats of the USFL until the league canceled play while preparing to start the 1986 season.
