By Larry Mahoney Of the NEWS Staff University of Maine-Orono Coach Shawn Walsh won't have to spend any time preparing a pregame speech for his team's Wednesday night (7:30) Hockey East showdown with visiting Northeastern University at the Alfond Arena. "He won't have to light a firecracker under our rear ends, admitted Bear captain and defenseman Rene Comeault. "We would like to really beat them badly on the scoreboard," agreed freshman defenseman Stan Czenczek who, like the rest of his teammates, has sour memories of Northeaster's 8-2 and 5-4 (overtime) wins over them in Boston on Nov. 16-17. The second game of that two-game series contained two brawls, a highly controversial no-goal call that went against Maine, which would have given the Bears a 5-3 lead with 3:24 remaining, and a dramatic comeback by the Huskies which wiped out a 4-1 deficit. The game's second brawl ensued shortly after O'Brien's goal when Husky cocaptain Jim Madigan taunted Walsh. who was crossing the ice to congratulate Northeastern Coach Fern Flaman. Maine tri-captain Ron Hellen, protecting his coach, cross-checked Madigan and fists began flying.
