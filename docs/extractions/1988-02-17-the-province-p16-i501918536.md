## Paragraphs

Staff Reporter Dale Sproule has discovered that threats can be costly. Sproule, owner, coach and general manager of the B.C. Junior Hockey League's Juan de Fuca Whalers, has been suspended from coaching until Nov. 30, 1988. He cannot be in an arena where his team is playing for an hour before the game and cannot enter the building until an hour after the game has concluded. The BCJHL. suspension stems from threats Sproule made against goaltender Willie Popp, who had been lent on an emergency basis by Richmond Sockeyes to the Chilliwack Eagles. Popp, the leading goaltender in the Coastal Division at the time, subsequently suffered a broken bone in his arm in the game between the Eagles and Whalers. A BCJHL. investigation followed, with results announced yesterday.
