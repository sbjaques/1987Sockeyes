Junior Hockey League final became a bestof -seven series this week when the Richmond Sockeyes and Vernon Lakers opted to scrap the longer format. "It was up to both teams and both teams agreed we would go seven," said Laker coach Ernie Gare from Vernon. "We decided we could decide a series in seven games. "I guess the idea behind a ninegame series was to generate more revenue, but it doesn't make sense to go back and forth and back and forth. It might have made more sense in the division finals where the travel is less." The Laker, 42-10-0, will entertain coach Orland Kurtenbach's Sockeyes, 34-16-2, in the first and second games Saturday and Sunday. The series switches to Richmond Arena next Tuesday and Wednesday.
