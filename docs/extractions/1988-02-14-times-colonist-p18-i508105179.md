Listless JdF fades slowly RICHMOND - The listless Juan de Fuca Whalers faded slowly and then fell apart in the third period Saturday night while dropping a 7-2 decision to the Richmond Sockeyes in B.C. Junior Hockey League action. "We don't seem to be functioning in all parts of the game right now," said Juan de Fuca vice-president Allan Sproule. "They're not working hard enough for this part of the season." The fourth straight loss leaves the Whalers with a 24-22-1 won-lost-tied record, holding down fourth place and the final playoff berth in the Coastal Division. "They're not firing on all cylinders," added Sproule. Barry Kress led the Richmond attack with four goals and one assist. Dwayne Wills added two tallies and Steve Rutlege added a single. The Sockeyes scored one power-play goal and two shorthanded markers. Mike McNamee and Charles Creyke countered for the Whalers. The score was tied 1-1 after the first period and Richmond took a 3-1 lead into the final frame of the fight-filled contest. Richmond led the e parade to the penalty box with 125 minutes while Victoria picked up 99. Five players on each team were assessed game misconducts. Rutlege picked up a double g game and was joined in the dressing room by Kress, Rich Herbert, Tony Bobbitt and Rob Clarke. Rick Cox, Kevin Robertson. Derek Johnstone, Paul Richie and Jason Gibson received the Juan de Fuca game misconducts. Juan de Fuca goaltender Tim Renton made 23 saves while Mike Doyle, an ex-Victoria Cougar netminder, blocked 24 shots for Richmond. The Whalers next take on the Delta Flyers at 1 p.m. today at the Juan de Fuca Recreation Centre in Victoria. VIRU teams
