Kurtenbach accepts Sockeye coach post VANCOUVER (CP) -- Orland Kurtenbach, former Vancouver Canuck player and head coach, is back in the hockey business. Kurtenbach will be the new director of hockey operations and head coach with Richmond Sockeyes of the B.C. Junior Hockey League. Kurtenbach finished his 11-year playing career with the Canucks in 1971 and coached the Canucks from 1976-78. Kurtenbach replaces Muzz Me. Pherson, who moves over to Langley Eagles, and Bill Wilms for new majority owner Bruce Taylor. Kurtenbach, who also has an insurance company, last coached Spring. field of the American League in the 1982-83 season.
