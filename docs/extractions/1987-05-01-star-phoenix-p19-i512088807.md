OTTAWA (CP) - With four finalists for the tier two Centennial Cup junior hockey final decided, the governing Canadian Amateur Hockey Association announced Thursday the playoff schedule for the roundrobin series in Humboldt. Starting Saturday, Atlantic champion Dartmouth, N.S., Fuel Kids open against the host Humboldt Broncos. On Sunday, Richmond, B.C., Sockeyes play Dartmouth, and Humboldt meets the Pembroke, Ont., Lumber Kings. The round-robin concludes with Pembroke against Richmond on Monday, Dartmouth playing Pembroke on Tuesday and Richmond meeting Humboldt on Wednesday. Based on the outcome of these games, the third- and second-placed teams meet in a semi-final on Thursday. The winner goes against the round-robin winner in the championship final on Saturday, May 9.

DARTMOUTH, N.S. (CP) It's been a good year for Richard Weanus, president of the Dartmouth Fuel Kids. His junior A hockey team has made it into the national Centennial Cup championships, scheduled to start Saturday in Humboldt. And the Fuel Kids are only $10,000 to $15,000 in debt. "There's a lot of relief now," Weanus said in an interview this week. "Last year, this time we were $30,000 in the red." Most people have no idea, Weanus said, of how much it costs to operate a junior A team in Canada. The Fuel Kids, who defeated Charlottetown Abbies last week to advance to the national championship, have operated in the past on annual budgets of $50,000 $55,000. "*Most of that money went to transportation, ice costs and equipment," Weanus said. *Those are the three big expenses.' This year. the team's budget soared to $80,000. "Part of that increase was due to a jump ice costs, travel costs and refereeing, Weanus said. "But it was also due to a recruiting cost - something we didn't have before this Christmas."
