## Paragraphs

Ken LeDrew, who had resigned as race manager at Queensbury Downs, became a driver at the racetrack. Bryan Maxwell quit as coach of the Western Hockey League's Medicine Hat Tigers to become an assistant coach with the National Hockey League's Los Angeles Kings. Glen Hawker took over as head coach of the WHL's Lethbridge Hurricanes. Prince Albert Raiders head coach Rick Wilson was offered the head-coaching position with the International Hockey League's Kalamazoo Wings, but he turned it down. Ex-Regina Pats Brad Hornung was interviewed for the first time since the March accident at the Agridome, which left him paralysed from the neck down. "Being alive makes me feel good," said Hornung. Later in the month, Hornung filed a lawsuit, seeking damages for the injuries he suffered when he was checked into the boards. Named in the suit were 13 parties. Due to health reasons, Jackie Parker resigned as head coach of the Canadian Football League's Edmonton Eskimos and was replaced by Joe Faragalli. Calgary Stampeders wide receiver Larry Willis was fined $2,500 for pushing an official. Bo Jackson said he would play for the National Football League's Los Angeles Raiders after his major league baseball season with the Kansas City Royals was over. Bill Bell of the Regina Red Sox was indefinitely suspended after he shoved umpire Kent Oliver during a Saskatchewan Major Baseball League game at Currie Field.

Kelvington native/ex-pro Barry Melrose was selected to succeed Bryan Maxwell as head coach of the WHL's Medicine Hat Tigers. Regina was awarded two Canada Cup hockey games to take place in September. Murray Cook quit as general manager of the Montreal Expos, citing personal and family reasons. Canadian Doug Frobel was called up by the Cleveland Indians from the minors but was later dropped. Paul Molitor of the Milwaukee Brewers had his batting streak stopped at 39 games. The Western Canada Summer Games were held in Regina with more than 2,400 athletes, coaches, managers and officials taking part. B.C. won the points standings with 93, Alberta had 76, Manitoba 35, Saskatchewan 31 and the Yukon 2. Total attendance was 28,150 for some 30 sports. The Canadian Football League's Calgary Stampeders purged front office by firing head coach Bob Vespaziani, general manager Earl Lunsford and personnel director Bud Riley. Lary Kuharich, an assistant coach, was hired as head coach and director of football operations, while club president Vern Siemens took over as the club's general manager. Saskatchewan Roughriders assistant coach John Hufnagel, whose playing rights were acquired from the Winnipeg Blue Bombers, was activated to the team's playing roster as a quarterback. Not long after, he suffered an Achilles tendon injury which ended his season and possibly his playing career.

The Edmonton Oilers won the National Hockey League's Stanley Cup beating the Philadelphia Flyers 4-3 in the best-of-seven series. Ron Hextall, the Flyers rookie goaltender, became fourth player on a losing team to win the Conn Smythe Trophy as the most valuable player in the playoffs. The Medicine Hat Tigers defeated the Oshawa Generals 6-2 to win their first Memorial Cup. The provincial curling scene dominated much of the sports news in February. With many name rinks already knocked out of the playdowns, Lemberg's Gardiner defeated Weyburn's Mark Lane 8-5 in the men's provincial final. the Saskatchewan women's championship, Regina's Kathy Fahlman recorded an 8-3 win over Regina's Susan Lang. The biggest news in hockey came from the Rendez-1 '87 series in Quebec City. The grand celebration was capped off by a twogame series between an NHL all-star team and the Soviet national team. The NHLers won the first game 4-3. The Russians won the second matchup 5-3. The city of Regina was swept up in the hype and hoopla of the Willie de Wit-Bert Cooper heavyweight fight. With CBS broadcasting the fight, and a capacity crowd at the Agridome, Cooper knocked down the Grande Prairie fighter four times in two rounds to earn a TKO. The Saskatchewan Roughriders picked up Winnipeg Blue Bombers' John Hufnagel as an assistant coach. The Riders also added Walter Bender, Tracey Mack, Hufnagel's playing rights and Donovan Rose in the CFL's equalization draft. On the downside, the Riders lost placekicker Dave Ridgway to the Edmonton Eskimos as part of a deal concocted by the Jack Gotta-Bill Quinter regime. That same group also traded the same second-round draft pick twice, which cost the Riders two third-round picks to the Toronto Argonauts as compensation.
