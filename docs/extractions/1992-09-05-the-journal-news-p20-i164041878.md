Saturday, September 5, 1992 IN THE NEWS Rangers ink deals with four The Rangers signed defensemen Joby HOCKEY Messier and Brad Tiley and left wings Craig Duncanson and Dave Thomlinson yesterday. Messier, a distant cousin of Rangers captain Mark Messier, played four seasons at Michigan State. Selected in the seventh round of the 1989 draft, the 22-year-old had 21 goals and 48 assists in 171 games for Spartans. He had 13 goals and 15 assists in 41 games last season. Tiley, Duncanson and Thomlinson were free agents. SABRES SIGN HAWERCHUK: Buffalo Sabres center Dale Hawerchuk signed a multi-year contract that will pay him $1.2 million per season. The length of the contract was not released. BRUINS ACQUIRE KIMBLE: The Boston Bruins acquired right wing Darin Kimble from the expansion Tampa 1 Bay Lightning for center 1 Ken Hodge : and defenseman Matt Hervey. Kimble, 23, has played four seasons in the NHL with Quebec and St. Louis. The 6-foot-2, 205-pounder had a goal with three assists in 46 games for the Blues last season. In 177 NHL games, Kimble has 12 goals, 15 assists and 747 penalty minutes. Hodge, 26, was named to the NHL's all-rookie team following the 1990-91 season. He split last season between the Bruins and their AHL affiliate in Maine. He had six goals and 11 assists in 42 games with Boston. Olajuwon threatens legal action Hakeem Olajuwon's agent has BASKETBALL threatened legal action against the Houston Rockets unless his client is traded and the team retracts statements it made about him during an injury controversy, according to a published report. Olajuwon was out for five games this spring after the center said he could not play because of a left hamstring injury. After club medical officials evaluated Olajuwon, Rockets management concluded Olajuwon was physically able to play and suspended him for failing to render services under his contract. Speculation surfaced that Olajuwon was faking the hamstring injury to gain leverage for a contract extension. Blancas sets pace with 65 Homero Blancas shot a 6-under-par 65 GOLF to take the lead after the first round of the Senior PGA Tour's $400,000 First of America Classic at the Elks Country Club's Highlands course in Grand Rapids, Mich.
