## Paragraphs

"If you can't get your limit off the (Ediz) Hook, you must be puttin' your riggin' on backwards,' was the report morning from Don Frizzell at Port Angeles Charters. "The coho moved in off the (Angeles) Point area on the Hook and we're now getting a mixed bag. The bait is in tight and has moved everything right in on 1 the Hook," Frizzell described. Since Sunday charters have been returning with limits in less than three hours. The kings are running 18-25 pounds, the blackmouth 8-15 and the silvers 4-8. "The boats are filling fast, now that the word is out," Frizzell said, but there still was space available all days (except Fridays of course) this morning. The reports from Sekiu were almost as good. The charter boats there limited Wednesday and had near limits yesterday. The largest chinook (king) Wednesday was a 46-pounder and the top catch yesterday went 36 pounds. Silvers (coho) are running 3-4 pounds. Some camping spots were open, but moorage and motel space has been taken through Monday. Weekend space for the next six weeks may be hard to find and reservations are recommended. Midweek openings are available, however. Accommodations and charters at Neah Bay and Westport, where the ocean salmon season opens, is booked tight through this weekend and most summer weekends. Midweek bookings remain open. Witnesses say it's possible to walk across (boat-to-boat) the southern end of Lake Washington where the sockeye salmon season opened this morning. Calm Seas Charters, Townsend Bottom fishing was better than salmon fishing at midweek, but the charter was not available this weekend. Brownsville Marina Fishing turned to spotty at midweek, but those who are going for rockfish are doing well, Chuck Mauws said. Kingston Marina - A few silver catches off Jeff Head have been reported. Point No Point Resort, Hansville There was some improvement over last week this week, but no real run has arrived, Vic Nelson relayed. Fishermen have been bringing in a mixed bag of kings, blackmouth and silvers. A couple of 20-pounders were the biggest this week. Captain's Landing, Hansville - Mike Klingman says salmon fishing is "picking up a little bit" with the kings still running small (7-8 pounds). There are lots of bottom fish available, he said.

Lindy Vivas, volleyball coach whose contract was not renewed, won her case against the University of Washington, but won't be holding her July 12-15 and July 19-22 camps afterall. A King County Superior Court issued a permanent injunction against the UW, which sought to block Vivas' attempts to hold camps after her contract expired yesterday. Vivas and assistant coach JoEllen Vrazel sued the university for $2 million each, alleging they were denied equal pay, that failure to renew their contracts was retaliatory, and that they were denied equal protection and due process. Vivas has sent letters to her 65 registrants and said she will call each registrant. Her reasons for cancellation are a $5,000 bond required by King County Superior Court, and her desire to avoid a UW appeal that might result in added rental costs for usce of the facilities. One of her staff members found other employment because of the uncertainty of the Vivas camps.
