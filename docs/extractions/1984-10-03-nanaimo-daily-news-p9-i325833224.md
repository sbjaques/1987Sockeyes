## Paragraphs

Henry Reimer scored four goals and added an assist to power Richmond Sockeyes to a 10-5 win over Burnaby Bluehawks in B.C. Junior Hockey League play in Richmond Tuesday night. In Abbotsford, Derrick Ruppel scored twice to lead Langley Eagles to a 9-4 win over the Flyers. In Richmond, the Sockeyes dominated the game with period leads of 3-1 and 7-3. Elliot Kagna scored twice for the winners and singles came from Mike Scardina, Lee McGowan, Rod Philpot and David Holmes. Rick Dietrich, Mike O'Brien, Glen Engevik, Pat Austin and Mike Moree replied for Burnaby. Richmond goalie Scott King made 43 saves while Ron Boyd and Ray Woodley combined to make 28 saves for Burnaby. In Abbotsford, Langley led 5-1 after the first period and 6-2 after 40 minutes. Scoring for the Eagles were Mark Recci, Richard Sloan, John Basco, Rod Ware, Keith Fair, Rob Clarke and Dean Duprey. Doug McFaul scored twice for the Flyers, while singles came from Peter Gill and Tony Carter. Langley's Jason Palmer made 24 saves, while Abbotsford's Bob Romano kicked out 55 shots on goal. BCJHL play resumes Thursday, when Nanaimo visits Burnaby, and Revelstoke plays host to Vernon.
