## Paragraphs

The season is fast winding down for the Abbotsford Falcons of the B.C. Junior Hockey League, and the young team has responded with two straight victories. Friday night at MSA Arena, the Falcons scored five goals in the third period to defeat Salmon Arm Tigers 8-4, and as coach Dave Cherniwchan said Tuesday morning, "Now they're playing the game for fun. There's less pressure so things are starting to come easier for them. The goals are coming easier." Falcons, after hosting Chilliwack •Eagles last night (Tuesday) at MSA Arena, have only two games remaining this season, tomorrow (Thursday) when the Eagles pay a return visit, and Saturday, when defending Centennial Cup champions Richmond Sockeyes also visit MSA Arena. "We're going to finish last but we're a better last-place team than Abbotsford has had for a few years," offered Cherniwchan. "Those lastplace teams had no points and lots of penalties. We've got lots of points by comparison." The Falcons, the youngest team in the league this season, now have 16 wins, compared to 13 for all of last season.
