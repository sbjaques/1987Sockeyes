## Paragraphs

Kings' assistant coach Mike Murphy, who spent 10 of his 12 years in the NHL playing in Los Angeles, said the team is "exploring every possible avenue to find a solution.' Murphy said the Kings even have gone back to using an old trick - putting the players up in a local hotel the day of a home game - to give the team the feeling of being on the road. Murphy's theory is that the NHL's west coast clubs, who put in more air miles and hotel nights than any other teams in the league, experience the same letdown after arriving home from extended road trips. "I know I've talked with (former Canuck coach) Harry Neale before and he's said the same thing," Murphy says. "After three, four or five games on the road there's a feeling of exhilaration when you get home and sometimes you can't focus on a home game the way you should." Murphy says the problem has been exacerbated this season because of the presence of 11 new faces in the Kings' lineup. "Only one of our newcomers has ever played on the west coast before," Murphy says, "and it takes some getting used to. You have to learn to deal with a non-hockey environment, warm weather: and jet lag. We're hoping this year will be a learning experience for some of them and we can turn our bad habits to good ones for next year." Glenn Anderson scored three goals for the Oilers - his fourth hat trick this season and 15th of his career. Kings' coach Pat Quinn benched his star centre, Marcel Dionne, in the third period. Gil Perreault scored a goal for the Sabres and became the 12th player in the NHL to score 500 goals as Buffalo edged New Jersey 4-3.

In the other Interior semi-final, Kelowna took a 3-2 lead in games Sunday by beating Vernon 5-3. On the Coast, the Nanaimo-Delta series is deadlocked at two wins apiece. The Sockeyes, who finished first on the Coast with 78 regular-season points while Abbotsford was fourth with 30, spread their scoring five ways Sunday, outshooting the Falcons 47-25.

Sockeyes finish off Falcons

Canadian Press As expected, Penticton Knights and Richmond Sockeyes have wrapped up their B.C. Junior Hockey League division semi-finals with little apparent exertion. Penticton swept Merritt in four games straight with a 7-3 victory Saturday, while Richmond took five games to eliminate Abbotsford, downing the Falcons 5-3 Sunday night.
