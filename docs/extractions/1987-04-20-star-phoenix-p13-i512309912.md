## Paragraphs

Although the Humboldt Broncos and Richmond Sockeyes planning to play for the Abbott Cup, perhaps the Abbott-and-Costello Cup would be a more suitable prize. With both teams already guaranteed a spot in the Centennial Cup tournament next month in Humboldt, the best-of-seven Western final, which begins at 7:30 tonight in Humboldt, has all the significance of a Bud-and-Lou flick. That doesn't mean, however, that the Broncos are planning to turn in their slapshots for slapstick. "People figure that because we're automatically in the Centennial Cup, we're going to slack off," said Humboldt captain Curtis Chamberlin. "But' we put a lot of pressure on ourselves. We want to go in the front door, more for ourselves and our pride than anything else." That's why Bronco coaches Bernie Lynch and Donn Clark went on a scouting mission last week, travelling to Red Deer to scout the Sockeyes in their series against the Alberta-champion Rustlers immediately after the Broncos swept the Selkirk Steelers in the Anavet Cup. "They play simple hockey, they're big and they work hard," Clark reported. "They're like Sel-* kirk, only they're like a better Steelers team. They dump and chase and play good aggressive hockey."
