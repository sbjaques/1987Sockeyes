- all Hockey Leaguers - National anchored the squad. Coach John Van Horlick says the officiating will go a long way toward determining any success the Bruins might have. "We intend to be physical against Kamloops," said the Bruins' rookie coach, "and we hope the refereeing is not like it was in our last game against Victoria. Whenever they call it really close, we have trouble." In that game, which the Bruins lost 5-2, Victoria had eight power play chances in the first half of the game while the Bruins had only one. "We play well when we can hit and get away with a little extra," added Van Horlick. "In the playoffs, the officiating is generally a little looser." Van Horlick's biggest problem is selecting the right goaltender to face the high-scoring Blazers. Veteran Ray Woodley started the season strongly but has been inconsistent since December. Rookie Dusty Imoo was such a disappointment that he is now out of the picture, tier two callup Olaf Kolzig having taken his job. "I haven't totally decided who will start but there's a pretty good chance Olie will get the nod," said Van Horlick. JUNIOR JOTTINGS - The Richmond Sockeyes and Vernon Lakers resume their best-of-seven B.C. Junior League final tonight, 7:30, at Richmond Arena. Vernon leads the series 2-0.
