The Atlanta Falcons signed third-year free safety Tracey Eaton to a Plan B contract Monday, before the midnight deadline, bringing the club's total number of veteran signees to nine, six by Plan B. Earlier Monday, the Falcons announced signing Plan B free agents Jeff Donaldson, an eight-year safety from the Chiefs; Harper Le Bel, a third-year tight end/long snapper from the Eagles; Jason Phillips, a third-year wide receiver from the Lions; Benji Roland, a secondyear defensive lineman from the Buccaneers; and Naz Worthen, a second-year wide receiver from the Chiefs. The team also announced the addition of veteran kicker Rich Karlis and first-year players Mike Williams, a wide receiver, and Pat Chaffey, a running back. Morris signs with Browns CLEVELAND Joe Morris, a two-time Pro Bowl running back with the New York Giants who did not play last season, was among 10 players who signed with the Cleveland Browns on Monday. Morris had three 1,000-yard seasons with the New York Giants and holds franchise records in total yards rushing (5,296) and touchdowns (48). He's also their career in career attempts (1,318) and set a team record with 21 rushing touchdowns in 1985. Morris suffered a broken foot in New York's last preseason game in 1989, was placed on injured reserve and missed the entire season. He rehabilitated the injury but was placed on waivers in September 1990 and went unsigned for the entire season.
