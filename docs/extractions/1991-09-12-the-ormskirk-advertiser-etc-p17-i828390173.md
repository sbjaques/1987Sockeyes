## Paragraphs

With new director Tommy Garner, secretary Ken Hilton who joined in the close season, and chairman Dave Tomlinson, they are aiming to restore Skem United t to their former glory. The plans already include: DA new match programme which all the advertising space has been sold. [Publicising all matches on the cable system operated by Tawd Valley Cable, believed to be the first time this has happened with a Bass North West Counties club. [Displaying of match posters on 30 sites around the town, concentrating on the area around Concourse. [Continued effort to find firms wishing to take advertising boards around the ground, or to act as match and ball sponsors. Chairman Dave Tomlinson said this week: "We are delighted that it is a local company which is sponsoring us and we are extremely pleased to be carrying their logo on our strip. "We look forward to King Ford being our guests this Saturday on what is a big day for the club in more ways than one." Certainly United face a monumental task in Saturday's 1st. qualifying round match which kicks off at 3 p.m. With several players on the injured list, the start to the season with only four points from five matches up to Saturday has been a poor one by the standards expected, though it's very early days yet. The biggest loss is leading scorer Paddy Scoggins who is likely to be out for several more weeks with suspected ligament trouble, Colin Knox is also on the casualty list, Neil Payne made his first appearance in the 3-1 defeat at Darwen last weekend, and O'Neill himself was unavailable for the first four games. But the Skem player manager plans to have a real go at the Vauxhall Conference side. "We have proved already this year against Southport and Marine that we play better against the -called superior sides and that we're capable of raising our game. "They will bring a lot of support with them, the atmosphere should be just right and the adrenalin will be flowing, and we will be trying really hard to do our stuff on the field of play now that things are starting to happen off it," he said. Admission for Saturday's match will be increased to £3.50 for adults and £2.50 for pensioners and children at the request of Macclesfield as the senior club. That's the standard charge in the Conference, and in any event the extra 'gate' money received from both home and away fans will be to Skem's benefit after the final share-out. If any replay is needed it will take place at Moss Rose next Tuesday night, but in the event of a definite result this Saturday, Skem will entertain Vauxhall GM on Tuesday, kick-off 7-30 p.m. Burscough too are in F. A. Cup action this Saturday with a visit to HFS Loans League side Buxton, a tough test for Russ Perkins' team, though confidence should be at a premium after three successive victories.
