News Services VERNON - The Vernon Lakers blew an early three-goal goal lead but hung on to defeat the Richmond Sockeyes 6-4 last night to take a 2-0 lead in their best-of-seven B.C. Junior Hockey League final series. "We stopped skating in the second period and when we don't skate we're just an average team," said Vernon coach Ernie Gare. "After winning 7-2 I was expecting a letdown." "We couldn't put the puck in the net when we had to and they did it with ease," said Richmond coach Orland Kurtenbach. "We outshot them and outworked them but we have to get our intensity level up." The next game is scheduled for Richmond tomorrow. The Lakers led 3-0 after the first period but Richmond rallied to a 3-3 tie before Mike Barkley scored with 15 seconds remaining in the second period to put Vernon ahead to stay. Dallas Drake, Greg Parnell, Chad Biafore, Jamie Steer and Dane Jackson were the other Vernon scorers while Marty Comeau, Rick Herbert, Wayne Stripp and Tony Bobbitt replied for Richmond which outshot Vernon 45-35. Weaklings rise up HARTFORD, Conn. - Temple and Richmond, universities with little basketball tradition from conferences considered weak, advanced to an NCAA matchup by beating major powers from the best leagues.
