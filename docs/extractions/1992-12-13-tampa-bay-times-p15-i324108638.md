Edmonton Oilers at New York Islanders at Oklahoma City (8:10 p.m. EST). Maybe playing the Oilers in a neutral site will help the Islanders they haven't beaten Edmonton since 1988. SELECTED The NHL Board of Governors on Friday chose Gary Bettman as the league's first commissioner. Bettman, currently the senior vice president and general counsel of the NBA, will take office on Feb. president Gil Stein will remain in officeague June and will help in the transition. STATS Pittsburgh's Mario Lemieux was held scoreless on Friday night, only the third time in 30 games that's happened this season. ... Winnipeg's road record is 3-11-1. The 11 losses are second-worst in the NHL to the expansion Ottawa Senators' 15. STREAKS Detroit's 4-2 victory over Philadelphia broke the Red Wings' four -game losing streak at Joe Louis Arena. They hadn't lost five in a row at home since 1986. ... Washington has won six straight games and eight of their last nine. SLUMPS Detroit was 1-6-1 without center Sergei Fedorov, who returned from a shoulder injury Friday night in a 4-2 victory over Philadelphia. ... Tampa Bay has lost 11 of its last 12 games. SHOTS ON GOAL Rangers rookie Steven King has scored a goal in all three games since his callup last Sunday. Two of the three have been gamewinners. SCHUEDLING The Calgary-Toronto game was the first Friday night regular-season contest ever played at Maple Leaf Gardens. SITES San Jose was selected on Friday to host the 1995 All-Star weekend and Winnipeg will host the 1994 entry draft. STARS Friday Theo Fleury, Flames, had two goals and two assists in the Flames' 6-3 victory at Toronto. The first goal came on a penalty shot. Teemu Selanne, Jets, had three goals in an 8-6 loss to Washington. SEATING The severe storm that lashed the New York metropolitan area held the crowd at the Pittsburgh-New Jersey garne to 8,126. The Devils said they had an advance ticket sale approaching 15,500. SIDELINED Detroit center Jimmy Carson was involved in a minor auto accident Friday morning. He escaped major injury, but missed Friday night's game on doctor's advice. STATUS Philadelphia was without Eric Lindros again on Friday nigh. He's expected to return from his knee injury early next week. SCOUTING Bob Joyce scored twice to lead Moncton (Jets) to a 9-2 AHL win over St. John's (Maple Leafs) on Friday night. SPEAKING "It was totally uncalled for. It's more than a farm boydeserves, but it is totally appreciated, too. I never doubted wehave great owners here, but the win was my best present." -Detroit defenseman Brad McCrimmon, who received a gold watch fromowner Mike llitch Friday night before stepping on the ice to playhis 1,000th NHL game.
