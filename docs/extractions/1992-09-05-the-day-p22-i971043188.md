In Buffalo, Buffalo Sabres center Dale Hawerchuk signed a multi-year contract that will pay him $1.2 million per season, the team announced Friday. The length of the contract was- not released. The agreement makes the 29-year-old Hawerchuk the second-highest paid Sabre behind Pat La Fontaine. Don MacAdam, who coached the Cape Breton Oilers to three playoffs in the American Hockey League, Friday was named head coach of the New Haven Senators. MacAdam, 41, had two second-place and a third-place finish in his three seasons with the Oilers. He was an assistant coach with the Detroit Red Wings for three seasons before heading to Cape Breton. The New York Rangers signed defensemen Joby Messier and Brad Tiley and left wings Craig Duncanson and Dave Thomlinson Friday. Messier, a distant cousin of Rangers captain Mark Messier, played four seasons at Michigan State. The Boston Bruins acquired right wing Darin Kimble from the expansion Tampa Bay Lightning Friday for center Ken Hodge and defenseman Matt Hervey. Kimble, 23, has played four seasons in the NHL with Quebec and St. Louis. The 6-foot-2, 205-pounder had a goal with three assists in 46 games for the Blues last season. In 177 NHL games, Kimble has 12 goals, 15 assists and 747 penalty minutes. Hodge, 26, was named to the NHL's all team following the 1990-91 season. He split last season between the Bruins and their AHL affiliate in Maine.
