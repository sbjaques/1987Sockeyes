by Alan Watson It's getting to be that time for the Abbotsford Falcons. Thanks to the ¿Juan de Fuca Whalers, who enjoyed a four-win-one-loss week - one of those victories coming at the expense of the Falcons Abbotsford now find themselves looking quite a ways up to the the fourth and final playoff position in the B.C. Junior Hockey League's Coastal Division. As of Saturday, Nov. 29, the Falcons were only four points back of the Whalers. As of Sunday, Dec. 6, Abbotsford were 10 points behind the Whalers, who still have three games in hand. Falcons have lost a number of onegoal games this season, and were victimized again Friday night in Nanaimo, when the Whalers escaped with an 8-7 triumph in overtime. Saturday at MSA Arena, the Falcons controlled the final two periods against the second -place Richmond Sockeyes, received a sensational goaltending performance from rookie John Bukowski and took an exciting 3-2 win to keep their playoff hopes -alives. Abbotsford have 21 games left in the regular-season. Tuesday night at MSA Arena, the Whalers played a disciplined road game and skated away with a crucial 4-2 victory. Falcon coach Dave Cherniwchan was in a jovial mood following the big win over Richmond, the Falcons' first taste of victory against the defending Centennial Cup champions this season.

### Region 2000,611 → 2866,2061

After being burned in a couple of B.C. Junior Hockey League deals this season, Abbotsford Falcon general manager Gary McCaskill is moving slowly towards finalizing a deal with the Coastal Division-leading Delta Flyers. The transaction is part-way completed, yet may not be finalized, McCaskill said on Monday. "There is a tentative deal in the works that could see Damien Holland, become an Abbotsford Falcon," said McCaskill. Holland, one of the premier goaltenders in the league, was in Abbotsford Saturday before the Falcons took to the ice against Richmond Sockeyes, and was ready to play, but McCaskill and Falcon owner Byron Hitchens decided at the last minute to hold off for a while before signing him to a playing card. "I got cold feet on the deal and I really can't say I know why," said McCaskill. "'We decided at the last minute not to card him. It was just a gut feeling. "I just got burned on the Shawn Crowthers' deal, and we've lost other guys we've traded for (Paul Thompson, Brent Jones). I pick up a couple players, and bang, they're gone. I want anybody we deal for to come in and want to be an Abbotsford Falcon. I didn't get that feeling from L Damien Holland right away. But, he's coming to practice and we'll have to see." If the deal with Delta for Holland goes through, it could involve as many as three Flyers coming to Abbotsford in exchange for one Falcon, that one obviously having to be a front-line player. Delta has given McCaskill a list of seven players to choose from, aside from Holland, while McCaskill has provided Delta coach and general manager Rick Hopper a list of five players. Flyer management are coming to the Falcons' Thursday (tomorrow) night game against Penticton Knights at MSA Arena (7:30 p.m.), following which the two sides are going to huddle and see if they can complete the deal.
