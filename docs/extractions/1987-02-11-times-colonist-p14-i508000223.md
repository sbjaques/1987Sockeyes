The Canadian Press OTTAWA - Canada will be represented in hockey and figure skating at the World University Winter Games later this month. The Canadian Interuniversity Athletic Union confirmed the University of Alberta Golden Bears, defending CIAU hockey champions, as Canada's representatives at the Games in Czechoslovakia from Feb. 21 to 28. In figure skating, Martin Marceau, 21, of Laval des Rapides, Que., will compete in the men's event, Julie Brault, 21, of Bellefeuille, Que., will compete in the women's, and Pamela Watson, 22, of Hudson, Que., and Aime LeBlanc, 22, of St. Leonard, Que., will compete in the ice dance. The CIAU said there would be no representation in Alpine or Nordic skiing due to financial constraints and eligibility requirements. "We are extremely gratified that the University of Alberta has accepted the CIAU invitation to represent our country at the Games," CIAU president Gib Chapman said Monday. NHL- SOVIET MATCHUPS Capsule looks at the prior matchups between National Hockey League players and various Soviet Union teams: 1985-86: The Soviet Red Army and Moscow Dynamo combined to post a 7-2-1 record on a tour of 10 NHL cities. Quebec (5-1 over Red Army) and Calgary (4-3 over Dynamo) were the only NHL winners and Pittsburgh tied Dynamo 3-3. 1984: Team Canada beat the Soviets 3-2 in overtime of the Canada Cup semifinals en route to a championship victory over Sweden. The Soviets had beaten Team Canada (6-3) and Team USA (2-1) in earlier round-robin play. 1982-83: The Soviet all-stars earned a 42 record against six NHL teams, with Edmonton (4-3) and Calgary (3-2) posting the only NHL victories. 1981: The Soviet team routed Team Canada 8-1 in the championship of the Canada Cup to avenge a 7-3 loss during round-robin play. The Soviets also beat Team USA 41 in round-robin competition. 1979-80: Moscow Dynamo and the Soviet Red Army combined for 5-3-1 record in a tour of nine NHL cities.
