Evening Times Globe MONCTON The Moncton Hawks whipped the visiting St. John's Maple Leafs 5-0 here yesterday to take a 3-2 lead in the best-of-seven Atlantic Division final in American Hockey League playoff action. Moncton can complete a serics upset on Tuesday night in St. John's. Game 7, if necessary, is on Thursday and that, too, would be in Newfoundland. Craig Fisher and Ken Gernander, each with a pair, and Dave Tomlinson paced the Hawks to intermission leads of 2-0 and 5-0. Goaltender Stephane Beauregard stretched his shutout streak to 130 minutes and 21 seconds, a span that dates back to the first period of Game 4. A crowd of 3,829 watched what looked like grown men playing against young boys just learning the sport. The Hawks capitalized on both their power-play opportunities in the first period with Fisher scoring the winner after 9:15 expired. Andy Brickley won a battle for the loose puck at the side of the net, sent a backhand pass to Fisher in the crease and he banged it home. The Insurance marker came at 12:17. United Press International The Toronto Maple Leafs had an easy time against the San Jose Sharks Sunday night, but recent history has demonstrated it is unwise to think those easy times will continue. Toronto waltzed to an 8-3 victory over the Sharks to even their Western Conference semifinal series at 2-2. The eight goals were the most scored by one team in one game of the 1994 Stanley Cup playoffs. But the Detroit Red Wings also thought they had things going their way when they scored seven goals against the Sharks in the sixth game of their quarterfinal series. Two nights later, the Red Wings were knocked out of the playoffs. With the Maple Leafs and upstart Sharks locked in a riveting duel, the Vancouver Canucks appeared ready to take a place in the conference finals. Vancouver, having won three straight overtime games to overcome a 3-1 deficit in the quarterfinals against Calgary, took a 3-1 lead over Dallas in their semifinal showdown yesterday by again winning in overtime. This time it was Sergio Momesso who scored a goal 11:01 into the extra period to produce a 2-1 victory. When the playoffs move back into the Eastern Conference semifinals tonight, the first of the ongoing series could come to an end. The New York Rangers, who failed to eliminate the Washington Capitals Saturday, will get another chance to do so In Madison Square Garden this evening. The Rangers hold a 3-1 lead in that series while Boston and New Jersey are deadlocked at 2-2. The Bruins and Devils will play their pivotal Game 5 in East Rutherford tonight.
