## Paragraphs

The Lakers last won the B.C. Junior Hockey League title in 1971- 72 when current head coach Ernie Gare and assistants George Fargher and ex-NHLer Eddy Johnstone played for the team, then called the Essos. They all get a chance to add to their silverware collection beginning Saturday in the opening game of this year's league final, a bestof-seven affair against the defending champion Richmond Sockeyes. The two squads meet again in Vernon before returning to Richmond Arena for 7:30 p.m. games Tuesday and Wednesday. Gare has just four 20-year-olds on his squad and only one player, former Portland Winter Hawks forward Trevor Pohl, with major junior experience. Of the remainder, eight players have committed to scholarships with NCAA Division 1 schools and several others are mulling over offers. In contrast, the Sockeyes have nine 20-year-olds, as they did last season when the squad won the Canadian junior A championship Centennial Cup. Richmond started the season with a younger team but gradually acquired a lineup that includes eight players with major junior experience.
