South Kitsap's baseball protest has been upheld, but school officials have decided not to replay the April 21 Narrows League loss (12-6) to Stadium. That move has left South coach Elton' Goodwin angry and confused. With the Wolves trailing 8-2 in the top of the fourth inning at Tacoma's Heidelberg Park, assistant coach Don Smith questioned the umpires about what he believed ! to be a Stadium balk under the 1987 definition of the term in the National Federation of High Schools rules. According to Goodwin, the umpire acknowledged the rule and the pitcher's action, but told the SK- coaches that his association, the Western Washington Umpire's Association, was not enforcing the infraction. The next day, South Kitsap, filed its protest to the Narrows League. At a Monday meeting of the league's athletic directors, the ADs voted 4-1 in favor of the Wolves.
