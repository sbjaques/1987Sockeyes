1982 entry draft. NANAIMO, B.C. (CP) The British Columbia Junior Hockey League is investigating an incident which cost the Richmond Sockeyes the services of their top goaltender while he was on emergency loan to a rival team. Willie Popp, 20, of St. Albert, Alta., suffered a broken wrist last Saturday while playing for the Chilliwack Eagles and will be sidelined for at least six weeks, Richmond coach Orland Kurtenbach said Tuesday. Popp broke the wrist of his catching hand when checked by a member of the Juan De Fuca Whalers during the BCJHL game in Victoria. Chilliwack requested the use of a 1 replacement goaltender when Glen Pencer was injured and two other Eagle affiliate netminders were under suspension.
