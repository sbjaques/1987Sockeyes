Mark Price erased a horrible shooting night by scoring 5 points in the final 23 seconds of overtime as the Cleveland Cavaliers beat the Los Angeles Lakers, 109-107, last night in Richfield, Ohio. Price won it by hitting two free throws with 4.5 seconds left after sneakily throwing up a wild shot as Elden Campbell fouled him. The Lakers had a foul to waste in the extra period, but Price saw Campbell coming and heaved the ball toward the basket, earning the two free throws. The Lakers' A.C. Green, whose 3-pointer with 17 seconds left had tied it at 107, missed his next try, and Cleveland's Steve Kerr knocked the rebound away from Sam Perkins to clinch it. Price was 10 of 10 from the line but only 4 of 15 from the floor. Suns 108, 76ers 104 - Kevin Johnson hit two free throws with 14 seconds left and Jeff Hornacek k added two more as Phoenix withstood a fourth-quarter rally by host Philadelphia. Hornacek passed his regular-season career high by 2 points with 35, shooting 4 for 4 from 3-point range and 5 for 5 from the foul line, while Johnson had 17 points and 15 assists. Charles Barkley had 31 points for Philadelphia. Trail Blazers 106, Hawks 95 - Clyde Drexler scored 33 points, including 8 in : a row in one stretch, as the Portland Trail Blazers extended their longest winning streak of the season to seven games with a victory in Atlanta. Bullets 118, Timberwolves 115 - Charles Jones hit a key free throw and made a key block in the last minute, as Washington beat Minnesota in Landover, Md., despite a 22-point, 17-assist performance by the Timberwolves' Pooh Richardson. Magic 98, Pacers 97 - Terry Catledge's rebound shot with 3.4 seconds remaining capped a 14-point fourth quarter and enabled host Orlando to stop a seven-game losing streak with victory over Indiana. Heat 113, Nuggets 109 - Glen Rice scored 29 points, 15 in the third quarter, and host Miami withstood a late Denver rally and handed the Nuggets their 10th loss in 11 games. Rockets 118, Hornets 100 - Kenny Smith scored 26 points and Houston pulled away from Charlotte in the closing minutes for its sixth straight home victory. Clippers 105, Mavericks 97 - Ron Harper scored 23 points and pulled down 12 rebounds as host Los Angeles handed Dallas its 19th straight road loss, tying a club record. Whalers 1, Jets 0 - In the NHL, Kay Whitmore stopped 39 shots for his second straight shutout and Bobby Holik scored the game's only goal with 7:19 remaining as Hartford beat host Winnipeg. Whitmore, who beat Los Angeles, 4-0, on Wednesday, turned back all 21 shots he faced in the first period, then preserved the shutout as the Whalers killed off a four-minute high-sticking call against Yvon Corriveau with 4:30 remaining in regulation.
