"We're winning but we're looking to start playing a lot better than we are right now." That's how Langley Eagle's head coach John Olver summarized the team's most-recent B.C. Junior Hockey League y win, a 4-3 decision over the Richmond Sockeyes at the Civic Centre on Friday. Since returning as Eagle's mentor two weeks ago, Olver has had only one practice with the team since taking over because the team has only two practice days a week. And recently, all the other available days have been games. Olver returned as coach following the surprising departure of Ron Coleman, who took a coaching job in Hamilton, Ont. After assisting with the Western Hockey League's New Westminster Bruins, Olver returned to lead the Eagles, a team he coached last year to the league championship.
