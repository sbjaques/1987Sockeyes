Special to The Sun CALGARY - "I'm cheering for the Jets," Y Tom Watt said while forcing a smile and nursing a beer. Cheering for Vancouver Canucks at the Saddledome Tuesday night wasn't much fun for Watt as the NHL's poorest excuse for a hockey team was blanked 2-0 by Calgary Flames. So, coach Watt jumped on the Jets' bandwagon, praying Winnipeg would do him a favor by beating Los Angeles Kings, the team Vancouver has to catch to avoid an early tee-time on the golf course. Yet, just as Watt was noting that the Jets might beat L.A., Flames' executive Al Coates strode by and piped: "Kings 8-3". Nothing like bad news to make a man's brew go flat: "Why don't you keep walking, rabbit ears?" quipped Watt, struggling to maintain his sense of humor after watching a red-hot Reggie Lemelin hang an old-fashioned goose egg on the Canucks. "I don't think we were a screwup defensively," Watt had noted earlier in grasping for a silver lining. "Our penalty killing improved." Although the Canucks kept Lemelin hopping with 33 shots, they're powerless play continued, going 0- 3. They've failed to pot a powerplay goal in their last 17 chances. Furthermore, the drought continued for captain Stan Smyl and Petri 10 Skriko, neither of whom has scored in the last 11 games. Smyl, who is also pointless in his last nine games, didn't even get a shot on goal. "We have a lot of guys having dry spells," Canucks understated Watt, whose trail the Kings by 11 points with 17 games remaining. "I've been pleased with our efforts all year, but, when you don't get points, you can't keep saying that." The last thing Vancouver needed last night was to run into a hot goaltender. But that they did in Lemelin, who hasn't lost since Dec. 11.

SPECIAL TEAMS BOOST LAKERS Canadian Press Special teams and an early goal helped the Vernon Lakers get the jump on the Penticton Knights with a 6-2 victory as the B.C. Junior Hockey League playoffs got under way on three fronts Tuesday night. Vernon scored four power-play goals and added a shorthanded marker to take a 1-0 lead in their best-of-seven series. Al Young, who scored once on the power play and got the shorthanded goal, and Mark Greyeyes counted twice for Vernon. In the other quarter-final openers, Kevin Taillefer scored four times as the Kelowna Packers drubbed the Merritt Warriors 11-1 and Brian Kozak scored twice in an overtime period as the Richmond Sockeyes registered a 7-4 win over Nanaimo Clippers. The playoffs continue tonight with Vernon at Penticton, Kelowna at Merritt, Nanaimo at Richmond and the Delta Flyers opening their series with the Eagles in Langley.
