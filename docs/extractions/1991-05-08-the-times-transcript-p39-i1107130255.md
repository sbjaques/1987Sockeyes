However, they might this summer. Right now, the key question concerning the Hawks is: Will Farrish be returning? With his contract expiring July 1, Farrish hasn't been told by the Winnipeg brass yet that he's definitely in their plans for next year. "I'm hoping to come back," said Farrish this week. "I think Mike (Winnipeg general manger Mike Smith) is more concerned about ironing out a coach for his team at this particular time." Smith fired Bob Murdoch just days after the conclusion of the regular season when the Jets didn't make the playoffs. Farrish said he's never entertained any thoughts about not returning as coach. • "I assume that Mike knows I really enjoy it here," he says. "If he's expecting me to be back I don't think there's going to be a problem signing. Conversely, if he didn't want me back I think he would have said something by now to give me an opportunity to look elsewhere." With the annual AHL meetings set for Hershey, Pa. later this month, Farrish feels there's a good possibility the 15-team league will feature three divisions next season. "One division could be the five teams in New York state (Adirondack, *Binghamton, Capital District, Utica and Rochester). Another the five teams in the Atlantic Provinces (St. John's, Nfld, Moncton, Fredericton, Halifax, Cape Breton) and a third featuring Portland, Me., Springfield, Mass., Hershey, Pa., Baltimore, My. and New Haven, Conn. "The final five are quite spread out but I'm sure there could be some sort of schedule arrangements worked out," offered Farrish. As for determining a playoff format for a three division league, Farrish said, "that's a different story. I have no idea how that might be handled." Summer hockey?
