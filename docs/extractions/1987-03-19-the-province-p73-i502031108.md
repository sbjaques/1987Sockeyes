Packers 0 Sockeyes 7

Romeo is Frank Romeo, who stopped 27 shots to anchor the Sockeyes in a 7-0 victory against his former team, the Kelowna Packers, in the opening game of the best-of-nine championship series Tuesday. Play between Sockeyes, Coastal Division champions, and the Interior Division-winning Packers continued last night. There was only a hint of drama in the blowout. Richmond led just 1-0 after one period, but scored 12 seconds into the second period and added four more goals before Kelowna could retreat to the dressing room. Ed Ruff, who coaches Kelowna with Bill Hogaboam, felt it was the Packers' worst game of the year. "They weren't intense and they weren't ready for the game," said Ruff of his squad, which compiled a league best 45-5-2 record.

After being let go by Kelowna, he tried out with Victoria of the Western Hockey League. Two weeks later he headed to Vernon and the Lakers, but before he could be signed the Sockeyes picked him up as a free agent.
