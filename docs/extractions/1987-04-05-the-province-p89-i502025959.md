By FRANK LUBA Sports Reporter An Australian playing ice hockey? You've got to be kidding! Damian Holland has received a fair amount of ribbing over his homeland but, like movie hero Crocodile Dundee, the Quesnel Millionaires' goaltender has proved he's no joke. Holland registered a strong effort Friday in the Millionaires' 4-3 loss to the B.C. Junior Hockey League champion Richmond Sockeyes in the opening game of the teams' best-of-three provincial junior A championship. The Sockeyes outshot the visitors from the Peace Cariboo Junior Hockey League 41-28, with Holland facing all but one of those shots when he went to the bench for an equipment repair. "Holland held us in the game in the first period," said Quesnel coach Tom Marsh. Holland may be just 18 and a rookie, but he doesn't lack experience. A native of Swindon, England, he grew up in Montreal but moved to Adelaide, South Australia five years ago - where he plays at the top level. He was also the Aussies' national goalie at the recent world C championships in Denmark. Although the team finished last and winless, Holland's stand-up, challengethe-shooter style earned him his team's most valuable player honors.
