The Atlanta Falcons signed their fourth-round draft pick and two veteran free agents. The free agents are quarterback Bob Gagliano, 35, who has played eight NFL seasons and two in the USFL, and wide receiver Jason Phillips. Gagliano joined the Falcons last Oct. 7, but did not play in a game. The rookie is quarterback Perry Klein, a 6-2, 218-pounder who set NCAA Division II passing records with 38 touchdown passes and 3,757 yards at C.W. Post. Dave D'Entremont saved his best game for last and won his first PBA title since 1992 when he captured the $100,000 PBA Oregon Open in Portland, Ore. D'Entremont beat tournament leader Joe Firpo 235-214 to earn his second career title and the winner's share of $16,000. The 33-year-old righthander qualified fourth and had to win four matches for the victory.

TENNIS/Medvedev upset Wild -card entry Guy Forget of France stunned second-seeded Andrei Medvedev in straight sets during the first round of the $485,000 Swiss Open. HOCKEY/O'Connell promoted The Boston Bruins turned to an old friend, naming former All-Star defenseman Mike O'Connell as assistant general manager. O'Connell, 38, played for the Bruins from 1980 to 1986, representing the team in the 1984 National Hockey League All-Star game. For the past two seasons, he has coached at the Bruins' minor league affiliate in Providence. Edmonton Oilers President and General Manager Gien Sather said Tuesday he's too busy to decide who will take over as the coach of the Edmonton Oilers and he doesn't expect to name his replacement until next month. Ron Low, an Oilers assistant coach for the past five seasons, and George Burnett, coach of the Oilers' AHL affiliate in Sydney, Nova Scotia, are considered frontrunners for the position. Defenseman Jyrki Lumme has signed a multiyear contract with the Vancouver Canucks, the team said. Lumme, 27, had 13 goals and 42 assists last season. He also accounted for 13 points - two goals and 11 assists - in the playoffs. ALSO/Tour de France Belgium's Johan Museeuw took the lead in the Tour de France after the team time trial, and American Lance Armstrong moved into fifth place while heading a surge by his team. In the battle of the two favorites, three-time winner Miguel Indurain of Spain gained a slight edge over Tony Rominger of Switzerland.
