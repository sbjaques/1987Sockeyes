The Canadian Press QUEBEC - Injuries have ravaged three members of the starting lineup of the National Hockey League all-star team, but coach Jean Perron is still optimistic his squad can defeat the Soviets in the Rendez-Vous 87 series this week. At a news conference Sunday night, Perron, who as coach of the Stanley Cup champion Montreal Canadiens will be in charge of the NHL all-star team, said he has been told that starting defencemen Paul Coffey and Mark Howe and right winger Mike Bossy won't be able to play in the games Wednesday and Friday. Coffey of the Edmonton Oilers, Howe of the Philadelphia Flyers and Bossy of the New York Islanders are all suffering from back injuries. Starting centre Mario Lemieux of the Pittsburgh Penguins has been nursing an injured right knee, but is expected to play. While Perron said it was "sad" that the fans who voted for the starting lineup wouldn't be able to see their picks play, he indicated there was enough talent on the 27- man NHL roster to compensate for the injuries. Twenty-two players are expected to dress for the games. Perron will meet with his assistants - Bob Johnson of the Calgary Flames and Michel Bergeron of the Quebec Nordiques - before the NHL's first practice tonight to discuss the revamped starting lineup and to determine the specialty teams. Another thing the coaches will decide is the starting goalie. Goalie Clint Malarchuk of the Nordiques was picked in the fan voting, but the coaches are free to choose whomever they want.
