He injured a knee and dislocated a shoulder during the 1989-90 season. He ended up with another knee injury during the 1990-91 season when Portland's Brad Harrison dove for his legs. TRUE BLUE - As an American, Steve Jaques. He combined toughness, heart, scoring and skating. In the 1988-89 season, he was named the West Division's top blueliner in a vote of coaches and players. Runnerup - Marshall. He didn't rack up the statistics like Jaques, but was a bit more stay-at-home. His shot from point ranks among the hardest for the Americans. Marshall took Todd Klassen under his wing and the two formed probably the best blueline tandem in Tri-City history. If fighting had no role in hockey, Klassen would surely grade out better than Jaques. MR. CONTROVERSY - Kyle
