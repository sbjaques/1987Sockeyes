## Paragraphs

The Campbell Conference's two regular-season division champions not only averted first-round upsets, they also made history last night. Detroit and Vancouver became the ninth and 10th teams in NHL playoff history to capture best-of-seven series after trailing, 3-1. Norris champion Detroit downed Minnesota, 5- 2, and Smythe king Vancouver whipped Winnipeg, 5-0, to complete the comeback double. Red Wings 5, North Stars 2 - Host Detroit, behind the dramatic goaltending of Tim Cheveldae, eliminated last year's conference playoff champions. Sergei Fedorov, Alan Kerr and Gerard Gallant each scored in the second period to stake the Red Wings to a 3-0 lead. Bob Probert and Shawn Burr scored in the third period. Brian Bellows stopped Cheveldae's string of scoreless playoff minutes, which included shutouts in Games 5 and 6, at 188:36 with a third-period powerplay goal. Canucks 5, Jets 0 - Former Bruin Geoff Courtnall scored three goals and Kirk McLean stopped 33 shots to spark host Vancouver. Ex-Bruin Tom Fergus, with his fifth goal of the series, and Trevor Linden also scored for the Canucks. The Canucks were stymied early in the series by red-hot goalie Rick Tabaracci, but regrouped to score 20 goals in the final three games.
