## Paragraphs

The Tri-City Americans delivered one hard hit Sunday night. Rob Krauss' body check midway through the first period caught Medicine Hat's Jason Krywulak off balance, sending him face-first into the boards. It took a few seconds for Krywulak to recover, Next home game: but once he did Friday, 7:30 p.m. he turned the vs. Kamloops game around. "It definitely woke me up," said Krywulak, who later assisted on three goals in Medicine Hat's 9-6 Western Hockey League victory. "A few of their good hits in the first period woke our whole team up. It was a bit choppy out there, but not a lot of hitting - just a lot of clutching and grabbing.' After an intense first period in which the Americans grabbed a 3-1 lead, the Tri-Cities played passively for the game's final 40 minutes and, as a result, were outscored 8-3. "It wasn't really that tough to go in front of the net," said Krywulak. "Plus their goalie (Don Blishen) was kicking a lot of rebounds out there." Tri-City defenseman Rob Krauss agreed. "We stunk the joint up,' said Krauss. "The first period was a good period for us, but then we lost our momentum, and they played a good road game." The loss dropped the Tri-Cities seven points behind second-place Seattle, which upset Kamloops 7-4 Sunday night. The Americans, once favorites to win the WHL title, now hover only one game above the .500 mark with a 15-14-3 record. "We're definitely not playing with our size and strength," said Tri-City Coach Rick Kozuback. Now it looks as if the entire structure is about ready to crumble. And some of the Americans' fans threw in the towel last night as they left for home in the waning minutes of Medicine Hat's 9-6 win in the Tri-Cities. In the 7-0 loss in Spokane, the Americans didn't seem to care. And in Kamloops, the Americans fell behind 6-0. Now the Americans have changed their captains for a second time this season - as Greg Spenrath, Steve Jaques and Rob Krauss all wore either a captain's "C' or the alternate captain's "A.'' It is apparent someone needs to step up and show some heart. And constant roster moves and trades have shaken the team's chemistry. Perhaps team owner Ron Dixon saw this problem shaping up. That may be why he hasn't been watching from his private box for four of the Americans' past six home games, including Sunday. But then again, he could be the problem.
