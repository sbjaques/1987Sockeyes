by Larry Pruner Boston Celtics and Los Angeles Lakers. Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier. Delta Flyers and Richmond Sockeyes. What appears to be building into one of the sports world's most intense rivalries, the Flyers/Sockeyes showdown had a little more fuel added to its fire when the two B.C. Junior Hockey League teams engaged in back-to-back games last week. And after all the smoke had cleared, the Flyers came away with two more victories to raise their win-loss record to an incredible 18-2 mark. In Richmond on Wednesday, the Flyers built an early 4-0 lead and coasted to a 5-2 win. Doug MacDonald, John Spencer, Jeff Nottingham, Aaron Nosky and Steve McNutt each scored once for the winners, while Jason Talo and Mario Desjardins replied for the Sockeyes. "We overpowered them,' Flyers' general manager and coach Rick Hopper said Thursday. "The last two games have been our best defensive performances of the year.
