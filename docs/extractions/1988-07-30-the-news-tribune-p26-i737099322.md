Westport, others targeted for salmon fishing closure Recreational salmon fishing will close for the season in the ocean around Westport Sunday night, and in waters around Neah Bay and La Push Tuesday night, the state Fisheries Department said. The Ilwaco area closed earlier. The decision was made Friday in a telephone call between the department and the National Marine Fisheries Service. Fisheries Department biologist Morris Barker said the areas are expected to use up their allocations of coho by those times. In an unrelated matter, the Fisheries Department has canceled the Lake Wenatchee sockeye salmon fishery scheduled to start Monday. Agency spokesman Tony Floor said the run of sockeye to the lake east of Stevens Pass, and the spawning grounds beyond, has been smaller than anticipated. COLLEGES:

Gators hoop players say coaches gave them money Three former University of Florida basketball players said this week that they were given money by either associate head coach Monte Towe or assistant coach Kenny McCraney, a possible violation of National Collegiate Athletic Association rules. Andrew Moten and Anthony Williams said they were given cash and another player who requested anonymity said he received from "$10 to $200" at a time while on the team.
