SPORTS SHORTS Fast start for B.C. News Services PETERBOROUGH, Ont. B.C. rinks got off to a perfect start at the Canadian senior curling championships yesterday. B.C. men's champion Bryan Mooney of Prince George won in the afternoon draw at the combined men's and women's championships. He drew to the four-foot in an extra end to edge Barry Coleman of Winnipeg 6-5. Helen Elsen of Nanaimo played her only game in the evening and also won at Manitoba's expense. She defeated Eileen Pennycook of Winnipeg 9-5. Lightweight surprise I PARIS Jose Luis Ramirez of Mexico retained his World Boxing Council lightweight title with a controversial split decision over 1984 Olympic gold medalist Pernell Whitaker of the U.S. Whitaker, 15-1, appeared to have an easy time from the start and peppered the champion with right jabs throughout the fight. Ramirez, making his second defence, was the aggressor but never hurt Whitaker. Kiefer attacked MESA, Ariz. Steve Kiefer, an infielder for the Milwaukee Brewers and former member of the Vancouver Canadians, suffered multiple cuts on his neck in a restaurant incident. Kiefer required about 200 stitches after being hit from behind with a beer bottle. A California man has been arrested for investigation of aggravated assault. GP race returns PARIS - The Grand Prix of Canada will be held June 12 in Montreal. The Formula 1 race replaces the Austrian Grand Prix, suspended for one year because of dangerous track conditions. Canada did not have a Formula 1 race in 1987 after a dispute in Montreal over sponsorship. Kelly cruises MANDELIEU, France Frenchman Patrice Esnault captured the fifth stage of the ParisNice cycling race, but Ireland's Sean Kelly seems set for his seventh straight victory in the event. Esnault won the 150-km stage from St. Tropez to Mandelieu by 10 seconds. Kelly enters today's final two stages to Nice with a 16-second margin. Kiwis outclassed CHRISTCHURCH, New Zealand - England E outclassed New Zealand for an easy six-wicket win in a one-day cricket international. New Zealand struggled to 186 for eight off = 45 overs. England scored 188 for four off 43 overs. England leads the series 2-0. Flyers crash Rob Clark scored two goals to lead the Richmond Sockeyes to a 5-3 win over the Delta Flyers in BCJHL playoff action Friday. The win put Richmond ahead 3-0 their bestof-seven Coast Division semifinal series. Jason Talo, Dan Walton and Keith Fair came up with the other Richmond markers.
