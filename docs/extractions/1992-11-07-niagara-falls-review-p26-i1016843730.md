By The Associated Press The Pittsburgh Steelers, reviving memories of the 1970s, will try to do something they haven't done since those Steel Curtain days - beat the Bills in Buffalo. The Bills (6-2) have won five straight at home against the Steelers since Pittsburgh's 28-17 victory in 1978, and they need to make it six Sunday to be assured of remaining co-leaders with Miami in the AFC East. Miami plays at Indianapolis. Pittsburgh (6-2) rolls into Rich Stadium as the leader in the AFC Central. The Steelers' resurgence has been sparked by the clutch passing of Neil O'Donnell, the running of Barry Foster (865 yards on 195 carries) and the AFC's third defence. That defence could be tested sorely by an offence featuring the passing of Jim Kelly, the catching of Andre Reed and Don Beebe and the running and catching of Thurman Thomas.
