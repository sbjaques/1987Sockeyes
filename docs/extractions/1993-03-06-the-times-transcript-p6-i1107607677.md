By The Canadian Press The Detroit Red Wings were streaking in the wrong direction before doing an about-face. In the process, they broke a significant streak for the Toronto Maple Leafs. "We've let a couple of leads slip away lately, and we weren't going to let this one go," Detroit forward Dino Ciccarelli said after the Red Wings beat the Maple Leafs 5-1 Friday night. That not only halted the Red Wings' -game losing skid and five game winless streak, but also ended Maple Leafs' 10-game unbeaten streak (9-0-1) their longest since 1967. "Obviously, we would have liked to go unbeaten for the rest of the year," Toronto's Dave Ellett said. "We're disappointed that it ended, but it was more media hype than anything else."
