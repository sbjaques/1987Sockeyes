The Tampa Bay Lightning acquired center Ken Hodge and defenseman Matt Hervey from the Boston Bruins, in exchange for right wing Darin Kimble and future considerations. Hodge was a 30- goal scorer in NHL 1990-91 and is the son of Ken Hodge Sr., who was a teammate of Lightning president and general manager Phil Esposito in the 1970s. Hodge had 30 goals and 29 assists as a rookie in 1990-91, earning

a berth on the all-rookie team. He struggled last season, splitting time between Boston and its minorleague affiliate in Maine. Hodge had six goals and 11 assists while with Boston. Hervey played in 16 games with the Bruins, recording one assist. Both Hodge and Hervey are 26. "In Ken Hodge, we are acquiring a player who has proven goal-scoring ability at the NHL level," Esposito said. "And Matt Hervey has shown consistent improvement in his professional career and provides added depth at defense."
