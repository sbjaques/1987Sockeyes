Sockeyes built for success By ELLIOTT PAP Sun Sports Reporter HUMBOLDT, Sask. - The timehonored theory that you can't build a winner overnight has taken a kick in the butt from the Richmond Sockeyes. The newly crowned western junior A hockey champions are a team for the present. Forget last year, forget next year. The future is now. Centennial Cup, here we come. A year ago, the 'Eyes captured the B.C. Junior Hockey League's Coastal Division title. But when training camp opened last fall, there were just two returning players - Jim Gunn and goalie Glenn Pencer. It wasn't so much a housecleaning as a demolition. The man behind it all is owner Bruce Taylor, a Burnaby roofing contractor. "Two years ago I was with the Burnaby Bluehawks, and we won the Coastal Division but lost the league final," says Taylor.
