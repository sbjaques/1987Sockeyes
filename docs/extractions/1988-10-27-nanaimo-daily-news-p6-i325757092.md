Clippers were the better on the night, outshooting the Sockeyes 49-25. "We have to be very proud of the guys for the way they played for two periods," said Bestwick, "but we have to be disgusted and embarassed with our first period. Until we can put together 60 minutes instead of 40, we're going to continue to lose.' Lack of offence in the first period cost the Clippers, who produced just three shots and trailed 3-0 after. 20 minutes. However in the second frame, the Clippers came to life. Aided by some bad Richmond penalties, Nanaimo got back into the game with three straight power play markers. First Hodgins cut it to 4-1 with a blast from the point that beat Sockeye goalie Kevin Baldridge. Then Stotts made it 4-2 when he walked in from the point and beat Baldridge with a shot high on the right side.
