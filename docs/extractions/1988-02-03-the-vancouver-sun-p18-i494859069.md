## Paragraphs

*MacPherson, the Chilliwack Eagles' general manager and coach, was referring to an incident in a B.C. Junior Hockey League game last Saturday in which goaltender Willie Popp, an emergency replacement from the Richmond Sockeyes, suffered a broken wrist. Popp, 20, of St. Albert, Alta., broke the wrist of his catching hand when checked by a member of the Juan De Fuca Whalers during the game in Victoria. Whalers' player Rick Cox was originally assessed a two-minute penalty on the play but it was increased to a five-minute major when it was established that Popp was injured. Chilliwack requested the use of Popp after Eagles' goaltender Glen Pencer was injured in a Friday game in Nanaimo and was under doctor's orders not to play Saturday. Two other Eagle affiliate netminders were unable to play Saturday because they were under suspension. Citing a B.C. Amateur Hockey Association rule that stipulates a team may use a replacement goaltender from a team of the same or lower division - providing permission is obtained from the BCAHA and the goalie's team - MacPherson requested Popp from the Sockeyes, who granted permission. But after learning that Popp would play for the Eagles, Sproule, the Juan De Fuca coach, telephoned BCJHL vice-president David Ferguson and Sockeyes' owner Bruce Taylor. They claim Sproule told them: "If Popp starts the game, he won't finish." Ferguson added: "He (Sproule) is an explosive sort of guy." Taylor said: "We were just trying to be good Christians. We'd do the same thing over again." "He (Ferguson) can say whatever he wants," said Sproule. "(Popp) was out of his crease playing the puck . it was a fair hit.' Popp was forced to leave the game less than five minutes into the third period. Juan De Fuca won the contest 3-1. A two-year veteran of the major junior Ontario Hockey League, he is expected to be sidelined for at least six weeks. "He got hurt because somebody intentionally ran him," said MacPherson. "Somebody deliberately tried to take him out." McPherson also took a shot at Sproule. "We've got a member in our league who puts all his priorities toward trying to injure someone.' The BCJHL is investigating the incident and Ferguson said a twoman arbitration committee was established Monday after the league's 12 governors held a telephone conference call Sunday night. Charlie McFarlane, former owner of the Sidney Capitals, and ex-Abbotsford Falcons' owner Del Thachuk form the arbitration commit• tee. Sproute contends it was improper for Chilliwack to use Popp in the first place because of a section in the BCAHA rule that states permission must be obtained from the goaltender's team prior to the game in question. Terry Skarbo, a BCAHA director, said permission from the branch was not requested by the Eagles until after the Chilliwack-Juan De Fuca game was completed.
