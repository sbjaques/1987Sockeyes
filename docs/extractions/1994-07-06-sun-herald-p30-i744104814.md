BOXING Report says Douglas in diabetic coma - In Columbus, Ohio, former heavyweight champion James "Buster" Douglas was r reportedly hospitalized Tuesday in a diabetic coma. WBNS-TV, citing no sources, said Douglas, 34, was upgraded from poor to stable condition at Grant t Medical Center on Tuesday Douglas afternoon. The hospital refused comment and hospital spokeswoman Karen Waldbillig said the family asked that no information be released. Unidentified relatives of the former boxer had told the station Douglas was taken to the hospital Monday and had slipped in and out of a coma since then. They said Tuesday afternoon that his condition was improving. •It was not known whether Douglas previously had - been diagnosed as a diabetic or which form of the disease he might have. In some cases, if diabetes goes untreated, the victim may go into a diabetic coma, which can lead to death. BASKETBALL Top Rank fights over Wolves - Top Rank of Louisiana asked a federal judge in Minneapolis on Tuesday to › reconsider an earlier decision that stopped Louisiana court proceedings about the fight over ownership of the Minnesota Timberwolves. Top Rank, which last month agreed to buy the NBA team for $152.5 million and move the team to New Orleans only to see the league reject the deal, also asked U.S. District Court Judge James Rosenbaum to dismiss the case. The group contends that because the state of Louisiana contributed $1 million to the purchase, it is an "indispensable" part of the case. And because a constitutional amendment doesn't allow citizens from o one state to sue another state in U.S. court, Rosenbaum should throw out the federal lawsuit and let the Louisiana court handle it. HOCKEY • Sather puts hiring on hold - Glen Sather said Tuesday he's too busy to decide who will take over as the coach of the Edmonton Oilers and he doesn't expect to name his replacement until next month. Sather, the man credited with building the Oilers into the most powerful NHL team of the 1980s, added the role of coach to his duties as general manager and president last November when he fired Ted Green. Green joins Buccaneers - On Tuesday, the Tampa Bay Buccaneers signed former Detroit Lions receiver Willie Green to a one-year contract worth about $500,000. The 6-foot-2, 181-pound Green was released by the Lions last month to make room under the salary cap for Minnesota Vikings free-agent receiver Anthony Carter. An eighth-round draft pick out of Ole Miss in 1991, Green finished his college career as the school's second all-time leading receiver. The Bucs outbid the division-rival Chicago Bears for the services of Green, who would've been reunited with former Lions quarterback Erik Kramer. Falcon signees include Gagliano, Klein - The Atlanta Falcons signed their fourth-round draft pick and two veteran free agents Tuesday. The free agents are quarterback Bob Gagliano, 35, who has played eight NFL seasons and two in the USFL, and wide receiver Jason Phillips. Gagliano joined the Falcons last Oct. 7, but did not play in a game. Phillips, a five-year veteran, has played in 56 games with 49 catches for 578 yards and two touchdowns. He joined Atlanta in 1991 after two seasons in Detroit. The rookie is quarterback Perry Klein, a 6-2, 218- pounder who set NCAA Division II passing records with 38 touchdown passes and 3,757 yards at C.W. Post.
