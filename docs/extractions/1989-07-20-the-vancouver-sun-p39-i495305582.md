Westview Fishing Charters also likes Harwood Point, Grant Reef's and Vivian Island for power mooching with live herring. CAMPBELL RIVER - High south-easterly winds have calmed, resulting in great coho off south end of Quadra Island, according to River Sportsman. Pink salmon spotted at Brown, Deepwater and Plumber Bays. COURTENAY - High winds limiting effort, reports Jasper's Fishing Resort. Coho prospects are likely to improve once the weather clears. NANAIMO - It's the Brickyard for good sized chinook (20 lbs.) as well as coho and pink. Island Tackle & Billiards also likes Entrance Island, Gray Rock and Ruxton Passage. Trolling and drift fishing; evening fishing best bet. PARKSVILLE - Beachcomber Marina says prospects much improved at Salinas and Lasqueti Islands. Green and silver hoochies working at 60 ft. PENDER HARBOR - Plenty of coho at Seal Reef, says Lowe's Resort. Bargain Harbor still good for chinook, especially during mid-afternoon tides. Dogfish still mixed in but not as bad as a week ago. PORT ALBERNI Good coho on surfline off Barkley Sound, otherwise chinook only fair. Harbour Sports reports sockeye closed. VICTORIA- to Plenty of coho lbs. from throughout three to up seven area waters; best bets are Church Rock and Eden Point about three quarters of a mile out, between the first and second tide lines. Castaway Tackle, North Vancouver, reports the coho in the Seymour River is only fair, better in the Cap, though both rivers are topping. Gerry Scott at West Coast Fishing Tackle likes Alouette Lake for both rainbow and cutthroat and recommends the flyfishing on the Coquihalla River. Ruddick's Fly Shop, Burnaby, has had good notices from a few of the lakes in Ellis Lake Prov. Park but notes the S Skagit River is dropping. The Fraser River is still fairly slow although some fish up to 30 lbs. have been caught, according to Fred's Custom Tackle, Vedder Crossing. The Vedder, although slowing, still offers some opportunities for rainbow and the chinook is picking up. Fred's rates the just opened Thompson River highly for chinook. KAMLOOPS - Evening fishing is the best bet, notes Yosh Yoshida of Yosh's Hook and Tackle, especially on Walloper Lake. The kokanee on Stump Lake has slowed while experienced fishermen will enjoy the challenge of Sheridan Lake (20 to 30 ft.) Also recommended: Shuswap Lake, near the river mouth. MERRITT Some area lakes have slowed, according to Ponderosa Sports, but excellent prospects remain Boot, Corbett, Douglas, Harmon, and Nicola Lakes. PRINCETON - Gold and silver flatfish are working well on Chain, Dry and Round Lakes, notes Princeton Sports, though reports remain scarce about the upper lakes. Flies, silver spinners are safe bets for the still productive rivers and creeks. KELOWNA Trout in the 12 lb. range are still to be had in Okanagan Lake, anywhere from the surface down to 40 ft., while the upper lakes are continuing to produce, notes Clem Lingor of Clem's Sporting Goods. SQUAMISH-PEMBERTON - Valley Sporting Goods in Squamishrates Daisy and Anderson Lakes as the hot spots for rainbow (six lbs.) and dolly varden (up to 14 lb.), while the Chekamus is the only real choice of the rivers. Spud Valley Sporting Goods recommends the Birkenhead River, as well as Lizzie and Duffey Lakes.
