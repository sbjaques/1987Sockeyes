## Paragraphs

Sockeyes wrap up Coastal series Sun News Services Greg Hadden scored at 2:59 of overtime to give the Richmond Sockeyes a 3-2 victory over the Delta Flyers at Sungod Arena Saturday to take the best-of-seven B.C. Junior Hockey League semi-final series in four straight games. Rob Clarke and Barry Kress had the other Richmond goals. Kress forced the overtime with his goal at 18:35 of the third period when Delta had two men in the penalty box. Both of Delta's goals came from Peter Holmes. Randy Beekhuizen made 28 saves in the Delta net while Mike Doyle turned aside 19 shots for the Sockeyes. Delta led 1-0 after the first period and the teams were tied 2-2 after the second. It's the third straight year that Richmond has beaten Delta to win the Coastal Division championship. Richmond will meet Interior champion Vernon Lakers Friday in Vernon in the first game of a best-of-nine final series. Richmond finished one point ahead of Delta atop the Coastal Division standings and everyone expected a close series. But Richmond assistant coach Murray Lawrence said Richmond established its supremacy early in the series. "The Flyers blew it in the second game," he said. "They tried to run us out of the rink and couldn't. It cost them their momentum.' Against Vernon, Lawrence said he expects "good fast games. Vernon's a strong team with a good work ethic."
