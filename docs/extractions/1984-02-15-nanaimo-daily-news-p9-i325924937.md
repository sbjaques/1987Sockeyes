In league action Tuesday night, Cowichan fell 8-3 to hometown Richmond Sockeyes, while Brett Hull's hat-trick led Penticton Knights to a 12-3 pounding of Summerland Buckaroos. Steve Tuttle, David Phipps and David Holmes each scored twice to pace the Sockeyes. Craig Tillberg, Kal Russell and Phil Lum replied for Cowichan. Richmond goalie Glenn Pencer made just 10 saves, while Cowichan's Ed Jones turned aside 37 shots. The Sockeyes are again tied with the Clippers for second place in the Coastal division.

Ken Clement breaking his left thumb during his first shift in Saturday's game against Richmond Sockeyes is not good news for Nanaimo Clippers. The local B.C. Junior Hockey League club was counting heavily on the veteran nicknamed "Pope" to provide the leadership and drive in the postseason. Clement is one of the nicest and most soft spoken players on the Clipper roster, but get him in the corner battling for the puck and he resembles a combination between Atilla the Hun and the Incredible Hulk. The 20-year-old, who has made up for his lack of playing skill through his intensity and hard work, may well have played his last ever junior hockey game. That's a real pity because the Clippers needed him to pull them through upcoming clutch situations in the playoffs. Maybe the other players on the team will now decide they really have their work cut out for them and pull together. Maybe their game song will include a line about winning the big one for "Pops." Clement is still attending practises and skating with the club and who knows, he might even help do some coaching on how the rest of the guys can play tough, grinding hockey which is his forte. There still a slight possibility that "Pops" could be back for the playoffs but only if the injury is not as bad as first feared and if the Clippers can get into a couple of long playoff series. Clement could conceivably be back if Nanaimo makes it to the BCJHL championship. But this latest bit of bad luck must be extremely frustrating for him. When the playoffs start, you can bet the most attentive of the spectators will be "Pops" anar urging his teammates to play their hearts out beat the enemy. Speaking of another hockey team, Vancouver Canucks, are they finally for real or is this business of winning on the road just one big mirage? The NHL club is suddenly beating good teams regularly and with a comeback style they haven't displayed since the Phil Maloney days. Maybe Harry Neale is doing something right, although I still have a sneaking suspicion the Canucks will settle back into their mediocore groove before too long. They've done it too long to become consistent winners. Vancouver is by no means guaranteed a playoff spot and as the Roger Nelson coached Los Angeles Kings are ever improving and hot on the Canucks' tall. The next 20 or so games will show whether the Vancouver players have smartened up or they just happened to catch several of the better clubs in the pro circut on off nights. Before I disappear from the sports desk andmove into the news department permanently, I'd like to pay tribute to one of this city's most forgettable sports teams: the Daily Free Press Walleyball squad.
