Clippers 0, Sockeyes 7. By KEVIN MITCHELL Staff Writer Richmond Sockeyes may as well bring out the shades. The Sockeyes' future looks pretty bright after four-game sweep of Nanaimo Clippers in the best-ofseven Coastal Division semi-final series. "I think they'll go all the way," said Clipper all-star blueliner Grant McDonald: "They've got a pretty strong team and they've got good coaching. They may be a little weak in their goaltending. They tend to give up the odd soft goal." Said Sockeye coach Orland Kurtenbach after Richmond waltzed to a 7-0 win Saturday night at Civic Arena, "In order to get to the Interior we have to win the next series. We have to take them one a time. It's going to be a challenge...I would say our club has been increasingly playing better over the last month and a half as we have been getting players back from injuries and suspensions.' Richmond, with an awesome defensive corps headed by former Western Hockey Leaguers Trevor Dickie and Matt Hervey and exMaine Black Bear University star Stan Czenczek, beat the Clippers 12 straight times, including the four playoff victories. "You've got to be disappointed losing 7-0 in the final game" said Clipper captain Sean Stotts, who had a rare bad game in front of 600 fans. "They forechecked us really well and we just couldn't get anything going." Stotts figures the Clippers will stay basically intact next year. "There's no doubt about it, I'll be back here next year, Stotts. "Hopefully everybody be back said. here next year. There were a couple of guys who weren't really trying (in the playoffs) but you have to live with it and pick up the slack." Clipper coach Rich Dutka, whose club fell to Delta Flyers (six games) in last year's semi-final series, is excited about the 1987-88 season. "I think we've started something here that will be successful ih the (future,' said Dutka. "We gave our fans, who were the greatest in the league, an honest effort in 24 of 26 games. Added Dutka, "Last year we were fairly healthy going into the playoffs, but this year we had our work cut out for us with three or four guys who shouldn't have been playing due to injuries.'
