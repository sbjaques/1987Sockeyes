An all-Spokane officiating crew will work the exhibition game between the U.S. and Canadian Olympic teams Nov. 28 in Tacoma. Dennis . LaRue, the lone American selected to referee in the 1988 Olympics, will be the referee in the Tacoma Dome. His linesmen will be Kerry Blair and Cory Bowles. All three formerly played Junior B hockey in Spokane, and LaRue played one year of senior amateur. LaRue and Blair also will work the U.S.-Canada game Nov. 27. in Los Angeles. Mark Rudolph, officiating program director of the Amateur Hockey Association of the U.S., said LaRue may receive other exhibition assignments. Don Cherry, who played pro hockey for the 1962-63 Spokane Comets, has been inducted into the Rochester (N.Y.) Americans Hall of Fame. Cherry played and coached for Rochester in the pro American Hockey League after leaving Spokane. He later coached in the National Hockey League. Comet defenseman, coach Joe Crozier, another, former and Rochester coach, was one of the original inductees in the Americans' Hall of Fame last year. Dan Holden, the only local player ever to play the Spokane Chiefs in the Western Hockey League, is playing for Acadia University in Wolfville, Nova Scotia. Holden played at the University. of Calgary in 1984-85, then joined the Chiefs the following season. Former Chief defenseman Rick Herbert, who holds the WHL record of 329 games played, has joined the Richmond Sockeyes of the Tier Two British Columbia Junior Hockey League. Marc Pezzin, who coached Herbert in Spokane and Brandon, said Herbert was not interested in playing a sixth season in t the WHL after being released by the Chicago Blackhawks. Herbert plans to play college hockey in Canada next year, Pezzin said.
