RICHMOND, B.C. Any hockey team that wins 17 consecutive post-season games, a feat Richmond Sockeyes have now accomplished, requires more than its share of breaks. Sockeyes got a large dose of good fortune with 13 minutes remaining in Wednesday's Western Canada Centennial Cup semifinal game against Red Deer Rustlers at the Richmond Arena. Trailing 4-3, Richmond was handed a five-minute power play when Rustler defenceman Keith Lightbown was ejected for pummelling Sockeye forward Dave Tomlinson, who never removed his gloves. To that point, the Rustlers had used the splendid goaltending of pick-up Ken Weiss and a disciplined defence to keep the explosive Sockeyes at bay. It was far and away a superior effort to the one they turned in Tuesday, when they were hammered 11-4 in the opener of the best-of-seven set. But killing a five-minute penalty without the Sockeyes inflicting massive damage was simply too much to ask. Richmond scored three goals while Lightbown was removing his equipment and pulled out a 6-4 victory. Sockeyes now lead the series 2-0, but will have to win it in Red Deer, where the remainder of the games will be played. The third game is Saturday at 8 p.m. and the fourth Sunday at 7 p.m. "We had a game plan and stuck to it for two periods, but then we had a little misfortune with the referee and a little misfortune with one of our players," said an angry Rustler coach Larry Billows. "That was a dumb play by Lightbown. I don't know what he was thinking of." Dean Rutledge, who started the season with Fort McMurray Oil Barons, drew the Sockeyes even at 9:52, defenceman Matt Hervey notched the winner his third of the game at 11:19, and Brian Kozak added some insurance 88 seconds later. "We took some dumb penalties tonight as well, so I guess they evened out," offered Richmond general manager-coach Orland Kurtenbach, who stressed his club had to work extra hard to pull out the victory. "It didn't come easy for us," he stated. "We had 36 shots in the first two periods and weren't getting the puck past their goaltender (Weiss).
