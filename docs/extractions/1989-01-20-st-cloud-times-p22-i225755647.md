About Colorado College: Things are looking up in Colorado Springs, where the Tigers have doubled their wins from last season under first-year coach Brad Buetow. Colorado College is 3-0-1 in its last four games, all on the East Coast. "I'm real proud of the guys," said Buetow, who coached at Minnesota from 1979-85 and U.S. International from 1985-88, when that program was disbanded. "The players have played hard all season long. We're just not playing good right now; we're playing with a lot of confidence," Buetow said. The key to turnaround has been improved goaltending. Derek Pizzey began the season as the No. 1 goalie, but Jon Gustafson holds the hot hand. He has played the last three games, all wins. "Jon will start against Michigan Tech, but we'd like to get Derek in there. He's had a rough year," Buetow said. The offense has been led by the line of senior left wing Tim Budy, freshman right wing Steve Strunk and freshman center Grant Block (4-8-12). Budy, who hasplayed in 147 consecutive games for the Tigers, has eight power-play and three short-handed goals this season. Strunk, from Wausau, Wis., has nine power-play goals. He was recruited to Colorado College by former Tigers' coach Mike Bertsch. "I have to thank Mike for that one," said Buetow, who brought four U.S. International players with him to Colorado.
