Last year: 2-14 Outlook: The Rams have one direction in which to go in and they believe they are headed in the right one. After going winless two seasons ago, they won a pair of games but, more important, they were in contention in more than half of their games. Of the 14 losses, two came in overtime and another five or six were lost by two goals or less. "We are headed the right way," coach Dean Goodale said. "Interest seems to be going up. We've got a junior high team going and I think hockey is going to stay in Kennebunk. We have to continue ih the direction we're going." This year's team is led by a group of eight sophomores Skating on the top line will be sophomores Chris Dumont (4 goals and 6 assists for 10 points last year) and Duarle Ward. (6-3-9). Junior Chris Healy (4-3-7) will center the line. Senior captain Dung Nguyen, who has skated for just two years, will help offensively.

Last season: 10-10-1 Outlook: The Golden Trojans are still fairly young, sending a squad made up mainly of juniors onto the ice, but coach Riley has plenty of talented performers to work with. They're big and strong, and ready to kick off the season next Thursday against Cheverus. "We're stronger physically than in the past, and bigger in terms of size," Riley said. "That's our biggest improvement." Returning to bolster Thornton's offense are highscoring forwards Jim MacLeod and Troy Caron. Last season both were well known for their knack of putting the puck in the net, and Riley expects them to continue the good work. Junior goalkeeper Jeff Marston will be back in the net this season, backed up by David Welch. According to Riley both have looked strong in the Trojans' preseason action. Other key returners will be: B.G. Chabot, Ryan Schmidt and Chris Grigsby. The Trojans certainly have the talent, but according to Riley, one thing TA is missing is speed. "We're not going to be a real quick team so we'll have to be exact with our passes," he said. "Our patterns will have to done precisely because that's the only way to compensate for quickness." And although he knows the league's other teams will be tough as always, he's confident that his team can continue the improvements it's been making for the past two season. "I'm sure everyone else has improved equal to us, and I think there will be a lot of good hockey games going on this year," said Riley, whose team competed in seven overtime contests last season. "I don't see anyone in this league running away with it."

Last season: 5-15 Outlook: Coach Jack Lowry and his team are keeping a low profile these days. No promises. No predictions. Just hockey. Nothing else. "We're just going to work hard and see what happens," Lowry said. "Attitude is the key for us." The Tigers return all but two players from last year's squad. Eight seniors will be counted upon to lead Biddeford. Among them is 1989-90 leading scorer Scott Guertin and goaltender Adam Sioch. Defensemen Chris Daigle and Dan Labonte anchor a steady defense. Goalie Troy Pickard is also back. Jason Radford, who missed most of the last two seasons with injuries, will be back at right wing. The junior class is led by center Frank Gregoire, a shrewd playmaker and winger Casey Small. Sophomore defenseman Reuben Sevigny and Chris Wilson have already had a year's experience and will be important. Freshman defenseman Jason Tremblay could also contribute. "Our big emphasis is on defense," Lowry said. "If we can get three goals a game, we're going to have to win it. "We're not deep in skilled players. That's why we're going to need the good attitude." Last season the team was decimated by injuries at Christmas when 10 players were injured within a week. Though no team could survive such a plague, this year Lowry feels the Tigers have more depth. That, he believes will make it easier to survive should injuries again take their toll. "Last year we just didn't develop consistency," Lowry said. In recent round robin tournaments the Tigers lost just one of nine periods they played in. "We're coming along but we've got a long ways to go," he said. "Hopefully, after Christmas we'll know what we've got."
