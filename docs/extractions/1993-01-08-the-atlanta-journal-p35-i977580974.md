By Raad Cawthon STAFF WRITER Atlanta Knights center Jock Callander is looking forward to this weekend, when the Peoria Rivermen come to town for two games. In two previous games against Peoria both Atlanta victories - Callander totaled five points, including three goals. Atlanta will play Peoria tonight and Saturday in The Omni. Both games begin at 7:35. Like the Knights, Peoria is a good skating team. The Rivermen have the IHL's fewest penalty minutes - 845. However, they're 0-2 against the Knights and are coming to Atlanta for the first time this season. The Knights opened their season in Peoria in October with a 6-5 victory and returned in November, winning 4-1. But the Rivermen are 7-3 in their last 10 games, including a one-point loss to league-leading San Diego. Peoria is in second place in the Midwest, 15 points behind Milwaukee. This weekend the Knights will find out if they have righted themselves from a difficult month-long stretch when defensemen Rick Lanz and Matt Hervey were injured and Atlanta played most games with an allrookie defense. During that period the Knights were 7-5.
