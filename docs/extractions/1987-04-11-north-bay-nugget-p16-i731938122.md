SELKIRK, Man. (CP) Humboldt Broncos scored three times in the third period Friday to score a 6-4 victory over Selkirk Steelers and take a 2-0 lead in the best-ofseven Centennial Cup western semifinal. The home ice advantage provided by the tiny Selkirk Arena ice surface proved meaningless in both games. "That was a big psyche-out," said Humboldt coach manager Bernie* Lynch. "You take what you're given and you play hard." Selkirk, already missing five regulars, lost Kevin Mayo to a game misconduct in the first period and Jeff Trapp to an ankle injury. "They're giving it their best shot," said frustrated Steelers' coach Frank Proco. *We just, don't have enough horses at this point." he said. Humboldt scored three times on the power play and collected a short-handed goal by Curtis Chamberlain, who finished the night with two goals.
