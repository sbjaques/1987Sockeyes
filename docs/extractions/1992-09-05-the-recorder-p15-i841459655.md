The Boston Bruins on Friday acquired right wing Darin Kimble from the expansion Tampa Bay Lightning for center Ken Hodge and defenseman Matt Hervey. Kimble, 23, has played four seasons in the NHL since Quebec selected him 66th overall in the 1988 draft. He played two-plus seasons for the Nordiques before being traded Feb. 4, 1991, to St. Louis. Kimble, 6-foot-2, 205 pounds, had one goal with three assists in 46 games for the Blues last season. In 177 NHL games, Kimble has 12 goals with 15 assists and 747 penalty minutes. ... In other track and field news, Dan O'Brien, the 1991 world decathlon champion, was on world record pace after five events Friday at a multievent invitational meet in Talence, France. He compiled 4,720 points, better than the record 4,698 he amassed on the first of the U.S. Olympic trials at Orleans in June when he failed to make the team missing Netter three times in the pole vault the second day. With another day and five events left, O'Brien was 43 points ahead of the pace set by Daley Thompson at the 1984 Olympics, when the Briton set the world record of 8,847 points. After the first day, Thompson had 4,677 points. O'Brien's total was 27 points less than the 4,747 he registered at the 1991 U.S. championships. But that total included two wind-aided marks.
