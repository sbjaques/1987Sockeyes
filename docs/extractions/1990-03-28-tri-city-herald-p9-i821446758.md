Sunday in Seattle the destination sign on the front of the Tri-City Americans bus read "War." Tuesday afternoon the diagram board in the Americans' dressing room broke word "War" down into "We Are Ready." Well, apparently the Americans weren't yet ready to play the disciplined hockey it takes to win playoff games. And now it may be too late to start. Once again the Seattle Thunderbirds blew out the Americans 8-2 in the Tri-Cities Coliseum. With the victory the third straight blowout - Seattle took a commanding 3-0 lead in the Western Division semifinal series. Maybe TriCity hockey fans had a premonition as just 5,507 paid to see Tuesday's game. Sitting outside the Tri-Cities Coliseum in the Columbia Cable van watching his team lose to the Seattle Thunderbirds on an eightinch screen, Rick Kozuback was a study of frustration. Kozuback, the deposed American coach who is still serving an "indefinite" suspension for his part in a brawl with fans in the Western Hockey League playoff opener Saturday night in Seattle, lashed out at the timing of league officials. Kozuback said he did not receive notice that top defenseman and captain Steve Jaques would be suspended until 14 p.m. Tuesday, hardly enough time to prepare to play the third game of the series Seattle now leads 3-0. "We were supposed to find out about the suspensions at noon today," Kozuback said. "We had planned to play without me there, but to have our pre-game skate and our pre-game meal and then find out Jaques was suspended is ridiculous. If we had known yesterday, maybe we could have handled it. Good heavens it seems unfair."
