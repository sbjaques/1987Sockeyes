## Paragraphs

Decisions concerning player eligibility have reversed the outcomes of two Saskatchewan Amateur Hockey Association playoff series and delayed the outcome of another. The Wynyard Juveniles were stripped of their provincial juvenile AA title Monday by the SAHA after four of their players were declared ineligible for being affiliated with more than one other team. A similar situation applied to the Central Butte Flyers, who lost their southern midget C title because they used an ineligible goaltender. As well, the Eston Ramblers' victory over the Watrous Winterhawks in the -of-three southern senior B final has been wiped out by the SAHA. The teams have been ordered to play a sudden-death final to decide the championship. Wynyard lost its juvenile AA (19- and-under) title after a protest by the Allan Flames, who lost to Wynyard in a semi-final game at the sixteam championship tournament in Delisle. The Juveniles went on to defeat the Kindersley Klippers in the final. Allan maintained the four Wynyard players were ineligible by citing a SAHA rule which states "at all times a player may be affiliated with only one team" in addition to his regular club. The four midget-aged (17-and-under) players - Ryan Martin, Dave Lendzyk, Sean Fewster and Pat Lalach played for the Juveniles in Delisle. Allan protested because the four also saw occasional duty with the senior Wynyard Monarchs. "We are being penalized because we elected to use players from our town," said Randy Martin, Wynyard's coach.
