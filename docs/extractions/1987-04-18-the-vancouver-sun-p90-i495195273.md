## Paragraphs

Richmond Sockeyes are going to the Centennial Cup. The B.C. Junior Hockey League champions qualified for the national tier two finals by blasting Red Deer Rustlers 9-1 in Thursday's seventh game of their rowdy western semi-final. Richmond now moves on to meet Saskatchewan's Humboldt Broncos for the Abbott Cup, also known as the western Canada title. In most years, the winner of the Abbott Cup would be the sole western representative in the Centennial Cup tournament. However, since Humboldt is host team for the 1987 tourney, , the 'Eyes are in regardless of how they fare against the Saskatchewanchamps. Sockeye coach Orland Kurtenbach doesn't feel this will take any, edge off the best-of-seven series that begins Monday in Humboldt.
