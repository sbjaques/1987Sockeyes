## Paragraphs

The Langley Eagles are hot on the trail of the Richmond Sockeyes - despite trading their top marksman to the Coastal Division leaders. The B.C. Junior Hockey League Eagles are three points behind Richmond in the race to the playoffs thanks to a string of wins in their past four games. Three of those wins were recorded without the services of top scorer Bryan Moller. Eagles coach and general manager Muzz MacPherson said some observers have questioned the trade, but he's sticking by it. "We gave them (Richmond) a lot of points" by trading Moller, MacPherson' conceded. "We'll miss him." In return for Moller who started with the Eagles this season - the team has received defenceman Philip Von •Stefenelli. "We needed a defenceman who could move the puck well,' MacPherson said of his former understudy.
