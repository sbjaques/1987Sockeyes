(CP) The Richmond Sockeyes exploded for three goals in the third period to beat Pembroke Lumber Kings 4-1 Monday night in the Centennial Cup junior A hockey championship tournament. The win gave the British Columbia champions a 2-0 record in the round-robin portion of the tournament, and a share of first place with the Saskatchewan champion Humboldt Broncos. It was the second straight loss for the Lumber Kings, the Ontario representatives in the fourteam tournament. The other team in the championship event, the Dartmouth Fuel Kids from British Columbia, are also at 0-2. The teams that finish second and third in the round-robin portion of the tournament meet in a semi-final game on Thursday night. The winner of the semifinal tangles with the team that finishes first after round-robin play. Dave Tomlinson led Richmond with two goals, while Brian Kozak and Stan Czenczek had one each. Czenczek scored the winning goal at 6:35 of the third period, after his team held a 1-0 lead through the first two frames. George Dupont scored for the Lumber Kings, who were outshot 26-25. The Sockeyes were assessed 11 of 15 minor penalties.
