## Paragraphs

Sockeyes tumble before Broncos Canadian Press HUMBOLDT, Sask. - Humboldt Broncos won a place in the final of the Centennial Cup junior A hockey championship tournament last night with a 6-1 victory over Richmond Sockeyes. The losing Sockeyes, with a round-robin record of 2-1, will meet Pembroke Lumber Kings in tonight's semifinal. The final will be played Saturday night. In their previous two games in the round-robin, the Broncos and Sockeyes had identical goals for (11) and against (4) records. Bill McDougall and Garnett Kazuik led Humboldt with two goals apiece, while Duncan Ryhorchuk and Curtis Chamberlin had singles. The win gave Humboldt, from Saskatchewan, a 3-0 record in the round-robin portion of the tournament. Stan Czenczek scored the Richmond goal. The two teams were tied 1-1 after the first period but Humboldt moved ahead 4-1 in the second. The Broncos outshot the B.C. champion Sockeyes 37-19 in a scrappy game that saw Humboldt take 15 of 27 minors. Both teams took two five-minute majors and two game misconducts. Humboldt also picked up two 10- minute misconducts. The Lumber Kings, from Ontario, finished 1-2 in the round-robin section of the tournament.
