Edmonton's Liz Czenczek is aiming to defy the odds in a comeback bid after a four-year absence from international field hockey. University of Alberta Pandas and Canadian junior women's team coach Dru Marshall is convinced Czenczek, 28, is capable of making 5 the national women's team again. Czenczek has played for the provincial team since leaving the national program after playing at the 1988 Seoul Olympics. She'll learn at a Dec. 27-Jan. 8 training camp at Rutgers University in New Jersey if she can make the big step back to the international level. "She hasn't lost too much," said Marshall. "She's a top student, tremendous athlete and one of my assistant coaches with the Pandas." Czenczek didn't seek a position with the team as it prepared for this year's Barcelona Olympics because she wanted to concentrate on her studies. She was accepted into the physiotherapy faculty at the U of A and wants to give international competition another try after earning her degree.
