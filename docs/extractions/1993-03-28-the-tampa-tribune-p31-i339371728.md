## Paragraphs

Many within the Tampa Bay organization believe Bergeron is their goalie of the future, but like Littman, he lacks NHL experience. Look then for the Lightning to protect Jablonski, whom many scouts believe will blossom into one of the league's best goalies, and take a chance that no one selects Bergeron. Defensemen: The Lightning can protect five in this category and four spots will automatically go to Joe Reekie, Shawn Chambers, Marc Bergevin and Bob Beers. As a first-year pro, Roman Hamrlik is exempt and does not have to be protected, so losing him is not a concern. Likewise, Chris LiPuma and Shawn Rivers are also exempt, so Tampa Bay can therefore decide between Matt Hervey, Eric Charron and Alain Cote for the fifth spot. Charron and Cote are new to the organization and probably won't get much consideration. So look for the club to protect Hervey, whose offensive capabilities are highly regarded. Forwards: Brian Bradley, John Tucker, Chris Kontos, Rob Zamuner and Adam Creighton are givens here. That leaves four open spaces on the protected list and three of them almost assuredly will go to Rob DiMaio, Marc Bureau a and Mikael Anderson. That leaves one spot to be filled by either Danton Cole, Steve Maltais, Steve Kasper, Randy Gilhen or any one of six Atlanta prospects. Chances are that none of the players from Atlanta - Jason Ruff, Dave Capuano, Shayne Stevenson, Keith Osborne, Tim Bergland and Jason Lafreniere will make the protected list. At 32, Kasper probably won't either. Age won't keep Maltais off the list. Statistics will. After a promising exhibition season, the winger has produced only y six goals and 12 assists through 54 games. Cole has similar numbers (nine goals, 12 assists) but he and Gilhen are both considered excellent penalty killers, so they will likely be the two most heavily considered for the final spot on the protected list. And after long consideration, don't be surprised if Esposito opts to protect the 30-year-old Gilhen instead of the 26-year-old Cole. After all, Esposito has wanted Gilhen on his team for more than a year. He isn't likely to let him go very easily.

Talk about playing games. The Tampa Bay Lightning did that figuratively so some Atlanta Knights could do that literally. The International Hockey League playoffs begin April 17, but rosters had to be set last week. The Knights have qualified for the playoffs, but with so many Lightning injuries of late, most of Atlanta's top players are playing with Tampa Bay. The IHL rule states that postseason players must be in the same city as the rest of the team on the day rosters are set. So players such as J.C. Bergeron, Jason Lafreniere, Jason Ruff, Matt Hervey, Keith Osborne, Dave Capuano, Shawn Rivers and Tim Bergland flew into Atlanta last week on the day rosters were set. Then, some of them flew right back out to join the Lightning. "It's a crazy rule," Lightning President/General Manager Phil Esposito said. "They have to be there. Most importantly, though, we want those players to be able to play in the playoffs for Atlanta." The Knights' postseason roster looks like this: Goalies: Bergeron, David Littman, Mike Greenlay. Defense: Hervey, Jeff Buchanan, Alain Cote, Eric Charron, Scott Boston, Chris LiPuma and Cory Cross, who is currently with the University of Alberta. Forwards: Osborne, Capuano, Ruff, Bergland, Jock Callander, Brent Gretzky, Shayne Stevenson, Stan Drulia, Jean Blouin, Herb RagIan, Christian Campeau and Marc Tardiff, who is with Sherbrooke of the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League.
