While playing with the Richmond Sockeyes (1983-84), Tuttle was an allstar forward and one of the top five scorers in the British Columbia Junior League. As a Badger freshman, though, he had to make an adjustment to a higher level of competition and a faster pace. He contributed only three goals in 28 games. Essentially, he was overshadowed by the players in his recruiting class Dean Anderson, Garry Bunz, Micky Doner, Pat Ford, Ken MacKenzie, Scott Mellanby, Vic Posa, Glenn Revak and Ranheim.
