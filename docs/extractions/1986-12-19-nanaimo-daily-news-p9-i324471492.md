Although New Westminster Bruins had Stotts as a regular this Western Hockey League season, he declined their offer. He's in no rush to leave Nanaimo. "They've got a good organization here and with the new money coming in, I thought this would be al better year than last year," said Stotts. "I'll probably come back here next year. I'd like to eventually play in Europe or maybe at a Canadian collegeided Stotts, "I really like it here. It's a great place. It's a lot bigger than Courtenay so there's more things to do." At five-foot-nine, 200 pounds, Stotts may be considered too small by National Hockey League standards. "I guess the NHL is in the back of everyone's mind. I'd.like to play in the NHL, but the odds are pretty slim." Stotts has 16 goals and 49 points heading into tonight's game against first place Richmond Sockeyes (8 p.m. at Civic Arena). He hasn't set any personal goals. "I'd like to help carry our team as far as we can," he said. "Actually I'm kind of disappointed because with the talent we have, fourth place isn't that good. I'm really not too sure what the problem is. If we knew, we wouldn't be in fourth place. 1 still think we have a slim chance to finish first and a good chance to finish second."
