his dues By BRIAN SMILEY Sun Staff Writer Rick Tabaracci has been touted to be the next Grant Fuhr by many hockey observers. And like Fuhr, the Winnipeg Jet goaltender will have to pay his dues in the minors. Yesterday Tabaracci was given the bad news that he was to report to farmclub Moncton Hawks. He'll be joined on the flight eastward by defenceman Todd Flichel, who was also assigned to the Hawks yesterday. Flichel, 6-foot-3, 195-pounds, really never distinguished himself in the two pre-season games. The demotion of Tabaracci was also no great surprise in light of two pre-season games he played, allowing 10 goals in 60 minutes worked. "Yeah, you could say I'm a little disappointed," said a subdued Tabaracci. "I thought the camp opened up pretty well for me. The two pre-season games were tough. "I didn't play as well as I could have." Obtained last June along with Randy Cunneyworth and Dave McLlwain from Pittsburgh Penguins, Tabaracci was touted to be a strong challenger for the Jets' No. 1 goaltender slot. The battle is now cut down to Daniel (The Bandit) Berthiaume, Pokey Reddick, Bob Essensa and long-shot contender Tom Draper. It was quite evident from the pre-season that Tabaracci would benefit greatly with some seasoning in the American League. Age is definitely on his side. Just 20 years old (he turns 21 Jan. 2) Tabaracci will be likely be given ample ice time with the Hawks. A confident young man, the product of Cornwall Royals heads to Moncton with a positive attitude and definite goal in mind. "I'm just going to keep working hard. I have to play as well as I can and hopefully I'll be up as soon as I can. "It's (Moncton) pro hockey so that's better than playing junior so that's going to help out no matter what. Whether it was up here or in Moncton, it's going to be a lot different that last year. It's going to be a good experience."
