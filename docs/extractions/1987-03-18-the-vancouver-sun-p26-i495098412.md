VICTORIA - Brent Hughes scored his third goal at 14:29 of the third period Tuesday night to lift the Victoria Cougars to a 6-5 win over the New Westminster Bruins in a Western Hockey League game. Hughes scored on a two-on-one break, electing to shoot the puck along the ice to the stick side of New Westminster goaltender Ray Woodley. Wayne Stripp, Clayton Young and Guy Burr added a goal each for the Cougars, who led 4-2 after two periods. The first period ended tied 2-2. Cal Zankowski scored twice for the Bruins. Mario Desjardins, Darren Schwartz and Stuart Sage had a goal each. Woodley faced 39 shots while Victoria netminder Mike Doyle was tested 35 times. Each team was assessed eight minor penalties. Bruins play Thursday at Queens Park Arena against Seattle.

By ELLIOTT PAP It wasn't difficult for Kelowna Packer coach Bill Hogaboam to analyze his team's stunning 7-0 loss to Richmond Sockeyes in Tuesday's opener of the B.C. Junior Hockey League championship series. The Packers, who posted a 45-5-2 record during the regular season, were an embarrassment last night, their uniforms about the only thing showing up at Richmond Arena. "They outhustled us all over the ice," said Hogaboam unhappily. "They bumped us and took the body all night. We lacked intensity. We didn't force them in their own end and they moved the puck fairly easily. "It's hard to believe we were that flat for the whole 60 minutes. Approximately 1,400 people attended the service, including some of Hayes' former players among them Paul Warfield, Archie Griffin, Art Schlichter and John Brockington - and a former rival from Michigan, coach Bo Schembechler. In 33 years of coaching -28 as the head man at Ohio State - Hayes compiled a remarkable 238-72-10 record. He died of a heart attack last Thursday at the age of ' 74. Soccer great retires Socrates, one of the last great Brazilian soccer stars, has decided to cut short his career by six months to return to his previous profession - physician. His contract with Flamengo of Rio de Janeiro was due to expire in September but the 33-year-old Socrates decided to terminate it when he announced Monday the end of a career that included trips to two World Cup campaigns, in Spain in 1982 and Mexico last year. WHL clubs fined The Western Hockey League fined the Spokane Chiefs and Victoria Cougars $2,500 each Tuesday and announced suspensions following a pre-game scuffle last Friday in Victoria. Spokane coach Peter Esdale and Wayne Naka, coach of the Cougars, along with players Tony Horacek of the Chiefs and Kent Lewis of Victoria, were suspended for three games.
