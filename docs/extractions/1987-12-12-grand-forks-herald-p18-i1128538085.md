Sports Clipboard 3 area players named • to 9-man all-state team Three players from Section 8A have been named to the Minnesota 9-man all-state high school football team. Area selections include Kennedy quarterback Jared Hagen, Argyle offensive lineman Mark Donarski and Hallock offensive lineman Shawn Stewart. : Hagen passed for more than 1,700 yards and threw for a school-record 22 touchdowns in leading Kennedy to a 7-3 season. The honors continue to mount for Stewart, a senior center. He's already been named to the Associated Press and Minneapolis Star-Tribune all-state teams as a linebacker. The versatile senior also is a quality placekicker who led Hallock to the Minnesota a 9-man state semifinals. Donarski, one of the finest guards veteran Argyle Coach Warren Keller said he has coached, also was a strong two-way performer. In addition to leading the way for the Eagles' backs, he led the defense in tacklers and defensive points. from the area were Plummer's Wade Grinde and Honorable mention picks to the all team Marshall County Central-Newfolden's Lee Pemberton. Entzion is NDSU finalist UND assistant athletic director Bob Entzion has made a list that narrows the candidates for the athletic director position at North Dakota State University to three, a school official said Friday. A search committee has sent the three names to interim President Robert Koob, who will make the final selection, said George Ellis, NDSU sports information director. 17 f Koob hopes to make his choice before Christmas, Ellis said. The other finalists are Ross Fortier, athletic director and head football coach at Moorhead, Minn., State University; and Douglas Hastad, chairman of the physical education department at Texas Christian University, said Dr. David Forbes, search committee chairman." More than 20 people applied for the job earlier this year after Ade Sponberg resigned to become athletic director at the University of Wisconsin.
