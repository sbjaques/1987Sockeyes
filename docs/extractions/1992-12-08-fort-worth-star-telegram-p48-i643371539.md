BY DAVID BOCLAIR Fort Worth Star-Telegram Mike McCormick has been misunderstood for much of his hockey life. His size - 6-foot-3, 225 pounds - has often pigeonholed him within the sport. Playing for the Fort Worth Fire, he figured, would be different. "[Head coach] Peter Mahovlich was a big forward," he said. "I'm a big forward. He appreciates me and wants me to play hockey - and not just go out and goon it up." Even Mahovlich, however, has been swayed by McCormick's size. For much of the past three weeks, the coach has moved his beefiest player to the defense adding not only size, but talent to the team's weakest area. "He's done a great job," Mahovlich said. "I am looking for some help so we can move Mike back up front." Assistant coach/defenseman Steve Harrison added: "He's big and strong and good at lugging the puck out of our zone. He's been a big plus.' McCormick said he has played little defense in his life but says the increased playing time is to his liking even if the position itself isn't his first choice. The native of Lynnwood, Wash., proved his skill up front with two goals within two minutes in the Fire's 8-1 victory against Tulsa on Nov. 13.
