CHARLOTTE - The expansion Carolina Panthers are not in any hurry to hire a coach. Having been turned down by Joe Gibbs, the Panthers decided to play the field. They start playing in 13 months. "What we're trying to do is sit back and observe right now," said Mark Richardson, director of business operations. "There are probably a dozen coaches that we'll keep a close eye on this year. We're watching them at all levels. There's some college coaches, there's some NFL coordinators and NFL head coaches that we're keeping track of." Trading cards show different Simpson ANAHEIM, Calif. - The latest O.J. Simpson trading cards show him running from the law, not on the football field. D & K Distributors sold thousands of the "In Pursuit of Justice" cards, which show police pursuing Simpson and arresting him. D & K plans to issue a set of 50 cards that will include a picture of the blood-splattered walkway where the slayings occurred, said Lowell Katz, a sports card dealer. "They weren't made in bad taste even though the whole subject is," Katz said. McMichael: tough goodbye to Chicago CHICAGO - Steve McMichael spent 14 years with the Chicago Bears, so it shouldn't have been a surprise when the defensive lineman had difficulty signing with the Green Bay Packers, Chicago's NFC Central rival. When McMichael started to sign the second page of his contract, the ink ran out. The pen had a Bears insignia on it. Atlanta signs pair of quarterbacks SUWANEE, Ga. The Atlanta Falcons signed their fourth-round draft pick and two veteran free agents. The free agents are quarterback Bob Gagliano, who has played eight NFL seasons, and veteran wide receiver Jason Phillips. Both were on the Atlanta roster last year. The rookie is quarterback Perry Klein, a 6-2, 218-pounder who set NCAA Division I1 passing records with 38 touchdown passes and 3,757 yards at C.W. Post.
