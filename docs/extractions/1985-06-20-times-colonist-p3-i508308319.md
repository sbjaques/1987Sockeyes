## Paragraphs

The Canadian Press VANCOUVER - The coachless New Westminster Bruins continue to wheel and deal in the Western Hockey League. The Bruins completed their second major trade when they sent winger Larry DePalma and midget prospect Marty Prazma to Saskatoon Blades for winger R.J. Dundas, who had 18 goals and 24 assists last season. On Monday, the Bruins traded goaltender Lance Carlson and left-winger Craig Berube to Kamloops Blazers for four players - left-wingers Brian Bertuzzi and Pat Mangold, defenceman Brian Benning and midget prospect Pat Bingham. Kamloops then sent Carlson to Portland Winter Hawks for wingers Trevor Hendry and Dave Mackey. New Westminster has been without a coach and general manager since May 17 when Al Patterson resigned and signed with Victoria Cougars. Bruin owner Ron Dixon has handled the trades. He also announced that defenceman Stan Czenczek has agreed to play for the Bruins after one season at the University of Maine. Czenczek, who played midget hockey in White Rock, was an all-star in the B.C. Junior Hockey League in 1983-84 with Nanaimo Clippers.
