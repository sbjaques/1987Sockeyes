The Boston Red Sox resolved one of their free agent problems Tuesday by agreeing with Rich Gedman on a one-year contract that reportedly makes him the highest paid catcher in the American League. Colleges: Sports come first College football and basketball players spend more time on their sports than on academics, according to a study released Tuesday. The survey was commissioned by the NCAA Presidents Commission. Football and basketball players spend an average of 30 hours a week on their sports even though most do not expect to make the professional ranks. And nearly all believe that earning a college degree was "of greatest importance" or "important" when they began their studies. Basketball: No clearance for Bedford William Bedford, the Detroit Pistons' 7-foot-1-inch center, had been expected to get clearance Monday to rejoin the Detroit Pistons, but drug counselors at the Adult Substance Abuse Program in Van Nuys, Calif., did not give their approval. The counselors said that Bedford, who was suspended without pay last March after he sought help for cocaine addiction, lacked the proper mental attitude to return. Locally: Squirt hockey team wins The Dayton Squirt All-Stars (10-11-year olds) won the Team Michigan Invitational Thanksgiving Hockey Tournament at Detroit last weekend by winning four games: 8-0 over Livonia, Mich.; 6-2 over Burlington, Ont.; 4-1 over Hamburg, N.Y., and 6-0 over Mississauga, Ont., in the championship game.
