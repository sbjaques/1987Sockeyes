## Paragraphs

The boys' relay team (Brad Gien, Tom McNabb, Hamish Tucker, Bob Tucker) was third in 200 medley relay and third in 200 free relay. The next Sockeyes meet is in Revelstoke on July 9-10. Almost all club members will attend this meet and head coach Hamish Tucker has set his sites on winning the team aggregate trophy. The Sockeyes would like to thank the families and businesses of Salmon Arm for their generous support of the annual swim-a-thon, the main fundraising avenue for the club.

Volunteers were kept busy counting laps as the members of the Salmon Arm Sockeye swim club held a fund-raising swim-a-thon last week. IS THAT ENOUGH? Younger swimmers tried to swim 100 lengths while seniors attempted 200. About 55 swimmers participated. Pledges are still being collected so the amount raised is not known yet.

Sockeyes yes

Salmon Arm Sockeyes swam to a second team aggregate finish at the Merritt swim meet on the weekend.

Marsh estimated the team's operating budget will run around $100,000. "It's not cheap to run a junior hockey club. People don't get involved in junior hockey to make money." "We feel we've got a good coach," said Couch. "We had a young team last year, but expect lots of returning players this year. We're not looking to place fourth or fifth in the league. think we can be right up there." Marsh said his team will play 60 games this year.
