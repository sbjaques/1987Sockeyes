Ballard was unavaiable for comment Tuesday, but he said in a written statement issued by the team that Brophy has "done a tremendous job as coach. . . and he fully deserves this contract." Terms of the new contract weren't revealed, but Brophy said he was pleased with his new salary. Assistant coach Garry Lariviere was also given a three-year contract Tuesday. Brophy, 54, joined the Toronto organization in 1984 as the Leafs' assistant coach, then became coach of the team's American Hockey Leagueaffiliate in St. Catharines, Ont., in 1985. He was promoted last July after Maloney left to join Winnipeg. Under Brophy, the Leafs record was 32 wins, 42 losses and six ties - an improvement over their 25-48-7 record in 1985- 86.
