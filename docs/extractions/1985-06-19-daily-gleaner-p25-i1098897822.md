## Paragraphs

Canadian Press Tom Watt is expected to become the ninth head coach of the bumbling Vancouver Canucks on Thursday. Watt apparently will give up the cozy confines of the University of Toronto campus for the second time in five years to join the Canucks. He left the Blues the first time in 1980 to become an assistant coach in Vancouver. His initial visit to the West Coast lasted a year. Watt bolted from the Pacific Coliseum to become head coach in Winnipeg, was named coach of the year in the NHL in 1982 and by November 1984 had been fired by Jets' general manager John Ferguson. You'd think Watt would have learned that pro hockey is a dirty business. Just ask Harry Neale, the affable general manager of the Canucks for the final 60 games last season. Neale was hung out to dry by management while the owners searched for a new superboss to become the director of hockey operations. After six weeks in limbo, Neale was finally fired and replaced by his silent assistant, Jack Gordon. Jack Who? Gordon is the new superboss after the Canucks were turned down by Sam Pollock, Scotty Bowman, Al Arbour, Keith Allen and who knows how many other experienced NHL executives. Watt wasn't the team's first choice to be coach, either. Dave King of the Olympic team accepted the job, but gave it back, all five years on an $800,000 contract. Lorne Henning, an assistant coach under Arbour with New York Islanders before spending a year in the minors as a head coach, could have had the Vancouver job, but turned it down when the Canucks apparently wouldn't match his minor league salary. Now the Canucks have settled on Watt, providing they can pursuade the Toronto native and agent Alan Eagleson to sign a contract. Watt will inherit a troubled team from Neale, who joined the Canucks in 1978 and saw Vancouver make the playoffs every season until 1984-85. Vancouver finished 23 points behind fourth Los Angeles Kings and had the only losing record in the Smythe Division. The Canucks are getting old in goal with Richard Brodeur, are too young defence to be effective and didn't have a 40-goal man on the forward lines. Neale, Bill LaForge, Roger Neilson, Orland Kurtenbach, Phil Maloney, Bill McCreary, Vic Stasiuk and Hal Laycoe have coached the Canucks during their first 15 years in the NHL. Watt will attempt to be successful where they have failed; no Vancouver coach has had a winning record, although Neilson came close at 51-61-21. Watt left the Canucks on good terms in 1981 to become head coach of the Jets. Winnipeg improved by 48 points that winter while the Canucks went to the Stanley Cup final with Neilson after a league suspension to Neale. It was downhill after that for both Watt and the Canucks. Watt eventually was fired by the strong-minded Ferguson after his coach had a run-in with scoring star Dale Hawerchuk, one of the NHL's most gifted players.
