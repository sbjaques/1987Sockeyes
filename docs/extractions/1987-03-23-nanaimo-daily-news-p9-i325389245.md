By The Canadian Press The availability of two key players could affect the playoff chances of the Kamloops Blazers and the Victoria Cougars, the clubs' coaches said Sunday night as the teams finished the regular Western Hockey League schedule. The Blazers will likely be missing all-star centre Rob Brown when they meet the Cougars Thursday night in the first game of their best-of-nine West Division semifinal. And the Cougars are hoping Buffalo Sabres of the NHL will soon be eliminated from playoff contention so they can release Ken Priestlay. Brown suffered an ankle injury as the Blazers set a goal-scoring record they downed the New Westminster Bruins 7-3 while the Cougars bowed 6-4 to the Portland Winter Hawks. The Spokane Chiefs thumped the Seattle Thunderbirds 7-3 in the other game. "We need a few days to get healthy and hope that Buffalo gets eliminated from the NHL playoffs," said Victoria coach Wayne Naka, referring to Priestlay, who had 82 points in 33 games with Victoria, is on emergency recall with the Sabres. 'A lot of people looking at the playoffs don't expect Kamloops to lose to anybody but we didn't get into the playoffs to die in five. There have been times that the Victoria Cougars have played well and have beaten the Kamloops Blazers. The key is winning one of the first three games." During the regular season, Victoria beat the Blazers five out of 14 games, winning three in a row against Kamloops which finished the WHL season at 55-14-3, the best record in Canadian major junior hockey. WHL playoffs begin Tuesday in the East Division with the Swift Current Broncos visiting the Prince Albert Raiders for the first game of their best-of-five' first-round matchup. On Wednesday, Prince Albert is at Swift Current and Spokane opens the other best-of-nine West Division semifinal in Portland. Clippers capture B.C. Bantam title Nanaimo Bantam B Clippers bounced back from a 10-6 loss Saturday to North Shore Winter Club to beat the same team 9-3 Sunday and win the provincial Bantam B hockey championship in Kamloops. The win caps a great year for the Clippers, who ran away with the Island title to advance to the provincial tourney. In the title game, Paul Scorer fired a hat-trick, while tourney Most Valuable Player Justin Heathcote scored twice. Jim Carpenter, Brad Dunn, Troy Miller and Steve Sloan added singles, while Mike Feser played an outstanding game in goal for the Clippers. In the Saturday loss, Dunn scored a pair of goals, while Lonnie Sullivan, Scorer, Jon McColm and Sloan scored singles in a losing cause. Earlier Saturday, Nanaimo beat defending champions Kamloops 7-2, with Scorer notching the hat-trick. McColm with two, Sullivan and Dunn also scored for the winners.
