By DARRON HARGREAVES Sun Staff Writer HUMBOLDT - The Humboldt Broncos are known to have searched at least a couple of the world's corners for talent. No fewer than 14 players on their 23-man roster hail from places other than Saskatchewan. They come from B.C., Ontario, P.E.l., Boston. The most extravagant of these imports calls Lulea, Sweden, home.

A 9-3 thrashing at the Uniplex last night all but ended Selkirk's chances of advancing past the Humboldt Broncos in Centennial Cup quarter-final play. "We've got too many good players hurt, too hard a series to get here,' said dejected Steeler coach Frank Porco. "It's not enough. It's as simple as that. "We'll come out tomorrow and give our best shot. That's all we can do.' The win gives the Broncos a 3-0 series lead, following two wins in Selkirk. "This was a very pivotal game for us," said Bronco coach Bernie Lynch. "We know about the Steelers' history of coming back after being down 2-0 and winning. "Now we've gotta go for the jugular." This game was all Humboldt. "We came out hard and kept it up," said Kevin Luke, who had three goals for the Broncos.
