MADISON, Wis. - The Madison Monsters got their power play rolling and it showed Saturday night in a 5-1 Colonial Hockey League defeat of the Quad City Mallards. Madison broke its power play drought Friday night in handing the Mallards a 4-1 loss at the Dane County Expo Center. "Our power play came up with three goals and I'm very happy with h the whole weekend," said Monster Coach Mark Johnson, whose club performed before a sparse crowd of 2.597. On the power play, Johnson said, "We worked on it the other day in practice" noting "you are going to go through some dry periods in an 80-game schedule." The Monsters' Matt Loen paced the Madison win with a hat trick, scoring a goal in each of the three periods. It was Loen's second hat trick of the year. Loen's third tally came near the end of the last period on a power play when he flipped an end over end wrist shot over Mallards' goalie Darryl Gilmour who was down on the ice from his previous stop. Prior to that, Madison's Brian Downey scored a short-handed goal on a tough angle to Gilmour's right. Starting with better than 17 minutes without a score, Madison came alive in the first period striking twice within nearly a minute. An unassisted goal by Brett Larson brought Gilmour to his knees and a second score by Marcel Richard was lifted into the net past Gilmour a little more than : a minute later.
