## Paragraphs

The defense tandem of Dave Nonis and Stan Czenczek did not allow any UNB goals and Nonis scored one; fellow defenseman Scott Drevitch played well; center Dave Wensley was chosen as the game's third star; right winger Dewey Wahlin made the pass that led to Steve Santini's goal, and center Paul Lelievre "set up two goals although he didn't have an assist to show for it" according to Walsh. Based on Friday's game, Walsh has made a few adjustments for Tuesday's Hockey East opener at New Hamp shire. Junior defenseman Scott Smith, who had played right wing against UNB, will move back to the blue line where he will be paired with John Baker. Smith will take the place of Roger Grillo. Drevitch will be paired with Comeault and the Nonis-Czenczek tandem will remain intact. "We need Scott back on defense and we have good depth up front," explained Walsh. Smith's place on a line with Santini and John McDonald will be taken by either Pete Maher, who has been sidelined with a severe groin pull, or Mike Hernon. Hernon or Maher will center the line. The other three lines will remain the same.
