Clippers' Al Johnston fends off Richmond Sockeye player Furlan's netting catches Sockeyes in BCJHL battle

Frank Furlan wasn't about to let a lucky goal by Richmond Sockeyes' David Holmes get on his nerves Saturday night at Frank Crane Arena, The goal, at 18:51 of the second period would be the last one Richmond would get as Furlan performed magic in the Nanaimo Clippers' net the rest of the way in a 5-4 Clipper victory before 350 B.C. Junior Hockey League fans. Holmes tied the score at 4-4 when he dumped the puck in off the end boards and watched in amazement as the puck took a wild bounce off Furlan and entered the Nanaimo goal. "This happens to me all the time in Beban," said Furlan. "'They don't get the edge (of the ice) properly hesitated though and made the wrong move. It's routine, I should have had it ..." The rookie goalie stopped Richmond sniper Henry Reimer twice from 'gimme' range early in the third period with the speedy Sockeye forwards foreing the Clipper defence into all kinds of shoddy clearing passes which developed into Richmond scoring chances. Furlan then made a superb pad save off. veteran blueliner Rock Baykey's blast from the slot and seconds later followed with a chest save off a Baykey wrist shot. Keith Street would later be foiled by Furlan on a clean break in front, but it was Bruce Major who shook his head the loudest when he was robbed by Furlan in the final minute. "I put my head in it and said 'I'm not going to lose it for you guys'," said Furlan of his third period performance. "You gotta be really happy with Frankie's play," said Clipper coach Doug Harding, who will start the 16- year-old again tonight when the Clippers take on Sidney Capitals in an 8 o'clock start at Frank Crane Arena. NICE GOAL Mitch Poulin's goal at 3:08 of the final frame on a breakaway stood up as the winner. Poulin made a nifty move to get past Street at the Richmond blueline and despite the fact that Street threw his stick, Poulin managed to 'manoeuvre puck around the stick and beat Sockeye goalie Glenn Pencer along the ice. • Dale Brisco with two goals, Chris Calverley with his first, BCJHL goal and 'Al Chatlain rounded out Nanaimo's scoring. Calverley's tally was a beauty as he rushed down left wing and from a sharp angle rifled a snapshot into the far side of the net.
