By GREG MEACHEM of The Advocate RICHMOND, B.C. - If Red Deer Rustlers find Richmond Sockeyes to be in a surly mood in tonight's opening game of their Western Canada Centennial Cup semifinal series, they shouldn't be surprised. After all, the Sockeyes' general manager-coach is none other than Orland Kurtenbach, who earned a reputation as a hard-nosed, no-nonsense player during an NHL career that featured stops in New York (Rangers), Toronto and Vancouver. "Yes, I like a physical game, but I also like to see a lot of puck movement," he offered Monday during practice in preparation for tonight's 8:30 p.m. MDT contest. "What I don't like is a lot of goony stuff. There's nothing wrong with good, tough hockey as long as it doesn't take away from the flow of the game."
