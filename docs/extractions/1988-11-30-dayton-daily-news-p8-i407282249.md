deer hunter killed One hunter was killed and five others were hurt on the first day of gun season for deer in Ohio, police said Tuesday. Donald McClain, 18, of Lucasville, was killed Monday in Pike County when he fell and his 12-gauge shotgun discharged, the sheriff's department said. Two hunters were killed and 14 were hurt in 1987's six-day deer gun season, the Ohio Division of Wildlife said. This year's gun season ends Saturday at sunset. Race car driver Gordon Johncock has been cited for allegedly poaching a deer on land he owns near Hastings, Mich., a state Department of Natural Resources officer said. Johncock, cited for possession of an untagged deer and for hunting with a .270-caliber rifle in a shotgunonly zone, has until today to answer the charges or face arrest, according to the DNR. Boxing: Solid gold sentiment Employees at the Pensacola, Fla., Naval Air Station gave a solid-gold medal Tuesday to boxer Roy Jones Jr. to replace the one they believe he was cheated out of at the Summer Olympics. Co-workers of the boxer's father, Roy Jones Sr., at' the base's Naval Aviation Depot contributed $650 to buy nearly 3 ounces of 14-karat gold that went into the medal inscribed "A Solid Gold Medal for a Solid Gold Champion, Roy Jones." Baseball: Gedman stays with Red Sox The Boston Red Sox resolved one of their free agent problems Tuesday by agreeing with Rich Gedman on a one-year contract that reportedly makes him the highest paid catcher in the American League. Gedman accepted a contract that reportedly will pay him slightly more than $1.1 million. Lance Parrish; at $1 million, has been the highest paid American League catcher. Colleges: Sports come first College football and basketball players spend more time on their sports than on academics, according to a study released Tuesday. The survey was commissioned by the NCAA Presidents Commission. Football and basketball players spend an average of 30 hours a week on their sports even though most do not expect to make the professional ranks. And nearly all believe that earning a college degree was "of greatest importance" or "important" when they began their studies. Basketball: No clearance for Bedford William Bedford, the Detroit Pistons' 7-foot-1-inch, center, had been expected to get clearance Monday to rejoin the Detroit Pistons, but drug c counselors at the Adult Substance Abuse Program in Van Nuys, Calif., did not give their approval. The counselors said that Bedford, who was suspended without pay last March after he sought help for cocaine addiction, lacked the proper mental attitude to return. Locally: Squirt hockey team wins The Dayton Squirt All-Stars (10-11-year olds) won the Team Michigan Invitational Thanksgiving Hockey Tournament at Detroit last weekend by winning four games: 8-0 over Livonia, Mich.; 6-2 over Burlington, Ont.; 4-1 over Hamburg, N.Y., and 6-0 over Mississauga, Ont., in the championship game. Jeff Hamilton of Englewood was named the tournament MVP as he scored 12 goals. Matt Neff of Springboro starred in goal for Dayton.
