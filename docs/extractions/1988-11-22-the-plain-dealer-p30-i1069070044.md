Boxing Ray (Boom Boom) Mancini and Hector (Macho) Camacho will stage their comeback fight March 6 under a formal agreement reached Saturday. The Reno-Sparks Convention & Visitors Authority in Reno, Nev., reached the agreement with promoters Joe Gagliardi of San Jose, Calif., and Lou Falcigno of New York for the bout's site rights. Under the agreement, the authority paid $650,000 for the site rights and will receive the first $750,000 in gate receipts. The promoters will receive the next $50,000, with the authority and promoters splitting the money after that. The authority will not share in any television money. Awards Barry Sanders and Rodney Peete, leading candidates for the Heisman Trophy, were named to the 1988 Kodak Coaches All-America Team. Sanders, a junior from Oklahoma State, became the third running back in college football history to rush for more than 2,000 yards. Peete, quarterback for Southern California, is the sixthranked passer in the nation. The senior has 185 completions in 294 attempts for 18 touchdowns and 2,429 yards. Also chosen were running back Tim Worley of Georgia; wide receivers Erik Affholter of Southern Cal and Jason Phillips of Houston; tight end Marv Cook of Iowa; offensive linemen Tony Mandarich of Michigan State, Anthony Phillips of Oklahoma, Mark Stepnoski of Pittsburgh, Mike Utley of Washington State and Steve Wisnewski of Penn State, and placekicker Kendall Trainor of Arkansas. On defense were linemen Tracy Rocker of Auburn, Mark Messner of Michigan, Broderick Thomas of Nebraska, Derrick Thomas of Alabama and Bill Hawkins of Miami; linebackers Britt Hager of Texas and Jerry Olsavsky of Pittsburgh; backs Deion Sanders of Florida State, Louis Oliver of Florida, Darryl Henley of UCLA and Donnell Woolford of Clemson, and punter Pat Thompson of Brigham Young. Football Woody Widenhofer. 45, resigned under pressure as Missouri football coach with a four-year record of 12-31-1. Widenhofer's resignation followed weeks of speculation that his contract, which expires Jan. 31, would not be renewed after he finished 3-7-1 overall in his fourth season. Uhlenhake, a senior co-captain from Newark, also won the Archie Griffin Award for outstanding offensive player and was honored as outstanding offensive lineman.
