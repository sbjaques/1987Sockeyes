D As they make CFL debut WINNIPEG (CP) - The expansion Birmingham Barracudas made their CFL debut with a decisive 38-10 victory over the veteran Winnipeg Blue Bombers Tuesday night. In a game that astonished the announced crowd of 22,200, the untried Alabama club walked all over the Bombers without any help from star quarterback Matt Dunigan. Dunigan, formerly a Bomber, faced his old teammates from the sidelines as he nursed a broken finger that also kept him out of action in the pre-season. Pivot Jimmy Klingler, a 22-year-old who spent last season with Saskatchewan, led Birmingham in its first regular season CFL game. But it was defensive back Andre Strode who scored the 'Cudas first touchdown on an interception. "When a defensive player does that, it kind of sets the tone for the night," said Barracuda coach Jack k Pardee. He was happy with the quarterbacking too. "For Jimmy Klingler to step in like that, and Reggie Slack, well, it makes me feel good." In the other dressing room, Bomber veteran Chris Walby decried the lack of "chemistry" on his team. "It don't take long to go from the penthouse to the outhouse and we're sinking in that mire," said the despondent offensive lineman. The Bombers are now 0-2. The 34-year-old Savard was among 72 National Hockey League players who were offered termination contracts by their clubs and were set free to start testing the market on , Tuesday. A player offered a termination contract has 30 days to negotiate a contract with a rival team. If he fails to catch on with another club, his team can buy out the option year of his contract and release him or keep him for one more season.
