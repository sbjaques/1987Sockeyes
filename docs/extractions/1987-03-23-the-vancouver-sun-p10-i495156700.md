Sockeyes one more win away

Kamloops star hurt in finale Kamloops Blazers learned a painful lesson in their pursuit of Western Hockey League records Sunday n night. Scoring champion Rob Brown, who had already established four individual scoring marks this season, suffered an ankle injury in the third period of Kamloops' final regular season game, a 7-3 victory over New Westminster Bruins at Queens Park Arena. "We're certainly hoping the injury isn't any more serious than a bad sprain but even at that, Brownie will still miss the first three, maybe four playoff games against Victoria,' said Kamloops coach Ken Hitchcock. "If it is bad, someone else will have to come to the forefront. You don't replace a guy like Rob. He's the best player in the league." The Blazers did manage to break the WHL record for most goals in a season as their total of 496 eclipsed the 1982-83 standard of 495 set by Portland Winter Hawks. The Bruins, meanwhile, concluded their dismal campaign at 18-50-4, going 2-22-1 in their last 25 games. They were 1-14-1 against Kamloops alone. "I don't worry about bad seasons because I've had many more good than bad ones and nobody remembers the bad ones anyway," said New Westminster coach Ernie McLean. The West Division best-of-nine semi-finals open this week with Victoria's Cougars facing Kamloops and Spokane Chiefs meeting Portland.

Sun News Services Richmond Sockeyes are in the driver's seat - and have the motor running - in the B.C. Junior Hockey League championship playoff series. The Sockeyes took a seemingly insurmountable 4-0 lead in the best-of-nine series against Kelowna Packers with a 5-1 win in the Okanagan city Saturday, A Sockeyes' win Tuesday night at Richmond Arena (7:30) will give them a series sweep. "The guys are on a natural high," said Sockeyes' coach Orland Kurtenbach. "It was a nice game for us to win. If they're willing to listen and cut down mistakes, we'll do well at home (Tuesday)." The Sockeyes scored three unanswered goals in the last half of the third period en route to the Saturday win. Jason Philips opened the Sockeyes' scoring with a short-handed goal in the first period. Dave Tomlinson extended the margin to 2-0 early in the second before Brent Mowery scored Kelowna's only goal at 13:23 of the middle frame. Richmond's third-period insurance markers came from Philips, Bill Hardy and Mike McCormick.

Special To The Sun WINNIPEG - Vancouver Canucks lost a hockey game they needed desperately to win here Sunday afternoon, a situation they found painfully familiar. To long-suffering Canucks fans, to say nothing of long-suffering Canucks' coaches, it must seem their star-crossed heroes have spent most of their 17-year National Hockey League history in similar straits - coming up just short in critical situations. Sunday's burden was a 3-2 loss to the Jets, fashioned before an audience of 13,832 at Winnipeg Arena. It was the 74th game of the season for both teams and yielded the Jets' 38th win and the Canucks' 41st loss. In addition to the two points, the Canucks also squandered a brilliant 46-save performance from goaltender Richard Brodeur. Even more painful were the circumstances. Midway through the second period, the out-of-town scoreboard showed the Whalers had defeated Los Angeles Kings 6-3 in Hartford. Victory would have allowed the Canucks to close within four points of the Kings - and the Smythe Division's fourth and final playoff berth. But the Kings' loss proved insufficient motivation to kick-start the Canucks' sputtering offence.
