Injury-prone Adams out four to six weeks Greg Adams, the Canucks forward whose career has been disrupted by a series of major injuries, is hurt again. Adams could be out until March with a severe charley horse suffered Saturday. Adams was hit by Whalers winger SLAPSHOTS and Nick flew Kypreos, into who Adams, left his his knee feet smashing into the Canuck's right thigh, his elbow just missing Adams' head. "It's quite a bad charley horse," team doctor Doug Clement said. "It definitely looks like four weeks, maybe six. It could be less if, in two or three days, there is a good response. But it is not a minor problem." There was no penalty on the play and the Canucks are contemplating sending a tape of the hit to league headquarters. GM Pat Quinn was livid after the game. "I'm very aggravated by that," huffed Quinn. "(Kypreos) not only went at him with the knee, but the elbow, too. If he missed with the knee, he would have taken his head off." Shannon's back with big club: Defenceman Darryl Shannon rejoined the Maple Leafs yesterday after spending two weeks with the St. John's Leafs for conditioning purposes. Shannon was sent down Jan. 2 after dressing for only three of Toronto's first 37 games. The addition of Shannon brings the Leafs roster to 24 players. Rookie forward Dave Tomlinson is expected to rejoin St. John's at the end of the Leafs' three-game road trip. Lindros might get roommate: The Philadelphia Flyers might assign rookie Eric Lindros a roommate. "'We're looking at it right now," Flyers GM Russ Farwell said yesterday. "It's a matter of not being completely by yourself." Lindros, charged earlier this season with aggravated assault in Oshawa, confirmed following Saturday's 5-4 win at Boston that he was present during an altercation on Jan. 9 in New Jersey. Barrasso does have chicken pox: Pittsburgh Penguins goalie Tom Barrasso has the chicken pox after all. Barrasso, who last week was diagnosed with the contagious disease before team officials said he had the flu, indeed does have chicken pox, Harry Sanders, a team spokesman, said last night. Sanders said he could not explain the changing diagnoses. Barrasso, 27, will miss tomorrow's game in Vancouver and Friday's in Edmonton.
