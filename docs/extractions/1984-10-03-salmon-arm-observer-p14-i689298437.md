## Paragraphs

The Salmon Arm Totems dropped two weekend games in B.C. Junior Hockey League action. On Saturday the Totems were edged 7-4 by Merritt and on Sunday the Sidney Capitals dropped them 8-4. "It was a disasterous weekend. Nobody played that well for us. It wasn't a good weekend for us, period,' says coach Don Stephen. Stephen says the players are learning his weave system but now have to learn when to use it. "They basically know what to do. Now they have to learn when to do it.' Stephen says they had a clean game against Merritt with the Totems picking up only three penalties and Merritt five. "Merritt is looking pretty good. They're a fast-skating, well-disciplined, well-coached (by Chuck Tapp) team. They're going to win some games this year." "But we gave them two points and only played 20 minutes of the game and almost beat them so I think when we're playing the full 60 minutes we'll be able to handle them." Merritt led 2-1 after the first frame and then moved ahead 5-1 after the second. In the third the Totems narrowed the gap but Merritt won 7-4. Albert Anthony, Troy Pohl, Tim Coghlin, Mike Robertson and Tony Bobbitt scored for the Totems.
