It should be a great experience." Dial, 20, is a right-winger on the Islanders' top scoring line with Darren Thompson and former Cowichan Valley Capital Bob Court. "I'm mostly an offensive player, like to go to the front of the net," said Dial. "I've got great linemates. They're both ahead of me in scoring.' Dial, who began his minor hockey career in Nanaimo 12 years ago, has produced 30 goals and 87 points with Kerry Park. He played Pee Wee hockey under Louis Beliveau, Bantam under Ric Ferris and Midget for current Clipper coach Rich Dutka. In Midget, Dial often played on a line with current Clipper Chris Calverley and Randy Brown. Strangways expects a tough series with Toyo. "They've got four divisions of eight teams and they're the top team," he said. "Other than that, we don't know much about

In Midget, Strangways played alongside Gord. Simpson, Jay Barner and Mark Schwager, a trio of ex-Clippers. Barszczewski is an 18-year-old Powell River product who spent last year with the Clippers and was a victim of the numbers game in Nanaimo this season. He was quickly grabbed by the Isles. "It was either play in Kerry Park or I wasn't going to play hockey this year, said Barszczewski. "I'd like to play Junior A again next year, but not here. It would depend on who's coaching here...The big difference from Junior A and Junior B is the speed and there's a lot more hitting in Junior A. Junior B isn't as serious either." As for the Japanese opposition, Barszczewski has heard firsthand reports. "I talked to a couple guys who have been there. One guy I know from Notre Dame played there for two and a half years and he said they have a lot of big guys." Asked if the Isles were serious about winning the five-game series, Barszczewski said, "We want to kick their butts." In addition to hockey, the Islanders will spend two days at a popular Japanese resort for some rest and relaxation. The Islanders take 17 skaters to Japan March 6 and return March 22. Kerry Park's playoff schedule will open upon their return from Japan.

By The Canadian Press Nanaimo product Bob Foglietta scored once as Moose Jaw Warriors edged Prince Albert Raiders in one of five Western Hockey League games played Wednesday night. In other games, Saskatoon beat Calgary 4-2, Regina got by Brandon 5-4 in overtime, Portland defeated Seattle 6-4, and New Westminster downed Spokane 5-2. Jerome Bechard's goal late in the third period lifted the Warriors to victory over the Raiders. Bechard's goal came at 17:39 in the third when he jammed his own rebound under Prince Albert goalie Kenton Rein. RICHMOND, B.C. (CP) Richmond's Dean Rutledge scored two goals as the Sockeyes defeated the Langley Eagles 5-2 in B.C. Junior Hockey League play Wednesday. The teams were scoreless after one period but the Sockeyes took a 3-0 lead after 40 minutes. Bruce Kenward scored both goals for Langley, which was outshot 61- 33.
