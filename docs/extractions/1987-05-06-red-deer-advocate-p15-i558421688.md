## Paragraphs

Rustlers honored Red Deer Rustlers, who came within one game of a berth in the Centennial Cup final, have been named the Carling O'Keefe-Radio 7 Central Alberta athletes of the month for April. Rustlers won the Alberta Junior Hockey League crown and took the Richmond Sockeyes to seven games in the Western Canada Centennial Cup semifinals. Bev Meyer, who won the Alberta five-pin bowling championship, and the Red Deer Moose Chiefs, who won the Alberta bantam hockey title Meanwhile, a pair of Rustler helpers trainer Terry McQueen, and promotions man Darryl Kuntz - were selected to share volunteer of the month honors.

The Ontario champions will meet the loser of today's final round-robin game between the Humboldt Broncos of Saskatchewan and the Richmond Sockeyes of British Columbia, who both have 2-0 records in the tournament. The semifinal game will be played Thursday. Bruce Coles led Pembroke to its first victory after two losses with two goals and three assists. Chris Clarke and Peter White each had two goals for the Lumber Kings, while Scott Mohns and Mike Eastwood had singles. Jasmin Breton, Steve Brown, Ron Petrie and Brian King scored for Dartmouth, the Nova Scotia representatives who lost Minor baseball North Star Sports rode the arm, and bat, of Terrill Trautman to a 9-4 win over McAuley's Electric in Red Deer senior 'A' Little League play Tuesday. Trautman and Todd Opatril each rapped a pair of singles for North Star Sports while Jerett Terlesky had two singles and Shane Opp a single and a double for McAuley's Electric. Ball hockey draw Bemoco and the Dairy Queen played to a 3-3 tie in Red Deer Ball Hockey League play Tuesday. Dan Bell, Dan Dobler and Scott Wnek shared the Bemoco scoring while Jeff Gosselin, Darren Tymchyshyn and Gordon Pruden replied for the Dairty Queen. TOP JUNIOR NAMED Cindy Laszlo was named overall champion of the weekend Archers Nook annual junior tournament. Laszlo topped the 10- 12 age class. joining Heidi Schmid (four-six).
