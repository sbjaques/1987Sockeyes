BRONCOS Mike Gaber RICHMOND, SERIES| overtime Tuesday night to give the Humboldt Broncos a 4-3 victory over the Richmond Sockeyes and new life in their Centennial Cup playoff series. The victory forced a seventh and deciding game in the Western Canada final here Wednesday night. Bill McDougall led the Broncos with two goals and Curtis Chamberlain was the other marksman for Humboldt, which trailed 1-0 at the end of the first period and was tied 2-2 entering the third Paul led the periderford Sockeyes with two goals while Dave Tomlinson added the other goal for Richmond which was outshot 46-45. Humboldt, which got spectacular netminding from Bruce Hoffort who made 42 saves, scored twice on the power play while Richmond counted once with the man advantage. There were two key mistakes Richmond coach Orland Kurtenbach felt gave Humboldt the victory. The first occurred seconds after Tomlinson scored to put the Sockeyes ahead 3-2 at 7:44 of the third period. Tony Bobbitt took a penalty for closing his hand on the puck and McDougall tied the game on the power play. The second incident came early in the 10-minute full-time overtime when Steve Jaques took a five-minute match penalty for -butting McDougall behind the play. McDougall picked up a holding minor on the play but the Broncos scored while the teams were playing even strength and then just kept the pressure on the Sockeyes with their power play. Jaques who was with the Broncos earlier this year faces at least a one suspension. •In Medicine Hat, Alta., Brian Gerrits scored at 15:23 of the first overtime period to lead the Portland Winter Hawks to a 3-2 win over the Medicine Hat Tigers in the second game of the Western Hockey League final.

... It's nice to see Nanaimo product Al Hill alive and well with Philadelphia Flyers in the playoffs. A lot of younger, people I've been talking sports with have never heard of Hill. He celebrated his 32nd birthday in game three by scoring the winner in Philly's 4-1 win over the Islanders. He then added another goal in game four Sunday, a 6-4 Flyer victory. Hill, who spent most of the year with Hershey Bears of the American League, collected 47 goals and 123 points in two seasons (1974-76) with Victoria Cougars. Ignored by the NHL, but drafted in 1975 by Indianapolis Racers of the World Hockey Association, Hill signed with the Flyers as a free agent and his best year with Philly came in 1979-80 when he counted 16 goals in 61 games. His biggest fan locally is his brother-in-law Jim Bayliss, who can be seen over the winter performing linseman duties at Clipper games. Greg Heakes of the Medicine Hat News tells me defenceman Wayne McBean is drawing high raves from NHL scouts as the June amateur lottery approaches. McBean has played both the powerplay and shorthanded situations as the Tigers have skated into the Western Hockey League finals against Portland Winter Hawks. David McNab, chief scout with Hartford Whalers, flew to Alberta in February for a private dinner meeting with McBean. "The idea of interviewing the kids is new," said Medicine Hat General Manager Russ Farwell. "They (scouts) are trying to judge a guy's character at 17. It is a shot in the dark for most teams.' McNab figures McBean will go in the top five picks, but Montreal Canadiens' Wilson wouldn't be as specific, saying McBean would be chosen in the first three rounds. New York Islander sniper Pat LaFontaine will this summer become the second New York hockey player to marry a pro model. LaFontaine will wed Marybeth Hopy, therefore joining Ranger Ron Greschner in model marriages, Greschner of course has been featured in every magazine there is along with his wife, super model Carol Alt. Humboldt Broncos had won 19 straight games until Richmond Sockeyes beat the Saskatchewan junior champions 5-3 Sunday night in Richmond. The Sockeyes have now played 27 post-season games and boast an impressive 21-6 record. You've got to wonder why the two clubs simply didn't play of series in Humboldt to decide the Abbott Cup. It would have made economical sense since both clubs are in the Centennial Cup tournament in Humboldt. Keith Gretzky appears to be doing alright in attempts to keep up with brother Wayne. The Hamilton Steelhawk forward was judged in the Ontario Hockey League coaches poll as being among the top two playmakers, smartest players and penalty killers.
