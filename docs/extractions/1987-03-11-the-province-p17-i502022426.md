By FRANK LUBA Sports Reporter Richmond Sockeyes coach Orland Kurtenbach knew his squad was going to be in for a tough time against Delta Flyers last night, he just didn't know HOW tough. It took a goal by Mike McCormick three minutes and 19 seconds into the second overtime period to give the Sockeyes a 7-6 victory against the Flyers in the opening game of the B.C. Junior Hockey League's Coastal Division championship. Delta really opened like Flyers, soaring to a 3-0 first period lead and going up 4-0 early in the second period before Richmond • narrowed the gap to 4-2. The second period ended with the visitors ahead 5- 3. They went up 6-3 in the third before the veteran Sockeyes fought back for three unanswered goals. Matt Hervey scored the key goal on the power-play at 16:54, sandwiched in between the second and third goals of the evening by Dean Rutledge. Rutledge's tying goal came with 51 seconds to play from a scramble in front of goaltender Jeff Clark. The Sockeyes outshot the

Flyers 12-5 in the first 10-minute sudden-victory overtime but there was no scoring, setting the stage set for the second suddenvictory overtime period. Rutledge led the scoring parade with his three goals and three assists. Linemate Dave Tomlinson added a goal and four assists. Also scoring for the Sockeyes was Steve Jacques. Coastal Division scoring champion Lance Momotani broke out of his playoff scoring slump with three goals for Delta. Peter Holmes added a goal and two assists, as did Jeff Kitsch, one of New Westminster Bruins' players on the team (along with Drew Sawtell). Randy Olson had the Flyers' other goal.
