## Paragraphs

By BILL SEYMOUR Herald-Tribune staff Victories don't go hand in hand for the North Stars. Grande Prairie was riding the wave of several victories that appeared to have them destined to finish second in the Peace Cariboo Junior Hockey League, when a disasterous encounter in Dawson Creek last night burst their bubble. Going into their last regular game of the season the North Stars needed only a tie or a win to break a tie for second place with the Fort St. John Huskies, who they defeated Saturday night 8-2. But the fifth place Capitals snatched the opportunity from the euphoric North Stars with a 4-1 win in a game that quickly turned the mood of Grande Prairie to one of soured disappointment. The result of some pre-victory celebrations the night before in Grande Prairie was not just a poor showing against the Capitals, but more importantly, the loss of home-ice advantage in the first round of playoffs. Third place Grande Prairie will now meet second place Fort St. John tomorrow night on the Huskies' home ice as the PCJHL best-of-seven semifinals begin. In the other series, the regular season champion Prince George Spruce Kings host the fourth-place Quesnel Millionaires. Yesterday, the Spruce Kings also wound up their season on a losing note as the cellar-dwelling Williams Lake Mustangs won 7-6 in overtime. On Saturday, Prince George dumped Quesnel 5-2.
