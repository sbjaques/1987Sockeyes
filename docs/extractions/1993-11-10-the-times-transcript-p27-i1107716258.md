By Dwayne Tingley * Sports Editor Moncton Hawks centre Dave Tomlinson admits he wasn't concerned : about defence when he played junior hockey in British Columbia. These days, however, Hawks coach Rob Laird calls Tomlinson the best two-way forward in the American Hockey League. "In junior, I didn't exactly rush back into the defensive zone," Tomlinson recalled. "I was saving all of my energy for the other end." Tomlinson, 24, has been the Hawks biggest offensive threat this season. • Heading into tonight's game in Fredericton, he's scored 11 goals in 12 .games, but there's much more to his game than filling the net. • "He's one of the best penalty-killers I've seen and he never stops checking," Laird said. "He can be a real pest defensively and he's also one of the league's : top scorers," the coach continued. "'He's a complete player and a very valuable member of our team." After playing Tier II junior in Richmond, B.C. Tomlinson went to * Boston University, where he had an outstanding four-year stay. After 4 his second year, he was the third overall selection in the 1989 supplemental draft by the Toronto Maple Leafs. He stayed at university, where he was twice named the league's defensive forward while establishing himself as the school's sixth alltime leading scorer. "That's when I first learned about smart defensive play," Tomlinson said. "My coach at BU (Jackie Parker), really opened my eyes and made me a more complete player." Upon graduation, he turned pro with the AHL's St, John's Maple Leafs. He played for two seasons on The Rock, where he caught the eye of Laird. "Dave Tomlinson killed us last year and I've always wanted that guy on my team," the Hawks coach said. "He's an NHL prospect who's a major contributor to the club in Moncton. I was really happy when we were able to acquire him this summer." The Leafs dealt Tomlinson to the expansion Florida Panthers, who sent him to the Winnipeg Jets organization for malcontent centre Jason Cirone, who played in Italy last winter.
