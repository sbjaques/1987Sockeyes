Bob Probert, the former Detroit Red Wing tough guy, knew nothing about an alleged $1 million scheme to fix the cocaine-smuggling case against him, a federal prosecutor said. A federal grand jury in Buffalo, N.Y., on Tuesday indicted a Canadian on charges of trying to bribe an undercover agent posing as a U.S. customs service agent. The agent was asked to lose the evidence against Probert, the indictment alleges. Dan DeFrancesco of Hamilton was charged with offering a bribe, obstruction of justice and conspiracy, and faces 15 years in prison and fines of $3 million. Assistant U.S. Attorney Kathleen Mehltretter said in a telephone interview from Buffalo that DeFrancesco, in his mid-50s, is alleged to have told the undercover agent he "was working on behalf of others so Mr. Probert could play hockey again." In Detroit, U.S. Attorney Stephen Markman said the bribery case was thoroughly investigated and Probert was cleared of any wrongdoing in connection with the scheme.

Chotowetz joins Pats By L-P Sports Staff Defenceman Kelly Chotowetz makes his Regina Pats debut Friday, when the Moose Jaw Warriors visit the Agridome. The Pats acquired Chotowetz, 19, from the Swift Current Broncos on Wednesday for future considerations. The Major-born Chotowetz, who is in his third Western Hockey League season, has also seen duty with the Saskatoon Blades and Tri-City Americans. "He's steady and experienced," said Brad Tippett, the Pats' head coach. "You can never have enough of those guys." Chotowetz's presence will bump Jamie Heward back to his customary right wing position. Heward had played defence the past two games. Swift Current had obtained Chotowetz from Tri-City on Oct. 3 for goaltender Don Blishen and centre Kyle Reeves. Chotowetz has one goal, no assists and 65 penalty minutes in 12 games this season. The 6-foot-2, 200-pound Chotowetz apparently was unhappy that Swift Current has no postsecondary educational facilities. The Broncos were willing to deal him because of a surplus of young blue liners. "We have 16-year-old defencemen (Wade Sambrook, Stu Scantlebury and Kurt Seher) and we're going to start giving them ice time," said Broncos assistant coach Lorne Frey.

From L-P Wire Services Detroit Red Wings coach Jacques Demers says no deal has been struck to acquire disgruntled forward Jimmy Carson from the Edmonton Oilers. Rumors of an impending trade between the two National Hockey Legaue clubs were fuelled Wednesday by comments from Carson's agent, Bill Watters of Toronto, who said a multi-player swap "is as good as done." "I think it is as done as it can be without announcing it," Watters said. "It (trade talks between Edmonton and Detroit) has been going on for three days and I suspect today (Wednesday) & Notes is the day." Apparently, the Oilers and Red Wings are negotiating a four-for-two trade, with Carson, who walked out on the team in mid-October, and Kevin McClelland heading to Detroit for forwards Petr Klima, Adam Graves, Joe Murphy, defenceman Jeff Sharples and a sizeable amount of cash. Klima, Graves and Murphy skated with the Red Wings on Wednesday in preparation for a night game against the visiting Philadelphia Flyers. "If we were going to announce a trade, do you think we would play those guys today?" said Demers. "If it is a done deal, the players would not be in the lineup tonight.'

By Rob Vanstone L-P Sports Writer Jackson Penney answered one question Wednesday, creating another in the process. The Prince Albert Raiders announced that the 20-year-old centre would report to the Western Hockey League team, ending 10 days of speculation about his future. But his arrival also gave the Raiders four 20-year-old players, one over the WHL limit. And the WHL's deadline for declaring those three 20-year-olds just happened to be Wednesday. Because of Penney's last-minute arrival, the WHL has apparently granted the Raiders an extension in declaring their chosen three. Prince Albert is expected to unload one player shortly. Right wingers Terry Black and Steve Young and defenceman Todd Nelson are the Raiders' other 20- year-olds. Prince Albert will release one of those players unless a trade is made, perhaps with the Portland Winter Hawks. Portland, with only one 20-yearold, is the lone WHL team under the limit. No other WHL team can accommodate an additional 20-yearold without releasing another. Black came to Prince Albert from Portland on Oct. 17 in a four-player trade. Terry Simpson, Prince Albert's head coach and general manager, said earlier this week that he had a contingency plan in case Penney arrived. The Raiders had acquired Penney from the Victoria Cougars on Oct. 22 for left-winger Terry Bendera, but Penney had refused to report. Penney, a 41-goal scorer last season, was then suspended. In Wednesday's other move, the Medicine Hat Tigers reached the limit by releasing 20-year-old centre Kent Dochuk. They elected to keep 2% year-old left-winger Norm Batherson instead.
