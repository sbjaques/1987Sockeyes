FOOTBALL: Buffalo Bills defensive end Bruce Smith, who led the team in quarterback sacks last season, was charged after allegedly punching a man in the face at a city bar early this morning, police said. Smith, 6-foot-4 and 278 pounds, was charged with 4-degree assault, a misdemeanor, after punching John Wallace ( of Buffalo in the face at about 2:25 a.m., according to city police. GENERAL: Visiting professional baseball, football and hockey players may be slapped with a 1 percent tax on their wages earned in Pittsburgh under a proposal being studied by city officials. The tax would be imposed only on the portion of a player's salary earned in Pittsburgh and only on players who live in states without a personal income tax or who live in foreign countries. HOCKEY: Former New York Islanders defenseman Gord Lane has been named head coach of the Springfield Indians of the American Hockey League. Lane, 34, had served as a player and assistant coach for the Islanders farm team in Springfield last season. He replaces Fred Creighton, who announced his retirement after two disappointing seasons as coach of the Indians. PENTATHLON: West Germany took the lead over the Soviet Union in the women's Modern Pentathlon after officials released delayed results of scoring in the fencing competition. The West German team has a score of 5,866 to the Soviets' 5,842. The results dropped the U.S. team to third place overall, with a total score of 5,556 going into the swimming competition Thursday. TENNIS: At the Volvo International tennis tournament, 17- year-old Andre Agassi upset Pat Cash, this year's Wimbledon champion, in two tiebreaker sets Wednesday in Vermont.
