MOSINEE, Wis. (AP) - For the second time in two months, the Mosinee School Board has voted to keep calling its sports teams the "Indians," setting up a possible court battle over the issue. "I voted to keep the name Indian for one simple reason: I don't think people are discriminating against the American Indian," board member Bernice Czenczek said. "I think it's an honor. Nobody knows prejudice more than myself," she said. "I survived the Nazi regime." The 7-2 vote came after Barbara Munson, an Oneida Indian whose daughter graduated from Mosinee High School in 1992,
