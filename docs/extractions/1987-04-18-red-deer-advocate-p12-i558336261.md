### Region 214,3985 → 777,5892

In the case of the Red Deer Rustlers, the disappointment was all too real and a touch bitter after dropping the Alberta-B.C. junior hockey playoff series to the Richmond Sockeyes Thursday. Placed in proper context, however, Red Deer's season must be considered a winner. Not only for the team but for junior hockey here. Crowds were always around 1,700 or better in the final 11 home playoff games which allowed management to balance the ledger. On the ice the Rustlers showed junior hockey has a place here. Off the ice the vast majority of fans showed they want it. The less said about the clowns, who nearly had the series cancelled after game four, the better. The true fans, to a large extent, helped the club. Their support was applauded by management. Their support I'm sure, spurred the club on when the going was rough or a series hung in the balance. And how many of those do we remember so well? The Rustlers pulled two stunning upsets by knocking off the favored Calgary Canucks in seven games and the St. Albert Saints in six to win the Alberta championship.

### Region 2665,1323 → 3239,4835

The mind was willing, but the legs wouldn't cooperate. Red Deer Rustlers, playing their 27th post-season game in 47 days, ran out of gas Thursday at the Arena. They were no match for the Richmond Sockeyes, who claimed a convincing 9-1 victory in the seventh and deciding game of the western Centennial Cup tier two junior hockey semifinal series. "There were too many (playoff) games in too few nights. It was a gruelling schedule and it took its toll," Rustler coach Larry Billows said sadly. "'The guys wanted it, but there was no intensity tonight. They just had nothing left." The Sockeyes, bigger and more talented than the Alberta Junior Hockey League champion Rustlers, blew open a scoreless game with five unanswered second-period goals. From there, it was simply a matter of if, and when, Red Deer would ruin goaltender Jamie Stewart's shutout. Pete deGraaf notched the lone Red Deer goal at 13:14 of the third period, deflecting Greg Pulliam's point shot into the Richmond goal with the Rustlers on a power play. The single goal was small consolation for the Rustlers, who dropped the first two games in Richmond, rallied to win three in a row at the Arena, then lost the final two. "I thought we were the better club the last three games," said a relaxed Orland Kurtenbach, the Sockeyes' general managercoach. "Certainly we deserved to win the last two, and the (7-5 Red Deer win) overtime game (Tuesday) could have gone either way. "I'm proud of these guys. They worked extremely hard." Equally as proud of his charges was Billows, who moved behind the Red Deer bench Feb. 4, just one month before the playoffs started. "I'm very happy with the way these guys played for me," he stressed. "I've never coached a team with so much character. There's a lot of heart in this dressing room." Rustler captain Pulliam agreed. "I'm just proud to be a part of this team," said the 20-year-old defenceman, who played the best hockey of his career along the playoff trail. "This team never quit, we never saw the end coming. "It seemed that there was always a light at the end of the tunnel. Even in the third period tonight, the light was still there."
