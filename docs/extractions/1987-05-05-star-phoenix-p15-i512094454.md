HUMBOLDT - Question: If Humboldt Broncos and Richmond Sockeyes were willing to battle each other like life-long enemies throughout a seven-game series which meant nothing, what will they do in a single game to advance to the Centennial Cup final? Richmond and Humboldt will meet in the final game of the Centennial Cup round robin at the Uniplex on Wednesday, with the winner earning an automatic berth in the championship game Saturday night. The loser will face either Pembroke Lumber Kings or Dartmouth Fuel Kids in the semi-final on Thursday.

"Those other teams had to be champions to get here, but we know who we have to beat to win the Centennial Cup," said Richmond defenceman Steve Jaques, after the Sockeyes defeated Pembroke 4-1 on Monday to pull even with Humboldt at 2-0 in the Centennial Cup standings. "It'll be a big game on Wednesday.' The Sockeyes needed two thirdperiod goals from Dave Tomlinson to stop the Lumber Kings, who fell to 0-2 in the tournament.
