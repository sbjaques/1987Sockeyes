By MONTE STEWART Herald-Tribune staff The North Stars were just a Head below their opponents. Quesnel Millionaires came from behind to defeat Grande Prairie North Stars 6-5 in sudden-death overtime to take the first game of the Peace Cariboo Junior Hockey League championship series at Johnny Macdonald Arena last night. Game 2 in the best-of-seven series will be held tonight (9 p.m.) at Johnny Mac If the Stars prevail, the third game will take place tomorrow night (not Sunday as previously reported) in Beaverlodge, starting at 9 p.m. If the Millionaires come out on top, the third game will be held in Quesnel next Thursday. "Now we know what to expect from them,' said North Stars' centre Murray Head. "We know that we can play with them and we know that we can beat them. I don't think we have to change a thing.' The final outcome negated Head's four goals - all in the first period. "Those are kind of hard on the guts, games like that," said Quesnel coach Tom Marsh, relieved his club had pulled out the win after a day-long bus trip. Marty Peters' goal at 7:06 of suddendeath finally decided the contest, which, because of referee Gary Friesen's reluctance to permit powerplay chaations, often resembled a wresting match. The teams had gone scoreless in a 10-minute overtime session.
