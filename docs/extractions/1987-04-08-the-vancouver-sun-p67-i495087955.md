squad. The Rustlers embarrassed themselves, their coach and their league Tuesday night, barely showing up in an 11-4 loss to Orland Kurtenbach's Richmond Sockeyes. "That's the worst game I've ever seen us play," muttered Red Deer coach Larry Billows, who took over the club in February. "Our defence stunk and our goaltender (Glen Furgason) played probably his worst game. We weren't skating, we were floating. We didn't play with any aggression. On their last two goals, we just gave up." The only consolation for Red Deer is the fact this Centennial Cup western semi-final series is a best-of-seven, not total goals. Richmond now leads it 1-0 with game two tonight, 7:30, at Richmond Arena. "I thought they played tougher hockey out in Alberta," said Sockeye goaltender Frank Romeo. "I'm surprised we beat them this bad. I don't think they took us that seriously. But the next game will probably be completely different. It will probably be really close.'
