TAMPA - Rob Ramage said he wanted to finish his career with the Tampa Bay Lightning. Instead, he may have to settle for the Stanley Cup. The 13-year veteran defenseman, considered to be Tampa Bay's team leader, was traded to the contending Montreal Canadiens following the Lightning's Saturday. The Lightning received minorleague defensemen Eric Charron, 22, and Alain Cote, 25, and future considerations - a draft pick next June or a player off the Canadiens' non-protected list. Although Charron and Cote are needed in Tampa Bay, neither will join the club for its game tonight in Chicago. Instead, the Lightning have recalled four players - goalie David Littman, forward Jason Lafrenier and defensemen Chris LiPuma and Matt Hervey - from their Atlanta farm club. "It's all part of the game," said Ramage, 34. "I'd heard the rumors lately and I kind of expected it.
