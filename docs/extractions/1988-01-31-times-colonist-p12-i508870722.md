Basketball centre Cam Aronetz, 18, of Centennial in Coquitlam was selected the top high school athlete. The award for the master athlete went to skier Jane Morris of Vancouver who competes in the 40-44 age category. The other finalists were badminton's Jane Youngberg of Surrey and blind lawn bowler Paul Beregon of Victoria. The Sanders rink, which included third Georgia Hawkes, second Louise Herlinveaux and lead Deb Massullo, was selected B.C.'s team of the year over the Richmond Sockeyes junior hockey club and the sailing team of Ross and Bruce Macdonald. Blind and deaf wrestler Pier Morton, 28, of Burnaby won the disabled athlete award. The other finalists were wheelchair's Paul Clark of Terrace, the winner in 1985 and '86, and Special Olympics swimmer Kathy Davis of Victoria. Bruce Enns of the University of B.C., who guided the Thunderbirds to the Canadian final, was named the province's coach of the year. Named the top amateur sport official was soccer's John Meachin of West Vancouver.
