• Russian Andrei Chesnokow breezed into the second round of the $313,750 Swedish Open in Bastad by beating Ola Kristiansson of Sweden 6-3, 4-6, 6-2. Police continue to investigate shooting Police have questioned a man who said he and a friend were target shooting near the site where professional golfer Kim Williams was shot. Police would not release the name ofthe Howland Township man who said he may have accidentally hit Williams. GOLF Police said the man told them he was firing his gun at junk cars in woods near his home and one of the bullets from his 9mm semiautomatic pistol may have hit Williams. Howland Township Police Chief Steven Lamantia said it is possible the bullet that struck Williams was not fired from the man's gun. Williams was shot around 9 p.m. Saturday as she was walking into a discount store in Niles, Ohio, about 60 miles southeast of Cleveland. She was in town for the YoungstownWarren LPGA Classic. Williams, 31, of Boca Raton, Fla., left the hospital Monday. Canucks re-sign defenseman Lumme Defenseman Jyrki Lumme has signed a multiyear contract with the Vancouver Canucks, the team said. Lumme, 27, had 13 goals and 42 assists last season. He also accounted for 13 points - two goals and 11 assists - in the playoffs. HOCKEY The native of Finland has played for Vancouver for four seasons after being acquired from the Montreal Canadiens in March 1990. Falcons sign draft pick, veterans The Atlanta Falcons signed their fourth-round draft pick and two veteran free agents. The free agents are quarterback Bob Gagliano, 35, who has played eight NFL seasons and two in the USFL, and wide receiver Jason Phillips. MISC. The rookie is quarterback Perry Klein, a 6-foot-2, 218-pounder who set NCAA Division II passing records with 38 touchdown passes and 3,757 yards at C.W. Post. Former Mississippi linebacker Pete Harris said he received cash from coach Billy Brewer and an assistant coach while he was a Rebel player. Harris said he received about $50 from Brewer on one occasion and similar amounts from then assistant coach James "T" Thomas on several occasions. Harris' charge is the first public allegation of specific wrongdoing by Brewer.
