## Paragraphs

Junior hockey has come such a long way here in the past year, it would be a shame to lose its gains in one short, ugly series. The Red Deer Rustlers dropped out of the league last season, and came back determined to sell the game as an inexpensive, fun family outing. They succeeded, on and off the ice. After a halting start, the team responded to the direction of a new coach and is making a serious run for the Canadian title. Inexpensive season tickets filled the stands with vocal, loyal fans. But in the series against the Richmond Sockeyes, some of those "fans" have turned ugly. They have littered the ice after every Red Deer goal. They have pelted the Richmond players with eggs, and tried to fight them. These yahoos can scarcely be called fans. They are ruining the game and seriously impairing the Rustlers' chances of winning this series. The Canadian Amateur Hockey Association simply won't put up with these antics. The association is acutely aware of junior hockey's bad image, and has taken steps to improve it. Fighting has been reduced and stickwork curbed. The association can dictate what's unaceptable on the ice; it has less control over what goes on in the stands. If the louts in the Red Deer Arena persist, it has few options. It can forfeit the series to Richmond, cancel it completely, or move the remaining games to a neutral site. All three options are no-win propositions for the Rustlers. The Rustlers really need those games here. The players 1 need their fans behind them to help them defeat a more experienced team. And Rustler management needs the home-gate revenue to pay its bills and carry on. Once the game begins, Rustler security staff is almost powerless to control the impulsive actions of individual fans. But Rustler management can and must take actions before the game to limit the chances of another fiasco. Security staff can screen customers carefully as they enter the Arena, to ensure vegetables or other projectiles don't get in. Tight screening like this is routine at rock concerts and other sporting events. The true Rustler fans won't object to the inconvenience, because they know what is at stake, and condemn the yahoo minority responsible for the problem. Rustler management can also make an announcement before the game, outlining the consequences if the ice is littered, or Richmond players are pelted. After that, it's up to the fans themselves. If they want to see the Rustlers win this series, if they want junior hockey to endure and prosper in Red Deer, they have to discipline themselves.
