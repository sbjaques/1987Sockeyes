Any time between 9 a.m. and 2 p.m. show up at the community centre in 1 Salmon Arm and participate in the event. You can walk, jog, ride, or cartwheel along one of the three routes for the run. It's not anywhere near as strenuous as running all the way across Canada, like Terry Fox did, but it's nearly as important. There is no entrance fee for the event. Pledge sheets can still be picked up at selected businesses in Salmon Arm, or participants can make a donation at the race site if they would like. Any amount of money goes a long way in helping to make Terry's dream come true - that is, finding a cure for cancer, said Roy Dagneau, local director for the Terry Fox Run in Salmon Arm. There will be three routes for the local race with each starting and finishing at the community centre. You can go three kilometres, five kilometres, or 10 kilometres. All participants will get a certificate and free juice. T-shirts will be available for sale as well. The community centre is opening its doors for participants to swim and enjoy the hot tub for free following their 'run.' It is the eighth annual run for cancer research. Dagneau is hoping Salmon Arm will show the kind of community spirit it has by turning out 400 people for the event. For more information about the local Terry Fox Run, contact Dagneau at 832-2247 or 832-4950.
