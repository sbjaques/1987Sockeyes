The North Stars lead the PCJHL bestof-seven semifinal 1-0 after walking into the Huskies' den Tuesday and slipping out with an 8-4 win. In the other semifinal, the Prince George Spruce Kings lead the Quesnel Millionaires 2-0 in games with game 3 tonight. "They stole one at our rink, so we're just going to have to work hard and try to steal one back at their rink, " says Webb, who was not too enthralled with losing home-ice advantage with the series' opening loss. If the Huskies are to "get even", however, Webb says they will have to "try to get everybody a little more emotionally involved and concentrate more on our defensive play". "We weren't very happy with our defensive play. We allowed two breakaways and that should never have happened." Although the North Stars' highpowered Dragon Line Murray Toews, Joe Dragon and Wade Wallan registered a five-point night, Webb felt it was held in check by linemate Blaine Moore, Mallory Mack and Jaret Wheeler. He will continue to use the trio against Grande Prairie's top line. "Their big line scored (four) goals, but you can't fault the (checking) line on any of them," said Webb, who realizes if he can pull the teeth of the Dragon he has a handle on the Stars offensive machine.
