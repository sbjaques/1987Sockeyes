Unanswered second period power-play goals by defenceman Steve Bancroft, his first of the year, and Bob Joyce, his 23rd, rallied Moncton from a 2-1 deficit after 20 minutes into a 3-2 lead after 40 minutes. In the third period the Hawks displayed their best mental toughness by killing off four straight Maple Leaf power-play opportunities in the opening 10 minutes. Then, quick goals by Rob Murray at 14:17 and John LeBlanc at 15:31 cemented their victory. Scott Levins had Moncton's first period goal while Yanic Perreault scored a brace for St. John's and Ted Crowley got a single. Moncton goaltender Mark Richards, improving his record to 4-3-1 since being summoned from Toledo of the Eastern League to replace injured Mike O'Neill, was also instrumental in the come-from-behind triumph. Richards stopped 33 of 36 St. John's shots, including all seven St. John's managed on their long powerplay stretch in the third. "This might be our most pleasing win of the year," boasted a pleased Laird." "That's the team we'll probably meet in the playoffs so we're going to need more performances like this.' The triumph was Moncton's third in their last four games at home and jumped them 12 points ahead of fifth-place Halifax Citadels in the stretch run for the final playoff spot in the Atlantic Division. While Laird talked about the great mental toughness his team displayed, St. John's coach Marc Crawford could only talk about his disappointment with the work of referee Lance Roberts. "I just don't think it's positive for anybody when the players are not the story of the game," he said, disappointed with how Roberts worked the game.
