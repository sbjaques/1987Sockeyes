MOST OF the time, when you find yourself in strong wrestling divisions like Manchester and Marlboro do, you measure advances toward respectability in inches. But last night Manchester and Marlboro took giant leaps toward reverence with major upsets. Manchester, getting key victories from Shawn Wirth (171) and Bryan Morrison (189), stunned No. 3 Lacey, 36-30, in a Shore Conference Class B South match. Marlboro, winning all five bouts between 130 and 152 pounds, shocked No. 10 Middletown South, 30-27, in a Class A North match. Manchester won four of the first five bouts _ three by pin - and held off the Lions, who wrestled without starters Justin DiNicola (103), John Stout (140) and Todd Hunt (189). With Nick Meyers (103), Josh Blair (119) and Glenn Sheets (130) scoring pins, the Hawks bolted to a 21-6 lead. Lacey won four of the next five bouts and tied the score. 27-27. But Manchester got a lift from Wirth, who pinned Gary Willis in 5:22 and then a 7-4 win by Morrison over Mike Peterson at 189 pounds. Marlboro fell behind early, 6-3, but won the next six bouts for a 28-6 lead over Middletown South, which wrestled without Doug and Bill Kirk. Jason Greiner (145) and Augie Calabro (152) had pins during the Mustangs' rush while Mike Kilayko (140) added a key 10-3 win over Dan Cartagena.
