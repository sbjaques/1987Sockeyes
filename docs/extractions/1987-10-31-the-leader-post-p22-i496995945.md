Long before defenceman Milan Dragicevic arrived in Regina to play in the Western Hockey League with the Pats, the praise heaped on him by scouts was enormous. The labels read like this: Can't miss National Hockey Leaguer. Sure high pick in 1988 NHL entry draft. That heady praise still may have some credence to it but Dragicevic knows he has to perform much better than he has so far this season - if he wants to be selected in the first three rounds of next year's draft. "He's a good-sized kid and he's strong but he has to play tougher. That's the way he has to play because he hasn't shown me he's an offensive player," said one pro scout. "He has to become more involved in his end and in front of the net with authority.' Said Dragicevic: "I'm off to a really slow start. It's a matter of time before I break out of my slump." And by 'slump', he said he won't be pre-occupied with being an offensive threat (he has five points so far; last season, he had 17). What he wants to do is tidy his own backyard first. "I'm going to have to stay home, play defence and be aggressive and not worry about points and going up and getting caught," he said. have to get more involved in the play. "I wish I could've played 72 games instead of 41 games," said Dragicevic. "Thirty-0 games would've made a difference. I had all the confidence in the world going into that Russian series." Before the Yugoslavian native left for his stint with Team Pacific, the Pats had glowing words about his performance as a 16-year-old. Said assistant coach Dennis Sobchuk: "I marked him on his grades and he was above average and coming into his own before Christmas. He was showing some of his potential. But with the injury, he was coming back this year with only half a year of experience." Dragicevic said he was highly touted in part because he was the only 15-year-old defenceman playing in the Tier Two Alberta Junior Hockey League in 1985-86. With the Sherwood Park Crusaders, he recorded two goals and 18 assists. "That (high rating) doesn't put pressure on me," said Dragicevic. "The only pressure on me is the pressure I put on myself because I want to be the best that I possibly can be." Despite missing most of the second half of last season, Dragicevic played for Canada's under-17 team against the U.S. in a series of games last summer in Colorado Springs, Colo. "Making the team and playing with the best players in Canada was a great step for my future," he said. "I know what I'm up against in the draft."

LETHBRIDGE (CP) - The Lethbridge Hurricanes Friday announced a restructuring in their front office with the addition of Wayne Simpson as new general manager. Simpson, most recently with the New Westminster Bruins as director of player personnel, will take over a job which until now has been shared by two people. Team president Steve Arisman said Glen Hawker will continue his duties as head coach and relinquish his role as director of hockey operations, while Ray Austring will no longer be business manager but will continue to assist the club in various other areas.
