The Canadian women's field hockey team has been slowed slightly by illnesses and injuries after a six-week training camp in South Africa. The Canadians played short for five matches in as many nights against the U.S. last week in st Philadelphia. The Canadians finished 1-2-2 in the series. Liz Czenczek is one of five Edmonton players on the Canadian team. The veteran of the 1988 Seoul Olympics believes the players lacking international experience are making strides as they prepare for the 12-team Intercontinental Cup tournament July 14-25 in Philadelphia. "They're getting used to what playing internationally is about," said Czenczek, adding that ineludes communicating on the field and more physical play. "We still need defensive work," said Czenezek. "And we have to put the the net so we need to work on our corners." Conditions have been more difficult in Philadelphia than in South Africa, with temperatures as high : as 33 C and high humidity. But now they know what to expect next month. The Canadians are training in Norfolk, Va., this week and playing more exhibitions against the U.S. The last three weeks of intense training will be in Toronto starting next week.
