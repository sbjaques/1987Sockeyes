BASKETBALL Vols forward might be' academically ineligible Tennessee is waiting to see whether forward Marcus Haislip will be academically eligible to start the basketball season. Haislip, a junior from Lewisburg, Tenn., and a probable starter, might have to sit out the Volunteers' first games during the fall semester because of poor grades in the spring. "We might not know until the first day of classes, which i is next Wednesday," Coach Buzz Peterson said. If he can't play in the fall, Haislip could become eligible when the semester ends in December. He was projected to replace departed senior forward Isiah Victor. Sonics add center: The SuperSonics signed center Calvin Booth to a six-year, $34 million contract, his agent said. • RUNNING Olympic medalist in 10,000 meters dies: Richard Chelimo, a former world-record holder at 10,000 meters and an 1992 Olympic silver medalist in the event, died of a brain tumor at age 34. He was admitted to a hospital in Eldoret, Kenya, on July 26 and died Wednesday. HOCKEY Devil wins big in arbitration: Petr Sykora of the New Jersey Devils was s awarded one of the biggest percentage salary increases in NHL arbitration history when his pay was raised 389 percent yesterday. Sykora's salary will jump from $675,000 to $3.3 million this season as part of the two-year contract.
