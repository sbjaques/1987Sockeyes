WINNIPEG (CP) - The Vancouver Canucks chased the Winnipeg Jets goal-for-goal through a National Hockey League exhibition game at the Arena on Monday night and came away with a 3-3 tie. Dale Hawerchuk, Matt Hervey and Dave McLlwain scored for the Jets, while the Canucks, who never held a lead, replied with goals from Rich Sutter, Tony Tanti and Tim Lenardon. Vancouver played with a split squad and were without Soviet stars Igor Larionov and Vladimir Krutov. "In preseason games the wins and losses don't count, obviously, but the way you play the game is important," said Vancouver coach Bob McCammon. "'We had to come back three times with a team split in half so you don't have much cohesiveness. "It's encouraging and I think when we merge the two teams we'll be pretty strong.". Winnipeg, playing for an announced crowd of is 9,829, 1-1-1. raised its preseason record to 4-3-1, while Vancouver's record The Canucks outshot their Smythe Division rivals 27-24. Daniel Berthiaume, looking for a starting role in the Winnipeg net, kicked out 24 of 27 shots and was named the game's first star. "I just want to play my game and I don't want to say who will be No. 1 and who will be No. 2," said the diminutive goaltender who ended up with the Jets' minor league affiliate in Moncton during a rocky 1988-89 campaign. I keep playing the way I am right now I might have a chance. " The teams scored nine seconds apart in the opening period. Sutter fired a shot through traffic that eluded Jets netminder Daniel Berthiaume at 3:59, and Hawerchuk blasted a shot from the left wing between the legs of Vancouver starter Kirk McLean at 4:08. The scoreless second period was a clinic in poor power-play execution. The Jets stickhandled away a five-minute manpower advantage to open the period with Vancouver's Mike McWilliams off for high-sticking, and the Canucks squandered a seven-minute power play when Brent Hughes was sent to the lockerroom for high-sticking and instigating a fight. Hervey broke the deadlock with a blast from the point at 2:54 of the third period, but Tanti restored the tie when he lifted his own rebound .over Berthiaume with Vancouver enjoying a man advantage at 9:04. McLlwain snapped a rebound over fallen rookie Canuck netminder Gus Morschausera at 12:53, and Lenardon converted a pass from Barry Pederson at 15:35 to send the game into overtime.
