## Paragraphs

It just wasn't Larry Billows' day. The Red Deer Rustlers' general manager-coach was involved in an automobile accident Friday morning on Gaetz Avenue. Last night, his club crashed for the final time in the 1987-88 AJHL season, falling 5-3 to the Calgary Canucks. But unlike Billows' vehicle, which will never be driven again, the Rustlers will be back in operation next fall. For the players slated to return next season, it will be a long summer, a time to reflect on what might have been. If rangy center Rob Nixon had been healthy in the post-season, if league scoring king Robin Dube had performed to his capabilities, if the club hadn't been attacked by a swarm of 'flu bugs, well, perhaps the Canucks wouldn't be wearing the South crown the Rustlers were convinced was theirs to keep. In all likelihood, however, the Calgary squad would have prevailed under any circumstances. Only a minor miracle would have prevented them from advancing to the AJHL final - the Canucks are that good. "They do all the little things so well," praised Billows. "They move the puck with authority, work hard all the time and keep their feet moving." "They'll have no trouble winning this league," added Rustler captain Ron Lebsack. "In fact, the Canucks could go a lot further." Calgary should roll over either the St. Albert Saints or Hobbema Hawks. Canucks picked the right year to proceed along the Centennial Cup trail.
