## Paragraphs

Anybody out there got a map to lend Trevor Hendry? "I'm still not sure where I am," says the newest member of the Fort McMurray Oil Barons. Hailing from the B.C. Junior Hockey League's Richmond Sockeyes, the talented left winger is sure of one thing - he's a lot closer to the Arctic Ocean than the Pacific. Still, he admits Fort McMurray has been a pleasant surprise so far. "I guess it's alright out here. The Barons organization has treated me really well, and I'm used to the (long) bus trips because I spent three-and-ahalf years in the (Western Hockey League)." Fort McMurray acquired the sixfoot-two, 185-pound winger in a deal for Dean Rutledge, the Alberta Junior Hockey League's third leading scorer who decided to finish his Junior career in Richmond. So far, the Barons seem to be profiting from the trade. In his first two games before a hometown crowd, Hendry scored five times and added three assists against the Hobbema Hawks last weekend. Playing on a line with Brian Shantz at centre and Barry Kress on right wing, Hendry looked like he'd been with the two all season. "Shantz is young (16) and has good speed a and everything.
