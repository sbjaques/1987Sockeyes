Something's rotten at the Red Deer Arena and it's not dead fish! The stink is coming from a small group of people who think that by paying their $6 or $4 admission, they can do anything short of pulling a shotgun. Those kind of people can't even be called fans. It's them who are throwing coins, empty aluminum cans, golfballs, and other garbage, most of which has been directed at the B.C. champion Richmond Sockeyes. The clowns throwing debris could force the Canadian Amateur Hockey Association to cancel the series or move it elsewhere. The culprits are not all adults or teenagers either. Many of them are youngsters who have been seen tossing aluminum cans onto the ice after every Red Deer goal. Beach balls aren't dangerous. Dead fish aren't safe either, but even National Hockey League crowds toss chickens and the odd octopus onto the ice. Coins on the ice can cause a player to trip and become seriously injured. Flying aluminum cans, eggs and golfballs can blind an eye or cause other injuries. Tossing cans at players involved in a scuffle could hit anyone, including linesmen or the referee. An errant toss could even fall short and hit someone in the crowd. Six dollars doesn't entitle anyone to throw objects onto the playing surface or at the other team. Six dollars allows you to holler for your team, clap and even get involved in a wave or two. Exercise your lungs and boo. Jeer the referee if you want. For a lousy $6, I certainly wouldn't want to live with the fact a tossed coin, can or golfball hurt someone. There are those who would argue they're only getting even for the treatment endured by Red Deer when they played last week in B.C. Richmond has already paid that price with a $500 fine for lack of security. Eggs and tomatoes flew at Red Deer players and there's even a report one Richmond fan was seen with rocks. Two wrongs don't make a right and could result in a serious injury. The behavior of the two teams doesn't help. I've watched junior hockey for about a dozen years now and Richmond is perhaps the most undisciplined team I've seen. As one Western Hockey League scout said Saturday when you have a junior club loaded with 20-year-olds you can go one of two ways. The older players will either march over the opposition with talent and experience or they will lose their composure and run amuck. Richmond has 10 players on its roster aged 20. Richmond, at least in the last two games, has attempted to intimidate Red Deer and it hasn't, and won't, work. The only thing its done is offer Red Deer a chance to work its powerplay. The two games here produced a ghastly 655 minutes in penalties, 360 to Richmond. Granted, a large portion of the time 300 minutes - is the obligatory 10-minute game misconduct for fighting. By GREG MEACHEM of The Advocate His numbers told the story Ken Weiss was the Alberta Junior Hockey League's best goaltender this season. He's now in the process of proving statistics don't lie. The 21-year-old native of Englewood, Colorado, backstopped Sherwood Park Crusaders to the North Division pennant, led them past Fort Saskatchewan Traders in a semifinal round, but couldn't prevent St. Albert Saints from winning a tough, seven-game North final. His season didn't end there, however. South Division winner Red Deer Rustlers ousted the Saints in six games in the AJHL championship series, named Weiss as their special affiliate, and headed west to take on B.C. champion Richmond Sockeyes. Rustler regular Glen Furgason opened the series, was the unfortunate victim of an 11-4 bombing, and Weiss has been in the Red Deer net ever since. With the Crusaders' puckstopper minding the store, the Rustlers fell 6-4 in Richmond, but have since rebounded to deadlock the best-of-seven series at 2-2 with 6-3 and 7-1 wins in Red Deer Saturday and Sunday.
