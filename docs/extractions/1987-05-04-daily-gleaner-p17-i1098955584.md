Flyers 5, Islanders 1 PHILADELPHIA (AP) - The Philadelphia Flyers may have won the battle and lost the war. The Flyers erupted for three firstperiod goals Saturday night to beat the New York Islanders 5-1, winning their NHL Patrick Division final series in seven games and advancing to the Stanley Cup semifinals against the Montreal Canadiens. The Canadiens reached the Wales Conference final with a 5-3 victory Saturday over the Quebec Nordiques, also in a seventh game. When the Flyers take the ice here tonight for the first game of the bestof-seven series, the injury-riddled division champs might best be called survivors. Coach Mike Keenan's best scorer, Tim Kerr, is listed as day-to-day with a dislocated shoulder. And captain Dave Poulin has a broken rib and played Saturday night loaded with pain-killer, seeing no action after the first period. "He was in pain every minute he was out there," defenceman Mark Howe said of Poulin. "But we needed him. He gave all he could give." Aches And Pains J. J. Daigneault is nursing a tender ankle, Ilkka Sinisalo has sore stomach muscles, Ron Sutter is getting over a 2½-month layoff with a back injury, and Murray Craven is gone with a broken foot. In Saturday's deciding game, a fast start by the Flyers spelled a quick end for the Islanders' playoff hopes. Brian . Propp and Brad Marsh scored short-handed goals 44 seconds apart midway through the first period as the Flyers raced to a 3-0 lead. Dave Brown scored at 6:21 before Propp and Marsh both scored with Lindsay Carson in the penalty box. The two short-handed goals in one period matched a Stanley Cup record shared by 16 other teams. Humboldt Leads Centennial Cup HUMBOLDT, Sask. (CP) - Humboldt Broncos beat Pembroke Lumber Kings 4-2 Sunday night to take an early lead in the round-robin Centennial Cup junior A hockey championship. Humboldt also won the tournament opener, beating Dartmouth Fuel Kids 7-2 Saturday night. The Fuel Kids, crowd favorites in the early going, fell 7-3 to Richmond Sockeyes in the only other game Sunday. The fourteam tournament ends Saturday.
