By ELLIOTT PAP Sun Sports Reporter HUMBOLDT, Sask. - If Richmond Sockeyes fulfil their fantasies and win the Centennial Cup tonight, there is every chance in the world that Sockeye winger Jason Phillips will be feted as the tournament's most valuable player. Phillips has had a wonderful week here, darting and dashing to four goals and five assists in Richmond's four games. He is the tourney's leading scorer and was named to the all-star team at the awards banquet Friday night. "I've had an okay tournament," acknowledged Phillips on the eve of the national junior A hockey final between the Sockeyes and host Humboldt Broncos.
