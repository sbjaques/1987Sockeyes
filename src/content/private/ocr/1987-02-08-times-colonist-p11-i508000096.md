QUEBEC- Former Montreal Canadiens great Bernie Geoffrion suffered a heart attack Saturday while touring a hockey hall of fame exhibit. Geoffrion, 55, is in hospital in stable condition. He collapsed while touring the exhibit with another ex National Hockey League great, Gordie Howe. Nicknamed Boom Boom because of his powerful shot, Geoffrion has had a history of heart problems. Geoffrion played 16 seasons in the NHL, scoring 393 goals and 429 assists, before retiring in 1968. He was the original coach of the Atlanta Flames franchise before the team moved to Calgary and also coached the Montreal Canadiens briefly in 1979.
