## Paragraphs

The University of Maine-Orono's hockey players may be putting the skates away for this season but next year's returnees are already setting their sights on the 1985-86 season. "I wish we were still practicing but we will be at this time next season," predicted freshman defenseman Dave Nonis after the Bears had been eliminated from the Hockey East quarterfinals by Boston University. "We finished strong this year, and I'm really looking forward to the future," said freshman defenseman Stan Czenczek. Czenczek and Nonis had a great deal of praise for the graduating senior class and said the fact the seniors didn't make it to Providence for the Hockey East semifinals and finals was their biggest disappointment. "I feel so bad for the seniors," said Nonis. "They worked their tails off and helped us (the underclassmen) out a lot, said Czenczek. "'They never turned against us. The extensive playing time given to the freshmen this past season will be extremely beneficial in the future according to the British Columbia natives.

"We (the freshmen) cost the team some games due to our freshman mistakes but receiving that much playing time enabled us to improve a lot over the season," said Nonis with Czenczek in agreement.
