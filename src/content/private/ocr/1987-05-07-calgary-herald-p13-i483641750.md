HUMBOLDT, Sask. (CP) Humboldt Broncos won a berth in the final of the Centennial Cup junior 'A' hockey championship tournament Wednesday night with a 6-1 victory over Richmond Sockeyes. The losers, with a round-robin record of 2- 1, will meet Pembroke Lumber Kings in tonight's semifinal. The final will be played Saturday Bill McDougall and Garnett Kazuik led Humboldt with two goals apiece, while Duncon Ryhorchuk and Curtis Chamberlin fired singles. The win gave Humboldt a 3-0 record in the round-robin portion of the tournament. Stan Czenczek scored the Richmond goal. The teams were tied 1-1 after the first period, but Humboldt led 4-1 after the second. The Broncos outshot the B.C.-champion Sockeyes 37-19. Humboldt take 15 of 27 minors. Both teams took two five-minute majors and two game misconducts. Humbolt also picked up two 10-minute misconducts. The Lumber Kings, from Ontario, finished with a 1-2 record in the round-robin section of the tournament. The fourth team involved, Dartmouth Fuel Kids from Nova Scotia, lost all three of its games.
