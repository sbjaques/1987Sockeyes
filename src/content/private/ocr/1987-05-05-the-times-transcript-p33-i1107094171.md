played here on Thursday. Sockeyes move to 2-0 HUMBOLDT, Sask. (CP) The Richmond Sockeyes exploded for three goals in the third period to beat the Pembroke Lumber Kings 4-1 Monday night in the Centennial Cup junior A hockey championship tournament. The win gave the British Columbia champions a 2-0 record in the round-robin portion of the tournament, and a share of first place with the Saskatche-. wan champion Humboldt Broncos. It was the second straight loss for the Lumber Kings, the Ontario representatives in the four-team tournament. The other team in the championship event, the Dartmouth Fuel Kids from British Columbia, are also at 0-2. The teams that finish second and third in the round-robin portion of the tournament meet in a semifinal game on Thursday night. The winner of the semifinal tangles with the team that finishes first after 1 roundrobin play.
