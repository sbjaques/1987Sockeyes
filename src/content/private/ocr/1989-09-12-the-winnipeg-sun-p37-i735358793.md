SASKATOON - Team unity, not team dissension, is the ultimate goal of Winnipeg Jet GM Mike Smith. Smith admitted yesterday he's aware there's going to be a number of players who hold resentment towards Fredrik Olausson, who attempted to walk out on his National Hockey League contract and return to Sweden to play for his former club Farjestad. In yesterday's Sun a number of players were quoted as saying they felt Olausson had quit on the Jets, and the entire NHL. These players quietly suggested Olausson could expect a reception reserved for those individuals who show up on the doorstep peddling religious material. Unsmiling faces and a cool greeting. Smith responded to this potentially volatile situation by saying his coaching staff would competently handle the problems which may arise. He said if the coaching staff felt a player was unwilling to change his frame of mind, Smith himself would deal with the matter. It doesn't take the mentality of a rocket

scientist to figure out Smith would quickly swing a trade. Team wins, not team dissension, is what the Jets desperately need this season. Long dead are the days of the battling New York Yankee mentality. "When you're talking about the lockerroom, that's the coaches' area," explained Smith. "We have three competent people, who have been in all situations before and won't be intimidated, nor threatened by the allegations which have been laid out."
