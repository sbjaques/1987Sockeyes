## Paragraphs

HUMBOLDT Jason Phillips scored three goals to lead the Richmond Sockeyes to the Centennial Cup junior A hockey championship Saturday night with a 5-2 win over the Humboldt Broncos. But the Sockeyes had to fall back on an outstanding performance in goal by Frank Romeo, who stood up to 46 Humboldt shots while his teammates managed only 23 at the Broncos net. "This is unbelievable," said Romeo, the tournament and final game MVP. "I'm the happiest guy in the world. Winning the cup, being MVP. it's like Christmas all over again." British Columbia's Sockeyes skated to period leads of 2-0 and 2-1 before Paul Rutherford scored the winning goal in the third. Dave Tomlinson also scored for Richmond, which took seven of the game's 12 minor penalties before a standing-room crowd of 2,400. "I think we were really afraid of making a mistake early in the game and wanted everything to go right," said Broncos general manager and coach Bernie Lynch. "It should have been the other way, we should have come out and taken it right to them. That's what Richmond did, they came right at us. They did a super job and they're deserving champions."
