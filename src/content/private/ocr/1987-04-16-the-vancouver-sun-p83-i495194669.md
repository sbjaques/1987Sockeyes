Sockeyes extend playoff to the limit Sun News Services RED DEER, Alta. - Richmond Sockeyes used a combination of a fast start and a key third-period shorthanded goal Wednesday to defeat Red Deer Rustlers 7-3 in a junior A hockey playoff game. The victory evened the best-ofseven western Centennial Cup semi-final series at 3-3. The seventh and deciding game goes this evening. The Sockeyes, winners of the B.C. Junior Hockey League, scored five first-period goals to lead 5-1 after 20 minutes. The Rustlers, Alberta Junior Hockey League champions, narrowed the margin to 5-3 after two periods. But Sockeye centre Brian Kozak scored during a Richmond penalty early in the third period to blunt the Red Deer comeback. "The goal by Kozak was the key," said Richmond coach Orland Kurtenbach. "That took the edge off Red Deer's game." Kurtenbach said improved officiating has helped turn the series around. "The referees have started to pick up the behind play stuff now," he "Earlier, they weren't seeing it and we were getting nailed on a lot of retaliation calls. Now, they're calling the first penalty." Rob Clarke scored three times for Richmond with Tony Bobbitt, Dave Tomlinson and Mike McCormick adding single goals.
