Junior Hockey League historian Fred Hume reports that there are now over 30 players in the NHL, who at one time played in the BCJHL and 22 ex-BCJHLers who have counted more than 200 career NHL points. Among the growing list of, former B.C. products in the NHL are Ray Ferraro, Dean Evason, Doug Lidster, Stan Smyl, Glenn Anderson, John Ogrodnick and Andy Moog. Some BCJHL graduates are now filtering into the coaching fraternity as well. Vernon Lakers, who are in town tonight, have ex-Vernon junior Ernie Gare behind the bench. His assistant is George Fargher, who also played with the old Vernon Essos. Penticton coach Dwayne. Pentland, Gary Scott of Delta, Merritt's Butch Tent, Mike O'Brien of Richmond and Nanaimo's assistant coach Mickey Willgress are other former BCJHL players now coaching in the league. . Canada's national team, silver medalist at the Izvestia Tournament in December, had three former BCJHL stars in Vaughan Karpan and Cliff Ronning (New Westminster Royals) and Ken Berry (Penticton-Bellingham). Hume also reports there are 81 former BCJHL players skating for American universities on scholarships. BCJHL grads can also be found playing for Canadian schools this season where Ward Carlson at Acadia, Stacey Wakabayashi at Alberta, Steve LaPointe at UBC and Harry Mahood at Manitoba are enjoying good years.
