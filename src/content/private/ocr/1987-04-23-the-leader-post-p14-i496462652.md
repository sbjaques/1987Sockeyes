## Paragraphs

Tony Bobbitt's goal at 1:04 of a 10-minute overtime period gave the Richmond Sockeyes a 4-3 win over the Humboldt Broncos in a Centennial Cup junior hockey playoff game Wednesday night in Humboldt. The victory gave the Sockeyes a 2-1 lead in the best-of-seven Tier 2 western final series. The fourth game will be played in Richmond on Saturday. Dave Tomlinson, Mike Claringbull and Rob Clarke scored the other goals for the Sockeyes, who were o outshot 39-23 and trailed 1-0 after the first period and 2-1 after the second. Bill McDougall had two goals and Curtis Chamberlin one for the Saskatchewan champion Broncos. Richmond, the British Columbia champion, took 13 of 25 minor penalties and the only major. In the eastern final, the host Pembroke Lumber Kings defeated the Nickel Centre Power Trains 8- 2 to lead their best-of-seven series 3-1. George Dupont and Mike Eastwood each scored twice for Pembroke and singles came from Bruce Coles, Karlo Pavich and Geoff Stanton. Gord Davy and Doug Parenteau scored for Nickel Centre. The next game in the seriestis set for tonight in Pembroke.
