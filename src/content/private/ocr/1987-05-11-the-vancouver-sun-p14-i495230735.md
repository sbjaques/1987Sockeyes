By ELLIOTT PAP Sun Sports Reporter HUMBOLDT, Sask. - It was a bedraggled, dishevelled •but euphoric group of Richmond Sockeyes who pulled out of this prairie town Sunday, their mission accomplished. Stowed among their gear and luggage, in an unassuming crate marked 'fragile', stood the symbol of their achievement. The Sockwere bringing home the Centennial Cup. Coach Orland Kurtenbach's troops stunned the cocky Humboldt Broncos 5-2 Saturday night to win the tier two junior hockey championship of Canada. The triumph capped? a remarkable post-season for the Sockeyes, who won 27 of 34 playoff games and five different titles: B.C. Junior League, Mowat Cup, AlbertaB.C., Abbott Cup (western Canada) and the Centennial Cup. "It's just an A-1 feeling," beamed Kurtenbach, a winner in his first crack at coaching junior. "I don't care if it's the Stanley Cup, Memorial Cup, Adams Cup (Central League) or Centennial Cup, The it's total elation." Sockeyes once again led by the goal-scoring of Jason Phillips and the goaltending of Frank Romeo, both of whom carted off major individual awards.
