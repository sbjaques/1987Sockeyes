SINGING IN COURT A Judge will decide next month whether singer Paul Anka and the company that owns the Ottawa Senators will go to trial over a controversial agreement that has escalated into a nasty legal battle. Terrace Investment Ldt., owner of the Senators, has argued that it signed an agreement with Anka in May 1991 solely on the strength of his claims he could raise $10 million for the team. Terrace says Anka "did absolutiey nothing" for his part of the deal. Anka claims the agreement still is valid.
