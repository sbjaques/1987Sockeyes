## Paragraphs

HUMBOLDT, Sask. Jason Phillips scored three goals to lead the Richmond Sockeyes to an easy 7-3 win over the Dartmouth Fuel Kids in the Centennial Cup junior A hockey championship yesterday. It was the second loss in two days for Dartmouth. The Nova Scotians opened the week-long, round-robin tournament Saturday with a 7-2 defeat at the hands of the host Humboldt Broncos. Bryon Moller, Steve Jaques, Dave Tomlinson and Matt Hervey also scored for the Sockeyes, who piled up 44 shots against Dartmouth's 29. Richmond zipped to a 3-1 lead in the first period and built the margin to 6-2 in the second. Jerry Scott had two goals and Brian King had one for the Fuel Kids, who had the bulk of the fans behind them. The Sockeyes took 11 of 17 minors and a major, and both teams had a misconduct. Humboldt was to play its second game later Sunday against the Pembroke Lumber Kings from Ontario. Richmond plays Pembroke in the only game today.
