Terriers never recovered, even though Mike Lappin's shot darted past PC's Matt Merten to tie the game, 4-4, at 10:56. Rick Bennett's power-play goal with 5:39 to play gave the Friars a 5-4 lead, which meant BU needed two goals to send the series into overtime but PC's Mario Aube scored to settle the issue. PC beat BU for the fourth time in five games this season. Mattice, who scored 13 goals his freshman season, had just 5 entering last night's game and was used sparingly Friday by coach Mike McShane. "He's got a world of talent." said McShane, whose team survived a total of 32 penalties in the two games - 16 each night. "I've tried him on different lines and I don't know if it was a matter of confidence or what. Tonight that talent really showed.' For BU coach Jack Parker, it was a matter of his team failing to mount much of anything when playing at even strength. "Three of our four goals were with a two-man advantage, said Parker, whose team had 16 power plays to PC's seven over the two games but cashed in just four times. "We had no flow through center ice and Providence not only forechecked well, they always had three men back to break us up." BU clicked early when Mike Sullivan (playing for the first time in four games because of a shoulder injury) scored five minutes into the game. So it became a 55-minute game, and Providence, which hadn't won at BU in four years until Friday night, never let the -Terriers take command. -Gord Cruikshank beat Fish from 25 feet at 9:15 for his 96th career goal, making him the school's all-time leading goal scorer. The Friars (13-16-5) made it 2- 1 on an all-freshman goal 8:35 Into the second period. Larry Rooney finished off a bang-bang passing play with Pat Madigan and lifted a liner over Fish's shoulder from 15 feet. Providence weht up, 3-1, less than two minutes later when Mattice took a Mike Dempsey pass and had Fish at his mercy. BU (14-17-3). playing without Injured Ville Kentala, a 15-goal scorer, lost forward Matt Pesklewis after the first period because of a fractured collarbone - but the Terriers mounted a comeback. Joe Sacco took a Dave Tomlinson feed and cut in front to beat Merten, and when the Terriers put on more pressure, Rooney was called for holding with 8:05 left in the period. It became a two-man advantage four seconds later when Jim Hughes was called for tripping. Tom Dion tied the game with a 50-foot shot to the corner. But then Mattice, Bennett & Co. took over. "The only thing I was worried about down the stretch." said McShane, "was the penalties. But we kept our poise. Give credit to Bennett, too. He had a broken hand earlier this season and was playing some defense, but he got a big goal tonight playing up front." But no goal was bigger than Mattice's second.
