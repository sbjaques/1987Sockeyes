By ELLIOTT PAP With both Richmond Sockeyes and Humboldt Broncos assured spots in next week's Centennial Cup tournament, their current western final series could have been a formality. Instead it has become a delight. The Sockeyes and Broncos will finally settle the Abbott Cup junior A hockey championship tonight (7:30) when they meet in game seven of their best-of-seven at Richmond Arena. Humboldt forced the decider Tuesday, edging Richmond 4-3 in overtime as Mike Gaber deflected home Brad Bergen's point shot at 3:31 of the extra 10-minute period. There is little to choose between the two powerhouse teams. In the first six games of this series, three were decided in overtime, one by one goal and the other two by two goals. Each team has won twice on the road and once at home. Richmond has outscored Humboldt by one goal, 21-20. "We made two really bad mistakes and it cost us the game tonight," lamented Sockeye coach Orland Kurtenbach. "I was very upset with those two plays." The first one came at 8:28 of the third period when Richmond's Tony Bobbitt closed his hand on the puck and Humboldt promptly scored on the power play, tying the contest 3-3. The second came at 3:02 of overtime when Sockeye defenceman Steve Jaques helmet-butted Bronco star Bill McDougall in the mouth after McDougall had interfered with him. Jaques received a five-minute match penalty and likely a three-game suspension while McDougall got a minor.
