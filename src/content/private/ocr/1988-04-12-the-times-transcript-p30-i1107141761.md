The Winnipeg-Edmonton playoff series has been a rough one. If only Joel Baillargeon played a smart game. No doubt, he could have helped the Jets. However, Baillargeon gave coach Bowness more problems this winter than the Hawks road record. At six-foot-one, 205 pounds, Baillargeon should have racked up at least 250 penalty minutes. Instead, he managed about 100 PIMS and fought more with teammates in practice than opponents in games. Baillargeon was capable of being a punishing bodychecker, but often played with the enthusiasm of a jersey cow. Too bad, he could have made it. Bowness has already told Baillargeon that he doesn't want to see him at training camp next September. So far, that's the only player Bowness hasn't invited back. Veteran Winnipeg defenceman Randy Carlyle says the Jets must find room for Randy Gilhen next year. "He was called up a couple of times this year and we always wondered why he was going back to the American League, Carlyle said Monday. "He's playing superb hockey for us....he's killing penalties and playing a strong two-way game. Randy Gilhen can play in the NHL and we have to give him that chance." While killing a penalty, Gilhen scored the game-winner against the Oilers Saturday night. marked the first time Winnipeg had defeated Edmonton in a playoff game.

Winnipeg Jets have already made plans to add some muchneeded experience to the Moncton Hawks roster for next season. Ron Wilson, who began his professional hockey career 12 years ago with the Nova Scotia Voyageurs, will join Moncton's American Hockey League team as an assistant to head coach Rick Bowness. Wilson, relegated to a penalty-killing role with the Jets during the past two years, is also expected to play between 40 and 50 games for the Hawks. "In the NHL, he's still one of the best skaters, but we have to make room for the younger guys," Bowness said Monday night. "Ron's going to do a lot to help the kids we have in Moncton. He's a great influence on the ice and in the dressing room. the NHL, he's known as a penalty-killer, but I'm sure he can still score some points for us in the American League." Wilson last played in the AHL five years ago. With Sherbrooke, the 32-year-old centre fired 30 goals and 55 assists in just 65 games. Bowness and Wilson are close friends and should work well together. An official announcement is not expected for several weeks, but Wilson will be offered the job and the Torontonian will accept.
