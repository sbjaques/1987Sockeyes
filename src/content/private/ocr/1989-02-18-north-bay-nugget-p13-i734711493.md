By The Canadian Press Alpo Suhonen registered his first AHL coaching victory Friday night and downplayed recent barbs hurled his way by TV commentator Don Cherry. Suhonen, former coach of Finland's national team, was named Moncton coach after previous coach Rick Bowness moved up to the NHL's Winnipeg Jets. Cherry reacted to the Jets' personnel switches on national TV one week ago, suggesting a Canadian might have been a better choice and that Suhonen's given name "sounds like a dog food to me. "I guess that's a part of hockey here," Suhonen said when asked, following the Hawks' 8-2 victory over the Cape Breton Oilers, to comment on Cherry's statements. "It's nothing to me. "I know many people think Europeans should not be here, but I say it's the other way. An exchange of people, an exchange of ideas is great for the sport." Suhonen's record is 1-1 since taking over the Hawks. Alfie Turcotte scored twice to pace the Hawks while Brent Hughes, Markku Kyllonen, Guy S Gosselin, Guy Larose, Matt Hervey and Ron Wilson each scored once as Moncton moved into a second-place tie in the Northern Division with the Halifax Citadels. Cape Breton's goals were scored by Shawn Van Allen and Fabian Joseph. WINNIPEG (CP) - Hockey Night in Canada viewers across the country watching the Boston Bruins-Calgary Flames game tonight may see Don Cherry take back remarks he made last week about the Winnipeg Jets. Cherry, the outspoken former National Hockey League coach, was taken 10 task this week by the Jets, who felt he slandered their organization on Hockey Night in Canada last Saturday night. Winnipeg president Barry Shenkarow has demanded a televised apology and is threatening legal action.

Cherry was tight-lipped when reached at his tavern in Mississauga, Ont., on Friday. "I've been told by Hockey Night in Canada I can't say anything and you can understand why when (the Jets) start talking legal action," said the former Boston Bruin and Colorado Rockies coach. "So for the

first time in my career I hate to say its gotta be 'No comment.' Cherry refused to say if he would apologize on air. Last Saturday, Cherry criticized the Jets for firing general manager John Ferguson and head coach Dan Maloney, and for hiring Mike Smith to replace Ferguson. He also took a few cracks at Alpo Suhonen, the new Finnish head coach of the Jets farm team in Moncton, N.B. Cherry reportedly said of Suhonen: "Why did they bring a Finn over to coach Moncton? Wasn't a Canadian good enough? *I don't wish him well in Moncton. What's his name, Alpo? Sounds like a dog food to me." Shenkarow, calling the comments racist, said Cherry's remarks have no place in a Western democracy. Ron Harrison, the executive producer of Hockey Night in Canada, could not be reached for comment.
