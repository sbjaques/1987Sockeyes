# Regular Season

The 1986-87 Richmond Sockeyes played 52 regular-season games between October 1986 and March 1987, finishing 38-14-0. They won the Coast Division. Their goal differential was +104.

Three Sockeyes finished in the top ten of BCJHL regular-season scoring. Dave Tomlinson put up 43 goals and 65 assists for 108 points, second in the league. Rob Clarke had 65 goals and 38 assists for 103 points, third. Bryon Moller finished sixth at 27-64-91. No other BCJHL team had three players in the league's top ten.

Jim Gunn, the defenceman who led the team in penalty minutes, described the roster Kurtenbach assembled as purpose-built: players recruited to win, with a mix of skill and physical play that could match any style of opponent. Gunn had four goals and 22 points in 46 games that season, along with 363 penalty minutes — nearly eight per game. The team's penalty minutes reflected a broader identity: the Sockeyes were not primarily a defensive team, but they were willing and capable in physical play.

The offensive structure centred on two primary lines. Jason Phillips centred a line with Mike Kozak and Bill Hardy that combined scoring punch with hard minutes. Tomlinson worked at centre on a second line. Clarke and Moller contributed from their positions in a roster that had depth across multiple forward combinations. These line groupings remained largely intact through the regular season and carried into the playoff run.

Frank Romeo, the late-acquired goaltender, did not play in the regular season. The starting goaltending responsibilities belonged to another netminder through the first 52 games. Romeo's role began when the playoffs started.

The Kelowna Packers finished the regular season 45-5-2 — the best record in Canadian Junior A hockey. Richmond was a strong team but not the statistical equal of Kelowna on the regular-season ledger. What that comparison did not account for was the configuration of the BCJHL playoff bracket and what Richmond's roster was capable of over a short series.

By March 1987, the Sockeyes had played themselves into the BCJHL playoffs as the Coast Division's top seed. They had not won a national championship before. The program had been competitive under Taylor's ownership — the team had won the Coast Division in 1986 as well — but had not made it past the provincial level. With a deeper, older, more experienced roster than in previous seasons, and with Kurtenbach directing operations, they entered the postseason as a legitimate contender for the Fred Page Cup.

Their championship campaign began the last week of February, when the BCJHL playoffs opened.
