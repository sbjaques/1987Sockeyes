# Penticton, July 2025

On July 12, 2025, the South Okanagan Events Centre in Penticton hosted the British Columbia Hockey Hall of Fame induction ceremony. Jim Hughson, chair of the BCHHoF, presided over a class of seven honourees: players Dan Hamhuis and Shawn Horcoff, builders Mike Penny and Ted Hargreaves, pioneer Larry Kwong, the 1977-78 Kimberley Dynamiters, and the 1987 Richmond Sockeyes — the first BC Junior A team to win the Centennial Cup national championship. The festivities ran across two days, July 11-12.

For the 1987 Sockeyes, the ceremony was something else first: a reunion. It was the first time the team had been together since they scattered after the Centennial Cup win in Humboldt, Saskatchewan in May 1987. Jim Gunn, the Prince George defenceman who had led the team in penalty minutes that season, described the coming together as almost as meaningful as the honour itself. Head coach Orland Kurtenbach, 88 years old and still living in Vancouver, was expected to make the trip. All the players from that team were still alive, spread across Canada and the United States, reconnecting in the months before the induction mostly through Facebook.

The 2025 BCHHoF class was announced on June 20 by the BCHL. Hamhuis had played 1,148 career NHL regular-season games with Nashville, Vancouver, and Dallas, and had won gold with Canada at the 2014 Sochi Olympics. Horcoff had played 1,008 NHL games and helped the Oilers reach the 2006 Stanley Cup Final after putting up a league-high 145 points with the Chilliwack Chiefs in 1995-96. Kwong, who died in 2018, had been the first player of Asian descent to appear in an NHL game, suiting up for the New York Rangers in 1948. The Kimberley Dynamiters of the Western International Hockey League had won the 1978 Allan Cup. Penny, in his 55th year as an NHL scout, had been credited with helping draft Pavel Bure, Cam Neely, and the Sedin twins for the Vancouver Canucks.

The 1987 Sockeyes were inducted as a team. Thirty-eight years had passed between the final horn in Humboldt and the ceremony stage in Penticton.
