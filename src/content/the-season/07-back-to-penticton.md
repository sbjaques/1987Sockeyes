# Back to Penticton

On May 9, 1987, the Richmond Sockeyes beat the Humboldt Broncos 5-2 at the Uniplex in Humboldt, Saskatchewan, and won the Centennial Cup. Frank Romeo was named tournament MVP. Jason Phillips was named Most Gentlemanly Player and to the tournament All-Star team. The team boarded buses and planes back to British Columbia.

Then they dispersed. Orland Kurtenbach returned to his work as a scout and hockey development coach. He would spend decades in that role. Players scattered: some to NCAA programs, some to minor professional leagues, some into careers with no further competitive hockey. Jim Gunn went to Mount Royal College in Calgary and helped the Cougars win the 1988 national collegiate championship. Trevor Dickie, the captain, moved on. The team had no shared reunion in the years that followed.

Thirty-eight years passed.

In the winter of 2024-25, word came that the British Columbia Hockey Hall of Fame had selected the 1987 Sockeyes for induction in its class of 2025. The announcement prompted reconnections that had not happened in decades. Players found each other through Facebook. Gunn, by then a retired Prince George firefighter, described the prospect of the reunion as nearly as significant as the induction itself. Kurtenbach, 88 years old, was still living in Vancouver. All the players from that team were reported to be alive.

On July 12, 2025, at the South Okanagan Events Centre in Penticton — the same city where Canadian junior hockey's national tournaments have long been hosted — the surviving members of the 1987 Sockeyes gathered on a ceremony stage together for the first time since Humboldt.

Two months later, the team returned to Minoru Arenas in Richmond to raise their Centennial Cup banner above centre ice, in front of the current Sockeyes and their fans.
