# Abbott Cup

The Abbott Cup — Western Canadian Junior A championship — was a best-of-seven against the Humboldt Broncos. The Broncos had gone 55-9 in the SJHL regular season and came in riding a seventeen-game winning streak of their own. Richmond had ended a longer streak in the Doyle Cup. Now they would face a team with comparable numbers and the same momentum.

The series opened in Humboldt on April 20. Curtis Chamberlin scored three times, including the overtime winner on a two-on-one pass from Joey Johannson 1:39 into the ten-minute OT. Richmond had led 2-1 after the second period and tied it 3-3 in the third. Bryon Moller, Matt Hervey, and Jason Phillips scored for Richmond; Phillips' goal came at 8:02 of the third on a power play (Star-Phoenix Apr 21 1987, images 512310223 and 512310237).

Richmond evened the series in Game 2 with a 5-3 win. Phillips recorded a hat trick; Stan Czenczek and Brian Kozak had the others. Phillips went 3-for-3 on the power play. Humboldt outshot Richmond 45-31 but went 0-for-8 on their power plays (Star-Phoenix Apr 22 1987, images 512310501 and 512310521).

Game 3 was a 4-3 overtime win for Richmond at the Uniplex. Tony Bobbitt scored 1:04 into OT on a pass from McCormick and Czenczek. Rob Clarke tied the game with 33 seconds left in the third, his goal at 19:27 pulling Richmond even from a 3-2 deficit. Romeo made 36 saves. Humboldt outshot Richmond 39-23 and generated 9 power plays — they scored on none of them (Star-Phoenix Apr 23 1987, images 512310808 and 512310816).

The series shifted to Richmond for Games 4 and 5. Humboldt took Game 4, 2-1 — McDougall and Brad Bergen scored for the Broncos, Dean Rutledge replied — to even the series at 2-2 (Calgary Herald Apr 26 1987, image 483768383). In Game 5, Richmond came from behind. Humboldt led 2-0 after the first period. Hervey scored twice; Tomlinson, Kozak, and Rutherford had the other Richmond goals in a 5-3 win that gave the Sockeyes a 3-2 series lead. McDougall scored twice for Humboldt; Romeo made 30 saves (Star-Phoenix Apr 27 1987, image 512311911; Nanaimo Daily News Apr 27 1987, image 325076956).

Game 6 was in Richmond. Humboldt forced a seventh game.

Richmond led 1-0 after the first and were tied 2-2 after two. Tomlinson put Richmond ahead 3-2 at 7:44 of the third. Two mistakes in the final minutes proved costly. Tony Bobbitt took a delay-of-game minor at 8:28 of the third for closing his hand on the puck. Humboldt tied the game 3-3 on the ensuing power play. Then at 3:02 of overtime, defenceman Steve Jaques received a five-minute match penalty for helmet-butting Bill McDougall — four contemporaneous wire reports describe the incident specifically as helmet-butting, with Jaques, who was wearing a helmet, striking McDougall in the mouth with it (Nanaimo Daily News Apr 29 1987, image 325077439; Vancouver Sun Apr 29 1987, image 495257020). McDougall received a minor for interference. At 4-on-4, Mike Gaber deflected home a Brad Bergen point shot at 3:31 of OT. Humboldt won 4-3. Paul Rutherford scored twice for Richmond; Tomlinson had the other goal. Goaltender Bruce Hoffort made 42 saves for Humboldt.

Game 7 was on April 29 at Richmond Arena.

Richmond led 3-1 after the first period and 4-3 going into the third. Humboldt tied it 4-4 at 16:48 of the third. Thirty-five seconds later, Dave Tomlinson scored to put Richmond ahead 5-4. The goal came at 17:23 and stood as the winner. Hervey, Rutledge, Phillips, and Hardy had the other Richmond goals. The Broncos outshot Richmond 40-35 across the game.

After the final horn, Humboldt declined to participate in the traditional post-game handshake at centre ice and were booed by the Richmond Arena crowd. Broncos coach Bernie Lynch explained: "We're just sending them a message: You ain't won nothin' yet. We're going to be the national champions." Both teams were now headed to Humboldt for the Centennial Cup (Leader-Post Apr 30 1987, image 496463470; Star-Phoenix Apr 30 1987, image 512312872; The Province Apr 30 1987, image 502021976).
