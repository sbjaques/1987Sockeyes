# Fred Page / Mowat Cup

The 1987 BCJHL playoffs began in late February. Richmond entered as the Coast Division's top seed. Across three rounds, they played fifteen games and won all fifteen.

The Sockeyes opened against the Nanaimo Clippers, sweeping the series four games to none. They followed with a four-game sweep of the North Delta Flyers. The BCJHL championship final — played for the Fred Page Cup — was a best-of-nine against the Kelowna Packers.

Kelowna had finished the regular season 45-5-2. That record was the best in Canadian Junior A hockey. The Packers were heavily favoured. Richmond coach Orland Kurtenbach acknowledged before Game 1 that his team knew what Kelowna was capable of. After Richmond won the opener 7-0, Kurtenbach was characteristically measured: "We know it's not going to be this easy." Packers coach Bill Hogaboam was direct about the loss: "They outhustled us all over the ice... we played horrible and everybody on our team knows it." (Vancouver Sun Mar 18 1987, image 495098412.)

Frank Romeo, who had been released by Kelowna the previous off-season, stopped 27 shots for the shutout in Game 1. He called it sweet revenge.

Dave Tomlinson scored twice in the opener; Jason Phillips, Bill Hardy, Mike Claringbull, Mike McCormick, and Steve Jaques added singles. In Game 2, Richmond trailed and recovered, winning 5-4 in overtime on a defenceman Matt Hervey goal at 3:37 of the first OT — a rebound off a Dean Rutledge shot on a two-on-one with Paul Rutherford (Vancouver Sun Mar 19 1987, image 495099771). Hervey suffered a shoulder separation in Game 3 but the Sockeyes won 7-5 in Kelowna, taking a 3-0 series lead. Tomlinson, Phillips, and Rutledge each scored twice. Game 4 was 5-1. Richmond went to the ninth game without losing one.

Game 5 was the clincher, played at Richmond Arena in front of more than 1,100 fans. The score was 3-2. Kelowna tied it in the third period; Phillips scored the winner at 9:31, flipping a rebound past Dusty Imoo after his initial deflection of a Steve Jaques point shot had been stopped. Phillips credited Brian Kozak and Hardy for creating the loose puck. Rutledge's second-period shorthanded goal — his shot deflecting off a Packer defender — had put Richmond ahead 2-1. Romeo played through sinusitis. Richmond outshot Kelowna 35-24 (Vancouver Sun Mar 25 1987, images 495157739 and 495157862; The Province Mar 25 1987, image 502054045).

After the final horn, captain Trevor Dickie hoisted the BCJHL trophy with Rutledge and Rob Sumner. The Vancouver Sun's post-series photo caption identified Dickie explicitly as team captain — "Richmond captain Trevor Dickie" (image 495157862). The Fred Page Cup was Richmond's first BCJHL championship in eight years.

Richmond then swept the Peace-Cariboo Junior Hockey League champions, the Quesnel Millionaires, two games to none in the Mowat Cup — the BC Tier II provincial final. They won the opener 4-3 at Richmond Arena (Times Colonist Apr 4 1987, image 508629153), then closed it out 5-1. Their post-season record stood at 15-0.

The Doyle Cup, against the AJHL champion Red Deer Rustlers, was next.
