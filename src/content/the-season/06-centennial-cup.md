# Centennial Cup

The 1987 Centennial Cup was held at the Humboldt Uniplex — Humboldt's home ice — with the Broncos as the host team. Richmond arrived having beaten Humboldt seven times in three weeks in the Abbott Cup. They were going back to play them again, in their building, in front of their fans.

The four-team tournament began with round-robin play. Richmond's first game, on May 3, was against the Dartmouth Fuel Kids, the Eastern champion. Richmond won 7-3. Phillips scored three goals; Moller, Jaques, Tomlinson, and Hervey added singles. Richmond outshot Dartmouth 44-29 (Star-Phoenix May 4 1987, image 512093788; The Province May 4 1987, image 502027542).

On May 4, Richmond faced the Pembroke Lumber Kings, the Ontario champion. Brian Kozak opened the scoring at 2:39 of the first period, assisted by Phillips and Hervey. The game was 1-0 after two periods. In the third, Pembroke's George Dupont tied it on a power play at 4:06. Richmond then scored three straight: Czenczek at 6:35, Tomlinson at 15:01 (from Phillips), and Tomlinson again at 18:26 (from Rutledge and Rutherford) to win 4-1. Romeo was in goal; Pembroke's Robb started for the Lumber Kings. Richmond went 0-for-2 on the power play; Pembroke converted 1-of-9. Kurtenbach's comment after the game: "All I'm asking for is a fair shake from the refereeing" (Star-Phoenix May 5 1987, image 512094503; Vancouver Sun May 5 1987, image 495157600).

The round-robin finale, on May 6, was against Humboldt.

It was the eighth meeting between the two teams across three weeks. Humboldt won 6-1. Bill McDougall opened the scoring on a power-play rebound at 6:17 of the first. Czenczek tied it at 17:26 with a power-play slap shot. Garnet Kazuik broke it open shorthanded at 13:20 of the second, stealing the puck from Steve Jaques at the Richmond blue line and taking a clear breakaway on Jamie Stewart. Humboldt led 4-1 after two. Humboldt finished round-robin play 3-0; Richmond finished 2-1. Kurtenbach: "We got outworked, outplayed, out-everythinged. The 'oomph' just wasn't there. Some nights it just doesn't go, and tonight was one of them" (Star-Phoenix May 7 1987, image 512096770; Vancouver Sun May 7 1987, image 495159747).

Richmond's semifinal was on May 7 against Pembroke. Nine different Sockeyes scored in a 9-3 win. Rutledge, Jaques, Hervey, Kozak, Rutherford, Phillips, Bobbitt, McCormick, and Hardy all scored. Richmond led 1-0 after the first, 3-0 after the second, and added six in the third. Outshot Pembroke 44-32 (Times Colonist May 8 1987, image 508631348; Calgary Herald May 8 1987, image 483667308).

The final, on Saturday May 9, 1987 at 7:30 PM CST, was Richmond against Humboldt.

The building held a standing-room crowd of approximately 2,400. Dave Tomlinson scored the first goal of the game, at 16:53 of the first, assisted by Rutherford and Claringbull. Phillips made it 2-0 at 18:13 from Hardy. Humboldt's Curtis Chamberlin pulled one back on a second-period power play at 10:47. In the third, Rutherford scored the game's eventual winning goal at 8:48 from Rutledge. Phillips extended the lead at 13:28 from Kozak. Humboldt's Kevin Luke scored at 16:31 to make it 4-2. Phillips added an empty-net goal at 19:07, also from Kozak, to seal it 5-2.

Romeo made 44 saves on 46 shots. Humboldt outshot Richmond 46-23. The final score was 5-2. A 2025 Richmond Sentinel retrospective article reported the final as 5-3; that figure is incorrect. The contemporaneous score of 5-2 is confirmed by the Star-Phoenix May 11 1987 (images 512098529 and 512098543), the Vancouver Sun May 11 1987 (images 495230735 and 495229991), and the Times Colonist May 10 1987 (image 508633163).

Frank Romeo was named the game's MVP and the tournament MVP — his third player-of-the-game recognition across three starts in the tournament, having allowed six goals total. The 2025 Richmond Sentinel article that credited Phillips with tournament MVP was wrong. Romeo won the award.

Jason Phillips won the tournament's Most Gentlemanly Player award (the box-score terminology used at the time) and was named to the tournament All-Star team at left wing. He recorded a hat trick in the final. On his tournament scoring line: contemporary accounts do not agree. Narrative scorer lists from the five-game tournament credit Phillips with seven goals. A Vancouver Sun tournament summary (May 11 1987, image 495229991, byline Pap) lists five goals and seven assists. The discrepancy remains unresolved.

The tournament All-Star team, as published in The Province (May 10 1987, image 502033093), was: C Bill McDougall (Humboldt), LW Jason Phillips (Richmond), RW Duncan Ryhorchuk (Humboldt), D Rob Rice (Humboldt), D Matt Hervey (Richmond), G Grant Robb (Pembroke). The selection was made before the semifinals; Romeo's tournament MVP was announced separately after the final.

Kurtenbach: "That third goal put the crimpers on it. They haven't failed me all year. In the stress situations, they've come through like gangbusters." Humboldt coach Bernie Lynch: "We're 156-29 in the last two years, but couldn't win the game we wanted to win. It's bitter."

Across the Abbott Cup and Centennial Cup, Richmond and Humboldt played nine times in three weeks. Richmond won five. Each team scored 30 goals.
