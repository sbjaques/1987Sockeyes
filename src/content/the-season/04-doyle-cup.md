# Doyle Cup

The Western Canada Centennial Cup semifinal — the Doyle Cup — was a best-of-seven against the Red Deer Rustlers, the AJHL champions. The series opened April 7 at Richmond Arena.

Richmond had won fifteen straight playoff games. They won the opener 11-4. Dave Tomlinson scored twenty-one seconds in; Matt Hervey doubled the lead moments later. Red Deer's Kevan Melrose and Greg Pulliam replied on the power play and it was 4-2 after the first period, but Richmond pulled away. They outshot the Rustlers 60-34 (Red Deer Advocate Apr 8 1987, image 558334501).

Game 2 put the streak to seventeen. Richmond trailed 4-2 after two periods. In the third, Red Deer defenceman Keith Lightbown drew a major penalty and game misconduct for attacking Dave Tomlinson, who never dropped his gloves. Richmond scored four times on the ensuing five-minute power play — Hervey completed a hat trick, Rutledge tied the game, and Kozak added insurance 88 seconds after the go-ahead goal. Final: 6-4. Later that night, Red Deer forwards Rich Anderson and Brad Wilson climbed into the stands and were involved in an altercation with Richmond fans; Anderson received a gross misconduct and Wilson a match penalty (Red Deer Advocate Apr 9 1987, images 558334781, 558334839, 558334897).

The series moved to Red Deer for Games 3 and 4. Richmond had gone 17 games without a loss. They lost the next two.

In Game 3, Richmond led 2-0 after the first period and were outscored 5-0 in the second in a 6-3 defeat before 1,628 fans. Game 4 was worse: a 7-1 loss with 392 combined penalty minutes (221 to Richmond) and a 30-minute second-period delay caused by four on-ice fights and a fracas behind the Richmond bench involving eggs thrown by the crowd. The CAHA warned both clubs that continued fan misconduct could force the series to be awarded, cancelled, or moved to a neutral site. The Rustlers scored four power-play goals. Jamie Stewart started in goal for Richmond in place of Romeo (Red Deer Advocate Apr 13 1987, images 558335595, 558335628, 558335639).

Red Deer won Game 5 in 10-minute overtime, 7-5, before 1,672 fans to take a 3-2 series lead. Dixon Ward scored the overtime winner fifty-nine seconds in. Jason Phillips had a penalty shot stopped by Rustler goaltender Weiss with 24 seconds left in regulation. Romeo started but was relieved by Stewart at 8:40 of the second period after falling behind 4-3. Richmond outshot Red Deer 51-45 across sixty-plus minutes. With the CAHA monitoring, the home crowd was notably better-behaved (Red Deer Advocate Apr 15 1987, images 558335948, 558335956; Vancouver Sun Apr 15 1987, image 495194273).

Richmond arrived at Game 6 needing a win or their season was over.

They won 7-3 before what official records listed as 1,732 fans (the Red Deer Advocate's game report put it at 2,020). Richmond led 5-1 after the first period. Rob Clarke recorded a power-play hat trick in the first period alone, scoring at 7:38, 19:13, and 19:29. Tomlinson and Kozak added two more. Stewart went the full distance in goal; Rustler starter Weiss was pulled after sixteen first-period shots and eleven saves (Red Deer Advocate Apr 16 1987, images 558336101, 558336127; Vancouver Sun Apr 16 1987, image 495194669).

Game 7 was in Red Deer. The Rustlers' coach, Larry Billows, would later say his team — playing their twenty-seventh playoff game in forty-seven days — simply ran out of gas.

The score was 9-1. It was scoreless after the first period, then Richmond scored five unanswered in the second and added four in the third. Tomlinson scored a hat trick. Clarke, Claringbull, Phillips, Gunn, McCormick, and Bobbitt each scored once — nine goals across seven different scorers. Pete deGraaf spoiled Stewart's shutout bid at 13:14 of the third on a Rustler power play. Richmond outshot Red Deer 39-34. Stewart went the distance again (Red Deer Advocate Apr 18 1987, images 558336223, 558336261; Calgary Herald Apr 18 1987, image 483730219; Edmonton Journal Apr 18 1987, image 473040181).

The individual game details for Game 7 — goal times, assists, and penalties — come from narrative accounts only. No period-by-period box score for that game has been found in the scraped newspaper corpus; the scorers listed above are confirmed but the precise sequences are not fully documented.

Kurtenbach said afterward that his team had been the better club across the final three games.
