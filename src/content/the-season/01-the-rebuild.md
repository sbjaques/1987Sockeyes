# The Rebuild

In July 1986, the Richmond Sockeyes changed coaches. Muzz MacPherson was out. Orland Kurtenbach was hired as head coach and director of hockey operations. Mike O'Brien was named assistant coach. The Vancouver Sun reported the appointments on November 4, 1986, in a piece by Arv Olson (image 494902975).

Kurtenbach came with credentials that were unusual in Junior A hockey. He had played 13 NHL seasons, mostly with the New York Rangers and Vancouver Canucks. When the Canucks entered the NHL in 1970, he was their first captain. He later served as an NHL head coach and had been working as a scout and development coach in the years before Richmond. He was in his mid-50s when the Sockeyes brought him in.

Behind him on the organizational chart was Bruce Taylor, the team owner. Taylor was a Burnaby businessman who had owned the Burnaby Bluehawks in 1985. He had taken ownership of the Sockeyes and set about funding a competitive roster. Under his ownership the team had won the Coast Division in 1986, and he had designs on taking the next step. Team president Horst Willkomm operated the club day-to-day under Taylor (The Province Apr 14 1987, p.45, image 502036260).

What Kurtenbach built in the summer and fall of 1986 was a roster heavy with overage players and former Western Hockey League veterans. The BCJHL had no restrictions on 20-year-olds, and Kurtenbach and Taylor recruited at least a dozen players with WHL experience. Jim Gunn, a Prince George defenceman who had played for the Victoria Cougars and Lethbridge Broncos before walking away from major junior at 19, described the recruiting call from Kurtenbach as the deciding factor. Approximately eight players from the eventual championship roster had been NHL draft picks. Many of the rest would go on to NCAA programs or professional tryouts.

The team's leadership structure was settled before the season started. Trevor Dickie, wearing number 21, served as team captain. Dickie had come to Richmond from the WHL's New Westminster Bruins, where he had played in 1984-85. The Vancouver Sun's March 25, 1987 photo caption following the BCJHL final identified him explicitly as "Richmond captain Trevor Dickie" (image 495157862). Bill Hardy, number 16, was assistant captain — confirmed in the Red Deer Advocate during the Doyle Cup run.

Frank Romeo was not part of the original roster. He was a goaltender acquired late in the season who started only in the playoffs. Dave Tomlinson described him in a 2012 Richmond News interview as a late pickup who became the team's playoff starter.

By October 1986, Kurtenbach had his roster. He had a owner willing to spend, a captain with major-junior experience, a goaltender for the regular season and another waiting in reserve, and a core of skilled, older players who had something to prove in Junior A. The season began with the Sockeyes carrying expectations they had not carried before.
